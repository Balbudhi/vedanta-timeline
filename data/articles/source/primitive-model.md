# PRIMITIVE_MODEL

## Purpose

A compact analytic framework. Grant a small number of structural primitives — each a precisely-defined axis with a small range of values — and from each consistent assignment derive a Vedāntic school's position. The point is not to flatten the schools but to make their actual structure visible, so that the difference between two schools is exhibited as a small, named delta in primitive-space rather than as an unanalyzable doctrinal incommensurability.

Cross-engagement: at the substrate structure axis the two texts choose differently. Akalanka ([primary text](cite://umasvati/tattvartha-sutra/1.34-35)) commits to `many-coordinate-reals`; this section commits to `one-self-standing`.

This document is the analytic complement to `COMPARATIVE_CLAIMS_FRAMEWORK.md`. The four-verdict schema there asks *what kind of disagreement is this?*; the primitive model here asks *what is the minimal world-model difference that produces this disagreement?* A pair of schools that share every primitive value except one cannot be in incommensurable disagreement; a pair that diverges on five primitives may be.

The model operates under the project's hard constraints. *Mithyā ≠ asat*: the modal status of *mithyā* is itself a primitive value (`mode_status = mithya-not-asat`), distinct from `unreal-asat`. Madhva is not "dualism"; he is a specific assignment whose substrate-axis is `hierarchical-dependent` (graded, *taratamya*-structured) rather than `many-substances`. Gods are not symbols; the manifest order is a real instantiation under whichever causation primitive the school selects.

## §1 — The Primitives

Nine axes, each with a small enumerated range. A school's position is, to first order, a tuple in the product space of these axes. (Genuine sub-school variation is captured by allowing two or more thinkers within a school to assign different values on a contested axis — e.g., Bhāmatī vs Vivaraṇa on `locus_of_avidya`.)

Cross-engagement: against Abhinavagupta: `sublatable-not-null` rather than `expressive-manifestation` for the manifestation status axis ([primary text](cite://abhinavagupta/tantraloka/1.16)).

### P1. `substrate_structure`
*How many fundamental substances and in what dependence-relation?*

Cross-engagement: Arthur Schopenhauer takes the identity relation axis to a different commitment — `self-expression-or-appearance` at [primary text](cite://schopenhauer/the-world-as-will-and-representation/1.1) against `non-otherness` here.

Range:
- `one-substance` — exactly one ontological substrate; everything that exists is either identical with it or a mode/appearance/transformation of it. (Advaita, Śuddhādvaita, Avibhāga-advaita, Pratyabhijñā.)
- `one-substance-with-real-internal-distinctions` — one substrate, but with real *aṃśa*-parts or *viśeṣa*-distinguishers internal to it. (Viśiṣṭādvaita, classical Bhedābheda in some readings, Acintya-Bhedābheda.)
- `hierarchical-dependent` — one wholly independent reality (*svatantra*) and a real plurality of dependent realities (*paratantra*) whose being is asymmetrically grounded in the independent. Dependence is the *form* of their reality, not its negation. (Madhva.)
- `many-substances-coordinate` — irreducibly many fundamental substances on the same ontological footing. (Sāṅkhya, Nyāya-Vaiśeṣika; *not* Vedānta — included only as the contrast Vedānta uniformly rejects.)

### P2. `mode_status`
*What is the ontological status of the world and finite selves qua not-being-the-substrate?*

Cross-engagement: Akshapada Gautama takes the selfhood structure axis to a different commitment — `substantial-self` at [primary text](cite://aksapada/nyaya-sutra/1.1.1) against `witness-self` here.

Range:
- `real-dependent-mode` — modes are fully real, their reality consisting in their dependence on the substrate. (Viśiṣṭādvaita; Madhva on *paratantra*; Vallabha on *jagat*.)
- `real-pariṇāma` — modes are real transformations of the substrate; the substrate genuinely undergoes (or "appears to undergo without alteration") modification. (Bhāskara's *aupādhika*; Vallabha's *avikṛta-pariṇāma*; Sāṅkhya as comparator.)
- `mithya-not-asat` — modes are *sad-asad-vilakṣaṇa*: neither absolutely real nor absolutely unreal; *vyāvahārika* under the substrate, but not *asat*. (Advaita.) **Distinct from `unreal-asat` by axis-fiat.**
- `acintya-bhedabheda` — modes are simultaneously identical with and distinct from the substrate, where the conjunction is *acintya* (beyond the law of non-contradiction as a metaphysical, not merely epistemic, claim). (Caitanya/Jīva Gosvāmī; with care, Nimbārka's *svābhāvika-bhedābheda* as a non-acintya neighbor.)
- `unreal-asat` — modes are simply non-existent. (No Vedānta school occupies this; the Mādhyamika comparator approximates it; Madhva's polemic *charges* Advaita with it, which the user's T2 rejects.)

### P3. `identity_relation_jiva_brahman`
*What is the relation between the individual self and the supreme reality?*

Cross-engagement: Anandabodha's reading at [primary text](cite://sarvajnatman/samksepa-sariraka/1.319-352) fills the finite cognition axis with `positive-ignorance`. The present section fills it with `adhyasa-or-superimposition`.

Range:
- `numerical-identity` — the *jīva* is, on final analysis, numerically identical with Brahman; apparent multiplicity is *upādhi*-conditioned. (Advaita: the *sākṣin/kūṭastha* IS Brahman.)
- `aṃśa-aprthak-siddha` — the *jīva* is a real *aṃśa* (part) of Brahman, *aprthak-siddha* (incapable of being established as separate) but not numerically identical. (Rāmānuja: *śarīra-śarīrī-bhāva*.)
- `bimba-pratibimba-with-eternal-distinction` — the *jīva* is a *pratibimba* (image) of Brahman as *bimba*, where the image-relation carries structural similarity (*sādṛśya*) but irreducible numerical distinction. (Madhva.)
- `bhedabheda-svabhavika` — *jīva* and Brahman are simultaneously different and non-different *by their own nature*, the conjunction being intelligible (not *acintya*). (Bhāskara *aupādhika*; Yādava *svābhāvika*; Nimbārka.)
- `bhedabheda-acintya` — same conjunction, but its consistency is beyond logic — a metaphysical *acintya*-modality. (Caitanya / Jīva Gosvāmī.)
- `pratyabhijna-recognition` — the *jīva* IS Śiva but in a self-veiled mode, and liberation is *recognition* (*pratyabhijñā*) of that fact via *svātantrya-śakti*. (Trika, comparator only.)

### P4. `causation_substrate_to_world`
*What kind of causal relation holds between Brahman and the world?*

Cross-engagement: on the epistemic authority axis Ayon Maharaj reads `comparative-theological-reading` ([primary text](cite://maharaj/infinite-paths-to-infinite-reality/1)); the present section reads `scripture-dominant`.

Range:
- `vivarta` — apparent transformation; substrate is unchanged, world is its appearance. (Classical Advaita post-Maṇḍana; Sureśvara, Vivaraṇa.)
- `pariṇāma-satkārya` — real transformation; effect pre-exists in the cause and is genuinely produced. (Sāṅkhya; Bhāskara on *jagat*.)
- `avikṛta-pariṇāma` — substrate transforms without alteration of its essential nature; the transformation is real but the substrate's *svarūpa* is preserved. (Vallabha; close to Acintya-Bhedābheda.)
- `nimitta-and-upādāna-by-real-dependence` — Brahman is both efficient and material cause, where "material" means *the locus on which the world's being depends*, not *the stuff the world is made of*. (Madhva's distinctive reading.)
- `body-soul-causation` — Brahman is cause as the *śarīrī* of a real body that includes *cit* and *acit*; *pariṇāma* applies to the body, not to Brahman's *svarūpa*. (Rāmānuja.)
- `acintya-śakti-pariṇāma` — Brahman's *śakti* really transforms while Brahman remains unchanged; the consistency is *acintya*. (Caitanya/Jīva.)
- `svātantrya-ābhāsa` — the world is a free self-projection (*ābhāsa*) of the supreme consciousness's *svātantrya-śakti*. (Pratyabhijñā, comparator.)

### P5. `locus_of_avidya`
*Where does ignorance reside, and what is its ontological status?* (Applies primarily to schools admitting *avidyā*; for schools that don't, this axis is `n/a` and the school is identified by that fact.)

Cross-engagement: same epistemological register, different commitments at the truth-structure axis. Baladeva Vidyabhushana: `single-absolute-truth`, [primary text](cite://badarayana/brahma-sutra/1.1.1). Here: `hierarchical-standpoint-truth`.

Range:
- `brahman-as-locus` — *avidyā* has Brahman as its locus, with Brahman as both locus (*āśraya*) and content (*viṣaya*) under different aspects. (Vivaraṇa: Prakāśātman, Sarvajñātman.)
- `jiva-as-locus` — *avidyā* is located in the *jīva*; Brahman is its content but not its locus. (Bhāmatī: Vāchaspati.)
- `tritiya-padartha` — *avidyā* is a third entity, neither *sat* nor *asat* nor identical with either Brahman or *jīva*; *anirvacanīya*. (Ānandabodha, Citsukha; later Advaita scholasticism.)
- `aupādhika` — the limiting condition (*upādhi*) is real but conventional; no *anirvacanīya* third entity is required. (Bhāskara.)
- `n/a-no-avidya-posited` — the school does not admit *avidyā* in the Advaitic technical sense; it accounts for finite cognition by other means (e.g., *taratamya*-graded *svarūpa-jñāna* in Madhva; *jīva-svarūpa-saṅkoca* in Viśiṣṭādvaita; *tirodhāna-śakti* in Vallabha; *ābhāsa* in Pratyabhijñā). (Madhva, Rāmānuja, Vallabha, Pratyabhijñā.)

### P6. `bheda_count`
*How many irreducible differences (*bheda*) does the school treat as real-at-the-final-level?*

Cross-engagement: Bannanje Govindacharya lands on `devotion-and-grace` for the practice-path axis ([primary text](cite://madhva/tattva-sankhyana/1-4)); this section lands on `knowledge-discipline`.

Range:
- `0` — no *bheda* is real; all difference is *vyāvahārika*. (Advaita.)
- `1-internal` — one substrate with internal difference between *cit*, *acit*, and *Iśvara*-as-śarīrī, real but not ultimate-substrate-multiplying. (Viśiṣṭādvaita.)
- `5-pañca-bheda` — Brahman/jīva, Brahman/jaḍa, jīva/jīva, jīva/jaḍa, jaḍa/jaḍa. None *vyāvahārika*; none dissolves in *mokṣa*. (Madhva.)
- `bhedabheda-counted-as-both` — difference and non-difference both real; counting *bheda* alone misdescribes the position. (Bhāskara, Nimbārka, Caitanya.)

### P7. `epistemic_authority_structure`
*What is the *pramāṇa* hierarchy and the role of immediate experience?*

Cross-engagement: same soteriological register, different commitments at the soteric end axis. Alfred North Whitehead: `not-soteriological`, [primary text](cite://whitehead/process-and-reality/1.2). Here: `identity-with-ground`.

Range:
- `sruti-supreme-with-anubhava-confirming` — *śruti* is the sole source of supra-empirical truth; *anubhava* (immediate experience) confirms it but is not an independent *pramāṇa* for Brahman-knowledge. (Śaṅkara, classical Advaita.)
- `sruti-coordinate-with-anubhava` — *śruti* and *anubhava* are coordinate; the saving cognition is itself a kind of *anubhava* whose object is given by *śruti*. (Maṇḍana; some Vivaraṇa.)
- `sruti-anumana-pratyaksha-equally-valid-each-in-domain` — three *pramāṇa*-s, each fully valid in its own domain; *śruti* does not override perception within perception's domain. (Madhva's *yathārtha-khyāti*: every cognition is true relative to its determinate object.)
- `sruti-and-bhakti-experience` — the saving cognition is *bhakti*, an experiential mode whose validity is co-constituted with *śruti*. (Caitanya/Jīva, Vallabha.)
- `agama-and-svatantrya-recognition` — scriptural *āgama* plus the recognitive self-disclosure of *svātantrya-śakti*. (Pratyabhijñā.)

### P8. `moksha_state`
*What is the structure of liberation?*

Range:
- `kaivalya-isolation` — isolation of pure consciousness from non-conscious matter. (Sāṅkhya, comparator.)
- `identity-without-distinction` — *aikya*: the *jīva*-as-such was never anything other than Brahman; "liberation" is the cessation of *adhyāsa*. (Advaita.)
- `union-with-internal-distinction-preserved` — the *jīva* attains *sāyujya* with Brahman while retaining its *aṃśa*-status. (Viśiṣṭādvaita.)
- `eternal-service-with-irreducible-distinction` — *mukti* is *bhakti*-mediated *aparokṣa-jñāna* of Viṣṇu, with *jīva*-distinction permanent and *taratamya* preserved. (Madhva.)
- `loving-participation-acintya` — *prema* in the *līlā* of Kṛṣṇa, simultaneously identical with and distinct from him. (Caitanya/Jīva.)
- `recognition-of-svatantrya` — *pratyabhijñā* of one's identity with Śiva. (Trika, comparator.)

### P9. `bhakti_role`
*What is the role of devotion in the path and in the goal?*

Range:
- `preparatory-only` — *bhakti* is *sādhana*, useful for purifying the mind to receive *jñāna*; not constitutive of liberation itself. (Classical Śaṅkara-bhāṣya.)
- `coordinate-with-jnana` — *bhakti* and *jñāna* are coordinate disciplines, neither subordinate to the other. (Maṇḍana on *upāsanā*; some Bhāmatī.)
- `constitutive-of-saving-cognition` — *bhakti* is *itself* the saving cognition; *jñāna* without *bhakti* does not liberate. (Rāmānuja, Madhva, Vallabha.)
- `sole-and-self-sufficient-means` — *bhakti* alone, and especially *prapatti/śaraṇāgati*, suffices; even disciplines normally required are dispensable. (Tenkalai; in a different register, Caitanya's *rāgānuga-bhakti*.)
- `pratyabhijna-as-non-bhakti-recognition` — the saving act is recognitive, not devotional in the *bhakti*-tradition's sense. (Trika.)

## §2 — Analogies as Primitive Instantiations

Each classical analogy compresses a specific tuple of primitive values. Reading the analogy off correctly tells you which school it natively serves. Reading it off incorrectly is a frequent source of cross-school polemic.

| Analogy | Sanskrit | Primitive content | Native school |
|---|---|---|---|
| Wave and ocean | *taraṅga-jala-nyāya* | `substrate = one`; `mode_status = real-dependent-mode`; `identity = aṃśa-aprthak-siddha`. Modes are real, not illusory; their being is the ocean's being-as-undulated. | Viśiṣṭādvaita-leaning; Vallabha; *not* Advaita in its strict reading (Advaita uses it only loosely, with disclaimers). |
| Spark and fire | *agni-visphuliṅga-nyāya* | `substrate = one`; `identity = bhedabheda` (one substance, distinct identities); from *Bṛhadāraṇyaka* 2.1.20. The sparks are *of* the fire (same substance) but *are not* the fire (distinct identity). | Bhedābheda — Bhāskara, Yādava, Nimbārka; later Acintya-Bhedābheda re-reads it under `acintya`. |
| Rope and snake | *rajju-sarpa-nyāya* | `substrate = one (real)`; `mode_status = mithya-not-asat`; `causation = vivarta`. The substrate is real; the appearance is *not asat* (it was experienced) but is sublated on cognition of the substrate. | Advaita (Śaṅkara, Sureśvara). Critical: the snake is neither *sat* (it isn't there) nor *asat* (you saw it). This is where `mithya-not-asat` gets its content. |
| Pot and clay | *mṛd-ghaṭa-nyāya* | `substrate = one`; `causation = pariṇāma-satkārya`; `mode_status = real-pariṇāma`. The pot is real-as-modification of real clay. From *Chāndogya* 6.1.4. | Sāṅkhya for the cosmological *prakṛti*-claim; Bhāskara and Vallabha for Vedāntic *pariṇāma*; Advaita explicitly *re-interprets* this passage as *vivarta*-supporting via the "name-and-form" reading. |
| Reflection in mirror / reflected sun in water | *bimba-pratibimba-nyāya*, *jala-sūryaka-nyāya* | Two readings: **(a) Vivaraṇa-Advaitic** — `substrate = one`; `identity = numerical-identity`; the reflection has no independent substance, only conventional locus. **(b) Madhva-realist** — `substrate = hierarchical-dependent`; `identity = bimba-pratibimba-with-eternal-distinction`; the reflection has its own real *svarūpa*, asymmetrically grounded in the *bimba*. | The *same analogy carries opposite primitive content depending on whether the image is treated as substantively-empty (Sarvajñātman) or substantively-real-but-dependent (Madhva). This is the canonical case of analogy-underdetermines-doctrine.* |
| King and servant | *rāja-bhṛtya-nyāya* | `substrate = hierarchical-dependent`; `identity = bimba-pratibimba`; `bheda_count = 5`; `mokṣa = eternal-service`. Full ontological distinction with asymmetric dependence. | Madhva. |
| Body and soul | *śarīra-śarīrī-bhāva* | `substrate = one-substance-with-real-internal-distinctions`; `identity = aṃśa-aprthak-siddha`; `causation = body-soul-causation`. The body is real, distinct from the soul, yet inseparable and *for-the-soul*. | Rāmānuja. The defining Viśiṣṭādvaitic primitive. |
| Drop and ocean | *bindu-sāgara-nyāya* | `mode_status = acintya-bhedabheda`; the drop is simultaneously identical with the ocean (same substance) and distinct (numerable, locatable). The *acintya* operator forbids dissolving the conjunction in either direction. | Caitanya, Jīva Gosvāmī. **Distinguish carefully** from the wave-ocean analogy, which is *not* acintya-loaded. |
| Iron in fire | *taptāyas-piṇḍa-nyāya* / *agni-loha-nyāya* | Shared property without identity of substance: the iron becomes hot but is not fire. Used as a counter-example: warns against inferring identity from shared attributes. | Polemical instrument across schools — Madhva uses it against Advaitic identity-inference; Advaita uses it for *cit-acit-saṃyoga* analysis. |
| Self and dream | *svapna-dṛṣṭānta* | `causation = vivarta`; `mode_status = mithya-not-asat`; the dream-world is the witness's content, real-while-experienced, sublated on waking. | Gauḍapāda's *Vaitathya-Prakaraṇa*; foundational for Advaita's *vivarta*. Used cautiously by other schools because over-extension yields *asat*-collapse. |
| Sun and rays | *sūrya-raśmi-nyāya* / *sūryāṃśu-nyāya* | `substrate = one-substance-with-real-internal-distinctions`; `identity = aṃśa`. Rays are not other than the sun yet are distinguishable in their own right. | Viśiṣṭādvaita; some Bhedābheda usage; Madhva re-reads under *bimba-pratibimba*. |
| Mirage / deer-thirst | *māyā-mṛga-nyāya*, *mṛga-tṛṣṇikā* | `mode_status = mithya-not-asat`; the appearance is not *asat* (it appears) but is sublated on approach. | Advaita. **Misuse note:** translating this as "the world is unreal" violates the *mithyā ≠ asat* constraint. |
| Limiting adjunct | *upādhi-nyāya* (e.g., the same space appearing as pot-space and jar-space) | `substrate = one`; the apparent multiplicity of selves is *upādhi*-conditioned, not substantive. | Advaita, especially Vivaraṇa. Bhāskara accepts the analogy but reads the *upādhi* as real-and-substantive (*aupādhika-bhedābheda*) rather than as *anirvacanīya*. |
| Milk and whiteness / fire and heat | *kṣīra-śaitya-nyāya*, *agni-uṣṇa-nyāya* | `viśeṣa`-distinction within a single substance: attribute is genuinely distinguishable from substance without being a separate entity. | Madhva's *viśeṣa*-doctrine; Rāmānuja's *dharma-bhūta-jñāna*. |
| Recognition of a long-lost friend | *pratyabhijñā-dṛṣṭānta* | The object was never not-itself; the cognitive act is recognition, not acquisition. | Pratyabhijñā / Trika as defining primitive; Advaita uses a related formula in *tat-tvam-asi* exegesis. |

**Cross-cutting observation.** Several analogies carry different primitive content in different schools' hands. *Bimba-pratibimba* is the cleanest case: identical analogy, opposite identity-primitive (Sarvajñātman: numerical-identity; Madhva: bimba-pratibimba-with-eternal-distinction). This means analogy-citation alone never settles a doctrinal question; one must read the analogy *together with* the primitive-tuple the author has independently committed to.

**Notes on intra-school divergence captured by the table.**

- **Advaita on `locus_of_avidyā`** — the only axis on which classical Advaita splinters. Bhāmatī (jīva), Vivaraṇa (Brahman), later scholastic (third-entity). All other primitives are shared.
- **Viśiṣṭādvaita on `bhakti_role`** — Vaḍakalai/Tenkalai differ only on the sub-axis of bhakti's self-sufficiency (monkey vs cat). All eight other primitives identical.
- **Acintya-Bhedābheda vs Nimbārka** — differ only on `mode_status` and `identity_relation` by the *acintya* operator's presence/absence; otherwise structurally close.
- **Vallabha vs Acintya-Bhedābheda** — differ on `causation` (avikṛta-pariṇāma vs acintya-śakti-pariṇāma) and on the modal force of the bhedābheda. Often confused; the primitive table separates them cleanly.
- **Vijñānabhikṣu** — uniquely double-valued on `mokṣa_state`, which is the explicit harmonization-move of his Avibhāga-advaita.

## §4 — Cross-School Comparison: Testing the Convergence Hypothesis

The user's working hypothesis: *the schools may not be disagreeing as much as the surface polemic suggests, once their world-models are unpacked*. We test this by computing, for each pair, the set of shared primitive-values and the residual divergent set, then asking whether the residual divergence is **substantive** (a real fork in metaphysics), **terminological** (the same structure differently labeled), or **contested** (scholars disagree about which).

**Most-revealing finding from §4.**

The pair-comparison shows that **the count of shared primitives is consistently high — typically 4 or more out of 9 — even between maximally-polemical pairs (Advaita-Tattva-vāda)**. The residual disagreement is concentrated on a small, well-named set of axes: principally `mode_status` (P2), `identity_relation` (P3), `bheda_count` (P6), and the modal force of the bhedābheda relation (the *acintya* operator within P2/P3).

This gives the user's hypothesis a precise, defensible form: **the schools are not in massive incommensurable disagreement; they share most of their world-model and diverge on a small number of named axes**. The convergence is real *and* the divergence is real. The schools are neither "all saying the same thing" (the residual divergences are substantive on specific named axes) nor "talking past each other" (the shared primitives are genuinely shared, not merely verbally aligned).

The most-revealing single finding is on **P1 substrate_structure**: Madhva's `hierarchical-dependent` and Advaita's `one-substance` carve isomorphic two-tier hierarchies (independent / dependent reality). The labels *svatantra/paratantra* and *pāramārthika/vyāvahārika* differ; the structure does not. The genuine disagreement at P1 is not about whether there is a two-tier hierarchy but about whether the dependent tier is *real-as-dependent* (Madhva) or *mithyā-not-asat* (Advaita) — which is itself a P2 question, not a P1 question. **The headline metaphysical disagreement between Vedānta's most-opposed schools is not a substrate disagreement at all; it is a `mode_status` disagreement under a shared substrate-structure.**

## Constraint compliance

- *Mithyā ≠ asat* is enforced as **axis-fiat** in P2: `mithya-not-asat` and `unreal-asat` are distinct values, and no Vedānta school occupies the latter.
- Madhva is captured by `substrate_structure = hierarchical-dependent` (not `many-substances-coordinate`); his realism is the realism-of-dependence, not Cartesian dualism.
- Gods are not symbols: the manifest order is whichever `mode_status` value the school selects, and every value (except `unreal-asat`, which no school holds) treats it as ontologically meaningful.
- Non-duality is not world-denial: Advaita's tuple is `mithya-not-asat`, `vyāvahārika` reality, `jīvanmukti` — explicitly not annihilationist.
