# COMPARATIVE_CLAIMS_FRAMEWORK

## Purpose

The user's serious philosophical conviction is that the Vedāntic schools may not actually be disagreeing once their world-models and language are properly unpacked. This framework operationalizes that conviction into a disciplined comparative method that the site can apply pairwise across all schools — without sentimentalism, without "all paths the same" elision, and without forcing convergence where genuine disagreement exists.

The framework is built on **honest disambiguation**: at each pairwise comparison, we sort claims into four mutually exclusive verdicts. The site's value is precisely that it does not pre-decide which verdict applies; it reconstructs the world-models in enough rigor that the verdict becomes visible.

## The Four-Verdict Schema

For any apparent disagreement between two thinkers (or two schools), we classify the claim into exactly one of:

| Verdict | Definition | Example diagnostic |
|---|---|---|
| **(a) shared-presupposition** | Both schools share a deep world-model commitment that an outside reader might not realize is shared. The "disagreement" is a foreground figure painted on a shared background. | Both Madhva and Śaṅkara accept that scriptural testimony (*śruti*) is *apauruṣeya* and yields knowledge of what perception cannot reach. |
| **(b) terminological-equivalence / parallel-structure** | The claims look contradictory because the two schools use the same word with non-overlapping referents, OR use different words with overlapping referents, OR carve the same ontological hierarchy with different labels. Once the referents and structures are reconstructed, the structural claim is the same. | Madhva's "Viṣṇu is the only *svatantra* (independent) reality, all else *paratantra* (dependent)" and Advaita's "Brahman alone is *pāramārthika*, all else *vyāvahārika*" — both carve a two-tier hierarchy of independent vs. dependent reality; the labels differ, the structure is identical. |
| **(c) genuine-disagreement** | After full reconstruction of both world-models, the two schools commit to incompatible propositions. Their disagreement is not an artifact of language but a real fork in metaphysics. | Whether the *jīva* is *aṃśa* (a part) or *pratibimba* (a reflection) or *abhinna* (non-different) of/from Brahman — the three positions cannot all be literally true if "part," "reflection," and "non-difference" are taken in their technical senses. |
| **(d) contested-whether-(b)-or-(c)** | Scholars and traditions themselves disagree on whether the apparent disagreement reduces under unpacking. The site honestly flags the meta-disagreement. | Whether Maṇḍana's *Brahma-Siddhi* and Sureśvara's *Naiṣkarmya-Siddhi* are intra-Advaita variants of one position (the traditional view) or two distinct positions only later harmonized (the position associated with Hiriyanna; Hiriyanna's writings are not on disk in our corpus, cited via field-standard summary). |

**Discipline note.** The verdict applies to one *specific claim*, not to the relationship between two thinkers as a whole. A given pair of thinkers will typically yield a *mixture* of verdicts across different topics: shared on epistemology, terminological on ontology, genuinely disagreeing on soteriology. The site's value is the *granularity*.

## The Five Claim-Categories

Every comparative claim is filed under exactly one of:

### 1. Ontology — what fundamentally exists

Sub-axes:
- **Number of fundamental existents** (one, two, three [Brahman + souls + world]?)
- **Independence-relation** (which existents are dependent on which?)
- **Status of the world** (real, *mithyā*, *sat-asat-vilakṣaṇa*, *acintya-bhedābheda* with Brahman?)
- **Status of individual selves** (numerically distinct? merely apparently distinct? eternally distinct *aṃśa*-s?)
- **Modal status of difference itself** (is *bheda* a positive entity or a *vyāvṛtti* — exclusion?)

### 2. Causation — relation between Brahman and world

Sub-axes:
- **Type of cause** (*vivarta-vāda* — apparent transformation; *pariṇāma-vāda* — real transformation; *satkārya* / *asatkārya*; *ārambha-vāda* — origination; *avikāra-pariṇāma* — change-without-change)
- **Where causal-power locates** (in Brahman alone, in *māyā/prakṛti*, in *Lakṣmī* as *cit-śakti*, in *svātantrya*?)
- **Status of *upādāna* vs. *nimitta* cause** (does Brahman serve both? only one?)
- **Locus of avidyā** (Brahman? *jīva*? both? a *tṛtīya-padārtha* — third entity?)

### 3. Epistemology — pramāṇa hierarchy and method

Sub-axes:
- **Number and status of *pramāṇa*-s** (3 — perception, inference, testimony, as in classical Advaita; 6 — adding *upamāna*, *arthāpatti*, *anupalabdhi*, as in Bhāṭṭa Mīmāṃsā; the Madhva refinement)
- **Status of *śabda* / *śruti***: self-validating? validated by something else? coordinate with or superior to other pramāṇas?
- **Status of *anubhava* (immediate experience)**: confirming testimony? independent pramāṇa? object of testimony?
- **Theory of error** (*anyathā-khyāti*, *anirvacanīya-khyāti*, *akhyāti*, *yathārtha-khyāti* — Madhva's all-cognitions-true thesis)
- **Saving-cognition status**: is liberating cognition *jñāna* (Sureśvara), *bhāvanā* (Maṇḍana), *upāsanā* (Bhāskara, Rāmānuja), *bhakti* (Caitanya, Vallabha), or *prapatti* (Tenkalai)?

### 4. Soteriology — what is liberation

Sub-axes:
- **Identity of the liberated state** (*ekatva* — identity with Brahman; *sāyujya* — communion; *sālokya* — co-residence; *sārūpya* — same-form; *sārṣṭi* — same-power; *brahma-loka* — graded *krama-mukti*)
- **Persistence of *jīva*-individuality post-liberation** (annihilated? preserved? "as a wave to the ocean"?)
- **Possible in this life?** (*jīvanmukti* — yes for Advaita, no for strict Madhva; nuanced for others)
- **Effective means** (*jñāna* alone? *jñāna* + *karma*? *bhakti*? *prapatti*? *līlā*-recognition?)
- **Role of grace** (*kṛpā*) vs. effort (*sādhana*) — the Vaḍakalai/Tenkalai monkey/cat distinction in fully-systematized form

### 5. Ethics-and-Devotion — role of bhakti, dharma, and surrender

Sub-axes:
- **Status of *bhakti*** (preliminary discipline? saving cognition itself? superior to *jñāna*? identical to *jñāna* under another aspect?)
- **Status of *karma*** (purificatory? to be abandoned? to be done as *niṣkāma*? to be done as *pūjā*?)
- **Status of *dharma***: socially-instrumental? cosmologically-real? a *lakṣaṇā* of liberation?
- **Status of the personal Lord** (*Iśvara*) — provisional? ultimate? identical to Brahman? superior to nirguṇa-Brahman?
- **Surrender** (*prapatti*, *śaraṇāgati*, *ātma-nivedana*) as supplementary to other paths or as itself sufficient

## Worked example: Madhva–Advaita on the central ontological claim

**Surface formulations.**

- Madhva: "Viṣṇu alone is *svatantra* (independent reality); all else — souls, world, time, space — is *paratantra* (dependent reality). The *jīva* is *eternally distinct* from Viṣṇu and *eternally subordinate*."
- Advaita (Śaṅkara): "Brahman alone is *pāramārthika-sat* (absolutely real); the world and the individual *jīva* are *vyāvahārika* (empirically real but ultimately *mithyā*). The *ātman* is *abhinna* (non-different) from Brahman."

**Apparent contradiction.** Madhva insists on eternal distinction; Advaita on identity.

**World-model unpacking.**

- *What does Madhva mean by "*jīva*"?* The eternally-distinct, *bimba-pratibimba*-related individual self, with intrinsic *svarūpa-jñāna* and *svarūpa-ānanda* graded by *taratamya* (hierarchy). The *jīva* is real precisely because it is dependent on Viṣṇu; dependence-relation is the *form* of its reality, not its negation.
- *What does Śaṅkara (in the technical *bhāṣya* layer) mean by "*ātman*"?* Not the empirical ego (*ahaṅkāra*), which Śaṅkara explicitly denies is Brahman; not even the *jīva* qua *upādhi*-conditioned cognizer; but only the *sākṣin* / *kūṭastha* — the witness-consciousness that is the substrate of all empirical states.
- *What does Madhva mean by "Viṣṇu"?* The *paripūrṇa* (fully-actual) personal Lord with all auspicious attributes, the *bimba* of which all *jīvas* are *pratibimba*-s.
- *What does Śaṅkara mean by "Brahman"?* In the sūtra-bhāṣya level: *nirguṇa* — without empirically-determining attributes — but *not* without intrinsic nature (*svarūpa-lakṣaṇa* of *sat-cit-ānanda*). Śaṅkara explicitly affirms *Iśvara* as the *saguṇa* aspect under whose *upādhi* the world appears.

**Verdict mapping.**

| Claim-axis | Verdict | Commentary |
|---|---|---|
| "Two-tier ontological hierarchy of independent vs. dependent reality" | (b) parallel-structure | Both schools carve the same hierarchy; *svatantra/paratantra* and *pāramārthika/vyāvahārika* label the same structure with different valences. |
| "The empirical ego is Brahman/Viṣṇu" | (a) shared denial | Both schools deny it. Śaṅkara's *ahaṅkāra-anātma* and Madhva's *jīva-pāratantrya* are structurally parallel denials. |
| "The substrate-witness consciousness is numerically identical with the supreme Lord" | (c) genuine-disagreement | Madhva says no (the *bimba-pratibimba* relation is a *real distinction* with structural similarity, not numerical identity); Śaṅkara (at the *bhāṣya* level) says yes (the *sākṣin* IS Brahman under no real *upādhi*-relation, only an apparent one). |
| "The world has its own independent existence" | (a) shared denial | Both deny it. Advaita: world is *mithyā* under Brahman; Madhva: world is *paratantra* on Viṣṇu. The *form* of denial differs, but neither is an independence-realist about the world. |
| "*Mithyā* means non-existent (*asat*)" | (d) contested | Madhva's polemic charges Advaita with this (and the *Mithyātvānumāna-Khaṇḍana* targets exactly that reading); Advaita (esp. Vimuktātman, Vidyāraṇya) explicitly denies it (*mithyā* is *sad-asad-vilakṣaṇa* — neither sat nor asat). The user's T2 takes the Advaita-side: *mithyā ≠ asat*. So this is contested *between Madhva-as-polemicist and Advaita-as-self-interpretation*. |

**The user's argument** — that the schools may not actually be disagreeing — survives this analysis at exactly one critical place. The *only* genuine-disagreement claim is whether *sākṣin* IS the Lord or is *parallel-to-but-distinct-from* the Lord. Everything else is either shared, terminological, or contested-due-to-misreading. **The site can present this with full rigor without having to pretend the disagreement reduces to zero.**

## Worked example: Śaṅkara-as-bhāṣyakāra vs. Śaṅkara-as-stotra-author

**The user's specific challenge.** *Bhaja-Govindam*, *Saundarya-Laharī*, *Śivānanda-Laharī* (all `school-ascribed` to Śaṅkara) use Viśiṣṭādvaitan-sounding devotional language ("eternally yours, like a wave in the ocean," *Bhavāni tvaṃ dāse mayi vitara dṛṣṭiṃ sakaruṇām*). The Advaita living tradition embraces these as Śaṅkara's. So is the gap between Advaita-as-school and Viśiṣṭādvaita actually narrower than the academic *bhāṣya*-only level suggests?

**Verdict.** This is a meta-claim about *how to identify a school*, not a first-order doctrinal claim. The site should treat it as a separate axis: **school-as-corpus-of-securely-authored-bhāṣya** vs. **school-as-living-tradition-with-its-stotra-corpus**.

Concretely, every thinker entry's `engaged_works[]` includes `ascription_tier`, and the comparative-claims engine can be filtered: "compare Śaṅkara-as-securely-authored vs. Rāmānuja-as-securely-authored" yields one set of verdicts; "compare Śaṅkara-as-school-ascribed vs. Rāmānuja-as-school-ascribed" yields a distinguishably different set. **The site should expose this filter as a first-class control.**

## Pairwise comparison registry (initial scope)

The site should populate comparative-claim entries for at least the following pairs, in this order of priority:

1. **Advaita ↔ Viśiṣṭādvaita** — Śaṅkara/Sureśvara/Maṇḍana ↔ Yāmuna/Rāmānuja/Vedānta-Deśika. The *sapta-vidhā-anupapatti* axis. The *śarīra-śarīri-bhāva* alternative. Stotra-corpus disambiguation.
2. **Advaita ↔ Dvaita** — Madhusūdana ↔ Vyāsatīrtha. The *Advaita-Siddhi* / *Nyāyāmṛta* / *Tarka-Tāṇḍava* triangle. The *mithyātva* axis. The user's T2 polemic-defense.
3. **Advaita ↔ Bhedābheda (classical)** — Śaṅkara ↔ Bhāskara. The *māyā-as-Buddhist-import* charge. The *aupādhika-bhedābheda* alternative.
4. **Advaita ↔ Acintya-Bhedābheda** — Madhusūdana ↔ Jīva Gosvāmī. The *bhakti-as-saving-cognition* axis. The *acintya* (inconceivable) modal operator as the user's "non-duality of duality and non-duality" formula.
5. **Viśiṣṭādvaita ↔ Dvaita** — Rāmānuja/Vedānta-Deśika ↔ Madhva. Both reject Advaitic *mithyātva*; both affirm personal Lord; differ on whether *jīva*-multiplicity is a real distinction or an *aṃśa*-relation.
6. **Vaḍakalai ↔ Tenkalai (intra-Viśiṣṭādvaita)** — Vedānta-Deśika ↔ Pillai-Lokācārya. The monkey-cat distinction as the surrender-axis.
7. **Bhāmatī ↔ Vivaraṇa (intra-Advaita)** — Vāchaspati ↔ Prakāśātman. The locus-of-avidyā axis. The user's T7 builds explicitly on Vivaraṇa.
8. **Śuddhādvaita ↔ Advaita** — Vallabha ↔ Madhusūdana. *Avikṛta-pariṇāma* vs. *vivarta*. Identity-without-*māyā*.
9. **Acintya-Bhedābheda ↔ Dvaita** — Caitanya/Jīva ↔ Madhva (with the disputed Madhva-line claim of Caitanya). The *acintya-bhedābheda* reading of Madhva's *bimba-pratibimba*.
10. **Vijñānabhikṣu ↔ each school** — Avibhāga-advaita as explicit attempt to harmonize, hence interesting limit-case for the framework.
11. **Pratyabhijñā / Trika ↔ Advaita (Vivaraṇa)** — Abhinavagupta ↔ Prakāśātman. The *svātantrya* / *māyā-as-divine-self-veiling* axis. The user's T7 explicitly invokes this. **Flag clearly that Trika is comparator-only, not Vedānta.**

## Anti-patterns the framework rules out

- "All paths the same" — the framework forbids verdict (b) (terminological / parallel-structure) when the structures actually differ. The *only* path to verdict (b) is full reconstruction showing structural identity.
- Privileging one school as the "true" frame — verdicts must be symmetric. If Madhva-Advaita on the world is verdict-(b), it is so from both directions.
- Treating *mithyā* as *asat* — an explicit user-prohibition, baked into the framework (it shows up as a contested-(d) claim with a specific disambiguation note, not as a feature of Advaita).
- Treating Tattvavāda as crude dualism — Madhva entries must use *svatantra/paratantra* terminology, not "dualism."
- Treating gods as mere symbols — the framework's category 5 (ethics-and-devotion) treats divine forms as ontologically meaningful at the level where manifestation is meaningful (per the user's "Forms" section in the position brief).
