## Editorial introduction

These loci are dispersed, but the dispersion is systematic. No single short contiguous *adhikaraṇa* of the *Tantra-Dīpikā* contains all the passages already load-bearing in the site corpus for Rāghavendra's reading of the first *adhyāya*. The natural unit is therefore composite. It consists of the adhyāya-summary, the *ānandamayādhikaraṇa* at TD 1.1, the *suṣuptyutkrānti* discussion at TD 1.3, and the *jñeyatvavacana* discussion at TD 1.4. Taken together, they display one argumentative arc.

The adhyāya-summary states the semantic rule. Vedic *śabda* (authoritative word) of several lexical kinds converges on Viṣṇu, either immediately or by *paramparā* (mediated succession of signification). The *ānandamaya* passage then applies that rule to a difficult exegetical case. Bliss-language is graded, not flattened, and the *ānandamaya* is separated from dependent enjoyers precisely by the grammar of difference. The deep-sleep and death-transition passage adds a phenomenological test. Scriptural descriptions of *suṣupti* (deep sleep) and *utkrānti* (departure at death) preserve relation between *jīva* and Īśvara, but do not collapse the two. Finally, the discussion of *jñeyatvavacanāc ca* fixes the soteriological terminus. For the *mumukṣu* (seeker of liberation), Brahman alone is the proper object of liberating knowledge; what is merely inferential cannot occupy that place.

This is enough to show Rāghavendra's larger position. He is not merely repeating sectarian theses. He is treating lexical distribution, case-relations, and quoted Upaniṣadic fragments as philosophically binding. That is the point. In the architecture of the work, these first-adhyāya loci give a compact map of how ontology, scriptural semantics, and soteriology are meant to interlock.

## Translation

### TD 1.1, adhyāya summary, line 1

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *te ca śabdā anyatra-prasiddhā ubhayatra-prasiddhā anyatraiva-prasiddhāś ca*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Those words, moreover, are words established elsewhere, words established in both places, and words established only elsewhere.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| te | tad | nom pl masc | those |
| ca | ca | indecl | and, moreover |
| śabdā | śabda | nom pl masc | words |
| anyatra-prasiddhā | anyatra-prasiddha | nom pl masc | established elsewhere |
| ubhayatra-prasiddhā | ubhayatra-prasiddha | nom pl masc | established in both places |
| anyatraiva-prasiddhāś | anyatraiva-prasiddha | nom pl masc | established only elsewhere |
| ca | ca | indecl | and |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *anyatra-prasiddhā* [avyayībhāva-like lexical compound]: *anyatra prasiddhāḥ*.
- *ubhayatra-prasiddhā* [avyayībhāva-like lexical compound]: *ubhayatra prasiddhāḥ*.
- *anyatraiva-prasiddhāḥ* [avyayībhāva-like lexical compound]: *anyatra eva prasiddhāḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *śabdā*
- viśeṣaṇa: *anyatra-prasiddhā*, *ubhayatra-prasiddhā*, *anyatraiva-prasiddhāḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb is expressed in this line; the predicative force carries forward to *sādhayanti* in the next line.

**Note:**
The classification is preparatory. Rāghavendra first sorts Vedic words by their prior range of usage, and only then states what they prove. The semantic rule announced here is applied in the three later loci of this dossier.

### TD 1.1, adhyāya summary, line 2

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *sākṣāt paramparayā vā viṣṇum eva sṛṣṭy-ādi-vartṛtva-sādhya-guṇa-pūrṇatvena sādhayanti*

**English (line-by-line):**
> They establish Viṣṇu alone, either directly or by *paramparā* (mediated succession), as full of qualities established through creatorship and the rest of the divine functions.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| sākṣāt | sākṣāt | indecl | directly |
| paramparayā | paramparā | instr sg fem | by mediated succession |
| vā | vā | indecl | or |
| viṣṇum | viṣṇu | acc sg masc | Viṣṇu |

| sṛṣṭy-ādi-vartṛtva-sādhya-guṇa-pūrṇatvena | sṛṣṭy-ādi-vartṛtva-sādhya-guṇa-pūrṇatva | instr sg neut | by the state of being full of qualities established through creatorship and the rest |
| sādhayanti | √sādh | 3 pl laṭ parasmaipada, causative-active sense | establish, prove |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *sṛṣṭy-ādi-vartṛtva-sādhya-guṇa-pūrṇatvena* [extended tatpuruṣa chain]: *sṛṣṭyādibhiḥ vartṛtvaṃ sādhyate ye guṇāḥ, taiḥ pūrṇatvena*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: implicit *śabdāḥ* from the prior line
- karman: *viṣṇum*
- karaṇa: *paramparayā*
- hetu-karaṇa: *sṛṣṭy-ādi-vartṛtva-sādhya-guṇa-pūrṇatvena*

**Verbal modality:**
- *sādhayanti*: laṭ / parasmaipada / active-causative sense

**Note:**
This is the hermeneutic thesis in compact form. Not every word reaches Viṣṇu in the same way, but all are ordered toward that end. Compare the soteriological restriction in TD 1.4 *jñeyatvavacanāc ca*, where not every knowable can function as the terminus of liberating knowledge.

### TD 1.1, *bhedavyapadeśāc ca*, line 1

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *ānandamayo netara iti vartate |*

**English (line-by-line):**
> The reading stands as: "The *ānandamaya* (the bliss-full one) is not the other."

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| ānandamayo | ānandamaya | nom sg masc | the bliss-full one |
| na | na | indecl | not |
| itara | itara | nom sg masc | the other |
| iti | iti | indecl | thus |
| vartate | √vṛt | 3 sg laṭ ātmanepada | stands, is stated |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *ānandamayaḥ* [taddhita with *mayaṭ*]: *ānandena mayaḥ* / "constituted by bliss," in the inherited Upaniṣadic designation.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: implicit quoted proposition *ānandamayo na itaraḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *vartate*: laṭ / ātmanepada / middle

**Note:**
Rāghavendra begins with the construal itself. He does not yet argue for it. The philosophical work lies in identifying what the negation excludes.

### TD 1.1, *bhedavyapadeśāc ca*, line 2

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *ānandamayād bhedavyapadeśāc cānandamayo netara ity arthaḥ |*

**English (line-by-line):**
> The meaning is this: because there is a predication of difference from the *ānandamaya*, the *ānandamaya* is not the other.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| ānandamayād | ānandamaya | abl sg masc | from the ānandamaya |
| bhedavyapadeśāt | bhedavyapadeśa | abl sg masc | because of the predication of difference |
| ca | ca | indecl | and |
| ānandamayo | ānandamaya | nom sg masc | the ānandamaya |
| na | na | indecl | not |
| itara | itara | nom sg masc | the other |
| iti | iti | indecl | thus |
| arthaḥ | artha | nom sg masc | the meaning, purport |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *bhedavyapadeśāt* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *bhedasya vyapadeśāt*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- apādāna/hetu: *ānandamayād*
- hetu: *bhedavyapadeśāt*
- viṣaya of construed meaning: *ānandamayo na itaraḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb is expressed; the sentence is nominal, with *arthaḥ* functioning predicatively.

**Note:**
The ablative is decisive. Difference is said "from the ānandamaya," and that grammatical relation is what blocks identification with a lower referent. The same method reappears in TD 1.3, where case-relations in quoted śruti are made to bear ontological weight.

### TD 1.1, *bhedavyapadeśāc ca*, line 3

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *"sa eko brahmaṇa ānandaḥ" ityādinā viriñcadeḥ parimitānandam uktvā |*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Having stated, by the expression beginning "That is one joy of Brahman," a limited bliss in Viriñci and the rest,

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| sa | tad | nom sg masc | that |
| eko | eka | nom sg masc | one |
| brahmaṇa | brahman | gen sg neut | of Brahman |
| ānandaḥ | ānanda | nom sg masc | joy, bliss |
| ityādinā | ityādi | instr sg neut | by the expression beginning thus |
| viriñcadeḥ | viriñci-ādi | abl sg masc collective | from Viriñci and the rest |
| parimitānandam | parimitānanda | acc sg masc | limited bliss |
| uktvā | √vac | absolutive | having stated |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *ityādinā* [lexicalized compound]: *iti ādinā*.
- *viriñcadeḥ* [collective expression]: *viriñceḥ ādeḥ*.
- *parimitānandam* [karmadhāraya]: *parimita ānandaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- karaṇa: *ityādinā*
- apādāna/reference-base: *viriñcadeḥ*
- karman: *parimitānandam*

**Verbal modality:**
- *uktvā*: absolutive from √*vac*; subordinate prior action

**Note:**
The hierarchy is explicit. Viriñci, that is Brahmā, is not denied bliss; he is assigned a limited bliss. The later contrast with the *ānandamaya* therefore turns on scale and fullness, not on a flat opposition between bliss and non-bliss.

Line 3 and line 4 belong together. One text assigns limited bliss below; another marks the unsurpassable fullness of the *ānandamaya*. Rāghavendra's conclusion is therefore not merely lexical. It is a graded ontology read off from a graded scriptural diction.

### TD 1.3, *suṣuptyutkrāntyor bhedena*, line 1

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *na cābhedena sopapattiḥ |*

**English (line-by-line):**
> And there is no cogency on the basis of non-difference.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| na | na | indecl | not |
| ca | ca | indecl | and |
| abhedena | abheda | instr sg masc | by non-difference |
| sopapattiḥ | sa-upapatti | nom sg fem | cogency, tenability |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *sopapattiḥ* [prefixal formation used predicatively]: *sa-upapattiḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- karaṇa (rejected basis): *abhedena*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb is expressed; the nominal sentence has an understood copula.

**Note:**
The claim is negative and methodological. Rāghavendra is not yet restating the positive difference-thesis; he is saying that the relevant śruti cannot be made coherent by identity-language. The burden then shifts to the quoted texts.

### TD 1.4, *jñeyatvavacanāc ca*, line 1

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *brahmaṇa eva mumukṣu-jñeyatva-vacanād ānumānikāder jñeyatvāvacanāc cety arthaḥ |*

**English (line-by-line):**
> The meaning is this: because there is a statement that Brahman alone has *jñeyatva* (the status of being the proper object of knowledge) for the *mumukṣu* (seeker of liberation), and because there is no statement of knowability for what is merely *ānumānika* (inferentially posited) and the like.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| brahmaṇa | brahman | gen sg neut | of Brahman |
| eva | eva | indecl | alone |
| mumukṣu-jñeyatva-vacanād | mumukṣu-jñeyatva-vacana | abl sg neut | because of the statement of knowability for the seeker of liberation |
| ānumānikādeḥ | ānumānikādi | abl sg masc | from the inferential and the like |
| jñeyatvāvacanāc | jñeyatvāvacana | abl sg neut | because of the non-statement of knowability |
| ca | ca | indecl | and |
| iti | iti | indecl | thus |
| arthaḥ | artha | nom sg masc | the meaning |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *mumukṣu-jñeyatva-vacanād* [tatpuruṣa chain]: *mumukṣoḥ jñeyatvasya vacanāt*.
- *ānumānikādi* [collective expression]: *ānumānikaṃ cādi ca*.
- *jñeyatvāvacanāc* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *jñeyatvasya avacanāt*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- sambandha: *brahmaṇa*
- hetu: *mumukṣu-jñeyatva-vacanād*
- apavāda/exclusion-base: *ānumānikādeḥ*
- hetu: *jñeyatvāvacanāc*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb is expressed; the sentence is a nominal statement of purport.

**Note:**
The argument is restrictive, not merely affirmative. Brahman is not said to be one knowable among many; Brahman alone is marked out for the *mumukṣu*. This closes the door on a merely inferential ultimate as the soteriological end-point.

## Editorial closing

This dossier settles three things. First, Rāghavendra's first-adhyāya method is semantic before it is polemical. Second, scriptural language of bliss, deep sleep, departure, and knowability is read as ontologically discriminating. Third, the terminus of liberating knowledge is fixed as Brahman alone, not a merely inferred substitute. What the dossier does not settle is every later consequence of that fixation. It does not yet give the full later articulation of the relation between dependence and worship, nor does it exhaust the later sūtra-contexts in which difference is redescribed under other headings.

Even so, the argumentative line is clear. The adhyāya-summary announces a convergence of Vedic language on Viṣṇu. TD 1.1 *bhedavyapadeśāc ca* applies that convergence to graded bliss. TD 1.3 *suṣuptyutkrāntyor bhedena* applies it to liminal states. TD 1.4 *jñeyatvavacanāc ca* applies it to the seeker's final cognitive task. Later Mādhva scholastic discussion inherits precisely this pattern: semantics, ontology, and soteriology are not three topics, but one connected discipline.
