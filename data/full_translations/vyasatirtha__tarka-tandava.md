## Editorial introduction

The first *pariccheda* of the *Tarka-Tāṇḍava* begins where Vyāsatīrtha's larger project has to begin: with *prāmāṇya*, the validity of cognition. If validity had to wait upon an extrinsic certifier, then the authority of the Veda would be unstable at precisely the point where Vyāsatīrtha needs it to bear philosophical weight. The opening *prāmāṇyavāda* therefore does not merely ask an abstract epistemological question. It joins three theses into one argumentative arc: cognition is valid *svataḥ* (intrinsically), the Veda is *apauruṣeya* (unauthored), and the qualified Lord known through the Veda is not inferentially constructed in a Naiyāyika way. In the present GRETIL witness, however, the text is transmitted together with Rāghavendra Tīrtha's *Nyāyadīpa*, and the *mūla* appears not as a clean uninterrupted block but as brief, formally marked units embedded within the commentary.

For that reason, the natural unit for translation here is not an artificially smoothed continuous stretch, but the foundational recoverable *mūla* statements of the *prāmāṇyavāda* itself: TT 1.5; the section-head marking the inquiry into *jñapti*-*svatastva*; the formal definition of intrinsic validity as grasped; the clarification of *sākṣin* in dialectical terms; the corresponding definition of *utpatti*-*svatastva*; the heading that turns from definition to proof; the first inference for *svato-grāhyatva*; and the closing sentence that carries the argument directly into the ensuing *vedāpauruṣeyatvavāda*. This is the smallest defensible unit that preserves the internal movement of the section. It also shows, in miniature, Vyāsatīrtha's broader method: a distinctively Mādhva commitment is recast in the exacting idiom of *Navya-Nyāya*, not by reducing *śruti* to inference, but by specifying the logical conditions under which *śruti* can stand as *pramāṇa* at all.

## Translation

### Prāmāṇyavāda heading (pu. 3)

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *svamate jñaptau svatastvaniruktiḥ prāmāṇyavādaḥ*

**English (line-by-line):**
> The *prāmāṇyavāda* is: the definition of *svatastva* (intrinsicness) with respect to apprehension, according to the school's own view.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| svamate | sva-mata | locative singular neuter | in the school's own doctrine |
| jñaptau | jñapti | locative singular feminine | in apprehension, in the matter of being grasped |
| svatastvaniruktiḥ | svatastva-nirukti | nominative singular feminine | the definition of intrinsicness |
| prāmāṇyavādaḥ | prāmāṇya-vāda | nominative singular masculine | the discussion of validity |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *svatastva* [avyayībhāva]: *svataḥ bhāvaḥ*.
- *svatastvanirukti* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *svatastvasya niruktiḥ*.
- *prāmāṇyavāda* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *prāmāṇyasya vādaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- adhikaraṇa: *svamate*
- adhikaraṇa: *jñaptau*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb; the heading is nominal and classificatory.

**Note:**
The heading is already philosophically exact. Vyāsatīrtha distinguishes *jñapti*-*svatastva* from *utpatti*-*svatastva*; the former concerns the intrinsic graspability of validity, the latter its intrinsic origination. That distinction governs the rest of the section.

### TT 1.5

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *svataḥpramāṇair āmnāyair ādareṇādivarjitaiḥ / ākhyātānantakalyāṇaguṇaṃ śrīramaṇaṃ bhaje //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> I worship Śrī Ramaṇa, whose infinite auspicious qualities are declared  
> by the Vedic transmissions that are valid in themselves, devoid of an originating author, and received with reverence.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| svataḥpramāṇair | svataḥ-pramāṇa | instrumental plural masculine adjectival compound | by those that are self-valid |
| āmnāyair | āmnāya | instrumental plural masculine | by the Vedas, by scriptural transmissions |
| ādareṇa | ādara | instrumental singular masculine | with reverence |
| ādivarjitaiḥ | ādi-varjita | instrumental plural masculine adjectival compound | devoid of an originating source / author |
| ākhyātānantakalyāṇaguṇam | ākhyāta-ananta-kalyāṇa-guṇa | accusative singular masculine compound | whose infinite auspicious qualities are declared |
| śrīramaṇam | śrī-ramaṇa | accusative singular masculine | Śrī Ramaṇa, the blessed Lord |
| bhaje | bhaj | laṭ, 1st person singular, ātmanepada | I worship, I resort to |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *svataḥpramāṇa* [avyayībhāva-based adjectival compound]: *svata eva pramāṇāni ye te*.
- *ādivarjita* [bahuvrīhi]: *ādiḥ varjito yeṣāṃ te*.
- *ākhyātānantakalyāṇaguṇa* [bahuvrīhi]: *ākhyātā anantāḥ kalyāṇā guṇā yasya tam*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: implied *aham*
- karman: *śrīramaṇam*
- karaṇa: *svataḥpramāṇair āmnāyair*
- karaṇa / viśeṣaṇa: *ādivarjitaiḥ*
- karaṇa / bhāva: *ādareṇa*

**Verbal modality:**
- *bhaje*: laṭ / ātmanepada / active-middle

**Note:**
This verse is not ornamental. It compresses the entire sequence of the first *pariccheda*: intrinsic validity, Vedic unauthoredness, and the Lord whose qualities the Veda declares. Later in the same *pariccheda*, the closing sentence explicitly draws the consequence: because validity is intrinsic, the Veda, though *apauruṣeya*, is a *pramāṇa*.

### Prāmāṇyavāda, jñapti-svatastva-lakṣaṇa (pu. 6)

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *grāhyaprāmāṇyavirodhyupasthāpakasāmagryasamavahitagrāhyaprāmāṇyāśrayatattajjñānaviṣayakasākṣijñānaviṣayatvanaiyatyaṃ svatastvam /*

**English (line-by-line):**
> *Svatastva* is the invariability of being the object of a *sākṣijñāna* (witness-cognition)  
> whose object is that very cognition which is the locus of graspable validity,  
> when that witness-cognition is not accompanied by the complex that would present a defeater to that graspable validity.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| grāhyaprāmāṇyavirodhyupasthāpakasāmagryasamavahitagrāhyaprāmāṇyāśrayatattajjñānaviṣayakasākṣijñānaviṣayatvanaiyatyaṃ | grāhya-prāmāṇya-virodhi-upasthāpaka-sāmagrī-asamavahita-grāhya-prāmāṇya-āśraya-tat-tat-jñāna-viṣayaka-sākṣi-jñāna-viṣayatva-naiyatya | nominative singular neuter compound | the invariability of being the object of witness-cognition directed to the cognition that is the locus of graspable validity, unaccompanied by the complex presenting a defeater to that validity |
| svatastvam | svatastva | nominative singular neuter | intrinsicness |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *grāhyaprāmāṇya* [karmadhāraya]: *grāhyaṃ prāmāṇyam*.
- *grāhyaprāmāṇyavirodhi* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *grāhyaprāmāṇyasya virodhī*.
- *virodhyupasthāpaka-sāmagrī* [upapada-tatpuruṣa]: *virodhinam upasthāpayati sā sāmagrī*.
- *asamavahita* [nañ-tatpuruṣa]: *na samavahitam*.
- *grāhyaprāmāṇyāśrayatattajjñāna* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *grāhyaprāmāṇyasya āśrayaṃ yat tat-tat-jñānam*.
- *tattajjñānaviṣayakasākṣijñāna* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *tattajjñānaṃ viṣayo yasya तत् sākṣijñānam*.
- *sākṣijñānaviṣayatvanaiyatya* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *sākṣijñānasya viṣayatvasya naiyatyam*.
- *svatastva* [avyayībhāva]: *svataḥ bhāvaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- No overt kārakas; the sentence is a nominal definition.

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb; definitional force is carried by nominal predication.

**Note:**
This is the formal *lakṣaṇa* of *jñapti*-*svatastva*. The decisive word is *naiyatya* (invariable connection): validity is not said to be grasped only sometimes or accidentally, but by rule through the witness-cognition of the cognition itself, provided that no defeater-presenting condition intervenes. The dialectical target is the Naiyāyika demand for a further, external cognition to certify validity.

### Prāmāṇyavāda, sākṣi-clarification (pu. 7)

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *tārkikābhimatānuvyavasāya evātmakaṃ sākṣī /*

**English (line-by-line):**
> The *sākṣin* (witness) is, in this dialectical setting, precisely of the nature of the *anuvyavasāya* (reflexive cognition) admitted by the logicians.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| tārkikābhimatānuvyavasāya | tārkika-abhimata-anuvyavasāya | nominative singular masculine compound | the reflexive cognition admitted by the logicians |
| eva | eva | indeclinable | precisely, just |
| ātmakaṃ | ātmaka | transmitted as nominative/accusative singular neuter; syntactically expected as masculine agreeing with *sākṣī* | of the nature of, constituted by |
| sākṣī | sākṣin | nominative singular masculine | the witness |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *tārkikābhimata* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *tārkikānām abhimataḥ*.
- *tārkikābhimatānuvyavasāya* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *tārkikābhimataḥ anuvyavasāyaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- No overt kārakas; the statement is identificatory.

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb; the line is a nominal equation.

**Note:**
The transmitted reading *ātmakaṃ* is awkward against nominative *sākṣī*; the sense, however, is secure from the immediately following gloss. Vyāsatīrtha is not here offering a phenomenology of the witness in abstraction. He is marking a dialectical equivalence: for present argumentative purposes, the opponent's acknowledged *anuvyavasāya* can do the work of the *sākṣin*.

### Prāmāṇyavāda, utpatti-svatastva-lakṣaṇa (pu. 18; cited by the commentary as p. 154)

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *jñānajanakamātrādhīnajanmatvaṃ svatastvam*

**English (line-by-line):**
> *Svatastva* is origination dependent solely on the producer of the cognition.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| jñānajanakamātrādhīnajanmatvam | jñāna-janaka-mātra-adhīna-janmatva | nominative singular neuter compound | origination dependent solely on the producer of the cognition |
| svatastvam | svatastva | nominative singular neuter | intrinsicness |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *jñānajanaka* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *jñānasya janakaḥ*.
- *jñānajanakamātra* [karmadhāraya]: *jñānajanaka eva mātram*.
- *jñānajanakamātrādhīna* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *jñānajanakamātrasya adhīnam*.
- *jñānajanakamātrādhīnajanmatva* [bahuvrīhi/tatpuruṣa chain]: *jñānajanakamātrādhīnaṃ janma yasya tat*.
- *svatastva* [avyayībhāva]: *svataḥ bhāvaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- No overt kārakas; the line is a nominal definition.

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb; definitional predication only.

**Note:**
Here the focus shifts from validity as grasped to validity as produced. The point is not that the cognition has no conditions whatsoever, but that no further validity-making factor beyond the cognition's own producer is required. Later anti-Naiyāyika polemic in the same *prāmāṇyavāda* turns precisely on this refusal to treat an added *guṇa* as the source of truth.

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *svatastve pramāṇaṃ tu*

**English (line-by-line):**
> But as for the proof of intrinsicness:

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| svatastve | svatastva | locative singular neuter | with regard to intrinsicness |
| pramāṇam | pramāṇa | nominative singular neuter | the proof, the means of demonstration |
| tu | tu | indeclinable | but, now then |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *svatastva* [avyayībhāva]: *svataḥ bhāvaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- adhikaraṇa: *svatastve*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb; the line functions as a transition-heading.

**Note:**
The shift is architectonic. Vyāsatīrtha has given definitions; he now turns to the *pramāṇa* for them. The resulting move is characteristically Navya: a compressed inferential statement replaces looser scholastic assertion.

### Prāmāṇyavāda, first svato-grāhyatvānumāna (pu. 34)

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *jñānaprāmāṇyaṃ svato grāhyaṃ, parato 'gṛhyamāṇatve sati gṛhyamāṇatvāt / yadyad anyenāgṛhyamāṇatve sati grāhyaṃ tat tena grāhyaṃ / yathā cakṣur anyenāgṛhyamāṇaṃ rūpaṃ cakṣurgrāhyam //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> The validity of a cognition is graspable intrinsically,  
> because it is grasped even when it is not being grasped by something else.  
> Whatever is grasped, when not being grasped by another,  
> is grasped by that very thing.  
> For example: a visible form, when not grasped by something else,  
> is grasped by the eye itself.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| jñānaprāmāṇyam | jñāna-prāmāṇya | nominative singular neuter | the validity of a cognition |
| svataḥ | svataḥ | indeclinable | intrinsically, from itself |
| grāhyam | grāhya | nominative singular neuter | graspable |
| parataḥ | parataḥ | indeclinable | by another, extrinsically |
| agṛhyamāṇatve | a-gṛhyamāṇatva | locative singular neuter | in the condition of not being grasped |
| sati | sat | locative singular masculine used absolutely | when there is, while being the case |
| gṛhyamāṇatvāt | gṛhyamāṇatva | ablative singular neuter | because of being grasped |
| yadyat | yad-yad | correlative pronoun, nominative singular neuter used generically | whatever |
| anyena | anya | instrumental singular masculine/neuter | by another |
| agṛhyamāṇatve | a-gṛhyamāṇatva | locative singular neuter | in the condition of not being grasped |
| sati | sat | locative singular masculine used absolutely | when there is, while being the case |
| grāhyam | grāhya | nominative singular neuter | graspable |
| tat | tad | nominative singular neuter | that |
| tena | tad | instrumental singular neuter | by that very thing |
| grāhyam | grāhya | nominative singular neuter | is graspable |
| yathā | yathā | indeclinable | for example, just as |
| cakṣur | cakṣus | nominative singular neuter (sandhi form) | the eye |
| anyena | anya | instrumental singular masculine/neuter | by another |
| agṛhyamāṇam | a-gṛhyamāṇa | accusative singular neuter | not being grasped |
| rūpam | rūpa | accusative singular neuter | form, visible object |
| cakṣurgrāhyam | cakṣus-grāhya | nominative/accusative singular neuter | graspable by the eye |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *jñānaprāmāṇya* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *jñānasya prāmāṇyam*.
- *agṛhyamāṇa* [nañ with present passive participle]: *na gṛhyamāṇa*.
- *cakṣurgrāhya* [tṛtīyā-tatpuruṣa]: *cakṣuṣā grāhyam*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- pakṣa: *jñānaprāmāṇyam*
- hetu: *parato 'gṛhyamāṇatve sati gṛhyamāṇatvāt*
- dṛṣṭānta: *rūpaṃ cakṣurgrāhyam*
- karaṇa in the example: *cakṣur*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb; inferential force is carried by nominal predication, the ablative of reason (*-tvāt*), and the correlative *yadyad ... tat ...* construction.

**Note:**
The form is spare, but the architecture is exact. The claim is not merely that validity happens to be apprehended without a second cognition; it is that the very fact of its being apprehended under that condition licenses an inference to intrinsic graspability. The example of visible form and the eye gives the inferential template. This is the locus that the thinker's JSON rightly singled out as load-bearing.

### Prāmāṇyavāda conclusion (pu. 229-230)

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *tasmāt prāmāṇyasya svatastvād apauruṣeyo 'pi vedaḥ pramāṇam eva //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Therefore, because validity is intrinsic,  
> the Veda too, though unauthored, is indeed a *pramāṇa* (means of knowledge).

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| tasmāt | tad | ablative singular masculine/neuter | therefore, from that |
| prāmāṇyasya | prāmāṇya | genitive singular neuter | of validity |
| svatastvāt | svatastva | ablative singular neuter | from intrinsicness, because of intrinsicness |
| apauruṣeyo | apauruṣeya | nominative singular masculine (sandhi form before *api*) | unauthored |
| api | api | indeclinable | even, too, though |
| vedaḥ | veda | nominative singular masculine | the Veda |
| pramāṇam | pramāṇa | nominative singular neuter used predicatively | a means of knowledge |
| eva | eva | indeclinable | indeed, precisely |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *svatastva* [avyayībhāva]: *svataḥ bhāvaḥ*.
- *apauruṣeya* [nañ-tatpuruṣa]: *na puruṣakṛtaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- hetu: *prāmāṇyasya svatastvāt*
- kartṛ: *vedaḥ*
- predicative complement: *pramāṇam*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb; the conclusion is a compressed nominal assertion.

**Note:**
This sentence is the hinge on which the whole opening turns. What began in TT 1.5 as invocation becomes here a formally drawn conclusion: Vedic authority does not require authorial certification because validity is not parasitic upon such certification in the first place. The next *vāda*, the *vedāpauruṣeyatvavāda*, then takes up in detail what the present section has made possible.

## Editorial closing

What this passage settles is not every issue in the first *pariccheda*, but the order of dependence among them. First comes *svataḥ-prāmāṇya*; from that follows the defensibility of an *apauruṣeya* Veda as *pramāṇa*; and only on that basis can the subsequent Vedāntic claims about the Lord be pressed without Nyāya's author-based model of testimony. What remains open here is the full battery of objections: the later subsections of the same *prāmāṇyavāda* return to *paratastva*, to the problem of *prāmāṇyaniścayasya pravartakatvam*, and to the anti-Naiyāyika refinements of both *jñapti* and *utpatti*. Immediately afterward, the *vedāpauruṣeyatvavāda* takes up the `ādivarjitaiḥ` claim argued only in seed-form here. In Vyāsatīrtha's broader corpus, the same methodological signature reappears in the *Nyāyāmṛta*: doctrinal commitments inherited from Madhva are not merely repeated, but recast as sharply delimited inferential structures.
