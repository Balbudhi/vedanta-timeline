## Editorial introduction

The opening of Vyāsatīrtha's *Prāmāṇyavāda* states, in compressed form, the whole strategic burden of the first *pariccheda*. The issue is not merely whether cognition is valid, but whether its validity is *svataḥ* (intrinsic, non-derivative) or *parataḥ* (dependent on a further factor); and, because the Dvaita position makes Veda's authority and God's knowability stand or fall with that answer, the discussion immediately broadens into Vedic authorlessness and the means by which *Īśvara*'s omniscience is known. The sequence chosen here is therefore the natural doctrinal opening, even though the only on-disk Sanskrit witness is commentary-inclusive and does not furnish a clean continuous diplomatic extraction of the prose base text for the whole opening unit. What follows is accordingly an honest translation of the secure, load-bearing loci already recoverable in the corpus.

This sequence is also the right scale at which to read Vyāsatīrtha's method. TT 1 does not begin with diffuse praise and only later turn analytic. The invocation at TT 1.5 already braids together three theses: Veda is *svataḥ-pramāṇa* (self-valid), it is *ādivarjita* (without originating author), and it declares a Lord endowed with infinite auspicious qualities. The triad of disputes then shows why these claims must be handled together. The compact definition *jñānajanaka-mātrādhīna-janmatvaṃ svatastvam* fixes the sense of intrinsicness on the side of origination; the first inference, *jñānaprāmāṇyaṃ svato grāhyam*, fixes it on the side of graspability. Later portions of TT 1 extend these claims into the *vedāpauruṣeyatvavāda* and *īśvaravāda*; elsewhere Vyāsatīrtha deploys the same Navya discipline against Advaita in *Nyāyāmṛta* 2.

## Translation

### TT 1.5

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *svataḥpramāṇair āmnāyair*  
> *ādareṇādivarjitaiḥ /*  
> *ākhyātānantakalyāṇaguṇaṃ*  
> *śrīramaṇaṃ bhaje //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> By *āmnāya*s (revealed Vedic transmissions) that are self-valid,  
> and, with reverence, free from any originating author,  
> [I worship] him whose infinite auspicious qualities are declared,  
> the consort of Śrī; I worship him.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| svataḥpramāṇaiḥ | svataḥpramāṇa | instrumental plural masculine | by self-valid [means/texts] |
| āmnāyaiḥ | āmnāya | instrumental plural masculine | by the Vedas; by revealed transmissions |
| ādareṇa | ādara | instrumental singular masculine | with reverence |
| ādivarjitaiḥ | ādi-varjita | instrumental plural masculine | devoid of an originator; lacking an initial author |
| ākhyātānantakalyāṇaguṇam | ākhyāta-ananta-kalyāṇa-guṇa | accusative singular masculine | whose infinite auspicious qualities are declared |
| śrīramaṇam | śrī-ramaṇa | accusative singular masculine | the consort of Śrī |
| bhaje | bhaj | laṭ, 1st singular, ātmanepada | I worship |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *svataḥpramāṇa* [avyayībhāva]: *svata eva pramāṇam*.
- *ādivarjita* [tatpuruṣa]: *ādiḥ yasmin nāsti saḥ*.
- *anantakalyāṇaguṇa* [karmadhāraya]: *anantāś ca te kalyāṇā guṇāḥ*.
- *ākhyātānantakalyāṇaguṇa* [bahuvrīhi]: *ākhyātā anantakalyāṇaguṇā yasya saḥ*.
- *śrīramaṇa* [tatpuruṣa]: *śriyāḥ ramaṇaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *(aham)*, understood from *bhaje*
- karman: *śrīramaṇam*
- karaṇa: *āmnāyaiḥ*
- karaṇa: *ādareṇa*

**Verbal modality:**
- *bhaje*: laṭ / ātmanepada / active-middle devotional usage

**Note:**
This verse is not detachable ornament. The phrase *svataḥpramāṇair āmnāyaiḥ* anticipates the whole *prāmāṇyavāda*, while *ādivarjitaiḥ* points forward to the *vedāpauruṣeyatvavāda* immediately following in TT 1. Vyāsatīrtha's theology is already epistemic here: the Lord's infinite qualities are not inserted after the pramāṇa-discussion, but disclosed through the same Veda whose self-validity is at issue.

### TT 1, opening *vipratipatti* I

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *prāmāṇyaṃ svata iti vedāntinaḥ /*  
> *parata iti naiyāyikāḥ //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> "Validity is intrinsic": thus say the Vedāntins.  
> "It is extrinsic": thus say the Naiyāyikas.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| prāmāṇyam | prāmāṇya | accusative singular neuter | validity |
| svataḥ | svataḥ | indeclinable | intrinsically; from itself |
| iti | iti | indeclinable | thus |
| vedāntinaḥ | vedāntin | nominative plural masculine | the Vedāntins |
| parataḥ | parataḥ | indeclinable | extrinsically; from another |
| iti | iti | indeclinable | thus |
| naiyāyikāḥ | naiyāyika | nominative plural masculine | the Naiyāyikas |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- none

**Kāraka structure:**
- predication topic: *prāmāṇyam*
- pakṣa-speakers: *vedāntinaḥ*, *naiyāyikāḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb is expressed; the scholastic opposition is cast as elliptical nominal predication.

**Note:**
The compression matters. Vyāsatīrtha begins not with a bland definition of *prāmāṇya* but with *vipratipatti* (structured disagreement), because the very form of the problem is dialectical. The contrast announced here governs the later distinction between *jñapti-svatastva* and *utpatti-svatastva* within the same section.

### TT 1, opening *vipratipatti* II

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *vedāḥ apauruṣeyā nityāś ceti vedāntinaḥ /*  
> *īśvaroccaritā anityā iti naiyāyikāḥ //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> "The Vedas are authorless and eternal": thus say the Vedāntins.  
> "They are uttered by God and non-eternal": thus say the Naiyāyikas.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| vedāḥ | veda | nominative plural masculine | the Vedas |
| apauruṣeyāḥ | apauruṣeya | nominative plural masculine | authorless; not composed by a person |
| nityāḥ | nitya | nominative plural masculine | eternal |
| ca | ca | indeclinable | and |
| iti | iti | indeclinable | thus |
| vedāntinaḥ | vedāntin | nominative plural masculine | the Vedāntins |
| īśvaroccaritāḥ | īśvaroccarita | nominative plural masculine | uttered by the Lord |
| anityāḥ | anitya | nominative plural masculine | non-eternal |
| iti | iti | indeclinable | thus |
| naiyāyikāḥ | naiyāyika | nominative plural masculine | the Naiyāyikas |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *apauruṣeya* [negative tatpuruṣa]: *na puruṣakṛtāḥ*.
- *īśvaroccarita* [tatpuruṣa]: *īśvareṇa uccaritāḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ/predicate subject: *vedāḥ*
- pakṣa-speakers: *vedāntinaḥ*, *naiyāyikāḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb is expressed; the claim remains a nominal opposition.

**Note:**
This second dispute is not independent of the first. If Veda were a produced utterance, its authority would invite the Naiyāyika model of validity derived from a further qualifying factor, above all the reliability of a speaker. Vyāsatīrtha therefore lets the pramāṇa-question pass directly into the authorlessness-question; later in TT 1 the entire *vedāpauruṣeyatvavāda* unfolds this link.

### TT 1, opening *vipratipatti* III

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *īśvarasya sārvajñyādikaṃ vedenaiva siddhyatīti vedāntinaḥ /*  
> *dharmāgrāhakānumānair iti naiyāyikāḥ //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> "The Lord's omniscience and the rest are established by the Veda alone": thus say the Vedāntins.  
> "By inferences that apprehend *dharma*": thus say the Naiyāyikas.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| īśvarasya | īśvara | genitive singular masculine | of the Lord |
| sārvajñyādikam | sārvajñya-ādika | accusative singular neuter | omniscience and the rest |
| vedena | veda | instrumental singular masculine | by the Veda |
| eva | eva | indeclinable | alone; precisely |
| siddhyati | sidh | laṭ, 3rd singular, parasmaipada | is established |
| iti | iti | indeclinable | thus |
| vedāntinaḥ | vedāntin | nominative plural masculine | the Vedāntins |
| dharmāgrāhakānumānaiḥ | dharma-agrāhaka-anumāna | instrumental plural neuter | by dharma-grasping inferences |
| iti | iti | indeclinable | thus |
| naiyāyikāḥ | naiyāyika | nominative plural masculine | the Naiyāyikas |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *sārvajñyādika* [tatpuruṣa]: *sārvajñyaṃ cādikaṃ ca*.
- *dharmāgrāhakānumāna* [upapada/tatpuruṣa]: *dharmaṃ agrāhakāni anumānāni*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- sambandha: *īśvarasya*
- karman: *sārvajñyādikam*
- karaṇa: *vedena*
- karaṇa: *dharmāgrāhakānumānaiḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *siddhyati*: laṭ / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
The third dispute shows why the previous two matter. If Veda is intrinsically authoritative and authorless, then God's omniscience may be known from Veda without circular dependence on a prior proof of divine authorship. If, by contrast, the Veda is a divine utterance whose authority tracks speaker-reliability, then one seeks an independent inference to *Īśvara*. The whole architecture of TT 1 turns on this order of dependence.

### TT 1, *utpattau svatastva* lakṣaṇa

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *jñānajanakamātrādhīnajanmatvaṃ svatastvam*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Intrinsicness is origination that depends solely on the producer of the cognition.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| jñānajanakamātrādhīnajanmatvam | jñāna-janaka-mātra-adhīna-janmatva | nominative singular neuter | origination dependent solely on the producer of the cognition |
| svatastvam | svatastva | nominative singular neuter | intrinsicness |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *jñānajanakamātrādhīnajanmatva* [tatpuruṣa-complex]: *jñānajanakasya mātrasya adhīnaṃ janma yasya tat*.
- *svatastva* [avyayībhāva-derived abstract]: *svataḥ bhāvaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- predicate term: *svatastvam*
- definiendum/defined property: *jñānajanakamātrādhīnajanmatvam*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb is expressed; the sentence is a strict *lakṣaṇa*-form definition.

**Note:**
This is the positive heart of *utpatti-svatastva*. Vyāsatīrtha does not mean that validity is causeless; he means that no further validity-producing factor is required beyond what already produces the cognition. The polemical target is the Naiyāyika urge to add an extra qualifying condition on the side of truth-validation. Later in the same *prāmāṇyavāda* this definition is extended to *karaṇaprāmāṇya* as well.

### TT 1, first *svato-grāhyatvānumāna*

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *jñānaprāmāṇyaṃ svato grāhyaṃ,*  
> *parato 'gṛhyamāṇatve sati gṛhyamāṇatvāt /*  
> *yadyad anyenāgṛhyamāṇatve sati grāhyaṃ*  
> *tat tena grāhyaṃ,*  
> *yathā cakṣur anyenāgṛhyamāṇaṃ rūpaṃ*  
> *cakṣurgrāhyam //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> The validity of a cognition is graspable intrinsically,  
> because, while not being grasped by another, it is nevertheless grasped.  
> Whatever, while not being grasped by another, is grasped,  
> that is grasped by that very thing itself,  
> just as a visible form, while not grasped by another [instrument],  
> is grasped by the eye.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| jñānaprāmāṇyam | jñāna-prāmāṇya | nominative singular neuter | the validity of a cognition |
| svataḥ | svataḥ | indeclinable | intrinsically; from itself |
| grāhyam | grāhya | nominative singular neuter | graspable |
| parataḥ | parataḥ | indeclinable | by another; extrinsically |
| agṛhyamāṇatve | a-gṛhyamāṇatva | locative singular neuter | in the condition of not being grasped |
| sati | sat | locative singular masculine (absolute usage) | when there is; while being so |
| gṛhyamāṇatvāt | gṛhyamāṇatva | ablative singular neuter | because of being grasped |
| yadyad | yat | correlative pronoun, nominative singular neuter | whatever |
| anyena | anya | instrumental singular neuter | by another |
| agṛhyamāṇatve | a-gṛhyamāṇatva | locative singular neuter | in the condition of not being grasped |
| sati | sat | locative singular masculine (absolute usage) | when there is; while being so |
| grāhyam | grāhya | nominative singular neuter | graspable |
| tat | tad | nominative singular neuter | that |
| tena | tad | instrumental singular neuter | by that very thing |
| grāhyam | grāhya | nominative singular neuter | is graspable |
| yathā | yathā | indeclinable | just as |
| cakṣuḥ | cakṣus | nominative singular neuter | the eye |
| anyena | anya | instrumental singular neuter | by another |
| agṛhyamāṇam | a-gṛhyamāṇa | accusative singular neuter | not being grasped |
| rūpam | rūpa | accusative singular neuter | form; visible object |
| cakṣurgrāhyam | cakṣus-grāhya | nominative singular neuter | graspable by the eye |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *jñānaprāmāṇya* [tatpuruṣa]: *jñānasya prāmāṇyam*.
- *cakṣurgrāhya* [tatpuruṣa]: *cakṣuṣā grāhyam*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- pakṣa / predicate subject: *jñānaprāmāṇyam*
- hetu: *parato 'gṛhyamāṇatve sati gṛhyamāṇatvāt*
- vyāpti-correlate: *yadyad ... grāhyaṃ tat tena grāhyam*
- dṛṣṭānta-karman: *rūpam*
- dṛṣṭānta-karaṇa: *cakṣuḥ* (compressed in *cakṣurgrāhyam* and echoed by *tena*)

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb is expressed; the inference proceeds through nominal predication and ablative-hetu construction.

**Note:**
This is the formal inferential statement for *svato-grāhyatva*. The grammar is philosophically exact: *parato 'gṛhyamāṇatve sati gṛhyamāṇatvāt* makes the absence of grasp by another instrument a condition under which grasp nonetheless occurs, and so forces self-graspability as the explanatory remainder. The eye/form example supplies a Navya-style model for immediate apprehension. Later in TT 1 Vyāsatīrtha refines the notion of *sākṣi*-graspability that underwrites this claim; the broader anti-Naiyāyika payoff is developed again in the *karaṇaprāmāṇya* discussion.

## Editorial closing

What is settled by this opening sequence is the structure of the first *pariccheda*. The issue of cognitive validity cannot be isolated from Vedic authorlessness or from the means of knowing a fully qualified Lord; the three stand or fall together. Vyāsatīrtha has also fixed two distinct but coordinated senses of intrinsicness: intrinsicness in origination and intrinsicness in graspability. What remains open, at this point, is the dialectical defense of each claim against Naiyāyika objection. That work is taken up in the later stretches of TT 1, first in the fuller *prāmāṇyavāda*, then in the *vedāpauruṣeyatvavāda* and *īśvaravāda*. On the wider Dvaita front, the same inferential discipline reappears in *Nyāyāmṛta* 2, where formally structured proof is turned against the Advaitin rather than the Naiyāyika.
