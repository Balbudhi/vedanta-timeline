## Editorial introduction

The third Sandarbha has already done two preparatory kinds of work before it reaches the present unit. First, it has distinguished *jīva*, *īśvara*, *māyā*, and *kāla* as real and non-interchangeable *tattvas*; this is especially clear in *Paramātma-Sandarbha* §§34–41. Second, it has rejected the standard Advaitin explanatory models by which the difference between *jīva* and Brahman is treated as merely apparent, above all the *pariccheda* and *pratibimba* accounts. §§42–45 are therefore the natural closing unit of this debate. Jīva does not merely say that the *abheda-śāstras* ("non-difference texts") are false. He says something more exact. They must be retained, but retained under a disciplined reinterpretation: sameness holds at the level of *cid-rūpatva* (being of the nature of consciousness), while difference holds because the plurality of *jīvas* stands to Bhagavān through his natural *acintya-śakti* (inconceivable power) as ray-atoms stand to the sun.

This is the place where Jīva's broader position becomes unmistakable. The passage does not concede a weak dualism; nor does it relax into a monism that later devotion merely overlays. It argues that ontology itself requires a structured *bheda–abheda* relation, one not capturable by reductive dialectic. That is why §42 neutralizes the rival models, §43 states the positive reconciliation, §44 derives *abhidheya* (the operative spiritual means, here Bhagavān-bhajana), and §45 draws the *prayojana* (the consummating end) as *parama-prema*, supreme love for Bhagavān. Earlier, *Tattva-Sandarbha* §§9–15 had secured *śabda* as the decisive *pramāṇa*; *Tattva-Sandarbha* §52 had already used *aṃśatva* to deny crude identity while preserving sameness in consciousness. The present unit gathers those commitments into one compressed doctrinal decision and prepares the fuller elaborations of *Bhagavat-Sandarbha* §16 and *Kṛṣṇa-Sandarbha* §§28–29.

## Translation

### Paramātma-Sandarbha §42

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *tasmāt pariccheda-pratibimbatvādi-pratipādaka-śāstrāṇy api kathañcit tat-sādṛśyena gauṇyā eva vṛttyā pravarteran; ambuvad agrahaṇāt tu na tathātvam, vṛddhi-hrāsa-bhāktvam antarbhāvād ubhaya-sāmañjasyād evam, iti pūrvottara-pakṣamaya-nyāyābhyām.*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Therefore, even the scriptural texts that teach delimitation, reflection, and the like would function only by a secondary signifying force, and that only through a certain resemblance to the case at hand.
>
> “But not suchness, because it is not apprehended, as in the case of water”; “there is figurative attribution of increase and decrease because of inclusion; thus, because both sides are rendered coherent”: so runs the reasoning of the paired prior-and-subsequent arguments.

### Paramātma-Sandarbha §43

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *tata eva abheda-śāstrāṇi ubhayoś cid-rūpatve jīva-samūhasya durghaṭa-ghaṭanā-paṭīyasyā svābhāvika-tad-acintya-śaktyā svabhāvata eva tad-raśmi-paramāṇu-guṇa-sthānīyatvāt tad-vyatirekeṇa avyatirekeṇa ca virodhaṃ parihṛtya agre muhur api tad etad vyāsa-samādhi-labdha-siddhānta-yojanāya yojanīyāni.*

**English (line-by-line):**
> For that very reason, the *abheda-śāstras* too, insofar as both are of the nature of consciousness, and insofar as the aggregate of *jīvas* stands, by his natural and inconceivable power, in the position of the quality of atomic rays belonging to him, must first remove the contradiction by means of difference from him and non-difference from him.
>
> Thereafter they are repeatedly to be applied in what follows, for the sake of coordinating this doctrine received in Vyāsa’s *samādhi*.

- kartṛ: *abheda-śāstrāṇi*

**Verbal modality:**
- *parihṛtya*: absolutive; prior completed removal of contradiction.
- *yojanīyāni*: gerundive / future passive participle; prescriptive fitness or necessity.

**Note:**
This is one of the densest programmatic statements of Jīva’s ontology. The *abheda* texts are not read as teaching *svarūpābheda*, identity of essence; they are read as teaching sameness in *cid-rūpatva* within a relation already marked by dependence. The decisive phrase is *svābhāvika-tad-acintya-śaktyā*: *acintya-śakti* is not an ad hoc rescue-device here, but the formal reason why both *vyatireka* and *avyatireka* can be true without contradiction. Compare the earlier analysis of *aṃśatva* in *Tattva-Sandarbha* §52 and the later three-*śakti* doctrine in *Bhagavat-Sandarbha* §16.

### Paramātma-Sandarbha §44

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *tad evaṃ māyāśrayatva-māyā-mohitatvābhyāṃ sthite dvayor bhede tad-bhajanasya eva abhidheyatvam āyātam.*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Thus, once the difference between the two stands established through the one’s being the locus of *māyā* and the other’s being deluded by *māyā*, it follows that the worship of him alone has the status of *abhidheya* (the operative means to be practiced).

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *māyāśrayatva* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *māyāyā āśrayatvam*.
- *māyā-mohitatva* [tṛtīyā-tatpuruṣa sense]: *māyayā mohitatvam*.
- *tad-bhajana* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *tasya bhajanam*.
- *abhidheyatva* [taddhita abstract]: the condition of being *abhidheya*, that which is to be enacted as the central means.

**Kāraka structure:**
- locative frame: *dvayoḥ bhede sthite*
- karaṇa / reason: *māyāśrayatva-māyā-mohitatvābhyām*
- sambandha: *tad-bhajanasya*
- vidheya-predicate: *abhidheyatvam*
- kriyā: *āyātam*

**Verbal modality:**
- *sthite*: past passive participle in locative absolute-like construction.
- *āyātam*: past passive participle used predicatively; “has followed as a consequence.”

**Note:**
The doctrinal transition is exact. Ontology does not remain contemplative. Once *īśvara* and *jīva* are distinguished in the relevant way, *bhajana* is not one optional practice among others; it becomes *abhidheya*, the constitutive path. This anticipates the systematic expansion of *abhidheya* in *Bhakti-Sandarbha*, especially the long practical analysis culminating in §216.

### Paramātma-Sandarbha §45

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *ataḥ śrī-bhagavataḥ eva sarva-hitopadeṣṭṛtvāt, sarva-duḥkha-haratvāt, raśmīnāṃ sūryavat sarveṣāṃ parama-svarūpatvāt, sarvādhika-guṇa-śālitvāt, parama-prema-yogatvam iti prayojanaṃ ca sthāpitam.*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Therefore, it has also been established that Śrī Bhagavān alone is the proper object of *parama-prema* (supreme love).

**Note:**
The move from §44 to §45 is the classic Gauḍīya sequence from *abhidheya* to *prayojana*. Bhagavān is not only the one to be worshipped; he alone is the terminus of the highest love. The simile of sun and rays blocks any collapse of Bhagavān into the aggregate of *jīvas* even while preserving non-external relation. The later systematic unfolding of this claim belongs to *Prīti-Sandarbha* §1 and following.

## Editorial closing

What this passage settles is the status of the *abheda* texts after the rejection of crude identity-theories. They are neither denied nor allowed to govern the ontology alone. They are subordinated to a broader reading in which the *jīva* is conscious like Bhagavān, dependent on Bhagavān, non-separate from Bhagavān, and yet not identical in *svarūpa*. From that, Jīva derives two consequences in strict order: Bhagavān-bhajana as *abhidheya* and *parama-prema* for Bhagavān as *prayojana*. What remains open here is the fuller positive account of Bhagavān’s own intrinsic potency-structure and the identification of the fullest Bhagavān specifically with Kṛṣṇa. Those are taken up later, above all in *Bhagavat-Sandarbha* §16 on the three *śaktis*, in *Kṛṣṇa-Sandarbha* §§28–29 on *kṛṣṇas tu bhagavān svayam*, and then in *Bhakti-Sandarbha* and *Prīti-Sandarbha* on the practical and affective completion of the same architecture.
