## Editorial introduction

This file cannot honestly be a continuous translation of Nimbārka's prose bhāṣya for the chosen locus. The primary archive scans are on disk, but they are image-only in the present environment; `pypdf` yields no text layer, standard OCR utilities are unavailable, and Internet Archive's OCR derivatives for these scans are access-restricted. The right response is therefore not invention, but recovery. What can be secured with confidence is the opening doctrinal cluster of the *aṃśa-adhikaraṇa*: the three root sūtras through which Nimbārka states the point, together with the two proof-texts that Bose's English witness explicitly reports him as citing.

That cluster is philosophically central. Nimbārka's position does not say that the *jīva* (individual self) is identical with Brahman in such a way that real difference disappears. Nor does it say that the *jīva* is sheerly external to Brahman. It says that the *jīva* is an *aṃśa* (part, dependent share) of Brahman: really distinct, because scripture speaks in the register of plurality and subordination; really non-distinct, because scripture also speaks in the register of identity; and never a severed fragment. The argument unfolds in a neat sequence. BS 2.3.42 states the thesis from the balancing of difference-texts and non-difference-texts. BS 2.3.43 adduces explicit mantric warrant. BS 2.3.44 extends the warrant across the *smṛti* register as well. In Nimbārka's wider architecture this is the grammar of *svābhāvika-bhedābheda* itself. Compare the rejection of an *aupādhika* reading at *Vedānta-Pārijāta-Saurabha* 2.1.14, the account of *bhakti* as means at 3.2.24, the retained distinctness of the liberated self at 4.4.1-4, and the compressed doctrinal equivalent in *Daśa-Ślokī* 1: *hareḥ adhīnam* and *pratidehabhinnam*.

## Translation

### BS 2.3.42 (Bose/Wisdomlib numbering; standard BS 2.3.43)

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *aṃśo nānāvyapadeśād anyathā cāpi dāśakitavāditvam adhīyate eke ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> (The individual self is) a part, because scripture speaks in the register of plurality or difference, and also otherwise.  
> And some recensions read [Brahman as] being fishermen, gamblers, and the like.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| aṃśaḥ | *aṃśa* | nom. sg. masc. | part; dependent share |
| nānāvyapadeśāt | *nānā-vyapadeśa* | abl. sg. masc. compound | because of the designation of plurality / difference |
| anyathā | *anyathā* | indecl. adverb | otherwise; in another way |
| ca | *ca* | indecl. particle | and |
| api | *api* | indecl. particle | also |
| dāśakitavāditvam | *dāśa-kitava-āditva* | acc. sg. neut. compound | the state of being "dāśa, gambler, and the like" |
| adhīyate | *adhi-√i* | *laṭ*; 3rd sg.; ātmanepada; passive-middle | is read; is recited |
| eke | *eka* | nom. pl. masc. | some; certain reciters / recensions |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *nānāvyapadeśa* [avyayībhāva-tatpuruṣa]: *nānā iti vyapadeśaḥ*.
- *dāśakitavāditva* [dvandva-headed derivative]: *dāśaś ca kitavaś cādayaś ca; teṣāṃ bhāvaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- predicative thesis: *aṃśaḥ*
- hetu: *nānāvyapadeśāt*
- supplementary hetu: *anyathā ca api*
- karman of passive reading: *dāśakitavāditvam*
- kartṛ of *adhīyate*: *eke*

**Verbal modality:**
- *adhīyate*: *laṭ* / ātmanepada / passive-middle

**Note:**
Bose's English witness reports Nimbārka's prose sense as follows: the *jīva* is a part of the Supreme Self because scripture speaks both in the register of difference and in the register of non-difference, citing on the one side texts such as Śvetāśvatara Upaniṣad 1.9 and on the other side *tat tvam asi* (Chāndogya Upaniṣad 6.8.6ff.). The grammar of the sūtra matters. *Anyathā* does not cancel the first reason; it adds the contrary scriptural register and forces the relation of *aṃśa* as the reconciliation. This is the compressed seed of Nimbārka's later school-language of real difference and real non-difference by nature, not by temporary adjunct.

### BS 2.3.43 (Bose/Wisdomlib numbering; standard BS 2.3.44)

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *mantravarṇāc ca ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> And also because of the wording of the mantra.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| mantravarṇāt | *mantra-varṇa* | abl. sg. masc. compound | because of the wording of the mantra |
| ca | *ca* | indecl. particle | and; also |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *mantravarṇa* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *mantrasya varṇaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- hetu: *mantravarṇāt*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb occurs in the surface line; the ablative continues the preceding inferential structure with understood "it is established."

**Note:**
The movement is exact. BS 2.3.42 established the *aṃśa* relation from the double scriptural register of difference and non-difference. BS 2.3.43 now claims a more explicit warrant: a mantra whose very wording speaks in quarter-language. Bose's Nimbārka page identifies that mantra as Chāndogya Upaniṣad 3.12.6, parallel to Ṛgveda 10.90.3. The argument is no longer merely harmonizing two classes of text; it is now pointing to direct scriptural diction.

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *api ca smaryate ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> And it is also remembered in *smṛti* (remembered scripture and authoritative tradition).

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| api | *api* | indecl. particle | also |
| ca | *ca* | indecl. particle | and |
| smaryate | *√smṛ* | *laṭ*; 3rd sg.; ātmanepada; passive | is remembered; is stated in smṛti |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- None.

**Kāraka structure:**
- impersonal passive predicate: *smaryate*

**Verbal modality:**
- *smaryate*: *laṭ* / ātmanepada / passive

**Note:**
The argumentative force is architectural. Once the *aṃśa* relation has been tied to *śruti*, it is then confirmed from *smṛti* as well. Bose's Nimbārka page identifies the proof-text as Bhagavad-Gītā 15.7. The school's later habit of reading Gītā 15.7 as a decisive text for the dependent-yet-real status of the *jīva* stands already compressed in this tiny sūtra.

### Chāndogya Upaniṣad 3.12.6 (the mantra cited for BS 2.3.43)

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *tāvān asya mahimā tato jyāyāṃś ca pūruṣaḥ | pādo'sya sarvā bhūtāni tripād asyāmṛtaṃ divīti ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Such is his greatness.  
> And the *puruṣa* (supreme person) is greater than that.  
> All beings are one quarter of him.  
> His three quarters are immortal in heaven.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| tāvān | *tāvat* | nom. sg. masc. | so great; of such extent |
| asya | *idam* | gen. sg. | of him; of this |
| mahimā | *mahiman* | nom. sg. masc. | greatness; glory |
| tataḥ | *tatas* | abl. sg. indeclinable use | than that; beyond that |
| jyāyān | *jyāyas* | nom. sg. masc. comparative | greater |
| ca | *ca* | indecl. particle | and |
| pūruṣaḥ | *pūruṣa* | nom. sg. masc. | the person; the cosmic person |
| pādaḥ | *pāda* | nom. sg. masc. | foot; quarter |
| asya | *idam* | gen. sg. | of him |
| sarvā | *sarva* | nom. pl. neut. Vedic for *sarvāṇi* | all |
| bhūtāni | *bhūta* | nom. pl. neut. | beings; entities |
| tripād | *tripād* | nom. sg. masc. | three-quarters; three-footed share |
| asya | *idam* | gen. sg. | of him |
| amṛtam | *amṛta* | nom. sg. neut. predicative | immortal; deathless |
| divi | *div* | loc. sg. fem. | in heaven |
| iti | *iti* | indecl. quotation marker | thus |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *tripād* [dvigu]: *trayaḥ pādāḥ yasya saḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *mahimā* in the first clause
- predicative complement: *tāvān*
- kartṛ: *pūruṣaḥ* in the second clause
- predicative comparative: *jyāyān*
- kartṛ: *sarvā bhūtāni* in the third clause
- predicative complement: *pādaḥ asya*
- kartṛ: *tripād asya* in the fourth clause
- predicative complement: *amṛtam*
- adhikaraṇa: *divi*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb appears in the surface verse; the Sanskrit works through a sequence of nominal predications with understood *asti*.

**Note:**
This is the exact kind of line BS 2.3.43 means by *mantravarṇa*. Nimbārka's use of it is not ornamental. Quarter-language supplies the scriptural permission for speaking of the many as a real dependent share of the one. In his setting that share is not a cut-off piece. It is a way of speaking a dependence relation without abolishing either relata. Compare the doctrinal compression at *Daśa-Ślokī* 1, where the *jīva* is both *hareḥ adhīna* and *pratidehabhinna*.

### Bhagavad-Gītā 15.7a (the *smṛti* cited for BS 2.3.44)

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *mamaivāṃśo jīvaloke jīvabhūtaḥ sanātanaḥ |*

**English (line-by-line):**
> An eternal part of me, in the world of living beings, having become a living self.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| mama | *asmad* | gen. sg. | of me; my |
| eva | *eva* | indecl. particle | indeed; precisely |
| aṃśaḥ | *aṃśa* | nom. sg. masc. | part; share |
| jīvaloke | *jīva-loka* | loc. sg. masc. compound | in the world of living beings |
| jīvabhūtaḥ | *jīva-bhūta* | nom. sg. masc. compound | having become a living self |
| sanātanaḥ | *sanātana* | nom. sg. masc. | eternal |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *jīvaloka* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *jīvānāṃ lokaḥ*.
- *jīvabhūtaḥ* [karmadhāraya / participial compound]: *jīvaḥ bhūtaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *aṃśaḥ*
- sambandha: *mama*
- adhikaraṇa: *jīvaloke*
- predicative qualifiers: *jīvabhūtaḥ*, *sanātanaḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb appears in the cited half-verse; *bhūtaḥ* functions as a past participial qualifier within a nominal clause.

**Note:**
Nimbārka needs precisely the word *aṃśaḥ*, and this half-verse gives it from the *smṛti* side. The absence of a finite verb is not a defect for his purpose. The line serves as an ontological designation, not yet as a full predicative sentence. The school later treats this phrase as doctrinally parallel to *Daśa-Ślokī* 1's *hareḥ adhīnam*: dependence is real, intrinsic, and permanent.

## Editorial closing

What this recoverable cluster settles is the grammar of Nimbārka's metaphysics. The *jīva* is neither a sheer other over against Brahman nor a merely illusory appearance whose plurality evaporates at the highest level. It is an *aṃśa*: a real dependent share, scripturally warranted from difference-texts, non-difference-texts, mantra, and *smṛti*. That is already enough to see why later Nimbārka loci can reject Bhāskara's *aupādhika* handling of difference at 2.1.14 and still preserve a genuinely devotional soteriology at 3.2.24 and 4.4.1-4.

What remains unsettled in this file is the exact Sanskrit wording of Nimbārka's continuous prose between these anchor-lines. The doctrine is recoverable; the exact bhāṣya phrasing is not securely transcribable from the present environment. A future replacement file should therefore be made only after direct page-level transcription from the on-disk archive or a non-restricted OCR witness. Until then, this dossier marks the secure core and no more.
