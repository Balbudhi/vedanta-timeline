## Editorial introduction

The *Śikṣāṣṭakam* is short, but it is not slight. Across eight verses Caitanya Mahāprabhu gives, in compressed first-person form, the devotional arc that later Gauḍīya theology will expand into a full metaphysics and soteriology. Verse 1 establishes the efficacy of *saṅkīrtana* (collective chanting of the divine name) as transformative, purgative, illuminative, and bliss-bearing. Verses 2-4 then state the conditions under which that efficacy becomes existentially real: divine generosity in making the name available, the ascetic discipline of humility and non-claimancy, and the refusal of wealth, followers, beauty, or even self-regarding spiritual gain as the aim of devotion. Verses 5-8 move inward from discipline to relation: the *jīva* is confessed as Kṛṣṇa's servant, longing for even the dust at his feet; then follow the bodily marks of ripened devotion, the temporality of separation, and the culminating declaration that the Lord remains the soul's only master even if he embraces, abandons, or wounds.

The natural unit here is the whole hymn. No shorter excerpt would preserve the inner sequence of the text, and no longer scope is warranted, since this is the only securely authored text by Caitanya himself. The eight verses hang together as a deliberate progression: praise of the name, diagnosis of unresponsiveness, discipline of receptivity, purification of motive, surrender of status, bodily transformation, *viraha* (devotional separation), and unconditional fidelity. In the larger structure of Caitanya's position, the hymn functions as seed-text rather than system. The later Gosvāmī literature does not supersede it; it unfolds what these verses already fix. Thus the present scope is not merely convenient. It is the smallest complete doctrinal unit in which Caitanya's own voice can still be heard across praxis, ontology, and affect.

## Translation

### Verse 1

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *ceto-darpaṇa-mārjanaṃ bhava-mahā-dāvāgni-nirvāpaṇaṃ*  
> *śreyaḥ-kairava-candrikā-vitaraṇaṃ vidyā-vadhū-jīvanam |*  
> *ānandāmbudhi-vardhanaṃ prati-padaṃ pūrṇāmṛtāsvādanaṃ*  
> *sarvātma-snapanaṃ paraṃ vijayate śrī-kṛṣṇa-saṅkīrtanam ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> It cleanses the mirror of consciousness.  
> It extinguishes the great forest-fire of worldly becoming.  
> It diffuses the moonlight over the white lotus of blessedness. It is the life of the bride called knowledge.  
> It enlarges the ocean of bliss. At every step it gives the tasting of full nectar.  
> It bathes the whole self. May the congregational chanting of Śrī Kṛṣṇa be supremely victorious.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| ceto-darpaṇa-mārjanam | *cetas* + *darpaṇa* + *mārjana* | compound action noun; nom. sg. neut. | cleansing of the mirror of consciousness |
| bhava-mahā-dāvāgni-nirvāpaṇam | *bhava* + *mahā* + *dāvāgni* + *nirvāpaṇa* | compound action noun; nom. sg. neut. | extinguishing of the great forest-fire of becoming |
| śreyaḥ-kairava-candrikā-vitaraṇam | *śreyas* + *kairava* + *candrikā* + *vitaraṇa* | compound action noun; nom. sg. neut. | spreading of moonlight for the night-lotus of blessedness |
| vidyā-vadhū-jīvanam | *vidyā* + *vadhū* + *jīvana* | compound nominal; nom. sg. neut. | the life of the bride called knowledge |
| ānandāmbudhi-vardhanam | *ānanda* + *ambudhi* + *vardhana* | compound action noun; nom. sg. neut. | enlargement of the ocean of bliss |
| prati-padam | *prati-pada* | indeclinable accusative adverbial | at every step, at each moment |
| pūrṇāmṛtāsvādanam | *pūrṇa* + *amṛta* + *āsvādana* | compound action noun; nom. sg. neut. | tasting of full nectar |
| sarvātma-snapanam | *sarva* + *ātman* + *snapana* | compound action noun; nom. sg. neut. | bathing of the whole self |
| param | *parama* | acc. sg. neut. used adverbially | supremely |
| vijayate | *vi-jī* | *laṭ*; 3rd sg.; ātmanepada; active-middle | triumphs, is victorious |
| śrī-kṛṣṇa-saṅkīrtanam | *śrī* + *kṛṣṇa* + *saṅkīrtana* | compound nominal; nom. sg. neut. | the congregational chanting of Śrī Kṛṣṇa |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *ceto-darpaṇa-mārjanam* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa + tatpuruṣa]: *cetasaḥ darpaṇasya mārjanam*.
- *bhava-mahā-dāvāgni-nirvāpaṇam* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa chain]: *bhavasya mahān dāvāgniḥ, tasya nirvāpaṇam*.
- *śreyaḥ-kairava-candrikā-vitaraṇam* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa chain]: *śreyasaḥ kairavasya candrikāyā vitaraṇam*.
- *vidyā-vadhū-jīvanam* [karmadhāraya + tatpuruṣa]: *vidyā eva vadhūḥ, tasyā jīvanam*.
- *ānandāmbudhi-vardhanam* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *ānandasya ambudheḥ vardhanam*.
- *pūrṇāmṛtāsvādanam* [karmadhāraya + tatpuruṣa]: *pūrṇam amṛtam, tasya āsvādanam*.
- *sarvātma-snapanam* [karmadhāraya + tatpuruṣa]: *sarva ātmā, tasya snapanam*.
- *śrī-kṛṣṇa-saṅkīrtanam* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *śrī-kṛṣṇasya saṅkīrtanam*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *śrī-kṛṣṇa-saṅkīrtanam*
- kriyā: *vijayate*
- predicative nominatives: *ceto-darpaṇa-mārjanam*, *bhava-mahā-dāvāgni-nirvāpaṇam*, *śreyaḥ-kairava-candrikā-vitaraṇam*, *vidyā-vadhū-jīvanam*, *ānandāmbudhi-vardhanam*, *pūrṇāmṛtāsvādanam*, *sarvātma-snapanam*
- adverbial modifier: *prati-padam*, *param*

**Verbal modality:**
- *vijayate*: *laṭ* / ātmanepada / active-middle

**Note:**
The verse is grammatically a single triumphal sentence. The sequence of neuter predicate nouns does not merely ornament *saṅkīrtana*; it specifies its causal range. Later Gauḍīya system-builders repeatedly unpack just these functions: practice, cognition, affect, and beatitude are not detached stages here but simultaneous powers of the name. Compare the practical presupposition stated in verse 3 and the motivational purification stated in verse 4.

### Verse 2

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *nāmnām akāri bahudhā nija-sarva-śaktis*  
> *tatrārpitā niyamitaḥ smaraṇe na kālaḥ |*  
> *etādṛśī tava kṛpā bhagavan mamāpi*  
> *durdaivam īdṛśam ihājani nānurāgaḥ ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Of the names, there was made a multiplicity; into them your own entire power was placed.  
> In remembering them, no fixed time has been imposed.  
> Such is your grace, O Lord, even toward me.  
> Yet such is my ill-fortune here: no attachment has arisen.

| nija-sarva-śaktis | *nija* + *sarva* + *śakti* | nom. sg. fem.; visarga assimilated before *t* | one's own entire power |
| tatra | *tatra* | indeclinable adverb | there, in them |
| arpitā | *arpita* < *arp* | past passive participle; nom. sg. fem. | placed, deposited |
| niyamitaḥ | *niyamita* < *ni-yam* | past passive participle; nom. sg. masc. | fixed, regulated |

| api | *api* | indeclinable particle | even |
| durdaivam | *durdaiva* | nom. sg. neut. | misfortune, ill-fate |
| īdṛśam | *īdṛś* | nom. sg. neut. | such, of this kind |
| iha | *iha* | indeclinable adverb | here, in this case |
| ajani | *jan* | *luṅ*; 3rd sg.; ātmanepada/passive-middle | arose, was born |
| na | *na* | indeclinable negation; in sandhi with following word | not |
| anurāgaḥ | *anurāga* | nom. sg. masc. | attachment, loving attraction |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *nija-sarva-śaktis* [karmadhāraya + ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa sense]: *nijā ca sā sarvā ca śaktiḥ*.
- *durdaivam* [nañ/kutsā formation]: *duṣṭaṃ daivam*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- first clause:
  - karman: *nāmnām* (genitive governed idiomatically by the passive formation)
  - kriyā: *akāri*
  - kartṛ-like predicate: *nija-sarva-śaktis* ... *arpitā*
  - adhikaraṇa: *tatra*
- second clause:
  - adhikaraṇa: *smaraṇe*
  - kartṛ: *kālaḥ*
  - kriyā (understood with passive sense): *niyamitaḥ* + *na*
- third clause:
  - kartṛ: *kṛpā*
  - sambandha: *tava*, *mama api*
- fourth clause:
  - kartṛ: *durdaivam*
  - kriyā: *ajani*
  - negated predicate: *na anurāgaḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *akāri*: *luṅ* / ātmanepada / passive-middle
- *ajani*: *luṅ* / ātmanepada / passive-middle

**Note:**
The theological claim is double. The divine name is not an arbitrary sign, because the Lord's own *śakti* is said to have been placed in it. Yet accessibility alone does not guarantee response. The final turn to *durdaiva* makes failure internal, not external: the deficiency lies not in the gift but in the heart's non-arousal. This verse therefore mediates between the objective efficacy of verse 1 and the ethical preparation of verse 3.

### Verse 3

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *tṛṇād api sunīcena taror iva sahiṣṇunā |*  
> *amāninā mānadena kīrtanīyaḥ sadā hariḥ ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> By one more humble even than a blade of grass, and forbearing like a tree,  
> by one who claims no honour and gives honour, Hari is always to be chanted.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| tṛṇāt | *tṛṇa* | abl. sg. neut. | than grass, than a blade of grass |
| api | *api* | indeclinable particle | even |
| sunīcena | *su-nīca* | instr. sg. masc./neut. | by one very lowly, very humble |
| taror | *taru* | abl. sg. masc.; sandhi form before *iva* | than a tree |
| iva | *iva* | indeclinable particle | like |
| sahiṣṇunā | *sahiṣṇu* | instr. sg. masc. | by one forbearing, patient |
| amāninā | *a-mānin* | instr. sg. masc. | by one not seeking honour |
| mānadena | *mānada* | instr. sg. masc. | by one giving honour |
| kīrtanīyaḥ | *kīrt* | gerundive; nom. sg. masc. | to be chanted, to be praised |
| sadā | *sadā* | indeclinable adverb | always |
| hariḥ | *hari* | nom. sg. masc. | Hari |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *sunīcena* [upapada/karmadhāraya-like intensification]: *su nīcena*.
- *amāninā* [nañ-tatpuruṣa]: *na mānī yena saḥ*.
- *mānadena* [upapada-tatpuruṣa]: *mānaṃ dadāti iti*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- subject-predicate complex: *hariḥ* ... *kīrtanīyaḥ*
- instrumental qualifications of the adhikārin: *sunīcena*, *sahiṣṇunā*, *amāninā*, *mānadena*
- comparative ablatives: *tṛṇāt*, *taror*
- adverb: *sadā*

**Verbal modality:**
- *kīrtanīyaḥ*: gerundive / passive obligation / masculine nominative predicate

**Note:**
The verse gives not merely moral advice but the grammar of devotional eligibility. The instrumentals define the subject-position from which *saṅkīrtana* becomes sustained rather than episodic. Later Gauḍīya practice treats this as a standing hermeneutic for verse 1: the name is victorious in itself, but its victory becomes existentially continuous only where vanity has been dislodged.

### Verse 4

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *na dhanaṃ na janaṃ na sundarīṃ*  
> *kavitāṃ vā jagad-īśa kāmaye |*  
> *mama janmani janmanīśvare*  
> *bhavatād bhaktir ahaitukī tvayi ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> I do not desire wealth, nor people, nor a beautiful woman,  
> nor even poetic brilliance, O Lord of the world.  
> O Lord, birth after birth, for me,

| vā | *vā* | indeclinable particle | or even |
| jagad-īśa | *jagat* + *īśa* | voc. sg. masc. compound | O Lord of the world |
| kāmaye | *kām* | *laṭ*; 1st sg.; ātmanepada; middle | I desire |
| mama | *asmad* | gen. sg. | for me, of me |
| janmani | *janman* | loc. sg. neut. | in birth |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *jagad-īśa* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *jagata īśa*.
- *ahaitukī* [nañ-tatpuruṣa]: *ahetukī*, "not grounded in ulterior motive."

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: implied first person in *kāmaye*
- karman: *dhanam*, *janam*, *sundarīm*, *kavitām*
- sambodhana: *jagad-īśa*, *īśvare*
- benedictive clause:
  - kartṛ: *bhaktiḥ*
  - kriyā: *bhavatāt*
  - sambandha: *mama*
  - locative target: *tvayi*
  - temporal reiteration: *janmani janmani*

**Verbal modality:**
- *kāmaye*: *laṭ* / ātmanepada / middle
- *bhavatāt*: *āśīr-liṅ* / parasmaipada / benedictive

**Note:**
The refusal of *mokṣa* is not explicit in the lexicon of the verse, but it is structurally implied. Birth after birth is accepted; what is rejected is devotion as instrument toward some other acquisition. That is why *ahaitukī bhakti* becomes a decisive Gauḍīya formula. This verse clarifies the motivational purification presupposed by verses 1-3 and anticipates the non-transactional love of verse 8.

### Verse 5

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *ayi nanda-tanuja kiṅkaraṃ*  
> *patitaṃ māṃ viṣame bhavāmbudhau*  
> *kṛpayā tava pāda-paṅkaja-*

| viṣame | *viṣama* | loc. sg. masc./neut. | in the dangerous, uneven |

| kṛpayā | *kṛpā* | instr. sg. fem. | by grace, through compassion |
| tava | *tvad* | gen. sg. | your |

- *nanda-tanuja* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *nandasya tanujaḥ*.
- *bhavāmbudhau* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *bhavasya ambudhau*.
- *pāda-paṅkaja-sthita-dhūlī-sadṛśam* [compound chain]: *pādasya paṅkaje sthitā yā dhūlī, tasyāḥ sadṛśam*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- sambodhana: *ayi nanda-tanuja*
- karman: *kiṅkaram*, *patitam*, *mām*, *...sadṛśam* (all qualifying the same object)
- adhikaraṇa: *viṣame bhavāmbudhau*
- karaṇa: *kṛpayā*
- kriyā: *vicintaya*

**Verbal modality:**
- *vicintaya*: *loṭ* / parasmaipada / imperative

**Note:**
Here the speaker's self-description is ontologically decisive: the *jīva* is not identified with Kṛṣṇa but confessed as *kiṅkara*, servant. Yet the requested proximity is extreme. Not union by erasure, but placement as the dust at the feet. The verse is thus a compact locus for the Gauḍīya refusal both of self-sufficient autonomy and of impersonal absorption. Compare the thinker entry's summary of eternal servanthood, and compare verse 8, where servanthood persists even under apparent abandonment.

### Verse 6

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *nayanaṃ galad-aśru-dhārayā*

| vadanam | *vadana* | nom./acc. sg. neut. | mouth, face |
| gadgada-ruddhayā | *gadgada* + *ruddha* | instr. sg. fem. compound | with choked, broken |
| girā | *gir* | instr. sg. fem. | voice, speech |
| pulakaiḥ | *pulaka* | instr. pl. masc. | by horripilations, gooseflesh |
| nicitam | *nicita* < *ni-ci* | past passive participle; nom./acc. sg. neut. | covered, heaped over |

- *galad-aśru-dhārayā* [bahuvrīhi/tatpuruṣa chain]: *galantaḥ aśravaḥ, teṣāṃ dhārā, tayā*.
- *gadgada-ruddhayā* [karmadhāraya]: *gadgadā ca sā ruddhā ca*.
- *nāma-grahaṇe* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *nāmnaḥ grahaṇe*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- temporal-interrogative frame: *kadā*
- adhikaraṇa: *tava nāma-grahaṇe*
- instrumental qualifiers: *galad-aśru-dhārayā*, *gadgada-ruddhayā girā*, *pulakaiḥ*
- subjects/predicates: *nayanam*, *vadanam*, *vapuḥ nicitam*
- kriyā: *bhaviṣyati*

**Verbal modality:**
- *bhaviṣyati*: *lṛṭ* / parasmaipada / future

**Note:**
The verse asks not for doctrinal correctness but for embodiment. Tears, broken voice, and *pulaka* are treated as intelligible bodily consequences of ripened remembrance, not as accidental ornaments. Later Gauḍīya literature will classify such states with much greater technical precision, but the present verse already fixes the principle: devotion is affectively and somatically legible.

### Verse 7

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *yugāyitaṃ nimeṣeṇa*  
> *cakṣuṣā prāvṛṣāyitam |*  
> *śūnyāyitaṃ jagat sarvaṃ*  
> *govinda-viraheṇa me ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> A blink has become like an age.  
> My eye has become like the rainy season.  
> The whole world has become void.  
> Such is it for me in separation from Govinda.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| yugāyitam | *yuga* + *āyita* | past passive participial formation; nom./acc. sg. neut. | made into an age |
| nimeṣeṇa | *nimeṣa* | instr. sg. masc. | by a blink, by a moment |
| cakṣuṣā | *cakṣus* | instr. sg. neut. | by the eye, in the eye |
| prāvṛṣāyitam | *prāvṛṣ* + *āyita* | past passive participial formation; nom./acc. sg. neut. | made into the rainy season |
| śūnyāyitam | *śūnya* + *āyita* | past passive participial formation; nom./acc. sg. neut. | made void, rendered empty |
| jagat | *jagat* | nom./acc. sg. neut. | world |
| sarvam | *sarva* | nom./acc. sg. neut. | whole, entire |
| govinda-viraheṇa | *govinda* + *viraha* | instr. sg. masc. compound | by separation from Govinda |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *govinda-viraheṇa* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *govindasya viraheṇa*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- instrumental causes: *nimeṣeṇa*, *cakṣuṣā*, *govinda-viraheṇa*
- predicative states: *yugāyitam*, *prāvṛṣāyitam*, *śūnyāyitam*
- locus/subject of final predicate: *jagat sarvam*
- experiencer: *me*

**Verbal modality:**
- No overt finite verb; a series of participial predicates with the copula understood.

**Note:**
This is the hymn's most compressed statement of *viraha*. Time distends, tears become seasonal, and the world is not denied but experienced as emptied by absence. The grammar matters: the world is *śūnyāyitam*, "made void," not declared intrinsically unreal. Later Gauḍīya poetics of separation elaborate precisely this paradox, but the present verse already gives its experiential grammar.

### Verse 8

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *āśliṣya vā pāda-ratāṃ pinaṣṭu mām*  
> *adarśanān marma-hatāṃ karotu vā |*  
> *yathā tathā vā vidadhātu lampaṭo*  
> *mat-prāṇa-nāthas tu sa eva nāparaḥ ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Let that libertine embrace me, or let him crush me under his feet.  
> Or, by not appearing, let him make me stricken in the vital core.  
> Let him act in whatever manner whatever.  
> Yet he alone, and no other, is the lord of my life-breath.

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *pāda-ratām* [textually difficult local reading]: construed from the transmitted phrase as a feet-related adverbial expression with *pinaṣṭu*; compare the more commonly cited reading *pāda-ratāṃ*.
- *marma-hatām* [tatpuruṣa]: *marmaṇi hatām*.
- *mat-prāṇa-nāthaḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa chain]: *mama prāṇānāṃ nāthaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- main object throughout: *mām*
- alternative imperative clauses:
  - *āśliṣya* ... *pinaṣṭu mām*
  - *adarśanāt* ... *marma-hatām karotu*
  - *yathā tathā vā vidadhātu*
- kartṛ of all imperative verbs: *lampaṭaḥ*, resumed as *saḥ*
- final nominal assertion: *sa eva nāparaḥ* with predicate *mat-prāṇa-nāthaḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *āśliṣya*: absolutive
- *pinaṣṭu*: *loṭ* / parasmaipada / imperative
- *karotu*: *loṭ* / parasmaipada / imperative
- *vidadhātu*: *loṭ* / parasmaipada / imperative

**Note:**
The local witness's third word is textually awkward, and I have therefore translated conservatively while flagging the difficulty. The philosophical force of the verse lies elsewhere and remains stable: devotion is no contract. Embrace, trampling, and absence are all admitted as possibilities within the Lord's freedom, yet none dislodges exclusive allegiance. This is the hymn's final and hardest statement of *ahaitukī bhakti*: not merely devotion without ulterior reward, but devotion that persists even when reciprocity is withheld.

## Editorial closing

This passage settles three things with unusual firmness. First, the name is not ancillary; it is the principal operative medium of transformation. Second, devotional life requires a determinate ethical and affective discipline: humility, non-claimancy, and desirelessness are not adjuncts but conditions of sustained *saṅkīrtana*. Third, the highest devotional state is marked not by absorption without remainder but by intensified relation, especially in the form of service and separation. What the hymn does not settle is the later Gauḍīya system in its full technical breadth. It does not itself provide the metaphysical exposition of *acintya-bhedābheda*, the taxonomy of *rasa*, or the detailed ontology of *śakti*. Those later tasks are taken up elsewhere: in the *Caitanya-Caritāmṛta*'s doctrinal dialogues, especially *Madhya-līlā* 20-25; in Jīva Gosvāmin's *Tattva-Sandarbha* §51 ff. and *Bhagavat-Sandarbha* §16; and in Rūpa Gosvāmin's *Bhakti-Rasāmṛta-Sindhu* 2.5. But those works are elaborations, not replacements. The eight verses translated here remain the irreducible seed.
