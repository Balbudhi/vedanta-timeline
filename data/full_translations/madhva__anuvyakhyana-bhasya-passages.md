# Anuvyākhyāna — Extended Translation
**By:** Madhva  
**Locus scope:** Four programmatic loci already canonized in the site corpus for this work: Anuvy. 1.1.13-15; 1.4.111-112; 2.3.66-69; 3.3.1.  
**Source edition transcribed from:** GRETIL plaintext transformation of `sa_mAdhva-anuvyAkhyAna.xml`, based on Sansknet Project input, version 2020-07-31.  
**Translator:** Pāṇinian-rich extraction by Codex 5.4 (reasoning=high), 2026-05-10.

## Editorial introduction

These four loci are dispersed, but the dispersion is doctrinal rather than accidental. No single short contiguous unit of the *Anuvyākhyāna* contains all the passages already treated in the site corpus as load-bearing for Madhva's reading of Vedānta; the work returns to its basic theses under distinct *adhikaraṇas* and lets each sūtra-context sharpen a different edge. Taken together, Anuvy. 1.1.13-15, 1.4.111-112, 2.3.66-69, and 3.3.1 give a compact map of the whole argumentative project. In 1.1, commenting at the outset of the first adhyāya, Madhva fixes the asymmetry between the *svatantra* (the independent) and the *paratantra* (the dependent): substance, action, time, intrinsic nature, and the *jīva* stand only by Viṣṇu's favor, and *mokṣa* does not arise from knowledge as a self-sufficient natural property. In 1.4, still within the first adhyāya, he names the *bhedapañcaka* (fivefold complex of differences) as the very articulation of the world-manifold. In 2.3 he turns to the harder hermeneutic question: what to do with *abheda* (non-difference) texts. His answer is precise. Such texts are not erased; they are reassigned to *sādṛśya* (similarity), predominance, or honorific designation, never to essential identity. Finally, at 3.3.1, opening the third pāda of the third adhyāya, he states the soteriological order: *vairāgya* (dispassion) stabilizes *bhakti* (devotion), *bhakti* issues in *upāsā* (worshipful contemplation), and *upāsā* culminates in *āparokṣya* (immediate realization) of Viṣṇu. This dossier therefore presents not a random anthology but the doctrinal spine of the work in miniature.

## Translation

### Anuvy. 1.1.13

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *dravyaṃ karma ca kālaśca svabhāvo jīva eva ca*  
> *yadanugrahataḥ santi na santi yadupekṣayā //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Substance, action, and time, intrinsic nature, and indeed the individual self as well,  
> exist through whose favor; they do not exist through whose disregard.

Compare the same dependence-claim in *Brahma-Sūtra-Bhāṣya* 1.1.2, where cosmological origination is read as ontological dependence rather than mere temporal production.

### Anuvy. 1.4.111

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *jīveśvarabhidā caiva jaḍeśvarabhidā tathā*

- *jīvabhedaḥ* [tatpuruṣa]: *jīvānāṃ bhedaḥ*.
- *jaḍajīvabhidā* [tatpuruṣa]: *jaḍasya jīvasya ca bhidā*.
- *jaḍabhedaḥ* [tatpuruṣa]: *jaḍānāṃ bhedaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *prapañcaḥ*
- predicate nominative: *bhedapañcakaḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- Nominal sentence; no finite verb is expressed.

**Note:**
This is the locus classicus for the *bhedapañcaka*. The important point is grammatical, not merely doctrinal: the world-manifold is not said to possess five differences as accidental features; it is identified as that fivefold articulation. The verse therefore supports Madhva's realism at the level of the world's own structure, not merely at the level of epistemic appearance.

### Anuvy. 1.4.112

**Sanskrit (IAST):**

This verse is the natural sequel to 1.4.111 and the natural antecedent to 2.3.66-69.

### Anuvy. 2.3.66

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *śuddhaḥ sākṣī yadā siddho duḥkhitvaṃ vāryate katham*

Once the *sākṣī* is admitted as a cognition that discriminates, *bheda* is not an optional metaphysical add-on but already built into the epistemic ground.

- *pradhānatvāt* [abstract derivative]: from *pradhāna*, "predominant, chief."

- *svarūpābhidām* [tatpuruṣa]: *svarūpasya abhidām*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- hetu/apādāna: *sādṛśyāt*, *pradhānatvāt*, *svātantryāt*
- karman: *abhidām*, negated counterpart *svarūpābhidām*
- sambandha: *jīvasya*
- karaṇa/instrument of naming: *īśena*

**Verbal modality:**
- *āhuḥ*: laṭ / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
The verse gives Madhva's positive semantics for transferred predication. An honorific or analogical use of *īśa* with respect to the *jīva* is allowed, but *svarūpābhidhā* is explicitly denied. This is the cleanest local statement of the principle that *abheda* language may be admitted without conceding essential identity.

### Anuvy. 3.3.1

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *vairāgyato bhaktidārḍhyaṃ tenopāsā yadā bhavet*  
> *āparokṣyaṃ bhaved viṣṇoriti pādakramo bhavet //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> From dispassion comes firmness of devotion; then, when worshipful contemplation arises through that,  
> immediate realization of Viṣṇu arises: such is the sequence of this pāda.

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *bhaktidārḍhyam* [tatpuruṣa]: *bhakteḥ dārḍhyam*.
- *āparokṣyam* [negative tatpuruṣa]: *na parokṣam*.
- *pādakramaḥ* [tatpuruṣa]: *pādasya kramaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- apādāna: *vairāgyataḥ*
- kartṛ/predicate result: *bhaktidārḍhyam*
- karaṇa: *tena*
- kartṛ: *upāsā*
- kartṛ: *āparokṣyam*
- sambandha: *viṣṇoḥ*
- kartṛ in meta-statement: *pādakramaḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *bhavet* (all three occurrences): vidhi-liṅ / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
The verse is both doctrinal and editorial: it states the path and announces that this is the organizing order of the pāda. The sequence matters. *Bhakti* is not an ornamental accompaniment to knowledge; it is the condition under which *upāsā* matures into *āparokṣya*. Later in the same pāda, Anuvy. 3.3.170 and 3.3.193 return to Hari-directed *upāsanā* as necessary for *mokṣa*.

## Editorial closing

What these loci settle is the internal consistency of Madhva's mature architecture. They settle, first, that dependence on Viṣṇu is not confined to cosmology but extends to ontology and soteriology alike (1.1.13-15). They settle, second, that the world is not merely accompanied by difference but constituted as the fivefold articulation of difference (1.4.111-112). They settle, third, that identity-language in *śruti* and *Purāṇa* is to be read through likeness, predominance, and transferred designation, not through *svarūpābheda* (2.3.66-69). They settle, finally, that the path to liberation culminates in immediate realization of Viṣṇu through the ordered sequence of *vairāgya*, *bhakti*, and *upāsā* (3.3.1). What they do not yet settle is the fuller mechanics of karmic exhaustion, the exclusivity of Hari-*upāsanā*, and the graded hierarchy of liberated beings. Those are taken up later within the same pāda at Anuvy. 3.3.152-154, 3.3.170, and 3.3.193, and in compressed seed form already in *Brahma-Sūtra-Bhāṣya* 1.1.2.
