## Editorial introduction

The honest scope here is narrow. *Nyāya-Vivaraṇa* is, on the evidence of the local corpus, a short methodological companion to Madhva's *Brahma-Sūtra-Bhāṣya*: it lays out the *nyāya* (reasoned interpretive procedure) by which *adhikaraṇas* are parsed, *viṣaya-vākyas* identified, framing *sūtras* treated as *upakrama* and *upasaṃhāra*, and the joint use of *śruti*, *yukti*, and *anubhava* coordinated. That is the work's place in the system. It stands between the compressed doctrinal statements of the *Bhāṣya* and the more expansive dialectic of the *Anuvyākhyāna*. It is thus not a detachable ornament but part of Madhva's larger attempt to show that his realist reading of the *Brahma-Sūtra*, especially in the difference-laden materials of 2.3, is governed by a stable interpretive method rather than by ad hoc polemical pressure.

But that larger significance does not remove the philological limit. The on-disk primary corpus contains scanned Madhva *Brahma-Sūtra-Bhāṣya* PDFs and secondary discussions, yet no readable standalone text of *Nyāya-Vivaraṇa* itself. The one passage-level witness now recoverable on disk appears not in a primary edition but in B.N.K. Sharma's English study, where a line is cited in the course of explaining *aparokṣa-jñāna* and *bimbāparokṣa* in connection with *Brahma-Sūtra* 3.3.53. Since the OCR of that Sanskrit line is corrupt beyond secure normalization, a Cambridge-style line-by-line edition of a natural unit would here require invention. It is better to mark the limit precisely. What follows therefore gives the recoverable witness, states what can and cannot be done with it, and records the local secondary apparatus that points to the work's broader doctrinal role.

## Editorial closing

This file settles one thing and leaves several others open. What is settled is the textual situation on disk. The work is not absent in every sense: it is bibliographically attested, doctrinally summarized, and once explicitly cited in the *aparokṣa-jñāna* context. But no continuous readable Sanskrit unit of the work is presently available on disk, and so no honest full translation of an *adhikaraṇa*, chapter, or other natural unit can yet be offered here. What remains open are the very points for which *Nyāya-Vivaraṇa* is most valuable: the exact formulation of Madhva's rules for *adhikaraṇa* division, the determination of *viṣaya-vākya*, and the framing use of *upakrama* and *upasaṃhāra*, especially in relation to *Brahma-Sūtra* 2.3 and the contested reading of 2.3.43 noted in the local synopsis. Those matters are picked up downstream by Jayatīrtha's operational scholastic use of the method, and upstream by the corresponding interpretive practice visible in Madhva's *Brahma-Sūtra-Bhāṣya* and *Anuvyākhyāna*. A future file should be replaced from a readable primary edition once that witness is acquired or transcribed.
