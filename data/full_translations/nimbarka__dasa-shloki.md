# Daśa-Ślokī — Extended Translation
**By:** Nimbārka  
**Locus scope:** Entire ten-verse compendium (*Daśa-Ślokī* 1-10); this is the natural unit because the work itself is only ten verses, and the keyed passages for this work are distributed across the opening and middle verses in a way that presupposes the whole arc from ontology to *upāsanā* and *bhakti*.  
**Source edition transcribed from:** Nimbārka, *Daśa-Ślokī* with *Laghu-Mañjūṣā*, ed. Paṇḍit Dhundhiraj Śāstrī, Chowkhamba Sanskrit Series 358 (Benares, 1927), on-disk witness `materials/primary_texts/sanskrit/vedanta/full_corpus/nimbarka_dasasloki_archive.pdf`; for verses 3 and 6-10, and for variant control in verses 2 and 5, the text was cross-checked against V. Ramnarace, *Rādhā-Kṛṣṇa’s Vedāntic Debut* (University of Edinburgh, 2015), which quotes the full *Daśa-Ślokī*.  
**Translator:** Pāṇinian-rich extraction by Codex 5.4 (reasoning=high), 2026-05-09.

## Editorial introduction

The *Daśa-Ślokī* is Nimbārka’s shortest self-presentation, but it is not a miscellaneous anthology. It is a tightly ordered doctrinal synopsis. Verses 1-3 fix the three fundamental reals: *cit* (sentient selves), *acit* (the insentient order), and the categorial conditions under which bondage, plurality, and embodiment are to be understood. Verses 4-5 then identify *Parabrahman* not merely as a generic supreme Lord but as Kṛṣṇa, and indeed as Kṛṣṇa inseparably conjoined with the daughter of Vṛṣabhānu. Verses 6-10 turn from ontology to practice and end-state: constant *upāsanā* (meditative worship), the claim that cognition is not sheer falsification because all things are Brahman-grounded, the exclusivity of refuge in Kṛṣṇa’s feet, the rise of higher *bhakti* through grace, and the five topics that the aspirant must know.

That is why the whole ten-verse work, and not a smaller cluster, is the proper unit here. The keyed passages in the thinker file fall in verses 1, 2, 4, and 5. Yet verses 6-10 complete the argument those verses initiate. Without them one would have Nimbārka’s categorial map and object of worship, but not his account of why meditation is required, how grace operates, or how higher *bhakti* differs from merely instrumental devotion. The work also stands in a precise relation to Nimbārka’s larger corpus. The public Vedāntic idiom of the *Vedānta-Pārijāta-Saurabha* is here compressed and sharpened; compare especially *Vedānta-Pārijāta-Saurabha* 1.1.1 on Bhagavat-grace, 1.3.8 on the Kumāra-Nārada lineage, 3.2.24 on *upāsanā* as *bhakti*, and 4.2.16 on the path opened by grace. The later *Prapannakalpavallī* and *Mantrarahasyaṣoḍaśī* unfold what verses 8-10 here state only in germ.

## Translation

### Verse 1

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *jñānasvarūpaṃ ca harer adhīnaṃ  
> śarīrasaṃyogaviyogayogyam |  
> aṇuṃ hi jīvaṃ pratidehabhinnaṃ  
> jñātṛtvavantaṃ cānantam āhuḥ || 1 ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> The *jīva* (individual self) is of the nature of knowledge, and dependent on Hari.  
> It is fit for conjunction with a body, and fit for disjunction from it.  
> Minute indeed, and distinct in each body.  
> Endowed with knowerhood, and endless in plurality, so they say.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| jñānasvarūpam | jñāna-svarūpa | acc sg masc | of the nature of knowledge |
| ca | ca | indecl | and |
| hareḥ | hari | gen sg masc | of Hari |
| adhīnam | adhīna | acc sg masc | dependent |
| śarīrasaṃyogaviyogayogyam | śarīra-saṃyoga-viyoga-yogya | acc sg masc | fit for union with and separation from a body |
| aṇum | aṇu | acc sg masc | minute; atomic |
| hi | hi | indecl | indeed |
| jīvam | jīva | acc sg masc | the individual self |
| pratidehabhinnam | pratideha-bhinna | acc sg masc | distinct in each body |
| jñātṛtvavantam | jñātṛtva-vant | acc sg masc | possessing knowerhood |
| ca | ca | indecl | and |
| anantam | ananta | acc sg masc | endless; innumerable |
| āhuḥ | √ah | 3 pl laṭ parasmaipada | they say |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *jñānasvarūpa* [karmadhāraya]: *jñānaṃ svarūpaṃ yasya saḥ*.
- *śarīrasaṃyogaviyogayogya* [tatpuruṣa]: *śarīreṇa saṃyogāya viyogāya ca yogyaḥ*.
- *pratidehabhinna* [avyayībhāva + bahuvrīhi sense]: *prati dehaṃ bhinnaḥ*.
- *jñātṛtvavant* [taddhita/possessive formation]: *jñātṛtvaṃ yasya asti saḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- karman: *jīvam*.
- sambandha: *hareḥ* with *adhīnam*.
- adhikaraṇa (implicit by relation): *śarīra-* within *śarīrasaṃyogaviyogayogyam*.

**Verbal modality:**
- *āhuḥ*: laṭ / parasmaipada / active.

**Note:**
The verse is a definition, not an exhortation. All the accusatives stand in apposition to *jīvam*, and the final *āhuḥ* gathers them into a reported doctrinal determination. The dependence on Hari is not an added devotional flourish; it is one of the constitutive marks of the self. Compare *Vedānta-Pārijāta-Saurabha* on the self’s derivative status and on the rejection of a merely adventitious difference.

### Verse 2

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *anādimāyāpariyuktarūpaṃ  
> tv enaṃ vidur vai bhagavatprasādāt |  
> muktaṃ ca baddhaṃ kila baddhamuktaṃ  
> prabhedabāhulyam athāpi bodhyam || 2 ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> As having a form conjoined with beginningless *māyā* (insentient causal power), this one they indeed know,  
> and know only through the Lord’s grace.  
> As liberated, as bound, and, it is said, as bound-liberated.  
> And further, its abundance of subdivisions is also to be understood.

| tu | tu | indecl | but; now |
| enam | etad | acc sg masc | this one |
| viduḥ | √vid | 3 pl laṭ parasmaipada | know |
| vai | vai | indecl | indeed |
| bhagavatprasādāt | bhagavat-prasāda | abl sg masc | through the Lord’s grace |
| muktam | mukta | acc sg masc | liberated |
| ca | ca | indecl | and |
| baddham | baddha | acc sg masc | bound |
| kila | kila | indecl | it is said; indeed |
| baddhamuktam | baddha-mukta | acc sg masc | bound-liberated |

| atha | atha | indecl | further |
| api | api | indecl | also |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *anādimāyāpariyuktarūpa* [bahuvrīhi]: *anādyā māyayā pariyuktaṃ rūpaṃ yasya tam*.
- *bhagavatprasāda* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *bhagavataḥ prasādaḥ*.
- *baddhamukta* [dvandva-like karmadhāraya / doctrinal label]: one who is in a mixed state of bondage and release.
- *prabhedabāhulya* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *prabhedānāṃ bāhulyam*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- karman: *enam*.
- karaṇa/hetu: *bhagavatprasādāt*.
- predicative qualifiers: *muktam*, *baddham*, *baddhamuktam*.

**Verbal modality:**
- *viduḥ*: laṭ / parasmaipada / active.
- *bodhyam*: gerundive / passive necessity.

**Note:**
The locally verified thinker record preserves a divergent third pāda and omits *tu* in the second; the fuller transmitted text adopted here, supported by Ramnarace’s complete quotation and by an independent traditional transcription, yields the familiar threefold classification of selves: *mukta*, *baddha*, and *baddha-mukta*. The verse is important because grace is made epistemically load-bearing: the self is not simply inferred; it is known through Bhagavat’s favor. Compare *Vedānta-Pārijāta-Saurabha* 1.1.1 and 4.2.16.

### Verse 3

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *aprākṛtaṃ prākṛtarūpakaṃ ca  
> kālasvarūpaṃ tad acetanaṃ matam |  
> māyāpradhānādipadapravācyaṃ  
> śuklādibhedāś ca same 'pi tatra || 3 ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> The insentient is held to be non-prākṛta, and also of prākṛta form.  
> It is also of the nature of time.  
> It is denoted by terms such as *māyā* and *pradhāna*.  
> And within that one same domain there are distinctions such as white and the rest.

|---|---|---|---|
| aprākṛtam | aprākṛta | nom/acc sg neut | non-prākṛta; not made of ordinary prakṛti |
| prākṛtarūpakam | prākṛta-rūpaka | nom/acc sg neut | having prākṛta form |
| ca | ca | indecl | and |
| kālasvarūpam | kāla-svarūpa | nom/acc sg neut | of the nature of time |
| tat | tad | nom sg neut | that |
| acetanam | acetana | nom sg neut | the insentient |
| matam | mata | nom/acc sg neut ppp used predicatively | considered; held |
| māyāpradhānādipadapravācyam | māyā-pradhāna-ādi-pada-pravācya | nom/acc sg neut | denotable by terms such as māyā and pradhāna |
| śuklādibhedāḥ | śukla-ādi-bheda | nom pl masc | distinctions such as white and the rest |
| ca | ca | indecl | and |
| same | sama | loc sg neut | in the same; in what is one |
| api | api | indecl | even; although |
| tatra | tatra | indecl | there; in that regard |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *prākṛtarūpaka* [karmadhāraya]: *prākṛtaṃ rūpakaṃ yasya tat*.
- *kālasvarūpa* [karmadhāraya]: *kālaḥ svarūpaṃ yasya tat*.
- *māyāpradhānādipadapravācya* [tatpuruṣa]: *māyā-pradhāna-ādi-padaiḥ pravācyaṃ*.
- *śuklādibheda* [tatpuruṣa]: *śuklādīnāṃ bhedāḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ (logical subject): *tat acetanam*.
- predicative complements: *aprākṛtam*, *prākṛtarūpakam*, *kālasvarūpam*, *māyāpradhānādipadapravācyam*.
- adhikaraṇa: *same ... tatra*.

**Verbal modality:**
- *matam*: past passive participle used impersonally; “is held”.

**Note:**
The verse gives Nimbārka’s threefold *acit*: the non-prākṛta, the prākṛta, and time. The last pāda matters philosophically. Multiplicity of properties does not cancel the unitary categorial status of *acit*; *same 'pi* explicitly keeps sameness and differentiation together. The fuller anti-heterodox polemical defense of permanent reals is developed in *Vedānta-Pārijāta-Saurabha* 2.2.

### Verse 4

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *svabhāvato 'pāstasamastadoṣam  
> aśeṣakalyāṇaguṇaikarāśim |  
> vyūhāṅginaṃ brahma paraṃ vareṇyaṃ  
> dhyāyema kṛṣṇaṃ kamalekṣaṇaṃ harim || 4 ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Whose every defect is removed by his very nature.  
> Who is the single mass of endless auspicious qualities.  
> Who has the *vyūhas* (ordered divine emanations) as his limbs, the supreme Brahman, the most worthy of choice and worship.  
> Let us meditate on Kṛṣṇa, Hari, the lotus-eyed one.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| svabhāvataḥ | svabhāva | abl sg masc used adverbially | by his own nature |
| apāstasamastadoṣam | apāsta-samasta-doṣa | acc sg masc | from whom all defects are removed |
| aśeṣakalyāṇaguṇaikarāśim | aśeṣa-kalyāṇa-guṇa-eka-rāśi | acc sg masc | the sole mass of limitless auspicious qualities |
| vyūhāṅginam | vyūha-aṅgin | acc sg masc | having the vyūhas as limbs |
| brahma | brahman | acc sg neut | Brahman |
| param | para | acc sg neut | supreme |
| vareṇyam | vareṇya | acc sg neut | choiceworthy; worship-worthy |
| dhyāyema | √dhyai | 1 pl विधि-liṅ ātmanepada | let us meditate upon |
| kṛṣṇam | kṛṣṇa | acc sg masc | Kṛṣṇa |
| kamalekṣaṇam | kamala-īkṣaṇa | acc sg masc | lotus-eyed |
| harim | hari | acc sg masc | Hari |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *apāstasamastadoṣa* [bahuvrīhi]: *apāstāḥ samastā doṣāḥ yasmāt tam*.
- *aśeṣakalyāṇaguṇaikarāśi* [bahuvrīhi]: *aśeṣāṇāṃ kalyāṇaguṇānām ekaḥ rāśiḥ yasya tam*.
- *vyūhāṅgin* [bahuvrīhi]: *vyūhā aṅgāni yasya tam*.
- *kamalekṣaṇa* [bahuvrīhi]: *kamale iva īkṣaṇe yasya tam*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- karman: *kṛṣṇam*.
- appositional qualifiers to the same object: *apāstasamastadoṣam*, *aśeṣakalyāṇaguṇaikarāśim*, *vyūhāṅginam*, *brahma param vareṇyam*, *kamalekṣaṇam*, *harim*.

**Verbal modality:**
- *dhyāyema*: vidhi-liṅ / ātmanepada / middle.

**Note:**
This is the pivot of the whole poem. The public Vedāntic designation “supreme Brahman” is not left indeterminate; it is directly identified with Kṛṣṇa. Śrīnivāsa exploits this verse programmatically in the *Vedāntakaustubha*, and Ramnarace is right to see here the bridge between Nimbārka’s bhāṣya-voice and the more esoteric diction of the ten verses.

### Verse 5

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *aṅge tu vāme vṛṣabhānujāṃ mudā  
> virājamānām anurūpasaubhagām |  
> sakhīsahasraiḥ parisevitāṃ sadā  
> smarema devīṃ sakaleṣṭakāmadām || 5 ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> And on the left side of his body, the daughter of Vṛṣabhānu, shining in delight.

- *anurūpasaubhaga* [bahuvrīhi]: *anurūpaṃ saubhagaṃ yasyāḥ tām*.
- *sakhīsahasra* [dvigu]: *sakhīnām sahasram*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- adhikaraṇa: *aṅge*, *vāme*.
- karaṇa/manner: *mudā*.

- karman: *devīm*.

**Verbal modality:**
- *smarema*: vidhi-liṅ / parasmaipada / active.

**Note:**
The locally verified thinker record preserves a different recension: *aṅke ... smaret ... sadā*. The reading adopted here, supported by the fuller transmitted text, better matches the first-person plural liturgical sequence of verse 4’s *dhyāyema*. Philosophically the verse is decisive because Rādhā is not appended after the fact; she is placed at the left side of Kṛṣṇa’s own body, which later Nimbārka exegesis reads as the seed of *yugala-upāsanā*.

### Verse 6

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *upāsanīyaṃ nitarāṃ janaiḥ sadā  
> prahāṇaye 'jñānatamo'nuvṛtteḥ |  
> sanandanādyair munibhis tathoktaṃ

**English (line-by-line):**
> He is to be worshipped in constant *upāsanā* (meditative attendance), most especially, by people always,

| ajñānatamo'nuvṛtteḥ | ajñāna-tamas-anuvṛtti | gen sg fem | of the continued pursuit/persistence of the darkness of ignorance |
| sanandanādyaiḥ | sanandana-ādi | instr pl masc | by Sanandana and the others |

- *ajñānatamo'nuvṛtti* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *ajñānasya tamaḥ tasya anuvṛttiḥ*.
- *sanandanādi* [dvandva/tatpuruṣa shorthand]: Sanandana and the rest.
- *akhilatattvasākṣin* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *akhilasya tattvasya sākṣī*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ (logical, in passive sense): *uktam* with instrumental agents *sanandanādyair munibhiḥ*.

- karaṇa/agent: *janaiḥ* with gerundive obligation.
- prayojana: *prahāṇaye*.

**Verbal modality:**
- *upāsanīyam*: gerundive / passive necessity.
- *uktam*: past passive participle.

**Note:**
The verse turns from object to act. *Upāsanā* here is not casual devotion but sustained meditative attendance. Nimbārka ties that practice to a lineage claim: compare *Vedānta-Pārijāta-Saurabha* 1.3.8, where the Kumāras and Nārada again authorize the teaching. Grammar and pedagogy are aligned: gerundive obligation is grounded in transmission.

### Verse 7

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *sarvaṃ hi vijñānam ato yathārthakaṃ  
> śrutismṛtibhyo nikhilasya vastunaḥ |  
> brahmātmakatvād iti vedavinmataṃ

**English (line-by-line):**
> For this reason every cognition is veridical.  
> So say *śruti* and *smṛti* with regard to every entity whatsoever.

| śrutismṛtibhyaḥ | śruti-smṛti | abl pl fem | from śruti and smṛti; according to śruti and smṛti |
| nikhilasya | nikhila | gen sg neut | of the whole; of every |

- *śrutismṛti* [dvandva]: *śrutiś ca smṛtiś ca*.
- *brahmātmakatva* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *brahma ātmā yasya tasya bhāvaḥ*.
- *vedavinmata* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *vedavidāṃ matam*.
- *trirūpatā* [tatpuruṣa]: *trīṇi rūpāṇi yasyāḥ sā*.
- *śrutisūtrasādhita* [dvandva-tatpuruṣa]: *śrutibhiḥ sūtraiś ca sādhitā*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *sarvaṃ vijñānam*.
- hetu: *brahmātmakatvāt*.
- pramāṇa-source: *śrutismṛtibhyaḥ*.
- predicate: *yathārthakam*.

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb stated; copula is understood.

**Note:**
The line does not trivialize error. Its claim is rather that cognition is never sheer relation to an ontological nullity, because every object is Brahman-grounded. That is why Nimbārka can affirm multiplicity without granting unreality to the cognized field. The polemical expansion appears in *Vedānta-Pārijāta-Saurabha* 2.2, where heterodox denials of permanent reals are rejected more fully.

### Verse 8

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *nānyā gatiḥ kṛṣṇapadāravindāt  
> sandṛśyate brahmaśivādivanditāt |  
> bhaktecchayopāttasucintyavigrahād  
> acintyaśakter avicintyasāśayāt || 8 ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> No other refuge is seen than Kṛṣṇa’s lotus feet.  
> They are adored by Brahmā, Śiva, and the rest.  
> He assumes a form well suited to meditation in accordance with the devotee’s desire.  
> His *acintya-śakti* (inconceivable power), and his inward ground, are beyond thought.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| nānyā | na anya | nom sg fem | no other |
| gatiḥ | gati | nom sg fem | refuge; course; goal |
| kṛṣṇapadāravindāt | kṛṣṇa-pada-aravinda | abl sg neut | than the lotus-feet of Kṛṣṇa |
| sandṛśyate | sam-√dṛś | 3 sg laṭ ātmanepada/passive | is seen; is observed |
| brahmaśivādivanditāt | brahma-śiva-ādi-vandita | abl sg neut/masc | from that which is revered by Brahmā, Śiva, and the rest |
| bhaktecchayopāttasucintyavigrahāt | bhakta-icchayā-upātta-su-cintya-vigraha | abl sg masc | from him whose form, fit for meditation, is assumed according to the devotee’s wish |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *kṛṣṇapadāravinda* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *kṛṣṇasya padam eva aravindam*.
- *brahmaśivādivandita* [tatpuruṣa]: *brahmaśivādibhiḥ vanditam*.
- *bhaktecchayopāttasucintyavigraha* [complex tatpuruṣa]: *bhaktasya icchayā upāttaḥ su-cintyaḥ vigrahaḥ yasya*.
- *acintyaśakti* [karmadhāraya]: *acintyā śaktiḥ*.
- *avicintyasāśaya* [karmadhāraya/tatpuruṣa]: *avicintyaḥ āśayaḥ yasya*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *gatiḥ*.
- apādāna of exclusion/comparison: *kṛṣṇapadāravindāt*.
- predicate: *sandṛśyate* with *nānyā gatiḥ*.
- ablative qualifiers of the same refuge/Lord: *brahmaśivādivanditāt*, *bhaktecchayopāttasucintyavigrahāt*, *acintyaśakteḥ*, *avicintyasāśayāt*.

**Verbal modality:**
- *sandṛśyate*: laṭ / ātmanepada (passive sense) / middle-passive.

**Note:**
The verse is the hinge between ontology and surrender. Its diction anticipates the more explicit *prapatti* language of the *Prapannakalpavallī*. The last pāda is textually difficult. I render *avicintyasāśaya* conservatively as “whose inward ground is beyond thought”; Ramnarace paraphrases the phrase more loosely as “whose abode is inconceivable.” Either way the grammatical pressure falls on transcendence without abstraction: the Lord takes a thinkable form, yet is not exhausted by it.

### Verse 9

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *kṛpāsya dainyādiyuji prajāyate  
> yayā bhavet premaviśeṣalakṣaṇā |  
> bhaktir hy ananyādhipater mahātmanaḥ

**English (line-by-line):**
> His grace arises in one joined with humility and the like.

The grammar is exact: grace is prior, and higher *bhakti* is its fruit, not its autonomous cause.

### Verse 10

**Sanskrit (IAST):**

The five topics anticipated here are the later *artha-pañcaka*: the Lord, the soul, grace or the fruit, the obstacles, and attainment. Later Nimbārka materials make this pedagogical scheme explicit, but the seed is already here. The verse closes the poem not with emotional flourish but with curriculum: one must know what is worshipped, who worships, what blocks the path, and what the realized end is.

## Editorial closing

This passage settles three things with remarkable economy. First, Nimbārka’s *svābhāvika-bhedābheda* requires real plurality: the *jīva* is minute, many, embodied, and dependent, while *acit* is also real and internally differentiated. Second, the supreme object of Vedāntic worship is not left at the level of an undifferentiated “Brahman”; the identity is fixed as Kṛṣṇa, and, more strikingly, as Kṛṣṇa with the daughter of Vṛṣabhānu at his left side. Third, liberation is not reached by bare metaphysical knowledge alone. It requires grace, sustained *upāsanā*, and culminates in a higher *bhakti* marked by *prema*.

What remains open here is not the doctrinal direction but the later scholastic elaboration. The *Vedānta-Pārijāta-Saurabha* expands the public Vedāntic argument, especially at 1.1.1, 1.3.8, 3.2.24, and 4.2.16. The *Prapannakalpavallī* and *Mantrarahasyaṣoḍaśī* take up the surrender-language of verse 8 more explicitly. Śrīnivāsa’s *Vedāntakaustubha* and Puruṣottama’s *Vedāntaratnamañjūṣā* then turn these ten verses into a full school curriculum.
