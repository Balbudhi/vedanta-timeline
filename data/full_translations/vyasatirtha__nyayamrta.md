## Editorial introduction

The opening eight verses of the *Nyāyāmṛta* are not ornamental surplus. They are the programmatic threshold of the whole work. Before Vyāsatīrtha enters the formal *vipratipatti-vicāra* and the sequence of attacks on *mithyātva* beginning at *mithyātve parokṣānumānāni* and the first *mithyātvabhaṅga*, he states, in compressed doxographical form, the ontological and pedagogical order within which the polemic is to be read. Verses 1–2 place Viṣṇu, *Hari* (the supreme Lord), at the head of the argument as the all-denoting referent of scripture, the possessor of all auspicious qualities, and the real cause of the entire world. Verses 3–6 then locate the work in a living lineage: Madhva as *Ānandatīrtha*, Jayatīrtha as the luminous expositor of the guru’s intention, Brahmaṇyatīrtha as disciplinary preceptor, and Lakṣmīnārāyaṇa Muni as intellectual preceptor. Only after that does Vyāsatīrtha name his own labor: the churning up of a *nyāyāmṛta* from Madhva’s śāstric ocean, and the threefold justification of composition by collection, demonstration, and fresh statement.

This scope is the natural extended unit for the site because it is the smallest self-contained opening arc in which theology, lineage, authorship, and method are all explicitly joined. It also conditions the larger architecture of the work. Later destructive sections such as *dṛśyatvahetubhāṅgaḥ* and the several *mithyātvabhaṅga* chapters depend on the realist, theistic, and lineage-conscious commitments already announced here. The invocation thus does genuine philosophical work: it states, before any refutation begins, what counts as a real world, a valid scriptural orientation, and a legitimate argumentative inheritance.

## Translation

### Verse 1

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *nikhilaguṇanikāyaṃ nityanirdhūtaheyaṃ  
> śubhatamamatimeyaṃ śuddhasaukhyāptyupāyam /  
> sakalanigamageyaṃ sarvaśabdābhidheyaṃ  
> navajaladharākāyaṃ naumi lakṣmīsahāyam //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> I bow to the consort of Lakṣmī, whose body is a mass of all qualities.  
> He whose rejectable features are eternally shaken off; he who is knowable to the most excellent intelligence; he who is the means to the attainment of pure bliss.  
> He who is sung by the whole Veda.  
> He who is denoted by every word; he whose form is that of a fresh rain-cloud.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| nikhilaguṇanikāyam | nikhila-guṇa-nikāya | acc sg masc | the aggregate of all qualities |
| nityanirdhūtaheyam | nitya-nirdhūta-heya | acc sg masc | one from whom what is rejectable is eternally driven off |
| śubhatamamatimeyam | śubhatama-mati-meya | acc sg masc | knowable by the most excellent intellect |
| śuddhasaukhyāptyupāyam | śuddha-saukhya-āpti-upāya | acc sg masc | the means to attaining pure happiness |
| sakalanigamageyam | sakala-nigama-geya | acc sg masc | to be sung by the entire Veda |
| sarvaśabdābhidheyam | sarva-śabda-abhidheya | acc sg masc | denotable by every word |
| navajaladharākāyam | nava-jaladhara-kāya | acc sg masc | having the body/form of a fresh rain-cloud |
| naumi | √nam | laṭ, 1st sg, parasmaipada | I bow to |
| lakṣmīsahāyam | lakṣmī-sahāya | acc sg masc | Lakṣmī’s companion / consort |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *nikhilaguṇanikāya* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *nikhilānāṃ guṇānāṃ nikāyaḥ*.
- *nityanirdhūtaheya* [bahuvrīhi]: *nityaṃ nirdhūtaṃ heyaṃ yasya saḥ*.
- *śubhatamamatimeya* [tṛtīyā-tatpuruṣa]: *śubhatamayā matyā meyaḥ*.
- *śuddhasaukhyāptyupāya* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *śuddhasaukhyasya āpteḥ upāyaḥ*.
- *sakalanigamageya* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *sakalasya nigamasya geyaḥ*.
- *sarvaśabdābhidheya* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *sarvaiḥ śabdaiḥ abhidheyaḥ*.
- *navajaladharākāya* [karmadhāraya]: *navajaladhara iva kāyaḥ yasya saḥ*.
- *lakṣmīsahāya* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *lakṣmyāḥ sahāyaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *(aham)*, understood from *naumi*
- karman: *lakṣmīsahāyam*
- viśeṣaṇa-pada-s attached to the karman: *nikhilaguṇanikāyam*, *nityanirdhūtaheyam*, *śubhatamamatimeyam*, *śuddhasaukhyāptyupāyam*, *sakalanigamageyam*, *sarvaśabdābhidheyam*, *navajaladharākāyam*

**Verbal modality:**
- *naumi*: laṭ / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
This first verse states the theistic realism of the whole text in concentrated form. The phrase *sarvaśabdābhidheya* is especially weight-bearing: it claims that the Lord is not merely one scriptural topic among others, but the final denotatum under every authoritative verbal disclosure. The verse also refuses any doctrine that would treat defect-negation as merely privative; Viṣṇu is free from *heya* precisely while being positively full of *guṇa*.

### Verse 2

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *vighnaughavāraṇaṃ satyāśeṣaviśvasya kāraṇam /  
> karuṇāsindhum ānandatīrthabandhuṃ hariṃ bhaje //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> I worship Hari, the warder-off of the flood of obstacles, the cause of the entire real universe.  
> I worship Hari, the ocean of compassion, the friend of Ānandatīrtha.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| vighnaughavāraṇam | vighna-ogha-vāraṇa | acc sg masc | remover of the mass of obstacles |
| satyāśeṣaviśvasya | satya-aśeṣa-viśva | gen sg neut | of the whole real universe |
| kāraṇam | kāraṇa | acc sg neut | cause |
| karuṇāsindhum | karuṇā-sindhu | acc sg masc | ocean of compassion |
| ānandatīrthabandhum | ānandatīrtha-bandhu | acc sg masc | friend/benefactor of Ānandatīrtha |

| bhaje | √bhaj | laṭ, 1st sg, ātmanepada | I worship / serve |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *vighnaughavāraṇa* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *vighnaughasya vāraṇam*.
- *satyāśeṣaviśva* [karmadhāraya + bahuvrīhi-like cluster]: *satyaṃ ca tad aśeṣaṃ ca viśvam*.
- *karuṇāsindhu* [karmadhāraya]: *karuṇā eva sindhuḥ*.
- *ānandatīrthabandhu* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *ānandatīrthasya bandhuḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *(aham)*, understood from *bhaje*
- karman: *harim*
- karmasamānādhikaraṇa-pada-s: *vighnaughavāraṇam*, *kāraṇam*, *karuṇāsindhum*, *ānandatīrthabandhum*
- sambandha: *satyāśeṣaviśvasya* with *kāraṇam*

**Verbal modality:**
- *bhaje*: laṭ / ātmanepada / active-middle

**Note:**
The phrase *satyāśeṣaviśvasya kāraṇam* is polemically charged. Vyāsatīrtha does not say merely “cause of the world”; he says “cause of the wholly real world.” That adjective already anticipates the later anti-*mithyātva* campaign. The verse thus binds devotion to ontology: *Hari* is worshipped precisely as cause of what is real.

### Verse 3

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *abhramaṃ bhaṅgarahitam ajaḍaṃ vimalaṃ sadā /

**English (line-by-line):**
> I worship the peerless Ānandatīrtha: free from error, free from fracture, not dull, and always stainless.  
> I worship the peerless Ānandatīrtha, the remover of the threefold suffering.

**Word-by-word:**

|---|---|---|---|
| abhramam | a-bhrama | acc sg masc | free from error/delusion |
| bhaṅgarahitam | bhaṅga-rahita | acc sg masc | devoid of breakage / unobstructed |
| ajaḍam | a-jaḍa | acc sg masc | not dull, not insentient |

| ānandatīrtham | ānandatīrtha | acc sg masc | Ānandatīrtha (Madhva) |
| atulam | a-tula | acc sg masc | incomparable |
| bhaje | √bhaj | laṭ, 1st sg, ātmanepada | I worship / serve |
| tāpatrayāpaham | tāpatraya-apaha | acc sg masc | remover of the three afflictions |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *bhaṅgarahita* [bahuvrīhi]: *bhaṅgaḥ rahitaḥ yasya saḥ*.
- *tāpatrayāpaha* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *tāpatrayasya apahaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *(aham)*, understood from *bhaje*
- karman: *ānandatīrtham*
- viśeṣaṇa-pada-s attached to the karman: *abhramam*, *bhaṅgarahitam*, *ajaḍam*, *vimalam*, *atulam*, *tāpatrayāpaham*

**Verbal modality:**
- *bhaje*: laṭ / ātmanepada / active-middle

**Note:**
Here Vyāsatīrtha shifts from God to founder. The predicate-series is carefully chosen: Madhva is free from *bhrama* and from *jaḍatā*; he is thus doctrinally reliable and intellectually vivid. The verse is not biographical praise alone. It establishes Madhva as the normative human source of the argument that follows.

### Verse 4

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *citraiḥ padaiś ca gambhīrair vākyair mānair akhaṇḍitaiḥ /  
> gurubhāvaṃ vyañjayantī bhāti śrījayatīrthavāk //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> With lucid words, and with profound sentences, and with proofs that are unbroken by refutation,  
> the speech of Śrī Jayatīrtha shines, making manifest the guru’s intention.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| citraiḥ | citra | instr pl neut | with striking / lucid words |
| padaiḥ | pada | instr pl neut | with words |
| ca | ca | indeclinable | and |
| gambhīrair | gambhīra | instr pl neut | with deep / profound |
| vākyair | vākya | instr pl neut | with sentences / statements |
| mānair | māna | instr pl neut | with proofs, means of valid ascertainment |
| akhaṇḍitaiḥ | a-khaṇḍita | instr pl neut | unbroken, unrefuted |
| gurubhāvam | guru-bhāva | acc sg masc | the guru’s intention / meaning |
| vyañjayantī | vi-añj (caus./pres. part.) | nom sg fem pres act part. | making manifest |
| bhāti | √bhā | laṭ, 3rd sg, parasmaipada | shines |
| śrījayatīrthavāk | śrī-jayatīrtha-vāk | nom sg fem | the speech of Śrī Jayatīrtha |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *akhaṇḍita* [bahuvrīhi-like adjective]: *na khaṇḍitaḥ*.
- *gurubhāva* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *guroḥ bhāvaḥ*.
- *śrījayatīrthavāk* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *śrījayatīrthasya vāk*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *śrījayatīrthavāk*
- karman: *gurubhāvam*
- karaṇa: *citraiḥ padaiḥ*, *gambhīrair vākyaiḥ*, *mānair akhaṇḍitaiḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *bhāti*: laṭ / parasmaipada / active
- *vyañjayantī*: present active participle, feminine nominative singular, agreeing with *vāk*

**Note:**
The verse gives a miniature hermeneutics of Jayatīrtha. He does not merely repeat Madhva; he *vyañjayati* the guru’s intent, drawing it out through language and proof. The addition of *mānair akhaṇḍitaiḥ* is especially important for the *Nyāyāmṛta*: Tattva-vāda (the school often labeled Dvaita) exegesis is here represented as inferentially disciplined, not only devotionally loyal.

**Note:**

That framing matters for a text whose later chapters turn on what counts as valid disclosure and valid negation.

### Verse 7

The verse thus anticipates Vyāsatīrtha’s larger project of recasting Mādhva doctrine in a fully Navya-Nyāya-capable idiom.

### Verse 8

**Sanskrit (IAST):**

The claim matters for the whole work. The *Nyāyāmṛta* is neither a mere digest nor a mere commentary; it is a constructive intervention within the Madhva-Jayatīrtha inheritance.

## Editorial closing

By the end of this invocation, three things have been settled. First, the work’s theological horizon is explicit: the real universe is caused by Viṣṇu, who is positively qualified and scripturally omnidenotable. Second, the line of transmission is fixed: Madhva, Jayatīrtha, Brahmaṇyatīrtha, and Lakṣmīnārāyaṇa Muni are not ornamental names but the graded authorities under which the argument proceeds. Third, Vyāsatīrtha has stated what sort of book the *Nyāyāmṛta* will be: a gathered, demonstrated, and at points innovative re-presentation of Mādhva doctrine in sharpened logical form. What remains open at this point is the actual contest with Advaita. That begins in the immediately following *vipratipattivicāraḥ* (p. 8 in the 1994 edition), intensifies at *mithyātve parokṣānumānāni* (p. 24), and unfolds through the successive *mithyātvabhaṅga* sections (pp. 52, 66, 98, 104, 106, 109). The invocation, then, does not conclude the doctrine; it fixes the terms under which the later refutation and positive realist reconstruction are to be read.
