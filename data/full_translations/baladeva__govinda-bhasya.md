# Govinda-Bhāṣya

**By:** Baladeva Vidyābhūṣaṇa
**Genre:** *bhāṣya* on the *Brahma-Sūtra*

---

## Status

The Sanskrit text of the *Govinda-Bhāṣya* is not currently in our corpus. The Vedānta interactive timeline does not have a defensible Sanskrit witness on disk for this text, and producing an annotated translation would mean fabricating engagement we cannot ground.

What is widely known about this work:

- The *Govinda-Bhāṣya* is a complete commentary on Bādarāyaṇa's *Brahma-Sūtra* in four *adhyāyas*, composed in the early eighteenth century. It is the canonical *Brahma-Sūtra* commentary of the Gauḍīya Vaiṣṇava sampradāya and was undertaken in part to secure the sampradāya's standing among the recognized Vedāntic lineages.
- Its central interpretive move is to read the *sūtras* through the lens of *acintya-bhedābheda* (inconceivable difference-and-non-difference), the Caitanya-school metaphysics earlier articulated in prose by Jīva Gosvāmin's *Sat-Sandarbha*. Baladeva's commentary engages Śaṅkara, Rāmānuja, Madhva, and Nimbārka at the *adhikaraṇa* level.

What is in our corpus that bears on it:

- Baladeva's `core_thesis` and engaged_works summary in the timeline entry (see https://balbudhi.github.io/vedanta-timeline/ and locate Baladeva Vidyābhūṣaṇa).
- Jīva Gosvāmin's *Sat-Sandarbha* file, which articulates the Gauḍīya metaphysics that Baladeva codifies into a *Brahma-Sūtra* commentary.
