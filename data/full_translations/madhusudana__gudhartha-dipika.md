# Gūḍhārtha-Dīpikā

**By:** Madhusūdana Sarasvatī
**Genre:** *bhāṣya* / *vyākhyā* on the *Bhagavad-Gītā*

---

## Status

A self-standing, reliably edited Sanskrit text of the *Gūḍhārtha-Dīpikā* is not currently in our corpus. The Vedānta interactive timeline does not have a defensible Sanskrit witness on disk for this text, and producing an annotated translation would mean fabricating engagement we cannot ground.

What is widely known about this work:

- A continuous Advaita commentary on the *Bhagavad-Gītā* in eighteen *adhyāyas*, composed in the late sixteenth century. Madhusūdana takes Śaṅkara's *Gītā-Bhāṣya* as ambient backdrop but does not paraphrase it; he writes his own running interpretation, often opening each verse with a *saṃgati* (contextual link) and closing with a doctrinal point.
- Its philosophical signature is the integration of high Advaita non-dualism with a strong, non-eliminative bhakti: devotion is *parama-puruṣārtha* (supreme human good) when held alongside *jñāna*, and the *Gītā* is read as teaching both *jñāna-yoga* and *bhakti-yoga* without subordinating one to the other. The position is developed at length in Madhusūdana's *Bhakti-Rasāyana*. The work also presupposes the dialectical apparatus of his *Advaita-Siddhi*.

What is in our corpus that bears on it:

- Madhusūdana's `core_thesis` and engaged_works summary in the timeline entry (see https://balbudhi.github.io/vedanta-timeline/ and locate Madhusūdana Sarasvatī).
- Files for Madhusūdana's *Advaita-Siddhi*, *Bhakti-Rasāyana*, and *Siddhānta-Bindu*, which together articulate the metaphysics and devotional theory presupposed by the *Gūḍhārtha-Dīpikā*.
