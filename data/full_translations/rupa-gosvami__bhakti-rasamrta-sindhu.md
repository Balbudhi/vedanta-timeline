**By:** Rūpa Gosvāmī  
**Locus scope:** *Pūrva-vibhāga*, *sāmānya-bhakti*, first *laharī*, verses 1.1.7–1.1.16, the opening definitional cluster on *uttamā-bhakti*  
**Source edition transcribed from:** Haridās Dās (ed.), *Haribhaktirasāmṛtasindhu*, 1984 reprint; accessed here through the local GRETIL electronic transcription (TEI conversion dated 2020-07-31).  
**Translator:** Pāṇinian-rich extraction by Codex 5.4 (reasoning=high), 2026-05-10.

## Editorial introduction

This opening unit states the first strict thesis of the *Bhakti-Rasāmṛta-Sindhu*. Rūpa does not begin by praising *bhakti* (devotion) in merely encomiastic terms. He begins by ordering the work, isolating its first argumentative task, and then giving a formal *lakṣaṇa* (defining mark) of *uttamā-bhakti* (highest devotion). The chosen scope, 1.1.7–1.1.16, is therefore the natural unit. Verse 1.1.7 announces the four-part architecture of the *pūrva-vibhāga*; verses 1.1.8–1.1.10 narrow the inquiry to the first *laharī*; verse 1.1.11 gives the famous definition; verses 1.1.12–1.1.15 supply scriptural corroboration; and verse 1.1.16 explains why those citations matter, namely, because they display the *viśuddhatā* (purity) of devotion that the definition requires. The passage is thus internally complete.

Its place in the larger work is exact. The first *laharī* defines devotion in general. The second, third, and fourth *laharīs* of the *pūrva-vibhāga* then unfold *sādhana-bhakti* (disciplined practice), *bhāva-bhakti* (incipient devotional affect), and *prema-bhakti* (consummated love). Later, in the *dakṣiṇa-vibhāga*, Rūpa will transpose this same devotional structure into the technical language of *rasa*: see especially 2.1.5, where Kṛṣṇa-directed *rati* is identified as the *sthāyī-bhāva* of *bhakti-rasa*, and 2.1.16, where Kṛṣṇa and his devotees are specified as the two *ālambanas*. What is established here is therefore not a preliminary piety that can later be discarded. It is the rule of admissibility for the whole theological-aesthetic system.

The passage also displays Rūpa's broader doctrinal method. He proceeds by exclusion and qualification. Other desires are barred. *Jñāna* and *karma* are barred insofar as they cover or instrumentalize devotion. Favorability to Kṛṣṇa is required. Scriptural citations are then read not as ornament but as constraints on the definition. This is the characteristic Gauḍīya move: affect is not left vague, and theology is not left ungrammatical.

## Translation

### BRS 1.1.7

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *etasya bhagavad-bhakti-rasāmṛta-payonidheḥ / catvāraḥ khalu vakṣyante bhāgāḥ pūrvādayaḥ kramāt*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Of this ocean of the nectar of the *rasa* (relish) of devotion to Bhagavān, four divisions, beginning with the *pūrva* section, will now indeed be stated in order.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| etasya | etad | gen sg neut | of this |
| bhagavad-bhakti-rasāmṛta-payonidheḥ | bhagavad-bhakti-rasāmṛta-payonidhi | gen sg masc | of the ocean of the nectar of the rasa of devotion to Bhagavān |
| catvāraḥ | catur | nom pl masc | four |
| khalu | khalu | indecl | indeed, surely |
| vakṣyante | √vac | 3 pl lṛṭ ind Ā | will be spoken, will be stated |

| pūrvādayaḥ | pūrva-ādi | nom pl masc | beginning with the *pūrva* one |
| kramāt | krama | abl sg masc | in sequence, in due order |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *bhagavad-bhakti-rasāmṛta-payonidhi* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa chain]: *bhagavato bhaktiḥ, tasyā rasaḥ, tasya amṛtam, tasya payonidhiḥ*.
- *pūrvādi* [avyayībhāva/tatpuruṣa]: *pūrvaḥ ādiḥ yeṣām te*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- viṣaya-sambandha: *etasya bhagavad-bhakti-rasāmṛta-payonidheḥ*

**Note:**
The ocean metaphor is not ornamental excess. It names amplitude and ordered internal differentiation at once. The next three verses immediately make good on that promise by mapping the four *laharīs* of the first major division.

### BRS 1.1.8

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *tatra pūrve vibhāge 'smin bhakti-bheda-nirūpake / anukrameṇa vaktavyaṃ laharīṇāṃ catuṣṭayam*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Therein, in this prior division which sets forth the kinds of *bhakti*, a set of four waves is to be described in sequence.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| tatra | tatra | indecl | there, in that connection |
| pūrve | pūrva | loc sg masc | in the prior |

| catuṣṭayam | catuṣṭaya | nom/acc sg neut | a group of four |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *bhakti-bheda-nirūpaka* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *bhakteḥ bhedān nirūpayati iti*.
- *catuṣṭaya* [taddhita numeral formation]: a fourfold set.

**Kāraka structure:**
- adhikaraṇa: *tatra*, *pūrve vibhāge asmin*
- viśeṣaṇa-adhikaraṇa: *bhakti-bheda-nirūpake*
- kriyā-vidheya: *vaktavyam*

- *vaktavyam*: gerundive; prescriptive obligation, with an implied *asti*

**Note:**
Rūpa's self-ordering is strict. He is not merely saying that the work has four subsections. He is saying that the first division must be read as a progressive articulation of one subject: devotion first in general, then as practice, then as affective emergence, then as love.

### BRS 1.1.9

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *ādyā sāmānya-bhakty-āḍhyā dvitīyā sādhanānvitā / bhāvāśritā tṛtīyā ca turyā prema-nirūpikā*

- predicative subjects: *ādyā*, *dvitīyā*, *tṛtīyā*, *turyā*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb; nominal classification with implied *asti* in each member

**Note:**
The verse is programmatic. It also prevents a common misreading. *Prema* is not an optional embellishment added after devotion is already complete. It is the fourth and highest internally articulated stage of the same continuum.

### BRS 1.1.11

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *anyābhilāṣitā-śūnyaṃ jñāna-karmādy-anāvṛtam / ānukūlyena kṛṣṇānuśīlanaṃ bhaktir uttamā*

**English (line-by-line):**
> The cultivation of Kṛṣṇa, carried on with favorability, devoid of other desire, and not overlaid by *jñāna* (knowledge-discipline), *karma* (ritual or action-discipline), and the like, is *uttamā-bhakti*.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| anyābhilāṣitā-śūnyam | anyābhilāṣitā-śūnya | nom/acc sg neut | empty of other desire, devoid of ulterior wanting |
| jñāna-karmādy-anāvṛtam | jñāna-karma-ādi-anāvṛta | nom/acc sg neut | not covered by knowledge, ritual action, and the like |
| ānukūlyena | ānukūlya | instr sg neut | by favorability, in a favorable manner |
| kṛṣṇānuśīlanam | kṛṣṇa-anuśīlana | nom/acc sg neut | cultivation of Kṛṣṇa, sustained practice toward Kṛṣṇa |
| bhaktiḥ | bhakti | nom sg fem | devotion |
| uttamā | uttamā | nom sg fem | highest |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *anyābhilāṣitā-śūnya* [nañ-tatpuruṣa]: *anyābhilāṣitāyāḥ śūnyam*.
- *jñāna-karmādy-anāvṛta* [tṛtīyā-tatpuruṣa with *ādi*]: *jñānena karmaṇā ādibhiś ca anāvṛtam*.
- *kṛṣṇānuśīlana* [dvitīyā-tatpuruṣa]: *kṛṣṇam anuśīlanam*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- prakṛti/viśeṣya: *kṛṣṇānuśīlanam*
- karaṇa or mode: *ānukūlyena*
- viśeṣaṇas: *anyābhilāṣitā-śūnyam*, *jñāna-karmādy-anāvṛtam*
- predicate identification: *bhaktiḥ uttamā*
- internal karman within compound: *kṛṣṇa* as the object of *anuśīlana*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb; equational definition with implied *asti*

**Note:**
This is the governing definition of the work. Its logic is both negative and positive. The two first qualifiers exclude rival teleologies; the instrumental *ānukūlyena* prevents merely obsessive or hostile relation to Kṛṣṇa from counting as devotion; and *anuśīlana* names active, sustained cultivation rather than a punctiliar mood. Later in the work, 1.2.5 unfolds the practical side of this cultivation, while 2.1.5 and 2.1.16 show how the same structure becomes the basis of *bhakti-rasa*.

### BRS 1.1.12

### BRS 1.1.14

**Verbal modality:**
- *paryavasyati*: laṭ / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
This closing verse is exegetically decisive. Rūpa explains why the scriptural citations belonged inside the definitional unit: the cited refusal of non-devotional salvific attainments displays devotion's *viśuddhatā*, and that purity belongs constitutively to the *lakṣaṇa* of 1.1.11. The local GRETIL reading has a minor corruption in this verse; the normalized *nirūpaṇam* is demanded by grammar and sense.

## Editorial closing

This passage settles the formal grammar of the whole work. It settles that *bhakti* is not to be counted by intensity alone, nor by devotional vocabulary alone, but by a precise structure of exclusion and orientation: no alien desire, no subordination to *jñāna* or *karma*, positive favorability to Kṛṣṇa, and an integrity so strong that even liberation-states are refused if they come without service. What it leaves open is equally important. It does not yet say how such cultivation is practically undertaken, how it matures affectively, or how it becomes *rasa*. Those questions are deferred, not ignored. They are taken up in *Bhakti-Rasāmṛta-Sindhu* 1.2 on *sādhana-bhakti*, 1.3 on *bhāva-bhakti*, 1.4 on *prema*, and then in the *dakṣiṇa-vibhāga*, especially 2.1.5 and 2.1.16, where Kṛṣṇa-directed *rati* is installed as the *sthāyī-bhāva* of *bhakti-rasa*. Within Rūpa's wider corpus, the same line is intensified rather than revised in the *Ujjvala-Nīlamaṇi*, where the mature forms of *prema* are anatomized, and in the *Laghu-Bhāgavatāmṛta*, where Kṛṣṇa's theological supremacy secures the object toward which this whole definition is directed.
