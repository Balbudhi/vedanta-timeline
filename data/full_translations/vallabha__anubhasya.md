## Editorial introduction

These four loci are not contiguous, but they are the shortest natural doctrinal dossier in which Vallabha states the structure of *śuddhādvaita* in his own *bhāṣya* voice. BSB 1.1.4, in the opening *samanvaya* section, establishes Brahman as the *samavāyi-kāraṇa* (inherent or materially constitutive cause) of the world. BSB 1.1.12-19, still in the first *pāda*, makes a second move that is no less decisive: the attainment of *Puruṣottama* is not exhausted by bare *brahmajñāna*; knowledge removes *avidyā* and prepares the self, but divine choosing (*varaṇa*) and *bhakti* complete the movement to fruition. BSB 1.4.26 then supplies the famous causal formula usually treated as the load-bearing center of Vallabha’s ontology: the world is a real *pariṇāma* of Brahman, yet Brahman remains *avikṛta*, unmodified. Finally, BSB 2.3.43-45 gives the corresponding anthropology: the *jīva* is Brahman’s *aṃśa*, not by metaphor alone, but by a scripturally governed relation which cannot be settled from ordinary experience.

Taken together, these passages show why Vallabha’s non-dualism is neither Śaṅkara’s *vivarta* account nor a merely qualified non-dualism which needs a further body-soul schema to protect Brahman from the world. The causal relation is real, the world is real, the *jīva* is real, and the grammar of manifestation does the work. That is why this dossier is the right extended unit for the present purpose. It exhibits, in compressed form, the interlocking theses on causality, devotion, grace, and part-whole relation on which Vallabha’s larger reading of the *Brahma-Sūtras* depends.

## Translation

### BSB 1.1.4a

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *tat brahmaiva samavāyi kāraṇam.*

**English (line-by-line):**
> That *Brahman* itself is the *samavāyi-kāraṇa* (inherent/material cause).

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| tat | tad | nom sg neut pron | that |
| brahma | brahman | nom sg neut | Brahman |
| eva | eva | indecl | indeed; itself |
| samavāyi | samavāyin | nom sg neut adj | inhering; materially constitutive |
| kāraṇam | kāraṇa | nom sg neut | cause |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *samavāyi-kāraṇam* [karmadhāraya]: *samavāyī kāraṇam*; a cause characterized by inherence in the effect.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *tat brahma*
- predicative complement: *samavāyi kāraṇam*

**Verbal modality:**
- implied *asti*: ellipsed laṭ / parasmaipada / active copula

**Note:**
The sentence is lapidary. Vallabha isolates the material-inherent side of causality first; the stronger doctrine of real transformation is stated later at BSB 1.4.26.

### BSB 1.1.4b

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *kutaḥ? samanvayāt samyag-anugatatvāt.*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Why? Because of coherent pervasion; because of right pervasion throughout.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| kutaḥ | kim | abl sg pron in indeclinable use | why; on what ground |
| samanvayāt | samanvaya | abl sg masc | from systematic connection; coherent pervasion |
| samyag-anugatatvāt | samyag-anugatatva | abl sg neut | from the state of being properly pervaded |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *samyag-anugatatva* [secondary formation]: *samyak anugataṃ tad-bhāvaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- hetu: *samanvayāt*
- hetu: *samyag-anugatatvāt*

**Verbal modality:**
- implied reason-giving copula: ellipsed

**Note:**
The doubled ablative is not ornamental. Vallabha wants not merely textual consistency, but an ontological pervasion discoverable through the way being, manifestation, and dearness run through all experience.

### BSB 1.1.4c

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *asti bhāti priyatvena sac-cid-ānanda-rūpeṇānvayāt.*

**English (line-by-line):**
> It is; it shines; it does so as dear, because of pervasion in the form of *sat-cid-ānanda* (being-consciousness-bliss).

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| asti | as | 3sg pres ind P | is |
| bhāti | bhā | 3sg pres ind P | shines; appears |
| priyatvena | priyatva | instr sg neut | as dearness |
| sat-cid-ānanda-rūpeṇa | sat-cid-ānanda-rūpa | instr sg neut | in the form of being, consciousness, and bliss |
| anvayāt | anvaya | abl sg masc | because of pervasion; inherence-throughout |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *sat-cid-ānanda-rūpa* [composite formation]: *sat ca cit cānandaś ca rūpaṃ yasya tat*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: implied *brahma*
- karaṇa / mode: *priyatvena*
- karaṇa / form: *sat-cid-ānanda-rūpeṇa*
- hetu: *anvayāt*

**Verbal modality:**
- *asti*: laṭ / parasmaipada / active
- *bhāti*: laṭ / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
The triad *asti-bhāti-priya* is Vallabha’s phenomenological bridge from the manifest world to Brahman. The grammar is brief, but the claim is large: the world’s intelligibility already bears Brahman’s signature.

### BSB 1.1.4d

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *nāma-rūpayoḥ kārya-dharmatvāt.*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Because name and form have the status of properties belonging to the effect.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| nāma-rūpayoḥ | nāma-rūpa | gen dual neut | of name and form |
| kārya-dharmatvāt | kārya-dharmatva | abl sg neut | because of being effect-properties |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *nāma-rūpa* [dvandva]: *nāma ca rūpaṃ ca*.
- *kārya-dharmatva* [tatpuruṣa]: *kāryasya dharmatvam*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- sambandha: *nāma-rūpayoḥ*
- hetu: *kārya-dharmatvāt*

**Verbal modality:**
- no finite verb; causal ablative phrase completing the previous claim

**Note:**
This is the anti-*māyā-vāda* hinge in miniature. Name and form are not denied; they are relocated to the effect-side without compromising the reality of the cause.

### BSB 1.1.12-19a

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *atra brahmajñānenāvidyā-nivṛttyā puruṣottama-prāptau svarūpa-yogyatā sampādyate.*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Here, by Brahman-knowledge and by the cessation of ignorance, fitness for one’s own proper state is accomplished with respect to the attainment of *Puruṣottama*.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| atra | atra | indecl | here; in this connection |
| brahmajñānena | brahmajñāna | instr sg neut | by Brahman-knowledge |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *brahmajñāna* [tatpuruṣa]: *brahmaṇaḥ jñānam*.
- *avidyā-nivṛtti* [tatpuruṣa]: *avidyāyā nivṛttiḥ*.
- *puruṣottama-prāpti* [tatpuruṣa]: *puruṣottamasya prāptiḥ*.
- *svarūpa-yogyatā* [tatpuruṣa]: *svarūpasya yogyatā*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- karaṇa: *brahmajñānena*

**Note:**
Knowledge has real efficacy here, but only as qualification. Vallabha does not identify liberation with bare cognition; he marks a preparatory fitness, not yet the completed fruition.

### BSB 1.1.12-19b

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *tato jīve svīyatvena varaṇe bhakti-bhāvāt sahakāri-yogyatā-sampādya puruṣottama-prāptir bhavatīti nirṇeyate.*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Thereafter, when the soul is chosen as His own, and from the state of devotion, having effected the auxiliary fitness, the attainment of *Puruṣottama* comes to be: thus it is determined.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| tataḥ | tatas | indecl | then; thereafter |
| jīve | jīva | loc sg masc | in the soul |
| svīyatvena | svīyatva | instr sg neut | through being made His own |
| varaṇe | varaṇa | loc sg neut | in being chosen |
| bhakti-bhāvāt | bhakti-bhāva | abl sg masc | from the state of devotion |
| sahakāri-yogyatā-sampādya | sahakāri-yogyatā-sampādya | absolutive / gerund | having brought about the assisting fitness |
| puruṣottama-prāptiḥ | puruṣottama-prāpti | nom sg fem | attainment of Puruṣottama |

| iti | iti | indecl | thus |
| nirṇeyate | nir-ṇī | 3sg pres ind Ā, passive | is determined; is concluded |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *bhakti-bhāva* [tatpuruṣa]: *bhakteḥ bhāvaḥ*.
- *sahakāri-yogyatā-sampādya* [compound gerund]: *sahakāriṇī yogyatā sampādya*.
- *puruṣottama-prāpti* [tatpuruṣa]: *puruṣottamasya prāptiḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- adhikaraṇa: *jīve*
- karaṇa / mode: *svīyatvena*
- adhikaraṇa: *varaṇe*
- apādāna / hetu: *bhakti-bhāvāt*
- karman: *puruṣottama-prāptiḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *bhavati*: laṭ / parasmaipada / active
- *nirṇeyate*: laṭ / ātmanepada / passive

**Note:**
This is one of Vallabha’s clearest statements that grace and devotion are not decorative additions to metaphysics. *Varaṇa*, divine election or choosing, and *bhakti* are grammatically internal to the account of attainment.

## Editorial closing

What these passages settle is the skeleton of Vallabha’s system. BSB 1.1.4 fixes Brahman as the world’s inherent cause. BSB 1.1.12-19 blocks any reduction of liberation to impersonal cognition by making room for *varaṇa* and *bhakti*. BSB 1.4.26 states the causal doctrine in its characteristic form: real transformation without self-corruption of the cause. BSB 2.3.43-45 then secures the anthropology that matches this ontology, namely that the *jīva* is really Brahman’s *aṃśa*.

What remains open is the defense against the obvious objections these theses invite. Vallabha takes those up immediately. The *ānandamaya* discussion continues at BSB 1.1.20-21. The causal account is pushed forward at BSB 1.4.27, where the language of *yoni* is used to reinforce Brahman’s causal completeness. The *aṃśa* doctrine is then qualified in detail at BSB 2.3.46-51, especially where Vallabha denies transmission of defect to Brahman and explains bondage through *deha-sambandha* and *ābhāsa*. The present dossier therefore settles the principles, but not yet every objection to them.
