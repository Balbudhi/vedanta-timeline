# Ātma-Siddhi — Extended Translation
**By:** Yāmunācārya  
**Locus scope:** Ātma-Siddhi verses 2–8 (programmatic statement of purpose, self-definition, and refutation of *dehātma-vāda*)  
**Source edition transcribed from:** GRETIL electronic text, *Yamuna: Siddhitraya, Part 1: Atmasiddhi*, input by members of the Sansknet Project, converted from `yaatsi_u.htm` / `sa_yAmuna-Atmasiddhi.xml` (version dated 2020-07-31).  
**Translator:** Pāṇinian-rich extraction by Codex 5.4 (reasoning=high), 2026-05-09.

## Editorial introduction

This is the first proper argumentative unit of the *Ātma-Siddhi*. I begin with verse 2, not with the preceding benedictory verse, because the philosophical movement starts here: Yāmuna states the need for the treatise, gives the positive definition of the self in verse 3, and then devotes verses 4–8 to the first target, *dehātma-vāda* (the thesis that the self is just the body). The sequence is deliberate. Conflicting doctrines about the *ātman* and the *paramātman* obstruct liberation; therefore a work of “purification” (*pariśuddhi*) is required. That purification first takes the form of a compressed positive thesis: the self is distinct from body, senses, mind, vital breath, and cognition; it is *ananyasādhana* (self-manifest, not disclosed by another revealer); it is eternal; it is pervasive in capacity yet numerically distinct in each embodied field; and it is intrinsically happy. Only after this positive profile is stated does Yāmuna turn destructive. The body is rejected because the “I”-cognition and the “this”-cognition do not terminate in the same object, because a composite is *parārtha* (for another), because cognition cannot coherently be a bodily quality, and because the body bears the ordinary marks of produced, composite, elemental things. This block therefore fixes the argumentative architecture of the whole *Siddhi-Trayam*: what is announced here about the *paramātman* will be unfolded in the *Īśvara-Siddhi*; what is announced here about self-manifestness will be refined in the *Saṃvit-Siddhi*.

## Translation

### Verse 2

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *viruddhamatayo 'nekāḥ santy ātmaparamātmanoḥ /  
> atas tatpariśuddhyartham ātmasiddhir vidhīyate //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Many conflicting doctrines concerning the *ātman* (self) and the *paramātman* (supreme self) exist.  
> Therefore, for the sake of clarifying that matter, the *Ātma-Siddhi* is being composed.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| viruddhamatayaḥ | viruddha-mati | nom pl fem | conflicting doctrines, opposed views |
| anekāḥ | aneka | nom pl fem | many |
| santi | √as | laṭ, 3 pl, parasmaipada | exist, are |
| ātmaparamātmanoḥ | ātma-paramātman | gen/loc du masc, dvandva compound | of the self and the supreme self |
| ataḥ | etad | abl sg used adverbially | therefore, from this |
| tatpariśuddhyartham | tat-pariśuddhi-artha | acc sg masc/neut used adverbially | for the sake of the purification/clarification of that |
| ātmasiddhiḥ | ātma-siddhi | nom sg fem | the treatise called *Ātma-Siddhi* |
| vidhīyate | vi-√dhā | laṭ, 3 sg, ātmanepada, passive | is set forth, is composed |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *viruddhamatayaḥ* [karmadhāraya]: *viruddhā matayaḥ*.
- *ātmaparamātmanoḥ* [dvandva]: *ātmanaḥ ca paramātmanaḥ ca*.
- *tatpariśuddhyartham* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *tasya pariśuddheḥ artham*.
- *ātmasiddhiḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *ātmanaḥ siddhiḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *ātmasiddhiḥ*
- adhikaraṇa / viṣaya-reference: *ātmaparamātmanoḥ*
- prayojana: *tatpariśuddhyartham*

**Verbal modality:**
- *santi*: *laṭ* / parasmaipada / active
- *vidhīyate*: *laṭ* / ātmanepada / passive

**Note:**
The verse programmatically binds *ātman* and *paramātman* together from the start. That is structurally important: the *Ātma-Siddhi* is not an isolated psychology but the first movement of a triad whose larger argumentative horizon includes the *Īśvara-Siddhi*. The passive *vidhīyate* gives the treatise the character of a formal śāstric undertaking rather than a merely private opinion.

### Verse 3

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *dehendriyamanaḥprāṇadhībhyo 'nyo 'nanyasādhanaḥ /  
> nityo vyāpī pratikṣetram ātmā bhinnaḥ svataḥ sukhī //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> The *ātman* is other than body, senses, mind, vital breath, and cognitions; and it is *ananyasādhana* (not requiring another revealer, that is, self-manifest).  
> It is eternal, pervasive, distinct in each embodied field, and by its own nature happy.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| dehendriyamanaḥprāṇadhībhyaḥ | deha-indriya-manas-prāṇa-dhī | abl pl, collective dvandva | from body, senses, mind, vital breaths, and cognitions |
| anyaḥ | anya | nom sg masc | other, distinct |
| ananyasādhanaḥ | ananya-sādhana | nom sg masc | not requiring another means; self-manifest |
| nityaḥ | nitya | nom sg masc | eternal |
| vyāpī | vyāpin | nom sg masc | pervasive |
| pratikṣetram | prati-kṣetra | indeclinable avyayībhāva | in each field, in each body-locus |
| ātmā | ātman | nom sg masc | the self |
| bhinnaḥ | bhinna | nom sg masc, past passive participle | distinct, differentiated |
| svataḥ | sva | abl sg used adverbially | by its own nature |
| sukhī | sukhin | nom sg masc | happy, possessed of happiness |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *dehendriyamanaḥprāṇadhībhyaḥ* [dvandva]: *dehebhyaḥ indriyebhyaḥ manasaḥ prāṇebhyaḥ dhībhyāś ca*.
- *ananyasādhanaḥ* [nañ-tatpuruṣa]: *na anyat sādhanaṃ yasya saḥ*.
- *pratikṣetram* [avyayībhāva]: *prati kṣetram*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *ātmā*
- apādāna: *dehendriyamanaḥprāṇadhībhyaḥ*
- adhikaraṇa (distributive): *pratikṣetram*

**Verbal modality:**
- implied *asti*: *laṭ* / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
This is the already load-bearing verse in the thinker JSON, and rightly so: it is the compact doctrinal nucleus of the treatise. The transmitted GRETIL reading *ananyasādhanaḥ* should be retained; the attached commentary explicitly glosses it as “that whose manifestation is accomplished by itself alone,” rejecting older printed drift away from self-manifestness. The pair *vyāpī* and *pratikṣetram bhinnaḥ* is also philosophically exact: the self is not spatially local in the crude bodily sense, yet selves are not collapsed into a single universal subject. Compare the later sharpening of self-luminosity in the *Saṃvit-Siddhi*.

### Verse 4

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *na khalv ahamidaṅkārau ekasyaikatra vastuni //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Surely the “I”-cognition and the “this”-cognition do not belong, in one and the same case, to one single entity.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| na | na | indeclinable | not |
| khalu | khalu | indeclinable particle | surely, indeed |
| ahamidaṅkārau | aham-idaṃ-kāra | nom du masc, dvandva-like pair | the “I”-cognition and the “this”-cognition |
| ekasya | eka | gen sg masc/neut | of one, of a single |
| ekatra | eka-tra | indeclinable | in one place, in one case |
| vastuni | vastu | loc sg neut | in an entity, in a thing |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *ahamidaṅkārau* [dvandva]: *ahamkāraḥ ca idaṅkāraḥ ca*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- adhikaraṇa: *ekatra vastuni*
- ṣaṣṭhī-relation: *ekasya*

**Verbal modality:**
- implied *bhavataḥ* or *ghaṭete*: *laṭ* / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
The verse is transmitted in GRETIL as a compressed one-line kārikā; its force is unpacked immediately by the commentary. The whole argument turns on the irreducibility of first-person givenness to object-givenness: what is presented as *aham* is not presented as *idam*. This compressed distinction anticipates the more elaborate epistemological work of the *Saṃvit-Siddhi*.

### Verse 5

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *aparārthaṃ svam ātmānam ātmārthe 'nyac ca jānataḥ /  
> saṅghātatvāt parārthe 'smin dehe katham ivātmadhīḥ //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> For one who knows his own self as not for another, and what is other as for the sake of the self,  
> how could there be a cognition of self with respect to this body, which is for another because it is a composite?

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| aparārtham | a-para-artha | acc sg masc | not for another, not subordinate to another |
| svam | sva | acc sg masc | one's own |
| ātmānam | ātman | acc sg masc | self |
| ātmārthe | ātma-artha | loc sg masc | for the sake of the self |
| anyat | anya | acc sg neut | what is other, the other thing |
| ca | ca | indeclinable | and |
| jānataḥ | √jñā | gen sg masc, present active participle | of one knowing |
| saṅghātatvāt | saṅghāta-tva | abl sg neut | because of being a composite |
| parārthe | para-artha | loc sg masc/neut | being for another |
| asmin | idam | loc sg masc/neut | in this |
| dehe | deha | loc sg masc | body |
| katham | katham | indeclinable | how |
| iva | iva | indeclinable | indeed, possibly, how could |
| ātmadhīḥ | ātma-dhī | nom sg fem | self-cognition, cognition “this is self” |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *aparārtham* [nañ-tatpuruṣa]: *na parasya artham*.
- *ātmārthe* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *ātmanaḥ arthe*.
- *saṅghātatvāt* [taddhita abstract]: *saṅghāta-bhāvāt*.
- *parārthe* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *parasya arthe*.
- *ātmadhīḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *ātmani dhīḥ* or *ātmāyam iti dhīḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ (of the knowing): *jānataḥ*
- adhikaraṇa: *asmin dehe*
- hetu: *saṅghātatvāt*
- predicate-locative: *parārthe*

**Verbal modality:**
- *jānataḥ*: present active participle from √*jñā*
- implied *syāt*: *laṭ* or potential force by context

**Note:**
The inferential hinge is *parārthatva*: composites are functionally for something else. The self, as immediate subject, is not grasped as instrumental to another subject; the body is. This argument recurs in later Viśiṣṭādvaita polemic, but here it appears in concise seed-form. The philosophical pressure is anti-Cārvāka, but it also constrains any theory that identifies subjectivity with a merely organized aggregate.

### Verse 6

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *asphuṭatve 'pi bhedasya śarīre tadasaṃbhavāt /  
> tadguṇāntaravaidharmyād api jñānaṃ na tadguṇaḥ //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Even if the difference is not distinctly manifest, because that cognition cannot arise in the body,  
> and also because it is unlike the body's other qualities, cognition is not a quality of that body.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| asphuṭatve | a-sphuṭa-tva | loc sg neut | in the state of not being clear, even when not distinct |
| api | api | indeclinable | even |
| bhedasya | bheda | gen sg masc | of the difference |
| śarīre | śarīra | loc sg neut/masc | in the body |

| tadguṇāntaravaidharmyāt | tat-guṇāntara-vaidharmya | abl sg neut | because of dissimilarity from its other qualities |
| api | api | indeclinable | also |
| jñānam | jñāna | nom/acc sg neut | cognition |
| na | na | indeclinable | not |
| tadguṇaḥ | tat-guṇa | nom sg masc | a quality of that |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *asphuṭatve* [nañ-tatpuruṣa abstract]: *asphuṭasya bhāve*.
- *tadasaṃbhavāt* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *tasmin asaṃbhavāt*.
- *tadguṇāntaravaidharmyāt* [pañcamī-tatpuruṣa]: *tasya guṇāntarāt vaidharmyāt*.
- *tadguṇaḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *tasya guṇaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- hetu 1: *tadasaṃbhavāt*
- hetu 2: *tadguṇāntaravaidharmyāt*
- adhikaraṇa: *śarīre*

**Verbal modality:**
- implied *bhavati*: *laṭ* / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
The GRETIL string here is corrupt in minor detail; I adopt *vaidharmyāt* against the malformed transmission because both grammar and the accompanying commentary require “dissimilarity,” not the transmitted nonsense-form. The verse is important methodologically: even if direct apprehension of self-body difference is granted to be obscure, the body may still be rejected as the bearer of cognition by impossibility and by qualitative disanalogy.

### Verse 7

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *utpattimattvāt sanniveśaviśeṣataḥ /  
> rūpādimattvād bhūtatvād deho nātmā ghaṭādivat //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Because it is produced, because it is a particular configuration,  
> because it possesses color and the rest, and because it is an elemental thing, the body is not the self, like a pot and the like.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| utpattimattvāt | utpattimat-tva | abl sg neut | because of having production, because of being produced |
| sanniveśaviśeṣataḥ | sanniveśa-viśeṣa | abl sg masc/neut | because of being a specific arrangement/configuration |
| rūpādimattvāt | rūpādi-mat-tva | abl sg neut | because of possessing color and the rest |
| bhūtatvāt | bhūta-tva | abl sg neut | because of being an elemental/physical thing |
| dehaḥ | deha | nom sg masc | the body |
| na | na | indeclinable | not |
| ātmā | ātman | nom sg masc | the self |
| ghaṭādivat | ghaṭa-ādi-vat | indeclinable comparative | like a pot and the like |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *utpattimattvāt* [matup]: *utpattiḥ yasya asti tasya bhāvāt*.
- *sanniveśaviśeṣataḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *sanniveśasya viśeṣataḥ*.
- *rūpādimattvāt* [matup]: *rūpādīni yasya santi tasya bhāvāt*.
- *bhūtatvāt* [taddhita abstract]: *bhūta-bhāvāt*.
- *ghaṭādivat* [avyayībhāva-like comparative formation]: *ghaṭa-ādivat*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *dehaḥ*
- hetu-s: *utpattimattvāt*, *sanniveśaviśeṣataḥ*, *rūpādimattvāt*, *bhūtatvāt*
- upamāna: *ghaṭādivat*

**Verbal modality:**
- implied *asti*: *laṭ* / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
The body is here forced back into the ontological class of ordinary physical objects. That matters polemically: once the body falls under the same inferential markers as a pot, it cannot retain privileged claimant-status as subject. The verse condenses several lines of Nyāya-style inference into one metrical package.

### Verse 8

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *sacchidratvād adehitvād dehatvān mṛtadehavat /  
> ityādisādhanair nyāyyair niṣedhyā varṣmaṇaś citiḥ //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Because it has apertures, because it is not itself the embodied subject, and because it is a body, as in the case of a dead body,  
> the consciousness of the body is to be denied by such and similar proper inferences.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| sacchidratvāt | sa-chidra-tva | abl sg neut | because of possessing apertures |
| adehitvāt | a-dehi-tva | abl sg neut | because of not being the embodied self |
| dehatvāt | deha-tva | abl sg neut | because of being a body |
| mṛtadehavat | mṛta-deha-vat | indeclinable comparative | like a dead body |
| ityādisādhanair | iti-ādi-sādhana | instr pl neut | by such and similar proofs |
| nyāyyaiḥ | nyāyya | instr pl neut/masc | proper, logically fitting |
| niṣedhyā | ni-√ṣidh | gerundive, nom sg fem | to be denied |
| varṣmaṇaḥ | varṣman | gen sg neut | of the body |
| citiḥ | citi | nom sg fem | consciousness, awareness |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *sacchidratvāt* [bahuvrīhi abstract]: *sat chidraṃ yasya tasya bhāvāt*.
- *adehitvāt* [nañ-tatpuruṣa abstract]: *na dehī yasya tasya bhāvāt* or *dehin-bhinnatvāt* in force.
- *mṛtadehavat* [upamāna compound]: *mṛta-deha iva*.
- *ityādisādhanair* [avyayībhāva + tatpuruṣa]: *iti-ādibhiḥ sādhanair*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ (logical subject): *citiḥ*
- ṣaṣṭhī-relation: *varṣmaṇaḥ*
- hetu-s: *sacchidratvāt*, *adehitvāt*, *dehatvāt*
- karaṇa: *ityādisādhanair nyāyyaiḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *niṣedhyā*: gerundive expressing necessity/obligation of denial

**Note:**
This verse is inferential shorthand, and the commentary does not linger equally over each member. I therefore keep the translation close to the transmitted diction. The crucial point is not any single reason taken in isolation but the cumulative logical posture: once the body is classed with bodily objects generally, “consciousness of the body” becomes something to be rejected, not presumed. Verse 8 closes the *dehātma-vāda* block and clears the way for the subsequent examination of sense-, mind-, and breath-self theories.

## Editorial closing

By verse 8 Yāmuna has not yet completed a full positive metaphysics of the self; what he has done is more exact, and in a way more necessary. He has fixed the minimal predicates without which the *ātman* cannot be what liberation-discourse requires it to be, and he has shown that the body cannot bear those predicates. That settles the first and crudest reduction. It does not yet settle the status of the senses, mind, *prāṇa*, or pure cognition; those rival theses are taken up immediately in the ensuing sections of the *Ātma-Siddhi*. Nor does it yet settle the fuller problem of self-manifest awareness; that burden shifts, in Yāmuna’s own corpus, to the *Saṃvit-Siddhi*. Likewise, the brief inclusion of the *paramātman* in verse 2 is only promissory here; its systematic vindication belongs to the *Īśvara-Siddhi*. The present passage is therefore foundational rather than terminal: it removes the bodily claimant and establishes the space in which the rest of the triad must argue.
