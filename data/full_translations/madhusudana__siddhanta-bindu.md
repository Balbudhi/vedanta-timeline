**By:** Madhusūdana Sarasvatī

**Source edition transcribed from:** Divānji (ed.), *Siddhāntabindu*, Baroda: Oriental Institute, 1933; local GRETIL transcription (`madhusudana_siddhanta_bindu.txt`).  
**Translator:** Pāṇinian-rich extraction by Codex 5.4 (reasoning=high), 2026-05-10.

## Editorial introduction

The *upodghāta* of the *Siddhānta-Bindu* does more than preface the work. It fixes, in compressed form, the whole methodological posture by which Madhusūdana will read Śaṅkara's *Daśa-Ślokī*. First, it states the pedagogical intention of the work: Śaṅkara composed the ten verses in order to disclose the self by discrimination from the non-self. Second, it answers the obvious objection that the self is already known whenever one says "I." Madhusūdana's reply is characteristic: what is pre-philosophically given is not the pure self but the self under confusion with body, senses, and mind; hence the need for a specifically scriptural cognition of *brahmātmaikya* (identity of Brahman and self). Third, and most importantly, the section determines the operative *pramāṇa*: the *mahāvākya* alone establishes *ātmatattva*, and even the meanings of *tat* and *tvam* must be gathered under the discipline of śruti itself.

This is the natural opening unit because the later architecture of the text is already latent here. The three great tasks of the work are announced in advance: ascertainment of *tvam-padārtha*, ascertainment of *tat-padārtha*, and the non-fragmented meaning of the identity-sentence itself. What follows in the later *vibhāgas* is therefore not a change of subject but an unpacking of the hermeneutic program stated here. The same cluster of concerns governs Madhusūdana's broader Advaita as well: the status of śruti as liberating *pramāṇa*, the irreducibility of witness-consciousness, and the need to distinguish the pure self from its adventitious superimpositions recur in the *Advaita-Siddhi*, especially in the opening discussions of error, *avidyā*, and sublation. The *upodghāta* is thus small in compass, but architectonic in force.

## Translation

### Upodghāta, maṅgala-śloka

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *śrīśaṅkarācāryanavāvatāraṃ viśveśvaraṃ viśvaguruṃ praṇamya / vedāntaśāstraśravaṇālasānāṃ bodhāya kurve kam api prayatnam //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Having bowed to Viśveśvara, the Lord of the universe, the teacher of the world, the new descent as Śrī Śaṅkarācārya,  
> I undertake a certain effort for the awakening of those who are sluggish in hearing the Vedānta-śāstra.

**Word-by-word:**

|---|---|---|---|
| śrīśaṅkarācāryanavāvatāram | śrī-śaṅkarācārya-nava-avatāra | acc sg masc compound | the new avatāra as Śrī Śaṅkarācārya |
| viśveśvaram | viśveśvara | acc sg masc | the Lord of the universe |
| viśvagurum | viśvaguru | acc sg masc | the teacher of the world |
| praṇamya | pra-√nam | absolutive | having bowed |
| vedāntaśāstraśravaṇālasānām | vedānta-śāstra-śravaṇa-ālasā | gen pl | of those sluggish toward hearing Vedānta scripture |
| bodhāya | bodha | dat sg masc | for awakening |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**

- *viśveśvara* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *viśvasya īśvaraḥ*.
- *viśvaguru* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *viśvasya guruḥ*.
- *vedāntaśāstraśravaṇālasa* [tatpuruṣa]: *vedāntaśāstrasya śravaṇe ālasāḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: implicit *aham*

- karaṇa / pūrvakāla: *praṇamya*
- sampradāna: *bodhāya*

**Note:**
The verse is formally a *maṅgala* but already programmatic. The dative *bodhāya* turns the invocation outward: the work is not merely devotional but pedagogical.

### Upodghāta 3

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *jñātajñāpakatva* [upapada / tatpuruṣa]: *jñātaṃ jñāpayati iti bhāvaḥ*.

**Verbal modality:**
- no finite verb outside the conditional idiom; *cet* introduces the objection

**Note:**
The two charges are standard: redundancy and lack of soteriological efficacy. The answer must therefore show both that the self is not yet rightly known and that right knowledge does something.

### Upodghāta 4

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *na, cidbhāsyatvena lakṣaṇena idaṅkārāspadānām api dehendriyamanasāṃ pratibhāsato 'haṅkārāspadatvena tadavivekāt*

**English (line-by-line):**
> No. Because body, senses, and mind, though they are loci of the "this"-cognition by the mark of being illumined by consciousness, also appear as loci of the "I"-cognition,  
> there is non-discrimination of the self from them.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| na | na | indecl | no |
| cidbhāsyatvena | cid-bhāsya-tva | instr sg neut | by the property of being illumined by consciousness |
| lakṣaṇena | lakṣaṇa | instr sg neut | by the mark, characteristic |
| idaṅkārāspadānām | idaṅkāra-āspada | gen pl neut | of the loci of the "this"-notion |
| api | api | indecl | even though |
| dehendriyamanasām | deha-indriya-manas | gen pl neut | of body, senses, and mind |
| pratibhāsataḥ | prati-√bhās | gen/abl sg pres part used adverbially | while appearing |
| ahaṅkārāspadatvena | ahaṅkāra-āspadatva | instr sg neut | as possessing the status of locus of the "I"-notion |
| tadavivekāt | tad-aviveka | abl sg masc | because of non-discrimination from those |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *cidbhāsyatva* [tatpuruṣa]: *citā bhāsyatvam*.
- *idaṅkārāspada* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *idaṅkārasya āspadam*.
- *dehendriyamanas* [dvandva]: *dehaḥ ca indriyāṇi ca manaḥ ca*.
- *ahaṅkārāspadatva* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *ahaṅkārasya āspadatvam*.
- *tadaviveka* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *tebhyaḥ avivekaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- sambandha: *idaṅkārāspadānām*, *dehendriyamanasām*
- hetu: *tadavivekāt*
- hetv-antara / manner: *cidbhāsyatvena lakṣaṇena*, *ahaṅkārāspadatvena*

**Verbal modality:**
- *pratibhāsataḥ*: present participial force, active appearance

**Note:**
This is the crux of the reply. Ordinary awareness does not isolate the self; it confuses the witness with what is merely illumined.

### Upodghāta 8

### Upodghāta 24

**Sanskrit (IAST):**

## Editorial closing

This opening section settles three matters that the rest of the *Siddhānta-Bindu* presupposes. It settles, first, that the self as ordinarily lived is not yet the self as philosophically ascertained. It settles, second, that the *mahāvākya* is the decisive *pramāṇa* for *ātmatattva*. It settles, third, that inquiry is not a rival to śruti but the removal of impediments to śruti's liberating operation. What it does not yet settle is the detailed ascertainment of the two terms whose identity the *mahāvākya* teaches. That work begins immediately in the *Prathamo Vibhāgaḥ* on *tvam-padārtha*, continues in the *Dvitīyo Vibhāgaḥ* on *tat-padārtha*, and reaches its explicit culmination in the *Tṛtīyo Vibhāgaḥ* on the meaning of *tattvamasi*. Within Madhusūdana's larger corpus, the same questions reappear under more polemical pressure in the *Advaita-Siddhi*, especially in the opening discussions of error, *avidyā*, and the status of liberating knowledge.
