# Saṃvit-Siddhi — Extended Translation
**By:** Yāmunācārya  
**Locus scope:** Saṃvit-Siddhi verses 31-40, the conclusion of the *advitīya* exegesis and its opening royal analogy.

## Editorial introduction

These ten verses form a natural argumentative unit within the *Saṃvit-Siddhi*. Verses 1-30 had asked what exactly the Upaniṣadic predicate *advitīya* can mean if one reads it with grammatical seriousness rather than with doctrinal haste. Yāmuna had already argued there that neither a straightforward *tatpuruṣa* nor a straightforward *bahuvrīhi* construal can bear the burden of total world-negation. Verses 31-35 then state the result in compressed form: the real existence of the *prapañca* (the articulated world-manifold) is not cancelled by *advaita-śruti*; what the Upaniṣad excludes is not dependent plurality as such, but any rival countable as a second to Brahman, that is, any equal or superior counterpart. Verses 36-40 immediately add the first governing analogy, drawn from royal speech. This analogy is not decorative. It fixes the *tātparya* of the predicate by showing how ordinary language can deny a peer without denying subordinates, dependents, or internal differentiations of the sovereign's domain.

The scope is therefore exact. One should not separate verses 31-35 from verses 36-40, because the former give the thesis and the latter give the rule for reading it. Nor should one extend much further here, because from verse 41 onward Yāmuna turns to additional analogies and then to direct scriptural corroboration. In the architecture of the whole work, this section is where semantic analysis yields metaphysical consequence. In the architecture of Yāmuna's broader position, it is decisive for the Viśiṣṭādvaitin refusal to infer from non-duality any unreality of the world. That larger refusal is restated later in this very work at Svs 58-67 and 72-73, and it becomes foundational for the more expansive hermeneutic of Rāmānuja's *Śrī-Bhāṣya* on *Chāndogya Upaniṣad* 6.2.1.

## Translation

### Svs 31

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *tasmāt prapañcasadbhāvo nādvaitaśrutibādhitaḥ*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Therefore the real existence of the *prapañca* (articulated world-manifold) is not negated  
> by the scripture that teaches non-duality.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| tasmāt | tad | abl sg masc/neut pronoun | therefore; from that |
| prapañcasadbhāvaḥ | prapañca-sad-bhāva | nom sg masc | the real existence of the world-manifold |
| na | na | indeclinable | not |
| advaitaśrutibādhitaḥ | advaita-śruti-bādhita | kta-PPP nom sg masc | negated by non-duality scripture |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *prapañcasadbhāvaḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *prapañcasya sadbhāvaḥ*.
- *advaitaśrutibādhitaḥ* [tṛtīyā-tatpuruṣa]: *advaitaṃ bruvatī śrutiḥ tayā bādhitaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ (grammatical subject): *prapañcasadbhāvaḥ*
- karaṇa (embedded in compound): *advaita-śrutyā*

**Verbal modality:**
- *bādhitaḥ*: kta-participle from √*bādh*; passive participial predicate with the present copula understood.

**Note:**
This is the formal conclusion of the grammatical argument in Svs 3-30. Yāmuna does not say that the world is independent of Brahman; he says that its *sadbhāva* is not scripturally sublated. The distinction matters, and it is carried forward in Svs 66-67, where the world is positively redescribed as Brahman's *vibhūti*.

### Svs 32

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *svapramāṇabalāt siddhaḥ śrutyā cāpy anumoditaḥ*

**English (line-by-line):**
> It is established by the force of its own *pramāṇa* (valid means of knowledge);  
> and it is also ratified by scripture.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| svapramāṇabalāt | sva-pramāṇa-bala | abl sg neut | by the force of its own valid means of knowledge |
| siddhaḥ | siddha | kta-PPP nom sg masc | established; proven |
| śrutyā | śruti | instr sg fem | by scripture |
| ca | ca | indeclinable | and |
| api | api | indeclinable | also; indeed |
| anumoditaḥ | anumodita | kta-PPP nom sg masc | approved; ratified |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *svapramāṇabalāt* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *svasya pramāṇasya balāt*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ (understood from Svs 31): *prapañcasadbhāvaḥ*
- karaṇa: *svapramāṇabalāt*
- karaṇa: *śrutyā*

**Verbal modality:**
- *siddhaḥ*: kta-participle from √*sidh*; passive/resultative participial predicate.
- *anumoditaḥ*: kta-participle from *anu* + √*mud*; passive participial predicate.

**Note:**
The pairing is exacting. Ordinary *pramāṇas* are not overruled and then replaced by scripture; rather, their deliverance is first established and then confirmed. Compare Svs 72-73, where Yāmuna again says that the world is not sublated because it is Brahman's manifestation.

### Svs 33

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *tenādvitīyaṃ brahmeti śruter artho 'yam ucyate*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Therefore the meaning of the scriptural sentence, "*Brahman is advitīya* (*without a second*),"  
> is stated as follows.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| tena | tad | instr sg masc/neut pronoun | therefore; by that |
| advitīyam | a-dvitīya | nom/acc sg neut | without a second |
| brahma | brahman | nom/acc sg neut | Brahman |
| iti | iti | indeclinable | thus; quotation marker |
| śruteḥ | śruti | gen sg fem | of the scriptural statement |
| arthaḥ | artha | nom sg masc | meaning |
| ayam | idam | nom sg masc pronoun | this |
| ucyate | √vac | 3sg pres ind Ā/passive | is said; is stated |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *advitīyam* [nañ-samāsa]: *na dvitīyaṃ yasya* / *yasmin dvitīyaṃ na gaṇyate*; in this passage the bare compound is authoritatively narrowed by Svs 34-35.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ (grammatical subject): *arthaḥ ayam*
- sambandha: *śruteḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *ucyate*: laṭ / ātmanepada / passive.

**Note:**
The instrumental *tena* marks inference from the prior semantic discussion, not a fresh dogmatic stipulation. Yāmuna is telling the reader how the Upaniṣadic sentence must be read once its grammar and its doctrinal consequences are both handled without excess.

### Svs 34

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *dvitīyagaṇanāyogyo nāsīd asti bhaviṣyati*

**English (line-by-line):**
> No being fit to be counted as a second has existed, exists,  
> or will exist.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| dvitīyagaṇanāyogyaḥ | dvitīya-gaṇanā-yogya | nom sg masc | fit to be counted as a second |
| na | na | indeclinable | not |
| āsīt | √as | 3sg imperfect parasmaipada | was; existed |
| asti | √as | 3sg pres ind parasmaipada | is; exists |
| bhaviṣyati | √bhū | 3sg simple fut ind parasmaipada | will be; will exist |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *dvitīyagaṇanāyogyaḥ* [saptamī-tatpuruṣa]: *dvitīyagaṇanāyāṃ yogyaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *dvitīyagaṇanāyogyaḥ* (understood entity)

**Verbal modality:**
- *āsīt*: lāṅ / parasmaipada / active.
- *asti*: laṭ / parasmaipada / active.
- *bhaviṣyati*: lṛṭ / parasmaipada / active.

**Note:**
The triplet past-present-future excludes rivalry across all time. This is stronger than a merely contextual denial. It is also the verse that licenses the well-known Viśiṣṭādvaitin gloss: the Upaniṣad denies a second competitor to Brahman, not the dependent world.

### Svs 35

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *samo vābhyadiko vāsya yo dvitīyas tu gaṇyate*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Whether equal to it, or superior to it,  
> that indeed is what is counted as a second.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| samaḥ | sama | nom sg masc | equal |
| vā | vā | indeclinable | or |
| abhyadhikaḥ | abhyadhika | nom sg masc | greater; superior |
| vā | vā | indeclinable | or |
| asya | idam | gen sg pronoun | to it; of it |
| yaḥ | yad | nom sg masc relative pronoun | which; whoever |
| dvitīyaḥ | dvitīya | nom sg masc | second |
| tu | tu | indeclinable | indeed; however |
| gaṇyate | √gaṇ | 3sg pres ind Ā/passive | is counted; is reckoned |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- No samāsa requiring resolution in this line.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *yaḥ ... dvitīyaḥ*
- sambandha: *asya*
- predicate complement: *samaḥ vā abhyadhikaḥ vā*

**Verbal modality:**
- *gaṇyate*: laṭ / ātmanepada / passive.

**Note:**
This is the limiting clause that prevents the denial from expanding into absolute plurality-negation. The semantic target of *dvitīya* is a peer or over-peer. Later Śrīvaiṣṇava exegesis, above all in Rāmānuja's *Śrī-Bhāṣya* 1.1.1, will make this restriction a major hermeneutic principle.

### Svs 36

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *yato 'sya vibhavavyūhakalāmātram idaṃ jagat*

**English (line-by-line):**
> For this world is merely a fraction of the deployment of his manifested glory  
> and power.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| yataḥ | yataḥ | indeclinable | because; for |
| asya | idam | gen sg pronoun | of him; his |
| vibhavavyūhakalāmātram | vibhava-vyūha-kalā-mātra | nom/acc sg neut | a mere fragment of the array of glory/power |
| idam | idam | nom/acc sg neut pronoun | this |
| jagat | jagat | nom/acc sg neut | world |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *vibhavavyūhakalāmātram* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa, nested]: *vibhavasya vyūhasya kalā-mātram*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *idam jagat*
- sambandha: *asya*
- predicate complement: *vibhavavyūhakalāmātram*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb is expressed; a present copula (*asti*) is understood in a nominal sentence.

**Note:**
Yāmuna does not retreat from plurality here; he reclassifies it. The world is neither a rival to Brahman nor an illusion opposed to him. It is a dependent fraction of divine manifestation, anticipating the explicit *vibhūti* language at Svs 66-67. Whether one hears *vyūha* here in a specifically Pāñcarātra register or in the broader sense of ordered deployment, the polemical point is the same: dependence does not amount to rivalry.

### Svs 37

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *dvitīyavāgāspadatāṃ pratipadyeta tat katham*

**English (line-by-line):**
> How could that  
> come to occupy the status of being denoted by the word "second"?

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| dvitīyavāgāspadatām | dvitīya-vāk-āspadatā | acc sg fem | the status of being the locus of the word "second" |
| pratipadyeta | prati-√pad | 3sg optative ātmanepada | could attain; could come to assume |
| tat | tad | nom/acc sg neut pronoun | that; it |
| katham | katham | indeclinable | how |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *dvitīyavāgāspadatām* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *dvitīyāyā vācaḥ āspadatām*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *tat*
- gati / prāpti-goal: *dvitīyavāgāspadatām*

**Verbal modality:**
- *pratipadyeta*: liṅ / ātmanepada / active-middle; potential or rhetorical-optative force.

**Note:**
The optative gives the line a reductio shape: if the world is only a dependent fraction of divine manifestation, the very application of the predicate "second" becomes semantically improper. The issue is not merely ontological rank but linguistic fitness.

### Svs 38

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *yathā colanṛpaḥ samrāḍ advitīyo 'dya bhūtale*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Just as, "Today on earth the Cola king, the emperor, is without a second"  
> may be said.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| yathā | yathā | indeclinable | just as |
| colanṛpaḥ | cola-nṛpa | nom sg masc | the Cola king |
| samrāṭ | samrāj | nom sg masc irregular | emperor |
| advitīyaḥ | a-dvitīya | nom sg masc | without a second |
| adya | adya | indeclinable | today |
| bhūtale | bhū-tala | loc sg neut | on earth |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *colanṛpaḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *colānāṃ nṛpaḥ*.
- *advitīyaḥ* [nañ-samāsa]: *na dvitīyo yasya*.
- *bhūtale* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *bhūmeḥ tale*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *colanṛpaḥ samrāṭ*
- adhikaraṇa: *bhūtale*
- predicate complement: *advitīyaḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb is expressed; a present copula (*asti*) is understood in the quoted nominal sentence.

**Note:**
The worldly analogy begins with imperial idiom. "Without a second" in such a context plainly means unrivalled in rank. It does not mean solitary in existence. The point is hermeneutic, not merely rhetorical.

### Svs 39

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *iti tattulyanṛpatinivāraṇaparaṃ vacaḥ*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Such a statement is directed toward excluding  
> a king equal to him.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| iti | iti | indeclinable | thus; in this way |
| tattulyanṛpatinivāraṇaparam | tat-tulya-nṛpati-nivāraṇa-para | nom/acc sg neut | chiefly aimed at excluding a king equal to him |
| vacaḥ | vacas | nom/acc sg neut | statement; utterance |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *tattulyanṛpatinivāraṇaparam* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa, nested]: *tat-tulyasya nṛpateḥ nivāraṇe param*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *vacaḥ*
- prayojana: *tat-tulya-nṛpati-nivāraṇam* (embedded in compound)

**Verbal modality:**
- *param* functions predicatively in a nominal sentence; the present copula is understood.

**Note:**
The word *para* here is crucial. Yāmuna is identifying the principal purport of the utterance, not merely one incidental effect among others. This is why the analogy serves as a rule of interpretation for the Upaniṣadic predicate itself.

### Svs 40

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *na tu tadbhṛtyatatputrakalatrādiniṣedhakam*

**English (line-by-line):**
> It is not, however, a statement that negates  
> his servants, his sons, his wives, and the rest.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| na | na | indeclinable | not |
| tu | tu | indeclinable | however |
| tadbhṛtyatatputrakalatrādiniṣedhakam | tat-bhṛtya-tat-putra-kalatra-ādi-niṣedhaka | nom/acc sg neut | negating his servants, his sons, his wives, and so on |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *tadbhṛtyatatputrakalatrādiniṣedhakam* [mixed compound: dvandva within tatpuruṣa]: *tasya bhṛtyāḥ, tasya putrāḥ, kalatrāṇi, ādīni ca niṣedhayati iti*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ (understood from Svs 39): *vacaḥ*
- karman (embedded in compound): *tad-bhṛtya-tat-putra-kalatra-ādīn*

**Verbal modality:**
- *niṣedhakam* is a kṛt-derived adjectival noun from *ni* + √*ṣidh* / *sedh* "to prohibit, exclude"; nominal predicate with the copula understood.

**Note:**
This verse states the controlling restriction with unusual plainness. Dependent plurality is left untouched. The same point is then pursued by further analogies in Svs 41-48, especially the claim that "there is one sun" does not negate the sun's rays.

## Editorial closing

What this passage settles is narrow but decisive. It settles that Yāmuna will not allow the Upaniṣadic *advitīya* to be read as a blanket cancellation of the world's reality. The semantic target of negation is a rival second, not dependent multiplicity; and the world, precisely as divine manifestation, falls outside that target. What the passage does not yet settle is the full positive ontology of that manifestation. That work is taken up immediately in Svs 41-48 through further analogies, in Svs 49-57 through corroborating *śruti* citations, and in Svs 58-67 through the causal and *vibhūti* argument that describes the entire world as *cid-acin-maya* and as Brahman's own manifested glory. The local conclusion is then restated in Svs 68-73. Beyond the *Saṃvit-Siddhi*, the same hermeneutic ripens into the more architectonic scriptural method of Rāmānuja's *Śrī-Bhāṣya*, especially at 1.1.1 and in the treatment of *Chāndogya Upaniṣad* 6.2.1.
