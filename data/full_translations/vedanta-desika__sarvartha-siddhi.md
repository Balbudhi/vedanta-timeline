## Editorial introduction

This file treats a deliberately narrow but structurally decisive locus: the second verse of the *Jaḍa-dravya-sara* together with Deśika's own prose gloss in the *Sarvārtha-Siddhi*. The restriction is not arbitrary. In the witnesses presently available in this workspace, the immediately preceding verse (1.1) survives only in a corrupted OCR state in its first half, whereas 1.2 and its gloss are recoverable in stable Sanskrit. The result is that 1.2 is the first place where one can work with the text at the level of lexical and grammatical precision the site requires.

The verse does two things at once. First, it places the work inside a Śrīvaiṣṇava lineage: Deśika is named as the son of Ananta Sūri, the grandson of Puṇḍarīkākṣa Sūri, and the one who has heard from Rāmānujārya what is *sat* and what is *asat*. Second, the prose gloss sharply construes that lineage-claim as an epistemic claim. *Sat* here is not a loose term of approbation. Deśika glosses it as what is authoritative and fit for appropriation by the *mumukṣu* (seeker of liberation); *asat* is what stands outside that domain. Hearing the one as one and the other as the other is thus said to be causally decisive. The work's later ontological discriminations are already presupposed in this opening act of disciplined hearing.

Within the larger architecture of the *Tattva-Muktā-Kalāpa* and *Sarvārtha-Siddhi*, the verse therefore functions as a threshold statement. The ensuing metaphysics of *dravya* and *adravya* is not presented as free speculation, but as the unfolding of a received and sifted order of distinctions. That is fully consonant with Deśika's broader position elsewhere: technical analysis is rigorous, but its rigor is not autonomous from *sampradāya*; it is warranted through it.

## Translation

### Jaḍa-dravya-sara 1.2

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *nānā-siddhānta-nīti-śrama-vimala-dhiyo 'nanta-sūres tanūjo*  
> *vaiśvāmitrasya pautro vitata-makha-vidheḥ puṇḍarīkākṣa-sūreḥ |*  
> *śrutvā rāmānujāryāt sad-asad api tatas tattva-muktā-kalāpaṃ*  
> *vyātānīd veṅkaṭeśo varada-guru-kṛpā-lambhita-uddāma-bhūmā || 2 ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> [He was] the son of Ananta Sūri, whose intelligence had been made clear by the labor of mastering many doctrinal positions.  
> [He was] the grandson, in the Vaiśvāmitra line, of Puṇḍarīkākṣa Sūri, performer of expansive sacrifices.  
> Having heard, from Rāmānujārya, both what is *sat* (authoritative and to be appropriated) and what is *asat* (the contrary), then  
> Veṅkaṭeśa unfolded the *Tattva-Muktā-Kalāpa*, his amplitude of standing having been won through the grace of his teacher Varada.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| nānā-siddhānta-nīti-śrama-vimala-dhiyaḥ | compound ending in *dhī* | gen. sg.; bahuvrīhi qualifying *ananta-sūreḥ* | whose intelligence is purified by labor over many doctrinal systems |
| ananta-sūreḥ | *Ananta-sūri* | gen. sg. masc. | of Ananta Sūri |
| tanūjaḥ | *tanūja* | nom. sg. masc. | son |
| vaiśvāmitrasya | *Vaiśvāmitra* | gen. sg. masc. | of Vaiśvāmitra / of the Vaiśvāmitra line |
| pautraḥ | *pautra* | nom. sg. masc. | grandson |
| vitata-makha-vidheḥ | compound | gen. sg. masc. | of the performer of extended sacrifices |
| puṇḍarīkākṣa-sūreḥ | *Puṇḍarīkākṣa-sūri* | gen. sg. masc. | of Puṇḍarīkākṣa Sūri |
| śrutvā | √*śru* | absolutive (*ktvā*) | having heard |
| rāmānujāryāt | *Rāmānujārya* | abl. sg. masc. | from Rāmānujārya |
| sat | *sat* | acc. sg. neut. | the true, the authoritative |
| asat | *asat* | acc. sg. neut. | the untrue, the non-authoritative |
| api | indeclinable | particle | also, indeed; here “both” with the pair |
| tataḥ | indeclinable | adverb | thereafter, from that |
| tattva-muktā-kalāpam | *tattva-muktā-kalāpa* | acc. sg. masc. | the Tattva-Muktā-Kalāpa |
| vyātānīt | vi-ā-√*tan* | 3rd sg. past, parasmaipada, active | spread out, unfolded |
| veṅkaṭeśaḥ | *Veṅkaṭeśa* | nom. sg. masc. | Veṅkaṭeśa |
| varada-guru-kṛpā-lambhita-uddāma-bhūmā | compound | nom. sg. masc. bahuvrīhi | one whose elevated station has been obtained through the grace of teacher Varada |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *nānā-siddhānta-nīti-śrama-vimala-dhī* [bahuvrīhi]: *nānāsu siddhānta-nītiṣu śrameṇa vimalā dhīr yasya saḥ*.
- *vitata-makha-vidhi* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa / karmadhāraya-like]: *vitato makhaḥ yasya vidhīyate saḥ*; “one whose sacrificial performance is extensive.”
- *tattva-muktā-kalāpa* [tatpuruṣa]: *tattvānāṃ muktā-kalāpaḥ*; a “cluster/garland of pearls of reality.”
- *varada-guru-kṛpā-lambhita-uddāma-bhūmā* [bahuvrīhi]: *varada-guroḥ kṛpayā lambhitā uddāmā bhūmir yasya saḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *veṅkaṭeśaḥ*
- karman: *tattva-muktā-kalāpam*
- apādāna: *rāmānujāryāt*
- pūrvakāla-kriyā: *śrutvā*

**Verbal modality:**
- *vyātānīt*: past narrative form from vi-ā-√*tan* / parasmaipada / active.
- *śrutvā*: absolutive from √*śru*, marking prior accomplished action.

**Note:**
The force of the verse lies in its sequence: lineage, hearing, then composition. Deśika does not present the *Kalāpa* as a merely individual production. The work is unfolded only after the discriminative hearing of *sat* and *asat* from Rāmānuja's line. That same alignment of authorship with received discrimination reappears in Deśika's more explicit invocatory self-positioning elsewhere, including the opening of the *Nyāya-Pariśuddhi* and the sectarian self-location of the *Rahasya-Traya-Sāra*.

### Sarvārtha-Siddhi ad 1.2a

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *cikīrṣitasya śraddheyatvāya vaktṛ-saṃpradāya-vailakṣaṇyaṃ darśayati — nāneti |*

**English (line-by-line):**
> To make what is about to be undertaken worthy of trust, he shows the distinctiveness of the speaker's lineage, by the word “many”.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| cikīrṣitasya | desiderative of √*kṛ* | gen. sg. neut. ppp-like desiderative formation | of what is intended to be done |
| śraddheyatvāya | *śraddheyatva* | dat. sg. neut. | for trustworthiness |
| vaktṛ-saṃpradāya-vailakṣaṇyam | compound | acc. sg. neut. | the distinctiveness of the speaker's lineage |
| darśayati | √*dṛś* (causative/denominative usage) | laṭ, 3rd sg. parasmaipada | he shows |
| nāneti | *nānā iti* | quotative compound | “by ‘nānā’,” by the expression “many” |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *vaktṛ-saṃpradāya-vailakṣaṇya* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *vaktuḥ saṃpradāyasya vailakṣaṇyam*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: understood commentator referring to the verse-author
- karman: *vaktṛ-saṃpradāya-vailakṣaṇyam*
- prayojana: *śraddheyatvāya*

**Verbal modality:**
- *darśayati*: laṭ / parasmaipada / active.

**Note:**
The gloss explicitly reads the verse rhetorically, not merely genealogically. Lineage here is offered as a warrant-structure. The commentator's concern is not ancestry as ornament, but credibility for what follows.

### Sarvārtha-Siddhi ad 1.2b

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *sat — prāmāṇikaṃ mumukṣor upādeyaṃ ca; tad anyad asat |*

**English (line-by-line):**
> “*Sat*” means what is authoritative, and also what is to be appropriated by the seeker of liberation.  
> What is other than that is *asat*.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| sat | *sat* | nom./acc. sg. neut.; cited term | “sat” |
| prāmāṇikam | *prāmāṇika* | nom./acc. sg. neut. | authoritative, valid as pramāṇa-grounded |
| mumukṣoḥ | desiderative from √*muc* | gen. sg. masc. | of the seeker of liberation |
| upādeyam | *upādeya* | nom./acc. sg. neut. gerundive | to be taken up, to be appropriated |
| ca | indeclinable | particle | and |
| tad | pronoun | acc. sg. neut. | that |
| anyat | *anya* | acc. sg. neut. | other than |
| asat | *asat* | nom./acc. sg. neut. | asat, the non-authoritative |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- none beyond lexical derivatives.

**Kāraka structure:**
- predicate terms: *prāmāṇikam*, *upādeyam*, *asat*
- possessor / qualifying genitive: *mumukṣoḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb is expressed; the gloss is an equational definition.

**Note:**
This is the decisive scholastic move in the passage. *Sat* is not reduced to sheer “being.” It is read normatively and soteriologically: what is both authoritative and fit to be taken up by the *mumukṣu*. That keeps ontology and discipline tied together, rather than allowing “the real” to float free of liberative competence.

### Sarvārtha-Siddhi ad 1.2c

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *satas sattvenāsataś cāsattvena śravaṇam iheṣṭam, tataḥ — śravaṇād eva hetoḥ || 2 ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Here, the hearing of what is *sat* precisely as *sat*, and of what is *asat* precisely as *asat*, is what is desired.  
> Therefore, because hearing itself is the cause.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| satas | *sat* | gen. sg. neut. | of the sat |
| sattvena | *sattva* | inst. sg. neut. | in the character of being sat, as sat |
| asataḥ | *asat* | gen. sg. neut. | of the asat |
| ca | indeclinable | particle | and |
| asattvena | *asattva* | inst. sg. neut. | in the character of being asat, as asat |
| śravaṇam | *śravaṇa* | nom. sg. neut. | hearing |
| iha | indeclinable | adverb | here, in this matter |
| iṣṭam | √*iṣ* | nom. sg. neut. ppp used predicatively | desired, approved |
| tataḥ | indeclinable | adverb | therefore |
| śravaṇāt | *śravaṇa* | abl. sg. neut. | from hearing |
| eva | indeclinable | particle | precisely, indeed |
| hetoḥ | *hetu* | gen. sg. masc. | because of [its being] the cause |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- none.

**Kāraka structure:**
- viṣaya-specification: *satas sattvena*, *asataḥ asattvena*
- pradhāna-pada: *śravaṇam*
- hetu / explanatory ablative: *śravaṇāt*

**Verbal modality:**
- *iṣṭam*: past passive participle used as present predicative.
- No finite verb is stated in the second member; causal force is conveyed through *tataḥ* plus *hetoḥ*.

**Note:**
The instrumental forms *sattvena* and *asattvena* carry the philosophical weight. The point is not merely that both kinds of object are heard about, but that each must be apprehended under its proper determination. The later anti-Advaitic edge of Deśika's system already depends on that discipline of non-confusion.

## Editorial closing

This passage settles one thing with unusual clarity: the *Sarvārtha-Siddhi* does not open by separating scholastic rigor from lineage-authority, but by binding them together. Deśika's account of *sat* and *asat* is already polemical in germ, because it requires that what is to be appropriated by the *mumukṣu* be distinguished from what is not merely by sentiment or inherited preference, but by disciplined hearing and authorized discrimination. What this passage does not yet settle is the content of the ontological divisions themselves. That labor begins immediately afterward in the *Jaḍa-dravya-sara*, and becomes explicit in verses such as 1.4 and the later arguments on time, direction, and atomicity. The same structure of received distinction unfolding into technical analysis is carried forward in the *Nyāya-Pariśuddhi* and, in a polemical register, in the *Śata-Dūṣaṇī*.
