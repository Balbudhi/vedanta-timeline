# Advaita-Siddhi — Extended Translation
**By:** Madhusūdana Sarasvatī  
**Locus scope:** *Prathama-pariccheda*: opening *maṅgalācaraṇa* (vv. 1–4) and the first methodological section on *vipratipatti* (*vipratipatti-vākyasya vicārāṅgatvam*).  
**Source edition transcribed from:** *Advaita Siddhi*, vol. 1, part 1, Bombay: Tukaram Javaji, 1917; checked against the Śṛṅgeri Advaita Śāradā digital text for reading control.  
**Translator:** Pāṇinian-rich extraction by Codex 5.4 (reasoning=high), 2026-05-10.

---

## Editorial introduction

This opening block states the whole burden of the *Advaita-Siddhi* before the formal inferential machinery begins. The first four verses do three things at once. They identify Brahman as the substrate of the *mṛṣā-dvaita-prapañca* (the false dualistic manifold); they mark liberation as only “as if attained,” thereby refusing any real alteration in Brahman; and they announce that the work is written not merely for contemplative understanding but also for victory in debate. The prose that follows at once turns from devotional invocation to Navya-Nyāya procedure. Madhusūdana insists that the falsity of duality must be established first, because the rest of the treatise depends upon it; that this establishment must proceed both by proving the Advaitin’s own case and by dismantling the opponent’s; and that the inquiry must therefore begin from *vipratipatti* (a stated conflict of opposed theses), since philosophic examination takes shape through a doubt generated by opposed views.

This is the natural opening unit because the later first *pariccheda* merely unfolds what is here programmatically fixed. The successive definitions of *mithyātva* in later sections of the same *pariccheda* presuppose this initial staging of the issue. The passage also displays a trait central to Madhusūdana’s larger position: he joins a strongly Vedāntic devotional idiom to very hard-edged dialectic. The same conjunction appears, in another register, in the *Siddhānta-Bindu* upodghāta on the *mahāvākyas* as the decisive *pramāṇa*, and again in the *Bhakti-Rasāyana*, where affective devotion is not expelled from Advaita but given systematic place.

## Translation

### Maṅgalāśloka 1

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *māyākalpitamātṛtāmukhamṛṣādvaitaprapañcāśrayaḥ*  
> *satyajñānasukhātmakaḥ śrutiśikhotthākhaṇḍadhīgocaraḥ ।*  
> *mithyābandhavidhūnanena paramānandaikatānātmakaṃ*  
> *mokṣaṃ prāpta iva svayaṃ vijayate viṣṇurvikalpojjhitaḥ ॥ 1 ॥*

**English (line-by-line):**
> He whose substrate is the false dualistic manifold, beginning with knowerhood, imagined by *māyā* (cognitive projection),  
> whose nature is reality, cognition, and bliss, and who is the object of the undivided cognition arising from the crest of scripture,  
> by the shaking off of false bondage, as if having attained  
> liberation consisting in an unbroken continuity of supreme bliss, Viṣṇu shines forth of himself, free from conceptual construction.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| māyākalpitamātṛtāmukhamṛṣādvaitaprapañcāśrayaḥ | *māyā-kalpita-mātṛtā-mukha-mṛṣā-dvaita-prapañca-āśraya* | nom. sg. m. compound | whose support is the false dualistic manifold beginning with knowerhood, imagined by *māyā* |
| satyajñānasukhātmakaḥ | *satya-jñāna-sukha-ātmaka* | nom. sg. m. compound | having reality, cognition, and bliss as its nature |
| śrutiśikhotthākhaṇḍadhīgocaraḥ | *śruti-śikha-uttha-akhaṇḍa-dhī-gocara* | nom. sg. m. compound | accessible to the undivided cognition arising from the summit of scripture |
| mithyābandhavidhūnanena | *mithyā-bandha-vidhūnana* | instr. sg. n. compound | by the shaking off of false bondage |
| paramānandaikatānātmakaṃ | *paramānanda-ekatāna-ātmaka* | acc. sg. m. compound | consisting in an unbroken stream of supreme bliss |
| mokṣaṃ | *mokṣa* | acc. sg. m. | liberation |
| prāpta | √*āp* (with *pra*) | past passive participle, nom. sg. m. | having attained |
| iva | indecl. | particle | as if |
| svayam | indecl. | adverb | of itself; by itself |
| vijayate | √*ji* (vi- + Ā) | laṭ, 3rd sg., ātmanepada | shines forth; manifests victoriously |
| viṣṇuḥ | *viṣṇu* | nom. sg. m. | Viṣṇu; the all-pervading one |
| vikalpojjhitaḥ | *vikalpa-ujjhita* | nom. sg. m. compound | bereft of conceptual distinctions |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *māyākalpitamātṛtāmukhamṛṣādvaitaprapañcāśrayaḥ* [bahuvrīhi]: *māyayā kalpitaḥ mātṛtāmukhaḥ mṛṣā-dvaita-prapañcaḥ yasya āśrayaḥ saḥ*.
- *satyajñānasukhātmakaḥ* [bahuvrīhi/karmadhāraya-complex]: *satyaṃ jñānaṃ sukhaṃ cāsya ātmā svarūpaṃ ca*.
- *śrutiśikhotthākhaṇḍadhīgocaraḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa-complex]: *śrutiśikhāyām utthitā akhaṇḍā dhīḥ; tasyā gocaraḥ*.
- *paramānandaikatānātmakaṃ* [karmadhāraya]: *paramānandasya ekatānaṃ yasya tad*.
- *vikalpojjhitaḥ* [upapada-tatpuruṣa]: *vikalpān ujjhitaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *viṣṇuḥ*
- karman: *mokṣaṃ*
- karaṇa: *mithyābandhavidhūnanena*

**Verbal modality:**
- *vijayate*: laṭ / ātmanepada / active-intransitive middle

**Note:**
The force of *iva* is decisive. Madhusūdana does not say that Brahman literally undergoes liberation; rather, liberation is spoken of from the standpoint of the removal of adventitious bondage. This grammar anticipates the later Advaitin insistence that release is not production of a new state but manifestation of what already is.

### Maṅgalāśloka 2

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *śrīrāmaviśveśvaramādhavānāmaikyena sākṣātkṛtamādhavānām ।*

> May there be reverence to the dust risen from the feet of those Mādhavas who have directly realized Mādhava through the unity of Śrī Rāma and Viśveśvara,

**Note:**
The proper names make the exact grouping of the compound somewhat uncertain, and the verse is best read as a guru-lineage salutation. What matters philosophically is the movement from direct realization (*sākṣātkṛta*) to transmissible sanctifying contact.

### Maṅgalāśloka 3

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *bahubhirvihitā budhaiḥ parārthaṃ vijayante 'mitavistṛtā nibandhāḥ ।*

### Maṅgalāśloka 4

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *śraddhādhanena muninā madhusūdanena saṅgṛhya śāstranicayaṃ racitātiyatnāt ।*

### Prathama-pariccheda, Section 1a

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *tatrādvaitasiddher dvaitamithyātvasiddhipūrvakatvāt dvaitamithyātvam eva prathamam upapādanīyam ।*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Since the *Advaita-Siddhi* is preceded by the establishment of the falsity of duality,  
> the falsity of duality itself must first be demonstrated.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| tatrādvaitasiddheḥ | *tatra* + *advaita-siddhi* | indecl. + gen. sg. f. | in that context; of the *Advaita-Siddhi* |
| dvaitamithyātvasiddhipūrvakatvāt | *dvaita-mithyātva-siddhi-pūrvakatva* | abl. sg. n. compound | because of being preceded by the proof of duality’s falsity |
| dvaitamithyātvam | *dvaita-mithyātva* | acc. sg. n. compound | the falsity of duality |
| eva | indecl. | particle | indeed; precisely |
| prathamam | *prathama* | acc. sg. n. adverbial use | first |
| upapādanīyam | √*pad* (upa + caus.) | gerundive, nom./acc. sg. n. | to be demonstrated |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *dvaitamithyātvasiddhipūrvakatvāt* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa-complex]: *dvaitasya mithyātvasya siddhiḥ pūrvakaṃ yasya tat*.
- *dvaitamithyātvam* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *dvaitasya mithyātvam*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- karman: *dvaitamithyātvam*
- hetu: *dvaitamithyātvasiddhipūrvakatvāt*

**Verbal modality:**
- *upapādanīyam*: gerundive / passive necessity

**Note:**
The first sentence is purely architectural. Madhusūdana marks the dependency-order of the entire treatise: no further Advaitin claim can stand until *mithyātva* has been secured.

### Prathama-pariccheda, Section 1b

**Sanskrit (IAST):**

- karaṇa: *svapakṣasādhanaparapakṣanirākaraṇābhyām*

### Prathama-pariccheda, Section 1c

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *tatra ca vipratipattijanyasaṃśayasya vicārāṅgatvān madhyasthenādau vipratipattiḥ pradarśanīyā ।*

**English (line-by-line):**
> And here, because a doubt generated by contradictory views is an element of inquiry,

- *vicārāṅgatvān* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *vicārasya aṅgatvāt*.

- kartṛ (gerundive construction): *vipratipattiḥ*
- karaṇa: *madhyasthena*

### Prathama-pariccheda, Section 1d

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *yadyapi vipratipattijanyasaṃśayasya na pakṣatāsampādakatayopayogaḥ, siṣādhayiṣāvirahasahakṛtasādhakamānābhāvarūpāyās tasyāḥ saṃśayāghaṭitatvāt ।*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Although the doubt produced by contradictory views is of no use for producing *pakṣatā* (the status of being the inferential subject),

**Note:**
The sentence turns on late-logical definitions of *pakṣatā*. Madhusūdana’s point is negative: inquiry-doubt and inferential subjecthood are not identical categories.

### Prathama-pariccheda, Section 1e

**Sanskrit (IAST):**

## Editorial closing

This passage settles the opening order of business. It establishes that the *Advaita-Siddhi* is, from the first, a work of controlled dialectic: one must first state the opposed thesis, then generate the right kind of doubt, and only then move to proof and refutation. It also fixes the first great thematic burden of the work, namely the establishment of *dvaita-mithyātva*. What it does not yet settle is which precise definition of *mithyātva* will bear the weight. That question is taken up immediately in the later sections of the same *prathama-pariccheda*, especially the successive treatments of the first through fifth *mithyātva-lakṣaṇas*. The broader epistemic background reappears in the *Siddhānta-Bindu* upodghāta, where the *mahāvākya* is defended as the decisive *pramāṇa* for *ātmatattva*; the practical union of contemplative and polemical labor reappears, under a different affective mode, in the *Bhakti-Rasāyana*.
