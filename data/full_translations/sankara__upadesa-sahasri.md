## Editorial introduction

These two clusters state the doctrinal center of the metrical *Upadeśa-Sāhasrī* with unusual compression. UpS I.11.14-16 closes the *Ikṣitṛtva-prakaraṇa* by separating the action-bearing empirical self from the self disclosed by *neti neti* ("not this, not this"). UpS I.17.4-9 opens the *Samyaṅmati-prakaraṇa* by redefining all genuinely final gain as *ātma-lābha* (attainment of the self), by naming *anyadṛṣṭi* (the seeing of something as other) as *avidyā* (nescience), and by arguing that the Veda's single purport is *jñāna* (liberating knowledge), not ritual plurality. Taken together, the verses give Śaṅkara's independent, non-commentarial statement of the *jñāna-karma-vibhāga*: action has a preparatory place, but liberation itself is not a produced result and therefore cannot be the direct fruit of action.

The scope here is deliberately paired rather than continuous. The stored load-bearing passages for this work fall in two separate prakaraṇas, 11 and 17. A mechanically continuous span from chapter 11 through chapter 17 would exceed the project's verse ceiling and would bury the decisive doctrinal claims under five intervening topical units. The present file therefore takes the smallest bounded argument-clusters that contain every key passage while still preserving local intelligibility: the renunciatory conclusion of chapter 11 and the epistemic-exegetical opening of chapter 17.

Within the larger architecture of the work, these verses anticipate the fuller *mahāvākya* treatment of UpS I.18 and the explicit teacher-student unfolding of *abheda-pratipatti* (cognition of non-difference) in UpS II.1.26-31 and II.1.37-38. They also show how Śaṅkara's broader position hangs together: the self is not made by practice, action, or ritual production; it is disclosed through Vedic instruction whose terminus is sentence-meaning, not sacrificial performance.

## Translation

### UpS I.11.14

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *dehaliṅgātmanā kāryā vāsanārūpiṇā kriyā*  
> *netinetyātmarūpatvān na me kāryā kriyā kvacit*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Action is to be done by one whose self is [taken to be] body and subtle body, by one constituted by latent dispositions.  
> But because my nature is the self taught by "not this, not this," no action at all is to be done by me.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| dehaliṅgātmanā | deha-liṅga-ātman | `-nā`; instr sg masc, bahuvrīhi used substantively | by one whose self is [identified with] body and subtle body |
| kāryā | √kṛ | gerundive fem nom sg | to be done |
| vāsanārūpiṇā | vāsanā-rūpin | `-ṇā`; instr sg masc | by one constituted by latent dispositions |
| kriyā | kriyā | `-ā`; nom sg fem | action |
| neti | neti | indecl | "not this" |
| iti | iti | indecl | thus; quotation marker |
| ātmarūpatvāt | ātma-rūpatva | `-āt`; abl sg neut | because of being of the nature of the self |
| na | na | indecl | not |
| me | asmad | `-e`; gen/dat sg | for me |
| kāryā | √kṛ | gerundive fem nom sg | to be done |
| kriyā | kriyā | `-ā`; nom sg fem | action |
| kvacit | kvacit | indecl | anywhere; ever |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *dehaliṅgātmanā* [bahuvrīhi]: *dehaś ca liṅgaṃ ca ātmā yasya saḥ*.
- *vāsanārūpiṇā* [karmadhāraya]: *vāsanā eva rūpaṃ yasya saḥ*.
- *neti-nety-ātma-rūpatva* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *"neti neti" ity uktasyātmanaḥ rūpatvaṃ yasya tasya bhāvaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ (adhikārin): *dehaliṅgātmanā*, *vāsanārūpiṇā*
- viṣaya of obligation: *kriyā*
- sambandha: *me*

**Verbal modality:**
- *kāryā* (both occurrences): kṛtya-gerundive / passive sense / obligation

**Note:**
Śaṅkara distinguishes two standpoints with grammatical economy. The first hemistich speaks from the side of the embodied doer, still structured by *vāsanā*; the second speaks from the side of the self disclosed by negation. The verse therefore prepares 11.15's explicit claim that action cannot be a co-cause of liberation.

### UpS I.11.15

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *na tato 'mṛtatāśāsti karmaṇo 'jñānahetutaḥ*  
> *mokṣasya jñānahetutvān na tad anyad apekṣate*

**English (line-by-line):**
> From that there is no hope of immortality, because action has ignorance as its cause.  
> Since liberation has knowledge as its cause, it does not depend on anything else.

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| na | na | indecl | not |
| tataḥ | tad | `-tas`; abl sg neut used adverbially | from that |

| ajñānahetutaḥ | ajñāna-hetutā | ablative adverbial formation | because it has ignorance as its cause |
| mokṣasya | mokṣa | `-sya`; gen sg masc | of liberation |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *amṛtatāśā* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *amṛtatāyā āśā*.
- *ajñānahetutaḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa, abstract adverbial]: *ajñānaṃ hetur yasya tad-bhāvāt*.
- *jñānahetutvāt* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *jñānaṃ hetur yasya tad-bhāvāt*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- apādāna: *tataḥ*, *karmaṇaḥ*
- hetu: *ajñānahetutaḥ*, *jñānahetutvāt*
- karman: *tat anyat*

**Verbal modality:**

**Note:**
This is Śaṅkara's most compact metrical rejection of *jñāna-karma-samuccaya*. The paired ablatives do the philosophical work: action belongs to the side of ignorance-caused production, whereas liberation belongs to the side of knowledge-caused disclosure. Compare the closely parallel restatement at UpS I.17.7-9 and the prose development at UpS II.1.26-31.

### UpS I.11.16

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *amṛtaṃ cābhayaṃ nārtaṃ netītyātmā priyo mama*  
> *viparītam ato 'nyad yat tyajet tat sakriyaṃ tataḥ*

**English (line-by-line):**
> The self, taught as "not this, not this," is immortal, fearless, untroubled, and dear to me.  
> Therefore, whatever else is contrary to that, one should abandon, together with action.

**Kāraka structure:**

Compare UpS I.16.43: *tyajet tasmāt kriyāḥ sarvāḥ*.

### UpS I.17.4

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *ātmalābhāt paro nānyo lābhaḥ kaścana vidyate*  
> *yadarthā vedavādāś ca smārtāś cāpi tu yāḥ kriyāḥ*

- *vedavādāḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *vedasya vādāḥ; veda-vādena vihitāḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- apādāna (comparison): *ātmalābhāt*

The opening move is axial: ritual plurality is not denied, but subordinated. The relative clause makes Vedic and Smārta performance teleological, not ultimate. Śaṅkara will later restate the same priority in prose by making *ātmaikatva*-texts, not rites, the determinative means of liberation (UpS II.1.37-38).

### UpS I.17.5

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *ātmārtho 'pi hi yo lābhaḥ sukhāyeṣṭo viparyayaḥ*

### UpS I.17.6

**Note:**
The argument now turns phenomenological. What is truly the self cannot be gained as an external acquisition, because it is never literally elsewhere. External gain therefore presupposes *anyadṛṣṭi* before verse 7 explicitly names such other-seeing as *avidyā*.

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *jñānaikārthaparatvāt* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa chain]: *jñānasya ekaḥ arthaḥ yasya tasya paratvāt*.
- *vākyārthapratipattitaḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *vākyasya arthasya pratipatteḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- karman: *tam vākyam ekam*, *ekatvam*
- sambandha: *ātmanaḥ*
- hetu/apādāna: *jñānaikārthaparatvāt*, *vākyārthapratipattitaḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *viduḥ*: laṭ / parasmaipada / active
- *jñeyam*: kṛtya-gerundive / passive sense / obligation-to-be-known

**Note:**
The pronoun *taṃ vākyam* is best read of the Upaniṣadic identity-sentence, pre-eminently *tat tvam asi*, taken as a semantically unified statement rather than as a ritual or descriptive assemblage. This thesis is carried forward at once in UpS I.18, and in prose form at UpS II.1.28 and II.1.37-38, where Śaṅkara makes *abheda-pratipatti* the axis of liberation.

---

## Editorial closing

These verses settle three points. First, liberation is not a produced result; it is therefore not the direct fruit of action. Second, the cognition that binds is not action as such but *anyadṛṣṭi*, the taking of otherness as determinative of the self; hence only knowledge can remove it. Third, the Veda's authority in this domain is not primarily injunctive but revelatory: its single purport is the sentence-meaning by which the self's oneness is known.

What remains open here is the pedagogy by which that sentence-meaning becomes firm. Śaṅkara takes that up immediately in UpS I.18, where *mahāvākya* interpretation is widened, and again in UpS II.1.26-31 and II.1.37-38, where the teacher explicitly opposes difference-cognition and unfolds the *ātmaikatva* texts. The same grammar of negation and identity governs his *Bṛhadāraṇyaka-Bhāṣya* on *neti neti* (2.3.6) and his *Chāndogya-Bhāṣya* on *tat tvam asi* (6.8.7). What this passage leaves open, later Śaṅkara does not reverse; he amplifies it.
