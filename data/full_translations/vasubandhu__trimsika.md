# Triṃśikā — Extended Translation
**By:** Vasubandhu  
**Locus scope:** The entire *Triṃśikā* (all thirty kārikās), taken as a single natural unit because the work is itself short and architectonically complete: verses 1–17 set out the threefold *vijñāna-pariṇāma* analysis, verses 18–25 map that analysis into *trisvabhāva* and *tri-niḥsvabhāvatā*, and verses 26–30 turn to the path and *āśraya-parāvṛtti*.

## Editorial introduction

The *Triṃśikā* is one of the rare texts in Indian philosophy whose brevity is not a concession but a method. In thirty verses Vasubandhu compresses the mature Yogācāra account of experience into a sequence that is at once descriptive, polemical, and soteriological. The opening movement, verses 1–17, does not begin by denying the world. It begins more carefully. The language of self and thing, subject and object, is first relocated within *vijñāna-pariṇāma*, the transformation of cognition itself. That transformation is then differentiated into *ālaya-vijñāna* (the continuity-bearing seed-bed), *manas* (the self-appropriating reflex), and the six manifest consciousnesses. Verses 18–25 then give the philosophical compression for which the text became canonical: *parikalpita* (the imagined subject-object duality), *paratantra* (the dependently arisen cognitive flow), and *pariniṣpanna* (that very flow devoid of the imagined duality). The doctrine of the three non-natures is added precisely to prevent the three natures from hardening into three substances. Verses 26–30 then state the contemplative consequence. So long as cognition still places an object before itself, even the thought "this is only *vijñapti*" has not yet reached non-dual stability. The path culminates only in *anupalambha*, *lokottara-jñāna*, and the transformation of the basis.

The whole work is therefore the natural scope. Any narrower extract would break the text at exactly the wrong place. Verses 1–17 without 18–25 would leave the cognitive analysis without its metaphysical clarification. Verses 18–25 without 26–30 would leave the doctrine without its point. Within Vasubandhu's own corpus, the *Triṃśikā* stands between the more defensive *Viṃśatikā* and the yet more compressed *Trisvabhāva-Nirdeśa*. It is the load-bearing middle statement: less polemical than the former, less skeletal than the latter, and thus the best single locus for the mature Yogācāra position.

## Translation

### Verse 1

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *ātmadharmopacāro hi vividho yaḥ pravartate / vijñānapariṇāme 'sau pariṇāmaḥ sa ca tridhā //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> The manifold attribution of self and of *dharma*s that goes on  
> takes place in the transformation of cognition; and that transformation is threefold.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| ātmadharmopacāraḥ | *ātman* + *dharma* + *upacāra* | masculine nominative singular compound | the imputation / attribution of self and dharmas |
| hi | indeclinable | avyaya | indeed; for |
| vividhaḥ | *vi-vidha* | masculine nominative singular adjective | manifold; various |
| yaḥ | pronoun *yat* | masculine nominative singular relative | which |
| pravartate | pra-√*vṛt* | laṭ, 3rd singular, ātmanepada | proceeds; goes on |
| vijñānapariṇāme | *vijñāna* + *pariṇāma* | masculine locative singular compound | in the transformation of cognition |
| asau | pronoun | masculine nominative singular | that |
| pariṇāmaḥ | *pariṇāma* | masculine nominative singular | transformation |
| sa | pronoun | masculine nominative singular | that |
| ca | indeclinable | avyaya | and |
| tridhā | indeclinable | adverb | in three ways; threefoldly |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *ātmadharmopacāraḥ* [itaretara-dvandva + ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *ātmano dharmāṇāṃ copacāraḥ*.
- *vijñānapariṇāme* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *vijñānasya pariṇāme*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *ātmadharmopacāraḥ*
- adhikaraṇa: *vijñānapariṇāme*

**Verbal modality:**
- *pravartate*: laṭ / ātmanepada / active-middle intransitive usage

**Note:**
The first move is methodological. Vasubandhu does not yet argue that subject and object are unreal in every sense. He argues first that self-talk and thing-talk operate within a transformation of cognition. This opening compression is unpacked doctrinally in *Viṃśatikā* 1–22 and schematically refined in *Trisvabhāva-Nirdeśa* 1–5.

### Verse 2

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *vipāko mananākhyaśca vijñaptirviṣayasya ca / tatrālayākhyaṃ vijñānaṃ vipākaḥ sarvabījakam //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> [That transformation is] maturation, that called *manana* (mentation), and the presentation of the object.  
> Of these, the cognition called *ālaya* is maturation, and it is possessed of all seeds.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| vipākaḥ | *vipāka* | masculine nominative singular | maturation; resultative ripening |
| mananākhyaḥ | *manana* + *ākhya* | masculine nominative singular compound adjective | called mentation |
| ca | indeclinable | avyaya | and |
| vijñaptiḥ | *vijñapti* | feminine nominative singular | presentation; cognitive manifestation |
| viṣayasya | *viṣaya* | masculine genitive singular | of the object |
| ca | indeclinable | avyaya | and |
| tatra | indeclinable | avyaya | among these; there |
| ālayākhyam | *ālaya* + *ākhya* | neuter nominative singular compound adjective | called ālaya |
| vijñānam | *vijñāna* | neuter nominative singular | cognition; consciousness |
| vipākaḥ | *vipāka* | masculine nominative singular used predicatively | maturation |
| sarvabījakam | *sarva* + *bījaka* | neuter nominative singular compound adjective | containing all seeds |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *mananākhyaḥ* [karmadhāraya]: *mananaṃ nāma yasya saḥ*.
- *ālayākhyam* [karmadhāraya]: *ālaya iti ākhyam*.
- *sarvabījakam* [bahuvrīhi-like possessive]: *sarvāṇi bījāni yasmin tat*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- viśeṣya-list: *vipākaḥ*, *mananākhyaḥ*, *vijñaptiḥ*
- genitive relation: *viṣayasya*
- identification: *ālayākhyaṃ vijñānam* = *vipākaḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- No explicit finite verb.
- Nominal predication is supplied: “[it is] maturation ...”

**Note:**
Verse 2 distributes the threefold transformation announced in verse 1. The first is *ālaya-vijñāna*, the continuity-bearing maturation of karmic seeds; the second is reflexive *manas*; the third is manifest object-presentation. Compare the thinker record's summary of *Triṃśikā* 1–17 and the glossary entry on *ālaya-vijñāna*.

### Verse 3

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *asaṃviditakopādisthānavijñaptikaṃ ca tat / sadā sparśamanaskāravitsañjñācetanānvitam //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> And that is such that its appropriation and its presentation of location are not distinctly cognized.  
> It is always conjoined with contact, attention, awareness, apperception, and volition.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| asaṃviditakopādisthānavijñaptikam | *a-saṃvidita* + *ka-upādi* + *sthāna-vijñaptika* | neuter nominative singular compound | having uncognized appropriation and location-presentation |
| ca | indeclinable | avyaya | and |
| tat | pronoun | neuter nominative singular | that |
| sadā | indeclinable | avyaya | always |
| sparśa | *sparśa* | masculine stem in compound/list form | contact |
| manaskāra | *manaskāra* | masculine stem in compound/list form | attention |
| vit | *vid* / *vit* | masculine stem in list form | awareness; cognition |
| sañjñā | *saṃjñā* | feminine stem in list form | apperception; recognition |
| cetanā | *cetanā* | feminine stem in list form | volition |
| anvitam | anu-√*i* > *anvita* | neuter nominative singular past participle | conjoined with; accompanied by |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *asaṃviditakopādisthānavijñaptikam* [multi-member tatpuruṣa]: *asaṃviditā copādiḥ asaṃviditā ca sthānavijñaptiḥ yasya tat*.
- *sthānavijñaptiḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *sthānasya vijñaptiḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- viśeṣya: *tat*
- sahārtha / accompaniment: *sparśa-manaskāra-vit-sañjñā-cetanā-anvitam*

**Verbal modality:**
- No explicit finite verb.
- Predicative nominal structure continues verse 2.

**Note:**
The digital witness preserves the difficult string *kopādi*; Sthiramati's bhāṣya glosses it as *upādi*, appropriation or clinging-basis, and that sense is required by the doctrine. The point is that *ālaya-vijñāna* does not present its support and world-location as explicitly discriminated objects. It underlies them more obscurely.

### Verse 4

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *upekṣā vedanā tatrānivṛtāvyākṛtaṃ ca tat / tathā sparśādayastacca vartate srotasaughavat //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> There, the feeling is equanimous; and that is unobscured and ethically indeterminate.  
> So too are contact and the rest; and that proceeds like the rush of a stream.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| upekṣā | *upekṣā* | feminine nominative singular | equanimity; neutral feeling |
| vedanā | *vedanā* | feminine nominative singular | feeling |
| tatra | indeclinable | avyaya | there; in that |
| anivṛtāvyākṛtam | *anivṛta* + *avyākṛta* | neuter nominative singular compound | unobscured and indeterminate |
| ca | indeclinable | avyaya | and |
| tat | pronoun | neuter nominative singular | that |
| tathā | indeclinable | avyaya | likewise |
| sparśādayaḥ | *sparśa* + *ādi* | masculine nominative plural compound | contact and the rest |
| tat | pronoun | neuter nominative singular | that |
| ca | indeclinable | avyaya | and |
| vartate | √*vṛt* | laṭ, 3rd singular, ātmanepada | proceeds; flows on |
| srotasaughavat | *srotas* + *augha* + *vat* | indeclinable/adv. comparative form | like a stream-current |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *anivṛtāvyākṛtam* [karmadhāraya]: *anivṛtaṃ cāvyākṛtaṃ ca*.
- *sparśādayaḥ* [elliptic *ādi*-compound]: *sparśa-prabhṛtayaḥ*.
- *srotasaughavat* [upamāna-compound]: *srotasa ogha iva*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- adhikaraṇa: *tatra*
- kartṛ: *tat*
- upamāna: *srotasaughavat*

**Verbal modality:**
- *vartate*: laṭ / ātmanepada / intransitive

**Note:**
The ethical point is exact. *Ālaya-vijñāna* is neither wholesome nor unwholesome; it is *avyākṛta*. But it is also *anivṛta*: not morally obscuring in the way afflicted mentation is. The stream image becomes decisive later in Yogācāra explanations of continuity without substance.

### Verse 5

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *tasya vyāvṛtirarhatve tadāśritya pravartate / tadālambaṃ manonāma vijñānaṃ mananātmakam //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Its cessation occurs in arhatship.  
> Depending on that, and taking that as its object, there proceeds the cognition called *manas*, whose nature is mentation.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| tasya | pronoun | genitive singular | of it; of that |
| vyāvṛttiḥ | *vyāvṛtti* | feminine nominative singular | cessation; withdrawal |
| arhatve | *arhatva* | neuter locative singular | in arhatship |
| tat-āśritya | pronoun + ā-√*śri* | absolutive compound | depending on that |
| pravartate | pra-√*vṛt* | laṭ, 3rd singular, ātmanepada | proceeds; operates |
| tat-ālambam | pronoun + *ālambana* | neuter nominative singular compound adjective | taking that as object-support |
| manaḥ-nāma | *manas* + *nāman* | neuter nominative singular compound | called manas |
| vijñānam | *vijñāna* | neuter nominative singular | cognition |
| mananātmakam | *manana* + *ātmaka* | neuter nominative singular compound | of the nature of mentation |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *tadāśritya* [absolutive compound]: *tat āśritya*.
- *tadālambam* [dvitīyā-tatpuruṣa]: *tat ālambanam yasya tat*.
- *mananātmakam* [karmadhāraya]: *mananaṃ svabhāvo yasya tat*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- sambandha: *tasya*
- adhikaraṇa: *arhatve*
- āśraya: *tat* (in *tadāśritya*)
- ālambana: *tat* (in *tadālambam*)

**Verbal modality:**
- *pravartate*: laṭ / ātmanepada / active-middle intransitive

**Note:**
The referent of both *tat*-forms is *ālaya-vijñāna*. The seventh consciousness depends on the *ālaya* and appropriates it as its object. Later Yogācāra scholasticism will make this relation central to the account of ego-function.

### Verse 6

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *kleśaiścaturbhiḥ sahitaṃ nivṛtāvyākṛtaiḥ sadā / ātmaduṣṭyātmamohātmamānātmasnehasañjñitaiḥ //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> It is always accompanied by four afflictions, obscuring yet ethically indeterminate,  
> namely those called self-view, self-delusion, self-conceit, and self-attachment.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| kleśaiḥ | *kleśa* | masculine instrumental plural | by / with afflictions |
| caturbhiḥ | numeral *catur* | instrumental plural | four |
| sahitam | *sahita* | neuter nominative singular past participle | accompanied by |
| nivṛtāvyākṛtaiḥ | *nivṛta* + *avyākṛta* | instrumental plural compound adjective | obscuring and ethically indeterminate |
| sadā | indeclinable | avyaya | always |
| ātmaduṣṭi | *ātman* + *duṣṭi* | feminine stem in compound/list form | distorted self-view |
| ātmamoha | *ātman* + *moha* | masculine stem in list form | delusion concerning self |
| ātmamāna | *ātman* + *māna* | masculine stem in list form | self-conceit |
| ātmasneha | *ātman* + *sneha* | masculine stem in list form | self-attachment; self-love |
| sañjñitaiḥ | saṃ-√*jñā* > *sañjñita* | instrumental plural past participle | called; designated |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *nivṛtāvyākṛtaiḥ* [karmadhāraya]: *nivṛtaiś cāvyākṛtaiś ca*.
- *ātmaduṣṭi* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *ātmanaḥ duṣṭiḥ*.
- *ātmamoha*, *ātmamāna*, *ātmasneha* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *ātmanaḥ mohaḥ*, etc.

**Kāraka structure:**
- sahārtha / accompaniment: *kleśaiś caturbhiḥ ... sañjñitaiḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- No explicit finite verb.
- Predicative nominal continuation from verse 5.

**Note:**
The transmitted reading *ātmaduṣṭi* is doctrinally equivalent here to the more familiar "self-view." The point is that *manas* is not a neutral reflexivity. It is structurally afflicted reflexivity. In later East Asian reception this verse becomes one of the classic warrants for the "defiled mind" (*kliṣṭa-manas*).

### Verse 7

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *yatrajastanmayairanyaiḥ sparśādyaiścārhato na tat / na nirodhasamāpattau mārge lokottare na ca //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Together with the other factors, such as contact, that are born where it is born and are of its own kind, that is not present in the arhat.  
> Nor is it present in the attainment of cessation; nor on the supramundane path.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| yatrajais | *yatra-ja* | instrumental plural compound adjective | with those born where it is born |
| tanmayaiḥ | *tan-maya* | instrumental plural compound adjective | with those of its own nature |
| anyaiḥ | *anya* | instrumental plural | other |
| sparśādyaiḥ | *sparśa* + *ādi* | instrumental plural compound | with contact and the rest |
| ca | indeclinable | avyaya | and |
| arhataḥ | *arhat* | masculine genitive singular / in sense "in the arhat" | of the arhat; in the arhat-state |
| na | indeclinable | avyaya | not |
| tat | pronoun | neuter nominative singular | that |
| na | indeclinable | avyaya | not |
| nirodhasamāpattau | *nirodha* + *samāpatti* | feminine locative singular compound | in the attainment of cessation |
| mārge | *mārga* | masculine locative singular | on the path |
| lokottare | *lokottara* | masculine locative singular adjective | supramundane |
| na | indeclinable | avyaya | not |
| ca | indeclinable | avyaya | and |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *yatrajaiḥ* [avyayībhāva-like first member + kṛdanta]: *yatra jāyante taiḥ*.
- *tanmayaiḥ* [karmadhāraya]: *tanmayaḥ* = *tasyaiva svabhāvāḥ*.
- *nirodhasamāpattau* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *nirodhasya samāpattau*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- sahārtha: *yatrajais tanmayair anyaiḥ sparśādyaiḥ*
- adhikaraṇa: *nirodhasamāpattau*, *mārge lokottare*

**Verbal modality:**
- No explicit finite verb.
- Negated existential predication: *na tat* = “that is not [there].”

**Note:**
The verse marks where afflicted *manas* cannot persist. That matters philosophically because Yogācāra does not treat the ego-function as metaphysically necessary. It is removable. Compare the path-verses 26–30, where the removal of dual appropriation is restated contemplatively.

### Verse 8

| kuśala | *kuśala* | adjective/list form | wholesome |

**Verbal modality:**
- No explicit finite verb.

**Note:**
Verse 21 defines both *paratantra* and *pariniṣpanna* in one movement. The perfected is not a second world added to the dependent. It is the dependent insofar as it is devoid of the imagined duality. This relation is restated in *Trisvabhāva-Nirdeśa* 1–5 with even greater compression.

## Editorial closing

What this passage settles is the internal architecture of mature Yogācāra. The text has now stated, in compressed but explicit form, the three transformations of cognition, the three natures, the three non-natures, and the transition from dualized apprehension to *āśraya-parāvṛtti*. It has therefore fixed the minimum conceptual grammar without which later Yogācāra debates become unreadable. What it leaves open is equally important. The kārikā does not here answer, in detail, the realist challenge about spatial regularity, intersubjective convergence, and practical efficacy; those burdens fall more squarely on *Viṃśatikā* 1–22. Nor does it fully elaborate the contemplative phenomenology of the path; later scholastic exegesis, including Sthiramati on verses 26–30, does that work. Within Vasubandhu's own corpus, the next immediate pickup-point is *Trisvabhāva-Nirdeśa* 1–38, where the same architecture is restated with even greater ontological precision. Outside Yogācāra, the principal later pressure-points are Bhāviveka's *Madhyamaka-Hṛdaya* chapter 5, *Brahma-Sūtra-Bhāṣya* 2.2.28–32, and Yāmuna's *Saṃvit-Siddhi*, all of which seize on this very compression as the canonical statement to be answered.
