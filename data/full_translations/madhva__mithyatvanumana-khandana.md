## Editorial introduction

This tract is one page long in the collected witness, but it is not a mere scrap. It is the compressed formal core of Madhva's attack on the Advaitin *anumāna* (inference) for the world's *mithyātva* (illusoriness, falsity in the technical Advaitin sense). The target is the familiar pattern, later stabilized in scholastic form as *vimataṃ mithyā dṛśyatvāt śuktirajatavat*: "the disputed entity is mithyā, because it is perceived, like shell-silver." What Madhva does here is not yet the long *Navya-Nyāya* elaboration of Vyāsatīrtha; it is the prior diagnostic grid. He runs through the stock inferential defects one after another: *āśrayāsiddhi* (failure of a subject because its substrate is not established), *aprasiddha-viśeṣaṇa* (an unestablished qualifier), *siddhasādhana* (proving what is already granted), *asiddha-hetu* (an unestablished reason), *viruddha* (a contradictory reason), *anekāntika* (an inconclusive reason), *kālātyayāpadiṣṭa* (a reason contradicted by later knowledge), and so on. The tract then turns, with studied economy, to definitions of *pakṣa*, *sapakṣa*, *vipakṣa*, *pratijñā*, *hetu*, and *dṛṣṭānta*. The movement matters. Madhva is not only saying that the Advaitin's inference fails; he is reclaiming the grammar of inference itself.

The natural unit here is therefore the whole tract. Anything shorter would miss the way the text moves from polemical diagnosis to elementary logical definition. That structure also explains its place within Madhva's larger anti-Advaita triad: the *Māyāvāda-Khaṇḍana* attacks the ontological category; the *Upādhi-Khaṇḍana* attacks the mediating device; this work attacks the formal proof. Later Dvaita scholasticism, especially Vyāsatīrtha's *Nyāyāmṛta* and *Tarka-tāṇḍava*, presupposes precisely this compressed map.

## Translation

### p. 239, clause 1

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *vimataṃ mithyā dṛśyatvād ity atra tat tathā yathā saṃpratipannam*

**English (line-by-line):**
> In the inference "the disputed thing is *mithyā* because it is perceived,"  
> the subject is [to be taken as] that thing exactly as it is apprehended in that way.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| vimatam | vimata | nom/acc sg neut | the disputed thing, the item under contention |
| mithyā | mithyā | indecl / predicative adverbial form | as false, as illusory |
| dṛśyatvāt | dṛśyatva | `-āt`; abl sg neut | because of being perceived |
| iti | iti | indecl | thus |
| atra | atra | indecl | here, in this case |
| tat | tad | nom/acc sg neut | that |
| tathā | tathā | indecl | thus, in that manner |
| yathā | yathā | indecl | as |
| saṃpratipannam | saṃpratipanna | nom/acc sg neut past passive participle | apprehended, understood, accepted |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *dṛśyatva* [taddhita abstract]: *dṛśyasya bhāvaḥ*.
- *saṃpratipannam* is a simple participial form, not a samāsa.

**Kāraka structure:**
- pakṣa-nirdeśa: *vimatam*
- sādhya-predicate: *mithyā*
- hetu: *dṛśyatvāt*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb is expressed; the clause is inferentially elliptical.

**Note:**
The opening clause gives the inferential shell only. Madhva immediately fastens on the packing of the *pakṣa*: what exactly is "the disputed thing" as qualified in the inference? That is why the next clauses move at once to defects attaching not only to the *hetu* but to the very statement of the subject.

### p. 239, clause 2

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *ity ukte jagato 'bhāvād āśrayāsiddhaḥ pakṣaḥ*

**English (line-by-line):**
> If it is stated thus, then because the world would be absent,  
> the subject is one whose substrate is unestablished.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| iti | iti | indecl | thus |
| ukte | vac | loc sg neut past passive participle | when stated |
| jagataḥ | jagat | `-aḥ`; gen sg neut | of the world |
| abhāvāt | abhāva | `-āt`; abl sg masc | because of absence |
| āśrayāsiddhaḥ | āśraya + asiddha | nom sg masc | unestablished in respect of its substrate |
| pakṣaḥ | pakṣa | `-ḥ`; nom sg masc | the subject, inferential locus |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *āśrayāsiddhaḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *āśrayasya asiddhaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- hetu/apādāna: *jagato 'bhāvāt*
- viśeṣya: *pakṣaḥ*
- predicate: *āśrayāsiddhaḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *ukte*: past passive participle, locative absolute force.

**Note:**
The point is polemical and logical together. If the *pakṣa* is stated in a way that already presupposes the world's non-establishment, the inferential locus collapses before the *hetu* even begins to work. Madhva is pressing the standard Nyāya complaint that one cannot infer over a subject whose own footing has been undercut.

### p. 239, clause 3

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *anirvacanīyāsiddher aprasiddhaviśeṣaṇaḥ*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Because the indeterminable has not been established,  
> it has an unestablished qualifier.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| anirvacanīya | an-ir-vacanīya | adjective stem | indeterminable, not definitively characterizable |
| asiddheḥ | asiddhi / asiddha | `-eḥ`; abl/gen sg fem | because of non-establishment |
| aprasiddhaviśeṣaṇaḥ | aprasiddha + viśeṣaṇa | nom sg masc | possessing an unestablished qualifier |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *anirvacanīya* [upapada/kṛdanta]: *na nirvaktum śakyate iti*.
- *aprasiddhaviśeṣaṇaḥ* [karmadhāraya/tatpuruṣa]: *aprasiddhaṃ viśeṣaṇam yasya saḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- hetu: *anirvacanīyāsiddheḥ*
- predicate: *aprasiddhaviśeṣaṇaḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- Nominal sentence; no finite verb is expressed.

**Note:**
This is one of Madhva's most consistent complaints against Advaita: *anirvacanīya* is used as though it were a functioning qualifier, while its own status remains unproved. Compare the more expansive anti-*anirvacanīya* pressure in the *Māyāvāda-Khaṇḍana* and in *Anuvyākhyāna* 1.1.29-30.

### p. 239, clause 4

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *sadasadvilakṣaṇye mithyātve siddhasādhanatā*

**English (line-by-line):**
> If *mithyātva* is taken as being different from the real and the unreal,  
> there is the fault of proving what is already granted.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| sat | sat | nominal stem | the real |
| asat | asat | nominal stem | the unreal |
| vilakṣaṇye | vilakṣaṇya | loc sg neut | in being different from |
| mithyātve | mithyātva | loc sg neut | in mithyātva, in illusoriness |
| siddhasādhanatā | siddha + sādhana + tā | nom sg fem | the fault of proving an already established thing |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *sadasadvilakṣaṇya* [dvandva-tatpuruṣa]: *sataś cāsataś ca vilakṣaṇyam*.
- *siddhasādhanatā* [tatpuruṣa]: *siddhasya sādhanatā*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- adhikaraṇa: *sadasadvilakṣaṇye mithyātve*
- predicate: *siddhasādhanatā*

**Verbal modality:**
- Nominal sentence; no finite verb is expressed.

**Note:**
Madhva's complaint is not that the Advaitin fails to define *mithyā* at all; it is that one line of definition simply repackages what the opponent is already asking to be proved. This is one of the formal points later exploited by Rāmānuja and by the post-Madhva Dvaitins against the *sadasadvilakṣaṇa* formula.

**English (line-by-line):**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|

| abhāvāt | abhāva | `-āt`; abl sg masc | because of absence |
| asiddhaḥ | asiddha | `-ḥ`; nom sg masc | unestablished |
| hetuḥ | hetu | `-ḥ`; nom sg masc | reason, inferential mark |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**

### p. 239, clause 6

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *anirvacanīyāsiddher eva sapakṣābhāvād viruddhaḥ*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Precisely because the indeterminable is not established, and because there is no positive similar instance,  
> the reason is contradictory.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| anirvacanīya | an-ir-vacanīya | adjective stem | indeterminable |
| asiddheḥ | asiddhi / asiddha | `-eḥ`; abl/gen sg fem | because of non-establishment |
| eva | eva | indecl | precisely, indeed |
| sapakṣa | sapakṣa | nominal stem | positive similar instance |
| abhāvāt | abhāva | `-āt`; abl sg masc | because of absence |
| viruddhaḥ | viruddha | `-ḥ`; nom sg masc | contradictory, opposed |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *sapakṣa* [upapada]: *samaḥ pakṣaḥ*; the case sharing the sādhya-relevant property.

**Kāraka structure:**
- hetu/apādāna: *anirvacanīyāsiddheḥ*, *sapakṣābhāvāt*
- predicate: *viruddhaḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- Nominal sentence.

**Note:**
The exact OCR of this line is imperfect; the normalization adopted here follows the internal logic of the sequence. Madhva's point is that once *anirvacanīya* itself is unestablished, the alleged analogue-class that should support the inference also disappears. The inferential mark then turns back against the claim it was meant to prove.

### p. 239, clause 7

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *asann api dṛśyatvād anekāntikaḥ*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Since even what is unreal may be perceived,  
> the reason is inconclusive.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| asan | asat | nom sg neut used substantively | the unreal |
| api | api | indecl | even |
| dṛśyatvāt | dṛśyatva | `-āt`; abl sg neut | because of being perceived |
| anekāntikaḥ | anekāntika | `-ḥ`; nom sg masc | inconclusive, not pervaded one-way |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *anekāntika* [bahuvrīhi-like technical term]: *na ekāntaḥ yasya saḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- concessive element: *asan api*
- hetu: *dṛśyatvāt*
- predicate: *anekāntikaḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- Nominal sentence.

**Note:**
This is the central pressure on *dṛśyatva* as *hetu*. Perceptibility ranges too widely. The very breadth that was supposed to make the sign powerful makes it unstable. Madhva's successors repeatedly sharpen this into the complaint of *vyabhicāra*.

### p. 239, clause 8

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *jagato 'bhāve 'numānasyāpy abhāva iti tarkabādhitatvenānabhyavasitaḥ*

**English (line-by-line):**
> If the world is absent, then the inference too would be absent;  
> thus, because it is overruled by reasoning, it is not determinately established.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| jagataḥ | jagat | `-aḥ`; gen sg neut | of the world |
| abhāve | abhāva | loc sg masc | in the absence |
| anumānasya | anumāna | `-sya`; gen sg neut | of the inference |
| api | api | indecl | also |
| abhāvaḥ | abhāva | `-ḥ`; nom sg masc | absence |
| iti | iti | indecl | thus |
| tarka | tarka | nominal stem | reasoning, reductio-style reasoning |
| bādhitatvena | bādhitatva | `-ena`; inst sg neut | by the state of being contradicted |
| anabhyavasitaḥ | an-abhyavasita | nom sg masc | indeterminate, not firmly settled |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *tarkabādhitatvena* [tatpuruṣa]: *tarkena bādhitatvena*.
- *anabhyavasitaḥ* [nañ-tatpuruṣa]: *na abhyavasitaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- locative condition: *jagato 'bhāve*
- subject in genitival relation: *anumānasya*
- instrument/hetu: *tarkabādhitatvena*

**Verbal modality:**
- Nominal sentence with an implied *bhavati* in the first half.

**Note:**
This is a reflexive objection. The inference depends on the epistemic and ontological standing of the very world whose standing it seeks to dissolve. If that standing is removed too early, the instrument removes itself with it.

### p. 239, clause 9

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *pratyakṣādiviruddhatvād idaṃ satyam ityādivākyaviruddhatvāc ca kālātyayāpadiṣṭaḥ*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Because it conflicts with perception and the other means of knowledge, and because it conflicts with scriptural statements such as "this is real,"  
> it is a reason stated after its proper time, one already defeated by later knowledge.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| pratyakṣa | pratyakṣa | nominal stem | perception |
| ādi | ādi | indecl/nominal tail | and the rest |
| viruddhatvāt | viruddhatva | `-āt`; abl sg neut | because of conflict |
| idam | idam | nom/acc sg neut | this |
| satyam | satya | nom/acc sg neut | real, true |
| iti | iti | indecl | thus |
| ādi | ādi | nominal tail | and so on |
| vākya | vākya | nominal stem | statement, sentence |
| viruddhatvāt | viruddhatva | `-āt`; abl sg neut | because of conflict |
| ca | ca | indecl | and |
| kālātyayāpadiṣṭaḥ | kālātyaya + apadiṣṭa | nom sg masc | stated after the lapse of time; a technical fallacy |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *pratyakṣādiviruddhatva* [tatpuruṣa]: *pratyakṣādibhiḥ viruddhatvam*.
- *ityādivākyaviruddhatva* [tatpuruṣa]: *ityādīni vākyāni viruddhatvam*.
- *kālātyayāpadiṣṭaḥ* [tatpuruṣa]: *kālātyaye apadiṣṭaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- hetu/apādāna: *pratyakṣādiviruddhatvāt*, *...vākya-viruddhatvāt*
- predicate: *kālātyayāpadiṣṭaḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- Nominal sentence.

**Note:**
The appeal to "*idaṃ satyam*" should not be read as a careless collapse of Madhva's realism into a single scriptural token. The force is cumulative: perception, other *pramāṇas*, and realist scriptural usage all stand against the inference. That is why the charge is not merely *viruddha* but *kālātyayāpadiṣṭa*.

### p. 239, clause 10

**Note:**

The point, however, is plain enough: one can spin the same inferential shell in the opposite direction, and when one does so, the original prestige of the shell evaporates.

### p. 239, clause 11

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *vimataṃ sat pramāṇadṛṣṭatvād ity atra tat tathā yathā sad iti prayogāt satyasādhanaḥ*

**English (line-by-line):**
> In the inference "the disputed thing is real because it is seen by a valid means of knowledge,"  
> if one formulates it as "that, exactly thus, is real," the inference proves reality.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| vimatam | vimata | nom/acc sg neut | the disputed thing |
| sat | sat | predicative nominal stem | real |
| pramāṇa | pramāṇa | nominal stem | valid means of knowledge |
| dṛṣṭatvāt | dṛṣṭatva | `-āt`; abl sg neut | because of being seen |
| iti | iti | indecl | thus |
| atra | atra | indecl | here |
| tat | tad | nom/acc sg neut | that |
| tathā | tathā | indecl | thus |
| yathā | yathā | indecl | as |
| sat | sat | predicative nominal stem | real |
| iti | iti | indecl | thus |
| prayogāt | prayoga | `-āt`; abl sg masc | from the formulation |
| satyasādhanaḥ | satya + sādhana | nom sg masc | a proving of reality |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *pramāṇadṛṣṭatva* [tatpuruṣa]: *pramāṇena dṛṣṭatvam*.
- *satyasādhanaḥ* [tatpuruṣa]: *satyasya sādhanam*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- pakṣa: *vimatam*
- sādhya: *sat*
- hetu: *pramāṇadṛṣṭatvāt*
- karaṇa/hetu: *prayogāt*

**Verbal modality:**
- Nominal sentence.

**Note:**
Madhva turns the inferential machine around. If the structure can, with equal formal ease, be made to prove *satya* instead of *mithyā*, then the supposed inevitability of the Advaitin's proof is gone. The move anticipates later Dvaita counter-inferences.

### p. 239, clause 14

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *pramāṇaviruddhatvam upādhiḥ*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Conflict with a valid means of knowledge  
> is an *upādhi* (a defeating condition).

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| pramāṇa | pramāṇa | nominal stem | valid means of knowledge |

### p. 239, closing verse

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *yo hy avyakta-sadānanda-nityavyakta-cidātmanā*  
> *nirdoṣākhila-kalyāṇa-guṇaṃ vande ramāpatim //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Him indeed whose conscious self is unmanifest bliss, yet eternally manifest consciousness,  
> him, flawless and possessed of every auspicious quality, I praise: the Lord of Ramā.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| yaḥ | yad | nom sg masc relative pronoun | who |
| hi | hi | indecl | indeed |
| avyakta | avyakta | adjective stem | unmanifest |
| sadānanda | sadā + ānanda | compound stem | perpetual bliss |
| nityavyakta | nitya + vyakta | compound stem | eternally manifest |
| cidātmanā | cidātman | `-ā`; inst sg masc | by/with the nature of consciousness-self |
| nirdoṣa | nirdoṣa | adjective stem | faultless |
| akhila | akhila | adjective stem | entire, all |
| kalyāṇa | kalyāṇa | adjective stem | auspicious |
| guṇam | guṇa | acc sg masc | quality |
| vande | vand | 1 sg laṭ ātmanepada | I praise, I bow to |
| ramāpatim | ramāpati | acc sg masc | the Lord of Ramā |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *sadānanda* [avyayībhāva-like lexical compound]: *sadā ānandaḥ*.
- *nityavyakta* [karmadhāraya]: *nityaṃ vyaktaḥ*.
- *cidātmanā* [tatpuruṣa]: *cita ātmā yasya tena* / *cid-rūpeṇātmanā*.
- *nirdoṣākhila-kalyāṇa-guṇam* [multi-member tatpuruṣa/bahuvrīhi sequence]: *nirdoṣaś cāsāv akhila-kalyāṇa-guṇaś ca*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- karman: *ramāpatim*
- karaṇa / manner: *cidātmanā*
- kartṛ: implicit first-person speaker in *vande*

**Verbal modality:**
- *vande*: laṭ / ātmanepada / active-reflexive devotional usage

**Note:**
This closing verse is the most textually uncertain point in the OCR witness; the normalization given here is provisional. The second hemistich is much clearer than the first. Thematically, however, the verse is wholly consistent with Madhva's manner: the logical demolition of a rival inference closes in a theologically dense *maṅgala* gesture.

---

## Editorial closing

This page settles one thing decisively: Madhva will not let the Advaitin move from appearance to unreality by formal momentum alone. The tract does not yet give the long metaphysical alternative; what it does is strip the adversary's proof of compulsion. That is why the ending matters. After the catalogue of defects comes a pocket logic-manual: *pakṣa*, *sapakṣa*, *vipakṣa*, *pratijñā*, *hetu*, *dṛṣṭānta*. The suggestion is unmistakable. The issue is not that inference as such is weak; the issue is that this inference is malformed.

What remains open here is the larger constructive account of appearance, error, and dependence. Madhva addresses those elsewhere: the ontological side in the *Māyāvāda-Khaṇḍana* and *Tattvodyota*; the problem of adjunct-mediated identity in the *Upādhi-Khaṇḍana*; the broader architecture of dependence and difference in *Anuvyākhyāna* 1.1 and 1.4. Later Dvaita scholasticism, especially Vyāsatīrtha's *Nyāyāmṛta* and *Tarka-tāṇḍava*, takes this page's compressed diagnostics and expands them into a full anti-Advaita inferential campaign. Madhusūdana's *Advaita-Siddhi* is, in one important respect, a reply to this very compression.
