# Nyāya-Sudhā-Pariṃala — Extended Translation
**By:** Rāghavendra Tīrtha  
**Locus scope:** Adhyāya 1 opening *maṅgalācaraṇa* (verses 1–6) and the first prose framing sentence. This restricted unit is chosen because the on-disk witness is an image-only scan; beyond this opening, the available OCR becomes too unstable for a non-conjectural line-by-line Pāṇinian extraction.  
**Source edition transcribed from:** *Nyāya Sudhā Parimala of Raghavendra Tirtha, Adhyaya 1 Pada 1-4*, ed. Kṛṣṇācārya T. R. Āpa Śaṅkara Rāmācārya, 1895; image-only Internet Archive scan, checked against Archive derivative OCR and a web-crawled secondary transcription of the same scan.  
**Translator:** Pāṇinian-rich extraction by Codex 5.4 (reasoning=high), 2026-05-10.

---

## Editorial introduction

This opening *maṅgalācaraṇa* does real doctrinal and architectural work. It is not merely ornamental. In six verses and one programmatic prose sentence, Rāghavendra places the *Nyāya-Sudhā-Pariṃala* within the full Madhva commentarial cascade that the work itself inhabits: Hari first, then Brahmā/Vāyu, then the third *aṃśa* of Vāyu, then Jayatīrtha, then Vyāsatīrtha, then the commentator’s own teacher, and only then the act of glossing Jayatīrtha’s *Nyāya-Sudhā*. The sequence announces in miniature the hierarchy of dependence that the body of the work will defend in much subtler *Navya-Nyāya* idiom. The opening prose sentence makes this explicit by classifying the first verse as a *maṅgala* in the form of salutation to a deity marked by the properties that ground worthiness-to-be-saluted, desirability, and authority.

The present scope is the natural unit available for responsible extraction from the materials on disk. The printed 1895 witness survives here only as an image-only scan; its derivative OCR is usable for the opening verses, especially once checked against the page image, but quickly becomes too corrupt for secure diplomatic recovery of the denser prose. Since this file is meant to serve as a primary-text destination rather than a conjectural paraphrase, it is better to translate the recoverable opening with full grammatical apparatus than to extend the scope by guesswork. Philosophically, the unit matters because it shows Rāghavendra’s broader position in compressed form: devotion and scholastic exactitude are continuous, lineage is epistemically load-bearing, and commentary begins only after one has fixed the superior object, the authorized teachers, and the modality of one’s own subordination.

## Translation

### Verse 1

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *pūrṇāgaṇya-guṇodāra-mūrtaye puṇya-kīrtaye | namaḥ śrī-pataye bhakta-datta-svānanda-mūrtaye || 1 ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Salutation to him whose form is exalted through full and innumerable qualities, whose fame is holy.  
> Salutation to the husband of Śrī, whose very form is self-bliss bestowed upon devotees.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| pūrṇāgaṇya-guṇodāra-mūrtaye | pūrṇāgaṇya-guṇodāra-mūrti | dat sg fem | to him whose form is noble through full and countless qualities |
| puṇya-kīrtaye | puṇya-kīrti | dat sg fem | to him of holy fame |
| namaḥ | namaḥ | indecl | salutation |
| śrī-pataye | śrī-pati | dat sg masc | to the husband of Śrī |
| bhakta-datta-svānanda-mūrtaye | bhakta-datta-svānanda-mūrti | dat sg fem | to him whose form is self-bliss given to devotees |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *pūrṇāgaṇya-guṇodāra-mūrti* [complex tatpuruṣa-bahuvrīhi]: *pūrṇāś cāgaṇyāś ca ye guṇāḥ, taiḥ udārā mūrtiḥ yasya tasmai*.
- *puṇya-kīrti* [karmadhāraya]: *puṇyā kīrtiḥ yasya tasmai*.
- *śrī-pati* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *śriyāḥ patiḥ*.
- *bhakta-datta-svānanda-mūrti* [complex tatpuruṣa]: *bhaktebhyo dattaḥ svānanda eva mūrtiḥ yasya tasmai*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- sampradāna governed by *namaḥ*: *pūrṇāgaṇya-guṇodāra-mūrtaye*, *puṇya-kīrtaye*, *śrī-pataye*, *bhakta-datta-svānanda-mūrtaye*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb. The verse is a nominal salutation built around the indeclinable *namaḥ*, which governs the dative.

**Note:**
The opening string of datives is deliberately accumulative. The deity is first fixed by excellence of qualities, then by fame, then by relation to Śrī, then by salvific relation to devotees. The reading of the first long compound is secure enough in sense but rests on an image-only witness; the page image supports *pūrṇāgaṇya-* rather than a guṇa-reduplicating alternative.

### Verse 2

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *pūrṇāgaṇya-guṇodāra-nitya-sundara-mūrtine | śrī-brahma-vīndra-pūrveḍya-kīrtine brahmaṇe namaḥ || 2 ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Salutation to Brahmā, whose form is eternally beautiful and exalted through full and innumerable qualities.  
> Salutation to Brahmā, whose fame is praised by the illustrious Brahmā-and-Indra line [reading of the middle compound tentative].

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| pūrṇāgaṇya-guṇodāra-nitya-sundara-mūrtine | pūrṇāgaṇya-guṇodāra-nitya-sundara-mūrti(n) | dat sg masc | to him whose form is eternally beautiful and noble through full and countless qualities |
| śrī-brahma-vīndra-pūrveḍya-kīrtine | śrī-brahma-vīndra-pūrveḍya-kīrti(n) | dat sg masc | to him whose fame is praised by the exalted Brahmā/Indra sequence [tentative reading] |
| brahmaṇe | brahman / brahmā | dat sg masc | to Brahmā |
| namaḥ | namaḥ | indecl | salutation |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *pūrṇāgaṇya-guṇodāra-nitya-sundara-mūrti* [complex tatpuruṣa-bahuvrīhi]: *pūrṇāgaṇya-guṇaiḥ udārā ca nitya-sundarā ca mūrtiḥ yasya tasmai*.
- *śrī-brahma-vīndra-pūrveḍya-kīrti* [uncertain complex compound]: the printed image and OCR support a compound of “Śrī/Brahma/Indra” plus *pūrveḍya-kīrti*; the exact internal segmentation remains uncertain without a fresh diplomatic transcription.

**Kāraka structure:**
- sampradāna governed by *namaḥ*: *pūrṇāgaṇya-guṇodāra-nitya-sundara-mūrtine*, *śrī-brahma-vīndra-pūrveḍya-kīrtine*, *brahmaṇe*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb. Again the verse is a dative salutation governed by *namaḥ*.

**Note:**
This verse is the least textually stable item in the present scope. The overall syntax is clear, but the long middle compound cannot be normalized with complete confidence from the image-only leaf and OCR alone. I retain the best-supported reading and mark the uncertainty rather than silently smoothing it.

### Verse 3

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *viśvaṃ yasya vaśe sarvam aniśaṃ śaṅkarādikam | tasya vāyos tṛtīyāṃśam āśraye śamavāptaye || 3 ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> He in whose power the whole world lies, with Śaṅkara and the rest, at all times.  
> I take refuge in that third portion of Vāyu, for the attainment of tranquillity.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| viśvam | viśva | acc sg neut | the whole world |
| yasya | yad | gen sg masc/neut | of whom, whose |
| vaśe | vaśa | loc sg masc | in the power, under the control |
| sarvam | sarva | acc sg neut | all |
| aniśam | aniśam | indecl | continually, at all times |
| śaṅkarādikam | śaṅkara-ādika | acc sg neut | Śaṅkara and the rest |
| tasya | tad | gen sg masc | of that one |
| vāyoḥ | vāyu | gen sg masc | of Vāyu |
| tṛtīyāṃśam | tṛtīya-aṃśa | acc sg masc | the third portion |
| āśraye | ā-√śri | 1 sg pres ind Ā | I resort to, take refuge in |
| śamavāptaye | śama-āvāpti | dat sg fem | for the attainment of peace / tranquillity |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *śaṅkarādika* [tatpuruṣa]: *śaṅkaraḥ ādir yeṣām*.
- *tṛtīyāṃśa* [karmadhāraya]: *tṛtīyaḥ aṃśaḥ*.
- *śamavāpti* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa sense]: *śamasya āvāptiḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- locus: *vaśe*
- possessor: *yasya*
- object of refuge: *tṛtīyāṃśam*
- purpose: *śamavāptaye*
- kartṛ: implicit first-person in *āśraye*

**Verbal modality:**
- *āśraye*: laṭ / ātmanepada / active-middle reflexive sense (“I resort to”).

**Note:**
In Madhva lineage usage, Vāyu’s “third portion” is the third avatāric manifestation, namely Madhva/Ānandatīrtha after Hanumat and Bhīma. The verse therefore fixes the line not merely historically but ontologically: the commentator’s dependence is routed through a ranked manifestation-series.

### Verse 4

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *sat-padmabandhavo yasya gāvo viśva-tamo-nudaḥ | sa syāt tattva-prakāśāya jayatīrtha-ravir mama || 4 ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> He whose rays are the friends of good lotuses and the dispellers of the world’s darkness.  
> May that sun, Jayatīrtha, be for the illumination of truth for me.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| sat-padmabandhavaḥ | sat-padmabandhava | nom pl masc | friends of the good lotuses |
| yasya | yad | gen sg masc | whose |
| gāvaḥ | go | nom pl fem | rays |
| viśva-tamo-nudaḥ | viśva-tamas-nud | nom pl masc | dispellers of the world’s darkness |
| saḥ | tad | nom sg masc | that |
| syāt | √as | 3 sg opt | may he be |
| tattva-prakāśāya | tattva-prakāśa | dat sg masc/neut | for the illumination of reality / truth |
| jayatīrtha-raviḥ | jayatīrtha-ravi | nom sg masc | the sun Jayatīrtha |
| mama | asmad | gen sg | for me, of me |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *sat-padmabandhava* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa or bahuvrīhi-like epithet]: *satāṃ padmānāṃ bandhavaḥ*.
- *viśva-tamo-nud* [upapada-tatpuruṣa]: *viśvasya tamaḥ nudanti*.
- *tattva-prakāśa* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *tattvasya prakāśaḥ*.
- *jayatīrtha-ravi* [karmadhāraya]: *jayatīrtha eva raviḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- possessor: *yasya*
- kartṛ: *jayatīrtha-raviḥ*
- purpose: *tattva-prakāśāya*
- beneficiary: *mama*

**Verbal modality:**
- *syāt*: liṅ / parasmaipada / active optative.

**Note:**
The sun-metaphor is doctrinally loaded. Jayatīrtha’s work does not create *tattva*; it illumines it, as the sun reveals rather than fabricates the lotuses it opens. Compare the function assigned to Jayatīrtha throughout the Madhva scholastic tradition as the principal explicator of Madhva’s compressed *Anuvyākhyāna*.

### Verse 5

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *pada-vākya-pramāṇa-jño durmata-dhvānta-bhāskaraḥ | vyāsatīrtha-munīndro me bhūyāj jñāna-samṛddhaye || 5 ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> The knower of word, sentence, and valid means of cognition; the sun to the darkness of bad doctrine.  
> May the sage-lord Vyāsatīrtha be for the enrichment of knowledge in me.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| pada-vākya-pramāṇa-jñaḥ | pada-vākya-pramāṇa-jña | nom sg masc | knower of word, sentence, and means of valid cognition |
| durmata-dhvānta-bhāskaraḥ | durmata-dhvānta-bhāskara | nom sg masc | sun to the darkness of erroneous doctrine |
| vyāsatīrtha-munīndraḥ | vyāsatīrtha-munīndra | nom sg masc | the sage-lord Vyāsatīrtha |
| me | asmad | gen/dative sg enclitic | for me |
| bhūyāt | √bhū | 3 sg opt P | may he be |
| jñāna-samṛddhaye | jñāna-samṛddhi | dat sg fem | for the increase of knowledge |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *pada-vākya-pramāṇa-jña* [dvandva + tatpuruṣa]: *padānāṃ vākānāṃ pramāṇānāṃ ca jñaḥ*.
- *durmata-dhvānta-bhāskara* [upapada-tatpuruṣa]: *durmatasya dhvānte bhāskaraḥ iva*.
- *vyāsatīrtha-munīndra* [karmadhāraya]: *vyāsatīrtha eva munīndraḥ*.
- *jñāna-samṛddhi* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *jñānasya samṛddhiḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *vyāsatīrtha-munīndraḥ*
- beneficiary: *me*
- purpose: *jñāna-samṛddhaye*

**Verbal modality:**
- *bhūyāt*: liṅ / parasmaipada / active optative.

**Note:**
The triad *pada-vākya-pramāṇa* neatly compresses grammar, sentence-meaning, and epistemology. It is a miniature map of the scholastic competencies Rāghavendra takes for granted in Vyāsatīrtha and, by implication, in the idiom the *Pariṃala* itself will inherit and sharpen.

### Verse 6

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *asmad-deśika-pādānāṃ praṇamya caraṇāmbujam | śrīman-nyāya-sudhā-vākya-vyākhyāṃ kuryāṃ yathā-mati || 6 ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Having bowed to the lotus-foot of my own teacher.  
> I should make, according to my understanding, an explanation of the wording of the venerable *Nyāya-Sudhā*.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| asmad-deśika-pādānām | asmad-deśika-pāda | gen pl masc | of my teacher’s feet |
| praṇamya | pra-√nam | absolutive | having bowed to |
| caraṇāmbujam | caraṇa-ambuja | acc sg neut | the lotus-foot |
| śrīman-nyāya-sudhā-vākya-vyākhyām | śrīman-nyāya-sudhā-vākya-vyākhyā | acc sg fem | the explanation of the wording of the venerable *Nyāya-Sudhā* |
| kuryām | √kṛ | 1 sg opt P | I should make |
| yathā-mati | yathā-mati | indecl compound | according to my understanding |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *asmad-deśika-pāda* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *asmākaṃ deśikasya pādāḥ*.
- *caraṇāmbuja* [karmadhāraya]: *ambujam iva caraṇam*.
- *śrīman-nyāya-sudhā-vākya-vyākhyā* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *śrīmatyāḥ nyāya-sudhāyāḥ vākyasya vyākhyā*.
- *yathā-mati* [avyayībhāva]: *mateḥ yathā*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- prior action: *praṇamya*
- object of prior action: *caraṇāmbujam*
- intended object of composition: *śrīman-nyāya-sudhā-vākya-vyākhyām*
- manner/limit: *yathā-mati*
- kartṛ: implicit first-person in *kuryām*

**Verbal modality:**
- *kuryām*: liṅ / parasmaipada / active optative.

**Note:**
The optative matters. Rāghavendra does not say “I explain”; he says, in effect, “may I undertake the explanation, to the extent of my understanding.” The self-placement is scholastic and devotional at once: authorial initiative appears only after prostration and only under a modality of permissioned modesty.

### Opening prose sentence

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *ādya-padyam vivṛṇvāno granthādau namyatva-prayojaka-viśiṣṭatva-iṣṭatva-adhikṛtatva-yukta-devatā-nati-rūpaṃ maṅgalam ācarati.*

**English (line-by-line):**
> While explaining the first verse, at the beginning of the treatise, he performs an auspicious opening.  
> It takes the form of salutation to a deity endowed with the properties that ground worthiness-to-be-saluted, desirability, and authority.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| ādya-padyam | ādya-padya | acc sg neut | the first verse |
| vivṛṇvānaḥ | vi-√vṛ | pres ptcp nom sg masc Ā | explaining, unfolding |
| granthādau | grantha-ādi | loc sg masc | at the beginning of the treatise |
| namyatva-prayojaka-viśiṣṭatva-iṣṭatva-adhikṛtatva-yukta-devatā-nati-rūpam | namyatva-prayojaka-viśiṣṭatva-iṣṭatva-adhikṛtatva-yukta-devatā-nati-rūpa | acc sg neut | having the form of salutation to a deity endowed with distinctiveness, desirability, and authority as causes of worthiness-to-be-saluted |
| maṅgalam | maṅgala | acc sg neut | an auspicious opening |
| ācarati | ā-√car | 3 sg pres ind P | performs, undertakes |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *ādya-padya* [karmadhāraya]: *ādyam padyam*.
- *granthādi* [avyayībhāva-like locative use]: *granthasya ādau*.
- *namyatva-prayojaka-viśiṣṭatva-iṣṭatva-adhikṛtatva-yukta-devatā-nati-rūpa* [very long complex tatpuruṣa]: *namyatvasya prayojakāni viśiṣṭatvam, iṣṭatvam, adhikṛtatvam ca; taiḥ yuktā yā devatā, tasyai natiḥ; tadrūpam*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: implicit commentator referred to by *vivṛṇvānaḥ* and *ācarati*
- object: *maṅgalam*
- form/specification: *...devatā-nati-rūpam*
- temporal-locative frame: *granthādau*

**Verbal modality:**
- *vivṛṇvānaḥ*: present participial, middle/ātmanepada.
- *ācarati*: laṭ / parasmaipada / active.

**Note:**
The sentence is a meta-commentarial instruction manual. It tells the reader what kind of thing the opening verse is and, more importantly, by what criteria the saluted deity counts as the proper object of salutation. The triad *viśiṣṭatva-iṣṭatva-adhikṛtatva* anticipates the more technical discriminations that will govern the commentary proper.

## Editorial closing

This passage settles the order of approach. One does not enter the *Pariṃala* directly at the level of argument; one first fixes the superior deity, the authorized lineage, and the commentator’s own derivative standing. What it leaves open, by design, is the argumentative content of the saluted predicates themselves: why Hari alone fits the opening verse’s qualifiers, how the lineage-order is doctrinally warranted, and how the *Nyāya-Sudhā*’s technical claims should be reformulated in later *Navya-Nyāya* terms. Those issues are taken up immediately in the prose on the first verse and then throughout adhyāya 1 on *athāto brahmajijñāsā*. For parallels in Rāghavendra’s broader corpus, compare the Hari-centering semantic rule in *Tantra-Dīpikā* 1.1 and the analogous lineage-conscious opening in the *Tarka-Tāṇḍava-Pariṃala* preface.
