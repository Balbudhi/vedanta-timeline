# Taittirīya-Vārttika

**By:** Sureśvara
**Genre:** *vārttika* (versified critical gloss) on Śaṅkara's *Taittirīya-Upaniṣad-Bhāṣya*

---

## Status

A machine-readable Sanskrit text of the *Taittirīya-Vārttika* is not currently in our corpus. The Vedānta interactive timeline does not have a defensible Sanskrit witness on disk for this text, and producing an annotated translation would mean fabricating engagement we cannot ground.

What is widely known about this work:

- A verse-vārttika on Śaṅkara's *Taittirīya-Upaniṣad-Bhāṣya*, structured to follow the three *vallīs* of the Upaniṣad: *Śīkṣā-Vallī*, *Brahmānanda-Vallī*, and *Bhṛgu-Vallī*. It is shorter and more compact than the *Bṛhadāraṇyaka-Vārttika*, and is regularly used by later Advaitins as the sharper, more focused statement of Sureśvara's position on the *ānanda-mīmāṃsā* (the inquiry into Brahman as bliss) and on the *kośa* hierarchy.
- Its philosophical signatures include the defense of Brahman as *satyaṃ jñānam anantam* under a strict identity reading, the systematic refutation of *jñāna-karma-samuccaya* tracking the same line as the *Bṛhadāraṇyaka-Vārttika*, and a careful unpacking of the *pañca-kośa* analysis as a pedagogical descent toward the *ānandamaya-ātman*.

What is in our corpus that bears on it:

- Sureśvara's `core_thesis` and engaged_works summary in the timeline entry (see https://balbudhi.github.io/vedanta-timeline/ and locate Sureśvara).
- Files for Sureśvara's *Naiṣkarmya-Siddhi* and the *Bṛhadāraṇyaka-Vārttika* stub, which together situate the *Taittirīya-Vārttika* within Sureśvara's larger Advaita corpus.
