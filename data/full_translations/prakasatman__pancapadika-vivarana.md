**By:** Prakāśātman  
**Locus scope:** Composite doctrinal dossier from the extant opening *pañcapāda* commentary, comprising the four verified load-bearing discussions already keyed in the corpus: *jīvasya brahma-pratibimbatvāpādanam* (p. 65), *mahāvākyasyāparokṣa-prayojakatvam* (p. 103), the *samuccaya* discussion in *brahma-vicāre kim-ānantaryam atha-śabdāvagamyam* (pp. 164-165), and *brahmaṇo lakṣaṇopalakṣaṇe* (p. 212).

## Editorial introduction

These four loci are not contiguous in the printed edition, but together they form a natural doctrinal dossier for Prakāśātman's *Pañcapādikā-Vivaraṇa*. The work is school-founding not because it repeats Śaṅkara's large claims, but because it hardens them into a technical vocabulary. In the discussion of *jīvasya brahma-pratibimbatvāpādanam* (p. 65), the *jīva* is explained as *pratibimba* (reflection-image) rather than as a second self-standing consciousness. In *mahāvākyasyāparokṣa-prayojakatvam* (p. 103), Prakāśātman states the classic Vivaraṇa claim that the *mahāvākya* (great Upaniṣadic sentence) is genuinely *aparokṣa*-productive, though its efficacy is aided by *manana* and allied disciplines. In the anti-*samuccaya* discussion (pp. 164-165), he rejects the thesis that liberating knowledge and Vedic action jointly produce *mokṣa*. Finally, in *brahmaṇo lakṣaṇopalakṣaṇe* (p. 212), he articulates the grammar of causal Brahman as Brahman qualified by *anirvacanīya* *māyā*.

Taken together, these passages display the internal architecture of Vivaraṇa-Advaita. The self is a reflection of Brahman; bondage belongs to the reflected condition, not to Brahman as such. Scripture is not reduced to remote conceptuality; it gives immediate knowledge, though impediments in the mind may make that immediacy appear indirect. Since the fruit of knowledge is the cessation of beginningless false cognition, karma cannot stand beside it as a co-cause of liberation. And causal explanation requires Brahman to be spoken of as qualified by *māyā*, without thereby reducing Brahman to a mutable worldly stuff. What follows, then, is not a continuous chapter-translation but a rigorously keyed translation of the four passages that the corpus itself marks as load-bearing for Prakāśātman's mature position.

## Translation

### PV p. 65a

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *devadatto hi svātmānam andhatvādy-upetaṃ pratibimbe 'vagacchann api tattva-jñānatvān nānuśocati ।*

**English (line-by-line):**
> For Devadatta, indeed, even while cognizing his own self, as possessed of blindness and the like, in a *pratibimba* (reflection-image), does not grieve, because he possesses knowledge of the truth.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| devadattaḥ | devadatta | nom sg masc | Devadatta |
| hi | hi | indecl | indeed |
| svātmānam | sva-ātman | acc sg masc | his own self |
| andhatvādy-upetam | andhatvādi-upeta | acc sg masc | endowed with blindness and the like |
| pratibimbe | pratibimba | loc sg neut | in a reflection |
| avagacchan | ava-gam | pres act part nom sg masc | cognizing, apprehending |
| api | api | indecl | even though |
| tattva-jñānatvāt | tattva-jñānatva | abl sg neut | because of possessing true knowledge |
| na | na | indecl | not |
| anuśocati | anu-śuc | 3sg pres ind P | grieves |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *svātmānam* [tatpuruṣa]: *sva ātmānam*.
- *andhatvādy-upetam* [tatpuruṣa]: *andhatvādinā upetam*.
- *tattva-jñānatvāt* [tatpuruṣa]: *tattvasya jñānaṃ yasya tasya bhāvāt*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- `kartṛ`: *devadattaḥ*
- `karman`: *svātmānam andhatvādy-upetam*
- `adhikaraṇa`: *pratibimbe*
- `hetu`: *tattva-jñānatvāt*

**Verbal modality:**
- *anuśocati*: `laṭ` / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
The analogy is exacting. The reflected blindness belongs to the reflected presentation, not to Devadatta as knower. That is the logical preparation for the next line's claim about Brahman and the *jīva*. Compare the later causal discussion at PV p. 212, where qualification by *māyā* is likewise predicated for explanatory purposes without making Brahman intrinsically altered.

### PV p. 65b

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *evaṃ brahmāpi svātmani jīve pratibimbe saṃsāraṃ paśyad api tattva-jñānatvān nānuśocati ॥*

**English (line-by-line):**
> In the same way, Brahman too, even while seeing *saṃsāra* (bondage and transmigratory suffering) in the *jīva* (individual self) as a reflection in its own self, does not grieve, because it possesses knowledge of the truth.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| evam | evam | indecl | thus, in this way |
| brahma | brahman | nom sg neut | Brahman |
| api | api | indecl | also, too |
| svātmani | sva-ātman | loc sg masc | in its own self |
| jīve | jīva | loc sg masc | in the jīva |
| pratibimbe | pratibimba | loc sg neut | in the reflection |
| saṃsāram | saṃsāra | acc sg masc | saṃsāra, worldly bondage |
| paśyat | paś | pres act part nom sg neut | seeing |
| api | api | indecl | even though |
| tattva-jñānatvāt | tattva-jñānatva | abl sg neut | because of possessing true knowledge |
| na | na | indecl | not |
| anuśocati | anu-śuc | 3sg pres ind P | grieves |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *svātmani* [tatpuruṣa]: *sve ātmani*.
- *tattva-jñānatvāt* [tatpuruṣa]: *tattvasya jñānaṃ yasya tasya bhāvāt*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- `kartṛ`: *brahma*
- `adhikaraṇa`: *svātmani jīve pratibimbe*
- `karman`: *saṃsāram*
- `hetu`: *tattva-jñānatvāt*

**Verbal modality:**
- *anuśocati*: `laṭ` / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
The layered locative is philosophically decisive: *svātmani jīve pratibimbe*. The *jīva* is not external to Brahman; nor is it identical to Brahman without qualification. It is the reflected locus in which bondage appears. This is the ontological side of what PV p. 103 states epistemologically, namely that Brahman is immediately present in cognition even when that immediacy is obscured in appearance.

### PV p. 103a

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *svayaṃprakāśe 'pi mananādi-sahakārair eva mahāvākyasyāparokṣa-prayojakatvam ।*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Even in the case of what is *svayaṃprakāśa* (self-luminous), it is precisely through auxiliaries such as reflection that the *mahāvākya* (great Upaniṣadic sentence) has the power to produce immediacy.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| svayaṃprakāśe | svayaṃprakāśa | loc sg masc | in what is self-luminous |
| api | api | indecl | even |
| mananādi-sahakāraiḥ | mananādi-sahakāra | instr pl masc | by auxiliaries beginning with reflection |
| eva | eva | indecl | precisely, indeed |
| mahāvākyasya | mahāvākya | gen sg neut | of the great sentence |
| aparokṣa-prayojakatvam | aparokṣa-prayojakatva | nom sg neut | the efficacy of producing immediacy |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *svayaṃprakāśa* [bahuvrīhi]: *svayam eva prakāśate iti*.
- *mananādi-sahakāraiḥ* [tatpuruṣa]: *mananaṃ cādīni ca sahakārāḥ*.
- *mahāvākya* [karmadhāraya]: *mahat vākyaṃ*.
- *aparokṣa-prayojakatvam* [tatpuruṣa]: *aparokṣasya prayojakatvam*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- `karaṇa`: *mananādi-sahakāraiḥ*
- `sambandha`: *mahāvākyasya*
- `viśeṣaṇa-adhikaraṇa`: *svayaṃprakāśe*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb is expressed; the sentence is a nominal predication.

**Note:**
The restrictive *eva* matters. Prakāśātman is not denying immediacy to scripture; he is denying that sheer hearing, in an unprepared mind, is the whole story. The auxiliaries are not ritual acts but interior disciplines. That point is made explicit, and polemically protected, in the anti-*samuccaya* discussion at PV pp. 164-165.

### PV p. 103b

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *brahmaiva sarva-saṃvid-upādānatvād brahmakāra-śabda-pramāṇa-janya-saṃvedane 'pi brahmāpi prathamam evāparokṣatayāvabhāsate ।*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Because Brahman itself is the material basis of every cognition, Brahman too appears from the very first as immediate even in the cognition generated by the verbal *pramāṇa* (means of valid knowledge) that has Brahman for its form.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| brahma | brahman | nom sg neut | Brahman |
| eva | eva | indecl | itself, indeed |
| sarva-saṃvid-upādānatvāt | sarva-saṃvid-upādānatva | abl sg neut | because of being the basis of all cognition |
| brahmakāra-śabda-pramāṇa-janya-saṃvedane | brahmakāra-śabda-pramāṇa-janya-saṃvedana | loc sg neut | in the cognition generated by the verbal pramāṇa taking Brahman as its form |
| api | api | indecl | even |
| brahma | brahman | nom sg neut | Brahman |
| api | api | indecl | also, too |
| prathamam | prathama | acc sg neut used adverbially | at first, from the outset |
| eva | eva | indecl | indeed |
| aparokṣatayā | aparokṣatā | instr sg fem | as immediate, in the mode of immediacy |
| avabhāsate | ava-bhās | 3sg pres ind Ā | appears, shines forth |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *sarva-saṃvid-upādānatvāt* [tatpuruṣa]: *sarvāsāṃ saṃvidāṃ upādānatvāt*.
- *brahmakāra-śabda-pramāṇa-janya-saṃvedane* [tatpuruṣa]: *brahmakāreṇa śabda-pramāṇena janyaṃ saṃvedanam*.
- *aparokṣatayā* [abstract derivative]: from *aparokṣa*, "non-indirectness," used instrumentally to mark mode.

**Kāraka structure:**
- `hetu`: *sarva-saṃvid-upādānatvāt*
- `adhikaraṇa`: *brahmakāra-śabda-pramāṇa-janya-saṃvedane*
- `kartṛ`: *brahma*
- `prakāra-karaṇa`: *aparokṣatayā*

**Verbal modality:**
- *avabhāsate*: `laṭ` / ātmanepada / middle

**Note:**
*Prathamam eva* is unusually strong. The first Brahman-cognition generated by scripture is already immediate; what follows is not the production of a new object but the removal of obstacles to the cognition's unobstructed force. This is why the Vivaraṇa can affirm scriptural immediacy without appealing to ritual cooperation.

### PV p. 103c

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *tac ca cittasyātisūkṣmatvād viparyaya-saṃskāra-doṣāc ca pratibaddhaṃ bhrāntyā parokṣavad bhāsate ॥*

**English (line-by-line):**
> And that, because of the extreme subtlety of the mind and because of the defect constituted by residual impressions of error, being obstructed, appears through delusion as though it were indirect.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| tat | tad | nom sg neut | that |
| ca | ca | indecl | and |
| cittasya | citta | gen sg neut | of the mind |
| atisūkṣmatvāt | atisūkṣmatva | abl sg neut | because of extreme subtlety |
| viparyaya-saṃskāra-doṣāt | viparyaya-saṃskāra-doṣa | abl sg masc | because of the defect of contrary latent impressions |
| ca | ca | indecl | and |
| pratibaddham | prati-bandh | kta; nom sg neut past pass part | obstructed |
| bhrāntyā | bhrānti | instr sg fem | by error, through delusion |
| parokṣavat | parokṣavat | indecl | as if indirect |
| bhāsate | bhās | 3sg pres ind Ā | appears, shines |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *atisūkṣmatvāt* [abstract derivative]: *atisūkṣma* + *-tva*, "because of extreme subtlety."
- *viparyaya-saṃskāra-doṣāt* [tatpuruṣa]: *viparyayasya saṃskārasya doṣāt*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- `kartṛ`: *tat*
- `hetu`: *cittasyātisūkṣmatvāt*
- `hetu`: *viparyaya-saṃskāra-doṣāt*
- `karaṇa`: *bhrāntyā*

**Verbal modality:**
- *bhāsate*: `laṭ` / ātmanepada / middle

**Note:**
The apparent indirectness belongs to *bhrānti*, not to Brahman and not to the scriptural cognition as such. The grammar therefore blocks a common misreading: the *mahāvākya* is not merely preparatory information awaiting some later non-verbal revelation. The obstruction is psychological and beginningless; the cognition is already of the right object in the right mode.

### PV p. 164a

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *avidyayā mṛtyuṃ tīrtvā vidyayāmṛtam aśnuta iti phala-bhedāgamāt ।*

**English (line-by-line):**
> From the scriptural declaration, "having crossed death by *avidyā* (ignorance, here in the lower or preparatory sense), one attains immortality by *vidyā* (liberating knowledge)," because scripture teaches a difference of fruits.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |

| avidyayā | avidyā | instr sg fem | by avidyā |
| mṛtyum | mṛtyu | acc sg masc | death |
| tīrtvā | tṝ | absolutive (gerund) | having crossed |
| vidyayā | vidyā | instr sg fem | by vidyā |
| amṛtam | amṛta | acc sg neut | immortality |
| aśnuta | aś | printed here as cited; cf. standard *aśnute* | attains, partakes of |
| iti | iti | indecl | thus |
| phala-bhedāgamāt | phala-bheda-āgama | abl sg masc | because scripture teaches a difference of fruits |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *phala-bhedāgamāt* [tatpuruṣa]: *phalasya bhedaḥ yasmin āgame tasmāt*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- `karaṇa`: *avidyayā*
- `karman`: *mṛtyum*
- `karaṇa`: *vidyayā*
- `karman`: *amṛtam*
- `hetu`: *phala-bhedāgamāt*

**Verbal modality:**
- *aśnuta*: cited scriptural form; the intended sense is that of present attainment, standardly transmitted as *aśnute*

**Note:**
The point of the citation is not lexical but structural. If scripture itself distinguishes the fruits of the two, then knowledge and action cannot be folded into one undifferentiated saving instrument. The printed/transcribed locus gives *aśnuta*; the sense and Upaniṣadic source support the familiar *aśnute*.

### PV p. 164b

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *tasmān na samuccaye pramāṇam asti ।*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Therefore there is no valid warrant for a *samuccaya* (co-functioning combination of means).

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| tasmāt | tad | abl sg neut used adverbially | therefore |
| na | na | indecl | not |
| samuccaye | samuccaya | loc sg masc | in a combination, for combination |
| pramāṇam | pramāṇa | nom sg neut | valid warrant, authoritative proof |
| asti | as | 3sg pres ind P | is |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *samuccaya* [action noun]: from *sam-uc-ci*, "bringing together, combination."

**Kāraka structure:**
- `adhikaraṇa`: *samuccaye*
- `kartṛ`: *pramāṇam*

**Verbal modality:**
- *asti*: `laṭ` / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
The move is epistemic before it is soteriological. Prakāśātman does not merely say that *samuccaya* is undesirable; he says there is no *pramāṇa* for it. The debate is therefore situated at the level of scriptural warrant and causal intelligibility, not devotional preference.

### PV p. 164c

- *sarvānarthopādānānādi-mithyājñāna-nivṛttiḥ* [tatpuruṣa]: *sarvānarthānām upādānaṃ yad anādi mithyājñānaṃ tasya nivṛttiḥ*.
- *samuccayāvakāśaḥ* [tatpuruṣa]: *samuccayasya avakāśaḥ*.

- `kartṛ`: *sarvānarthopādānānādi-mithyājñāna-nivṛttiḥ*
- `adhikaraṇa`: *vijñāne*
- `predicative role`: *phalavat*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb is expressed; the sentence closes by nominal conclusion.

**Note:**
The long compound is not ornamental compression. It states the entire bondage-theory in one syntactic unit: the root of all *anartha* is beginningless false cognition, and the fruit of *vijñāna* is the cessation of precisely that. Once the fruit is so specified, karma cannot be a co-producer of liberation. The epistemological ground for that claim has already been laid at PV p. 103.

### PV p. 212a

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *māyāvidyayoḥ sāmānādhikaraṇyāc ca ।*

**English (line-by-line):**
> And because of the co-reference of *māyā* (cosmic projecting and veiling power) and *avidyā*.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| māyāvidyayoḥ | māyā-avidyā | gen du fem | of māyā and avidyā |
| sāmānādhikaraṇyāt | sāmānādhikaraṇya | abl sg neut | because of co-reference, appositional identity |
| ca | ca | indecl | and |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *māyāvidyayoḥ* [dvandva]: *māyā ca avidyā ca*.
- *sāmānādhikaraṇya* [technical compound]: *samāne adhikaraṇe bhāvaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- `sambandha`: *māyāvidyayoḥ*
- `hetu`: *sāmānādhikaraṇyāt*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb is expressed; the clause gives a reason for the conclusion that follows.

**Note:**
The argument is grammatical and ontological at once. Prakāśātman is not merely saying that the two words are casually interchangeable; he is using shared referential force as evidence for a doctrinal equation. That is the premise from which the final conclusion about causal Brahman is drawn.

### PV p. 212b

**Sanskrit (IAST):**

**English (line-by-line):**
> Therefore the conclusion is reached that Brahman, qualified by *anirvacanīya* (indeterminable as either real or unreal) *māyā*, is the cause.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| tasmāt | tad | abl sg neut used adverbially | therefore |
| anirvacanīya-māyā-viśiṣṭam | anirvacanīya-māyā-viśiṣṭa | nom sg neut | qualified by indeterminable māyā |
| kāraṇam | kāraṇa | nom sg neut | cause |
| brahma | brahman | nom sg neut | Brahman |
| iti | iti | indecl | thus |
| prāptam | pra-āp | kta; nom sg neut past pass part | is arrived at, is established |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *anirvacanīya-māyā-viśiṣṭam* [tatpuruṣa]: *anirvacanīyayā māyayā viśiṣṭam*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- `kartṛ/predicate complex`: *anirvacanīya-māyā-viśiṣṭaṃ kāraṇaṃ brahma*
- `conclusive marker`: *iti prāptam*

**Verbal modality:**
- *prāptam*: past passive participle used predicatively to mark inferential settlement

**Note:**
The qualification is exact. Causal Brahman is Brahman qualified by *māyā*; Brahman is not reduced to *māyā*, and *mithyā* is not collapsed into sheer non-being. The phrase secures world-explanation while preserving the asymmetry between Brahman and the superimposed order. It is the cosmological counterpart to the reflected-self account at PV p. 65.

## Editorial closing

These passages settle four points that later Vivaraṇa-Advaita will keep in play as a connected package. PV p. 65 establishes the reflected status of the *jīva*; PV p. 103 explains how scriptural cognition can be immediate without denying the need for interior preparation; PV pp. 164-165 exclude *jñāna-karma-samuccaya* by specifying the fruit of knowledge as the cessation of beginningless false cognition; PV p. 212 states the causal formula of Brahman qualified by *anirvacanīya māyā*. What they do not settle is every later scholastic refinement: they do not yet answer every Bhāmatī objection about *avidyā*'s locus, nor do they exhaust the later debates on the exact status of *akhaṇḍākāra-vṛtti*. But the architecture is already here. The ontological claim at p. 65 is taken up epistemologically at p. 103, soteriologically at pp. 164-165, and cosmologically at p. 212. Downstream, precisely these nodes become the characteristic inheritance of the Vivaraṇa line, most visibly in Vidyāraṇya's *Vivaraṇa-Prameya-Saṃgraha* and in the later sub-commentarial tradition around Citsukha and Nṛsiṃhāśramin.
