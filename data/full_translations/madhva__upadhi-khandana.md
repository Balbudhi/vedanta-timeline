# Upādhi-Khaṇḍana — Extended Translation
**By:** Madhva  
**Locus scope:** The entire tract *Upādhi-Khaṇḍana* as preserved in the short-polemics collection; the work is itself a natural compact unit, and the argument depends on the whole sequence from opening dilemma through scriptural closure.  
**Source edition transcribed from:** T. R. Krishnacharya, *Sarva-Mūla Granthas: Pramāṇalakṣaṇa, Kathālakṣaṇa, Upādhi-Khaṇḍana, Māyāvāda-Khaṇḍana, Prapañca-Mithyātvānumāna-Khaṇḍana, Tattva-Saṅkhyāna, Tattva-Viveka* (collected Sanskrit edition; local Archive scan in the corpus).  
**Translator:** Pāṇinian-rich extraction by Codex 5.4 (reasoning=high), 2026-05-10.

---

## Editorial introduction

The *Upādhi-Khaṇḍana* is the closing member of Madhva's short anti-Advaita triad. The *Māyāvāda-Khaṇḍana* had already argued that the doctrine of *māyā* is not a coherent ontological beginning. The *Mithyātvānumāna-Khaṇḍana* then attacks the formal inference by which the world is said to be established as *mithyā* (neither simply real nor simply unreal). The present tract turns to the fallback position that survives both defeats: even if full identity is not manifest, might *jīva* and *Brahman* still be one, with the difference produced only by an *upādhi* (conditioning adjunct, delimiting factor)? Madhva's answer is that this strategy fails on both sides. If the differentiating adjunct is real, then the difference it yields is real, and non-difference is no longer doing explanatory work. If the adjunct is unreal or ignorance-based, the position simply reimports the very *avidyā/mithyātva* structure already denied.

That is why the whole tract is the natural unit. Verses 2-6 set up the basic dilemma and deny that *upādhi* can function as a metaphysical maker of difference. Verses 7-10 move to experiential and karmic variants of the same strategy and expose their instability. Verses 11-17 shift from purely formal pressure to epistemic and scriptural pressure: the difference of selves is said to be directly experienced, while experienced limitation cannot be imposed upon Viṣṇu without contradicting *śruti*. Verses 18-20 then compress Madhva's positive closure: the relevant Vedic statements speak of transcendence, otherness, and resemblance, not identity. The tract therefore stands in exact relation to larger loci. Compare *Anuvyākhyāna* 2.2.128-131 and 147 for the more extended rejection of *aupādhika-bheda* (difference produced by adjuncts), and *Tattvodyota* for the broader insistence that real difference is not a merely verbal distortion of unity. Here the same case is stated with polemical economy.

## Translation

### Verse 1

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *nārāyaṇo 'gaṇya-guṇa-nityaika-nilayākṛtiḥ*  
> *aśeṣa-doṣa-rahitaḥ prīyatāṃ kamalālayaḥ // 1 //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> May Nārāyaṇa be propitious, he whose very form is the sole eternal abode of innumerable qualities;  
> he is devoid of every defect without remainder, and is the abode of Kamalā.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| nārāyaṇaḥ | nārāyaṇa | nom sg masc | Nārāyaṇa |
| agaṇya-guṇa-nityaika-nilayākṛtiḥ | compound | nom sg fem/masc used predicatively | whose form is the one eternal abode of uncountable qualities |
| aśeṣa-doṣa-rahitaḥ | compound | nom sg masc | free from every defect without remainder |
| prīyatām | prī | loṭ, 3 sg ātmanepada | may he be pleased; may he be propitious |
| kamalālayaḥ | kamalālaya | nom sg masc | the abode / lord of Kamalā |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *agaṇya-guṇa-nityaika-nilayākṛtiḥ* [multi-member tatpuruṣa with karmadhāraya force]: *agaṇyānāṃ guṇānāṃ nityaikasya nilayasya ākṛtiḥ yasya saḥ*.
- *aśeṣa-doṣa-rahitaḥ* [tatpuruṣa]: *aśeṣebhyo doṣebhyo rahitaḥ*.
- *kamalālayaḥ* [tatpuruṣa/bahuvrīhi by usage]: either *kamalāyā ālayaḥ* or "he in whom Kamalā dwells."

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *nārāyaṇaḥ*
- predicate qualifiers: *... nilayākṛtiḥ*, *aśeṣa-doṣa-rahitaḥ*, *kamalālayaḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *prīyatām*: loṭ / ātmanepada / benedictive-imperatival usage

**Note:**
The maṅgala-verse is already doctrinal. Madhva begins with plenitude of qualities and absolute faultlessness, because the tract will later argue that ignorance, limitation, and sorrow cannot be transferred to Viṣṇu through any *upādhi*. Compare the closing scriptural appeal in verses 13-14.

### Verse 2

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *ajñatā 'khila-saṃvettur ghaṭate na kutaścana*  
> *upādhi-bhedād ghaṭata iti cet sa svabhāvataḥ // 2 //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Ignorance cannot obtain, from any quarter whatsoever, in one who knows all.  
> If it is said to obtain because of a difference in conditioning adjuncts, then that difference is intrinsic.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| ajñatā | ajñatā | nom sg fem | ignorance; not-knowing |

| na | na | indecl | not |

| upādhi-bhedāt | compound | abl sg masc | from difference of conditioning adjuncts |
| ghaṭata | ghaṭat | pres. participle / verbal notion in reported claim | occurring, obtaining |
| iti | iti | indecl | thus |
| cet | cet | indecl | if |
| saḥ | tad | nom sg masc | that |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *akhila-saṃvettuḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *akhilaṃ saṃvetti iti tasya*.
- *upādhi-bhedāt* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *upādhīnām bhedāt*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- viṣaya: *ajñatā*

**Verbal modality:**
- *ghaṭate*: laṭ / ātmanepada / active-middle

**Note:**
This is the tract's opening horn. The omniscient cannot literally become ignorant. If the Advaitin shifts the burden to *upādhi*-difference, Madhva at once argues that the supposed differentiator is no mere explanatory overlay but a real feature. The same pressure recurs in *Anuvyākhyāna* 2.2.128-130.

### Verse 3

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *ajñānato vā dvaitasya satyatā svata eva cet*  
> *anavasthitir ajñāna-hetau vānyonya-siddhitā // 3 //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Or if the reality of duality is due to ignorance, or else if it is from itself alone,  
> then there is regress in making ignorance the cause, or there is mutual dependence.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| ajñānataḥ | ajñānatas | abl/indecl | from ignorance |
| vā | vā | indecl | or |
| dvaitasya | dvaita | gen sg neut | of duality |
| satyatā | satyatā | nom sg fem | reality, truth-status |
| svataḥ | svatas | indecl/abl sense | from itself; intrinsically |
| eva | eva | indecl | indeed, just |
| cet | cet | indecl | if |
| anavasthitiḥ | anavasthiti | nom sg fem | regress; non-termination |
| ajñāna-hetau | compound | loc sg masc | in ignorance as cause |
| vā | vā | indecl | or |
| anyonya-siddhitā | compound | nom sg fem | mutual dependence; reciprocal establishment |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *ajñāna-hetau* [saptamī-tatpuruṣa]: *ajñāne hetau*.
- *anyonya-siddhitā* [tatpuruṣa]: *anyonyaṃ siddhir yasmin sā*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- viṣaya: *dvaitasya satyatā*
- alternative hetus: *ajñānataḥ*, *svataḥ eva*
- doṣa: *anavasthitiḥ* / *anyonya-siddhitā*

**Verbal modality:**
- Elliptic inferential structure; no finite verb is expressed.

**Note:**
Madhva's dilemma is now explicit. If difference depends on ignorance, ignorance itself requires prior differentiation to be located or explained. If difference is self-grounded, the entire *upādhi*-strategy is idle. The argument is compactly destructive in Chakrabarti's sense: every exit opens into a new cost.

### Verse 4

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *cakrakāpattir athavā bhedaś copādhitaḥ kutaḥ*  
> *vidyamānasya bhedasya jñāpako naiva kārakaḥ // 4 //*

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|

| kutaḥ | kutaḥ | indecl/interrogative | how? from where? |
| vidyamānasya | vidyamāna | gen sg masc/neut | of what already exists |
| bhedasya | bheda | gen sg masc | of difference |

### Verse 5

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *upādhi-dṛṣṭa-pūrvo hi khe 'pi deśāntarasya saḥ*  
> *jñāpako vidyamānasya mūḍha-buddhi-vyapekṣayā // 5 //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> An adjunct is indeed something already observed, even in the case of another region of space.  
> It is an indicator of what is already there, and that only with reference to a confused understanding.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| upādhi-dṛṣṭa-pūrvaḥ | compound | nom sg masc | previously observed as an adjunct |
| hi | hi | indecl | indeed |
| khe | kha | loc sg neut | in space |
| api | api | indecl | even |
| deśāntarasya | deśāntara | gen sg neut | of another region/place |
| saḥ | tad | nom sg masc | that |
| jñāpakaḥ | jñāpaka | nom sg masc | indicator |
| vidyamānasya | vidyamāna | gen sg masc/neut | of what already exists |
| mūḍha-buddhi-vyapekṣayā | compound | inst sg fem | in dependence on a deluded intellect |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *upādhi-dṛṣṭa-pūrvaḥ* [tatpuruṣa]: *upādhiḥ dṛṣṭaḥ pūrvaṃ yasya saḥ*; sense: known beforehand as an adjunctive delimiter.
- *deśāntarasya* [tatpuruṣa]: *deśasya antarasya*.
- *mūḍha-buddhi-vyapekṣayā* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *mūḍhāyā buddheḥ vyapekṣayā*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ/predicate: *saḥ jñāpakaḥ*
- viṣaya: *vidyamānasya*
- adhikaraṇa: *khe api*
- upādāna of epistemic reference: *mūḍha-buddhi-vyapekṣayā*

**Verbal modality:**
- Nominal sentence; finite verb understood.

**Note:**
The example is compressed, but the thought is straightforward: delimitation in ordinary cases, such as differentiating one region of space from another, does not create what is delimited. It only helps a finite mind pick out what is already there. Madhva is thus refusing the illicit slide from epistemic aid to metaphysical constitutor.

### Verse 6

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *na ced upādhi-sambandha ekadeśe 'tha sarvagaḥ*  
> *ekadeśe 'navasthā syāt sarvagaś cen na bhedakaḥ // 6 //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> And if the relation to the adjunct is either localised in one part or else all-pervasive,  
> then, if localised, there is regress; and if all-pervasive, it is not a differentiator.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| na | na | indecl | not / and not |
| cet | cet | indecl | if |
| upādhi-sambandhaḥ | compound | nom sg masc | relation to an adjunct |
| ekadeśe | ekadeśa | loc sg masc | in one part; in one region |
| atha | atha | indecl | or else |
| sarvagaḥ | sarvaga | nom sg masc | all-pervasive |
| ekadeśe | ekadeśa | loc sg masc | in one part |
| anavasthā | anavasthā | nom sg fem | regress; non-termination |
| syāt | as | विधिलिङ्, 3 sg parasmaipada | would be |
| sarvagaḥ | sarvaga | nom sg masc | all-pervasive |
| cet | cet | indecl | if |
| na | na | indecl | not |
| bhedakaḥ | bhedaka | nom sg masc | differentiator |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *upādhi-sambandhaḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *upādheḥ sambandhaḥ*.
- *ekadeśa* [karmadhāraya]: *ekaḥ deśaḥ*.
- *sarvagaḥ* [bahuvrīhi/agentive]: *sarvaṃ gacchati iti*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- subject: *upādhi-sambandhaḥ*
- alternative predicates/conditions: *ekadeśe*, *sarvagaḥ*
- doṣa-result: *anavasthā*, *na bhedakaḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *syāt*: विधिलिङ् / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
The structure is exact: the adjunct-relation must be either partial or pervasive. A partial relation requires a further principle to explain why it is partial rather than elsewhere; a pervasive relation cannot differentiate at all. This is the same exhaustiveness that drives Madhva's anti-*māyā* and anti-*mithyātva* tracts.

### Verse 7

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *sukha-duḥkhādi-bhogaś ca svarūpaikye na bhedakaḥ*

**Note:**
This verse articulates the residual opponent move rather than Madhva's own settled conclusion: experiential variation might be assigned to adjuncts while substantial unity is retained. The next two verses attack exactly that move through the case of the yogin's multiple embodiments.

### Verse 8

### Verse 10

### Verse 11

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *ātma-svabhāva-bhedasya vidoṣatvena cākhilaḥ*  
> *pratyakṣādi-virodhāc ca duṣṭaḥ pakṣo 'yam añjasā // 11 //*

The colophon matters historically because the short polemics circulate both singly and in collected form. The designation *Ānandatīrtha Bhagavatpādācārya* is the standard authorial style in the Mādhva transmission.

---

## Editorial closing

What this passage settles is narrow but decisive. It does not yet prove the whole of Madhva's positive ontology; that work belongs to larger treatises. It does settle that *upādhi* cannot serve as the mediating device by which real experienced difference is downgraded without remainder into a merely conditional appearance. The tract leaves open, on purpose, the broader constructive elaboration of intrinsic difference, gradation among selves, and the positive theory of *viśeṣa*; those are taken up elsewhere, above all in *Tattvodyota* and in the extended arguments of *Anuvyākhyāna* 2.2 and 1.4. It also leaves implicit the more technical Navya-Nyāya recasting later achieved by Vyāsatīrtha in *Nyāyāmṛta*. But for the immediate anti-Advaita sequence the work is terminal. Once *māyā* has been denied coherence, once the inference for *mithyātva* has been denied force, and once *upādhi* has been denied metaphysical efficacy, the principal escape-routes are closed. What remains is not an unresolved middle position but Madhva's own realist reading of scripture and experience.
