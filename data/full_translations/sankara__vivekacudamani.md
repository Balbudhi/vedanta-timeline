## Editorial introduction

This unit is the first fully explicit pedagogical compression of the work. Verse 18 announces that four disciplines are required; verses 19-30 define them one by one; verses 31-32 then make a decisive move by redefining *bhakti* (devoted orientation) as *svasvarūpānusandhāna* or *svātmatattvānusandhāna*, sustained recollection and inquiry into one’s own real nature; verses 33-34 complete the sequence by specifying what sort of teacher is to be approached and how. That makes verses 18-34 the natural extended locus. They begin where the text itself says “here the four means are taught,” and they end just before the disciple’s formal supplication at verse 35. The section is thus a complete preparatory arc rather than an arbitrary anthology.

Within the larger architecture of the *Vivekacūḍāmaṇi*, this is the threshold between praise of human birth and liberation-effort in the opening verses and the more technical discriminations of body, senses, mind, witness, and Brahman that follow. The section states, in compact form, what kind of person can fruitfully undertake that later inquiry. It also shows the characteristic register of the school-ascribed Śaṅkara: *viveka* (discrimination), *vairāgya* (dispassion), *śama* (inner quieting), *dama* (sense-restraint), *uparati* (cessation from outward supports), *titikṣā* (forbearance), *śraddhā* (cognitive trust in scripture and teacher as valid means of knowledge), *samādhāna* (steady fixing of the intellect), and *mumukṣutva* (desire for liberation) are not optional devout excellences but epistemic and existential prerequisites.

Thematically, the unit stands close to the securely-authored *Upadeśa-Sāhasrī*, especially II.1.1-9, where the teacher is instructed to teach only a student already marked by renunciation, discipline, and serious desire for release. It also anticipates later Advaita’s habit of reading contemplative steadfastness and devotion not as competitors but as convergent intensifications of self-knowledge. The crucial point is grammatical as well as doctrinal: the section defines the means not by metaphor but by carefully predicated nominal sentences. It is a program for liberation stated as a sequence of exact identifications.

## Translation

### Verse 18

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *sādhanāny atra catvāri kathitāni manīṣibhiḥ / yeṣu satsv eva sanniṣṭhā yadabhāve na sidhyati //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Here four disciplines have been stated by the wise.  
> Only when these are present does firm establishment attain completion; in the absence of any one of them, it does not succeed.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| sādhanāni | sādhana- | nom/acc pl neut | disciplines; means |
| atra | atra | indecl | here; in this context |
| catvāri | catur- | nom/acc pl neut numeral | four |
| kathitāni | √kath | PPP, nom/acc pl neut | have been stated |
| manīṣibhiḥ | manīṣin- | inst pl masc | by the wise |
| yeṣu | yad- | loc pl neut | in which; when these |
| satsu | sat- (from √as) | loc pl neut pres. part. | being present |
| eva | eva | indecl | alone; only |
| sanniṣṭhā | sam-ni-√sthā | fem nom sg noun | firm establishment; consummation |
| yadabhāve | yad- + abhāva- | loc sg masc/neut compound | in the absence of which |
| na | na | indecl | not |
| sidhyati | √sidh | laṭ, 3 sg, parasmaipada, active | succeeds; is accomplished |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *yadabhāva* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa, with relative force]: *yasya abhāvaḥ*, “the absence of whichever one.”

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ (implicit in passive): the “wise” as the stating agency, expressed instrumentally by *manīṣibhiḥ*
- karman: *sādhanāni catvāri*
- adhikaraṇa: *atra*; *yeṣu satsu*
- nimitta/condition: *yadabhāve*

**Verbal modality:**
- *kathitāni*: past passive participle; passive predication
- *sidhyati*: laṭ / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
The verse is programmatic. The feminine *sanniṣṭhā* is left without an explicit noun, but the sense is clear: firm establishment in the liberating path, or liberation’s effective consummation, depends upon all four disciplines taken together. Compare *Upadeśa-Sāhasrī* II.1.1-5, where the teacher is told to examine whether the student possesses the requisite dispositions before instruction begins.

### Verse 19

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *ādau nityānityavastuvivekaḥ parigamyate*  
> *ihāmutraphalabhogavirāgastadanantaram*  
> *śamādiṣaṭkasampattirmumukṣutvamiti sphuṭam //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> First, discrimination between the eternal and the non-eternal reality is to be grasped.  
> Thereafter comes dispassion toward enjoyment of fruits here and hereafter.  
> Then the sixfold wealth beginning with *śama*; and then, plainly, the desire for liberation.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| ādau | ādi- | loc sg used adverbially | at the beginning; first |
| nityānityavastuvivekaḥ | nitya-anitya-vastu-viveka- | nom sg masc compound | discrimination between the eternal and the non-eternal |
| parigamyate | pari-√gam | laṭ, 3 sg, ātmanepada/passive | is understood; is grasped |
| iha | iha | indecl | here, in this world |
| amutra | amutra | indecl | there, in the next world |
| phalabhogavirāgaḥ | phala-bhoga-virāga- | nom sg masc compound | dispassion toward enjoyment of fruits |
| tadanantaram | tad-anantara- | indecl/acc sg used adverbially | immediately after that |
| śamādiṣaṭkasampattiḥ | śama-ādi-ṣaṭka-sampatti- | nom sg fem compound | the sixfold wealth beginning with *śama* |
| mumukṣutvam | mumukṣutva- | nom sg/acc sg neut | desire for liberation |
| iti | iti | indecl | thus |
| sphuṭam | sphuṭa- | indecl/adverbial neut | clearly; plainly |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *nityānityavastuviveka* [itaretara-dvandva within tatpuruṣa]: *nityaṃ ca anityaṃ ca vastu; tayoḥ vivekaḥ*.
- *phalabhogavirāga* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *phalabhogasya virāgaḥ*.
- *śamādiṣaṭkasampatti* [tatpuruṣa]: *śamādikaṃ ṣaṭkaṃ yasya sā sampattiḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- viṣaya of understanding: *nityānityavastuvivekaḥ*
- sequence marker: *ādau*, *tadanantaram*
- viṣaya of dispassion: *phalabhoga-*

**Verbal modality:**
- *parigamyate*: laṭ / passive-middle / passive sense

**Note:**
The verse compresses the whole preparatory discipline into a four-step order: discrimination, dispassion, sixfold inward discipline, and liberation-desire. The ellipsis after *śamādiṣaṭkasampattiḥ mumukṣutvam iti* is deliberate: the verb “is to be grasped” governs the list. Compare the compact instructional frame of *Upadeśa-Sāhasrī* II.1.2, where renunciation, *śama*, *dama*, and allied virtues are likewise preconditions for teaching.

### Verse 20

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *brahma satyaṃ jaganmithyety evaṃrūpo viniścayaḥ / so 'yaṃ nityānityavastuvivekaḥ samudāhṛtaḥ //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> “Brahman is real; the world is *mithyā*”: a determination of this form  
> is what is here called discrimination between the eternal and the non-eternal.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| brahma | brahman- | nom sg neut | Brahman |
| satyam | satya- | nom sg neut/predicative | real; true |
| jagat | jagat- | nom sg neut | the world |
| mithyā | mithyā | indecl/predicative adverbial | as dependently real; erroneous in status |
| iti | iti | indecl | thus |
| evaṃrūpaḥ | evaṃ-rūpa- | nom sg masc compound | of such a form |
| viniścayaḥ | viniścaya- | nom sg masc | settled determination |
| saḥ ('so) | tad- | nom sg masc | that |
| ayam | idam- | nom sg masc | this |
| nityānityavastuvivekaḥ | nitya-anitya-vastu-viveka- | nom sg masc compound | discrimination between eternal and non-eternal |
| samudāhṛtaḥ | sam-ud-ā-√hṛ | PPP, nom sg masc | has been declared; is designated |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *evaṃrūpa* [karmadhāraya]: *evaṃ rūpaḥ*, “of such character.”
- *nityānityavastuviveka* [itaretara-dvandva within tatpuruṣa]: *nityaṃ cānityaṃ ca vastu; tayoḥ vivekaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- predicative equations: *brahma satyam*; *jagat mithyā*
- viśeṣaṇa-viśeṣya: *evaṃrūpaḥ viniścayaḥ*
- predicate identification: *sa ayam ... vivekaḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- no finite verb in the first hemistich; nominal predication
- *samudāhṛtaḥ*: past passive participle; passive predication

**Note:**
This is one of the best-known formulas associated with later Advaita pedagogy. Its force must be handled carefully. *Mithyā* does not mean sheer non-being (*asat*). It means dependent, superimposed, and ultimately sublatable status. In Śaṅkara’s securely-authored corpus the thought is distributed through argument rather than cast in this exact mnemonic sentence; compare especially the anti-superimposition logic of the *Adhyāsa-Bhāṣya* and the insistence, in *Upadeśa-Sāhasrī* I.11.15, that liberation is knowledge-born rather than action-produced.

### Verse 21

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *tadvairāgyaṃ jihāsā yā darśanaśravaṇādibhiḥ / dehādibrahmaparyante hyanitye bhogavastuni //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> That dispassion is the wish to abandon  
> the object of enjoyment that is impermanent, extending from the body up to Brahmā, through seeing, hearing, and the like.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| tat | tad- | nom/acc sg neut used anaphorically | that |
| vairāgyam | vairāgya- | nom/acc sg neut | dispassion |
| jihāsā | jihāsā- (desiderative from √hā) | nom sg fem | desire to abandon |
| yā | yad- | nom sg fem | which |
| darśanaśravaṇādibhiḥ | darśana-śravaṇa-ādi- | inst pl neut compound | by seeing, hearing, and the rest |
| dehādibrahmaparyante | deha-ādi-brahma-paryanta- | loc sg neut compound | in that which extends from body to Brahmā |
| hi | hi | indecl | indeed |
| anitye | anitya- | loc sg neut | impermanent |
| bhogavastuni | bhoga-vastu- | loc sg neut compound | object of enjoyment |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *darśanaśravaṇādi* [dvandva + ādi]: *darśanaṃ śravaṇaṃ cādīni ca*.
- *dehādibrahmaparyanta* [tatpuruṣa]: *dehād ārabhya brahma-paryantam*; “from the body up to Brahmā.”
- *bhogavastu* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *bhogasya vastu*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *yā jihāsā*
- karaṇa: *darśanaśravaṇādibhiḥ*
- adhikaraṇa/viṣaya: *anitye bhogavastuni*, specified by *dehādibrahmaparyante*

**Verbal modality:**
- no finite verb; nominal identification: *tad vairāgyaṃ ... jihāsā yā*

**Note:**
The compound *dehādibrahmaparyante* is doctrinally important. *Brahma* here is Brahmā, the creator-god within the cosmic order, not nirguṇa Brahman. The range of enjoyable but non-eternal objects therefore runs from the most immediate embodiment to the highest cosmic office. The verse does not turn the gods into symbols; it places them within the field of the non-eternal when they are objects of enjoyership.

### Verse 22

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *virajya viṣayavrātāddoṣadṛṣṭyā muhurmuhuḥ / svalakṣye niyatāvasthā manasaḥ śama ucyate //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Having become detached from the mass of objects, through repeated seeing of their defects again and again,  
> the mind’s settled abiding in its own proper aim is called *śama*.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| virajya | vi-√rañj / virāga | absolutive | having become detached |
| viṣayavrātāt | viṣaya-vrāta- | abl sg masc | from the host of sense-objects |
| doṣadṛṣṭyā | doṣa-dṛṣṭi- | inst sg fem | by seeing defects |
| muhurmuhuḥ | muhur muhuḥ | indecl | again and again |
| svalakṣye | sva-lakṣya- | loc sg neut | in its own target |
| niyatāvasthā | niyata-avasthā- | nom sg fem compound | settled abiding |
| manasaḥ | manas- | gen sg neut | of the mind |
| śamaḥ | śama- | nom sg masc | inner quieting; tranquility |
| ucyate | √vac | laṭ, 3 sg, ātmanepada/passive | is called |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *viṣayavrāta* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *viṣayāṇāṃ vrātaḥ*, “the whole troop of objects.”
- *doṣadṛṣṭi* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *doṣāṇāṃ dṛṣṭiḥ*, “seeing defects.”
- *svalakṣya* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa/karmadhāraya nuance]: *svasya lakṣyam*.
- *niyatāvasthā* [karmadhāraya]: fixed, regulated abiding.

**Kāraka structure:**
- apādāna: *viṣayavrātāt*
- karaṇa: *doṣadṛṣṭyā*
- adhikaraṇa: *svalakṣye*
- ṣaṣṭhī relation: *manasaḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *virajya*: absolutive
- *ucyate*: laṭ / passive-middle / passive sense

**Note:**
*Śama* is not mere blankness. It is the mind’s redirection away from defect-ridden objects toward its proper target, ultimately the Self. The grammar makes that clear: detachment from the object-cluster is followed not by vacancy but by *niyatāvasthā*, stable abiding. Compare the demand for interior steadiness in *Taittirīya-Bhāṣya*’s closing summary of the qualified seeker as *śānto dānta uparatas titikṣuḥ samāhitaḥ*.

### Verse 23

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *viṣayebhyaḥ parāvartya sthāpanaṃ svasvagolake*  
> *ubhayeṣāmindriyāṇāṃ sa damaḥ parikīrtitaḥ*  
> *bāhyānālambanaṃ vṛttereṣoparatiruttamā //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Turning them back from the objects, and placing each within its own proper sphere,  
> that is declared to be *dama* for both classes of the senses.  
> The mental modification’s non-reliance on external supports: this is the highest *uparati*.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| viṣayebhyaḥ | viṣaya- | abl pl masc | from the objects |
| parāvartya | parā-√vṛt | absolutive/gerund causative sense | having turned back |
| sthāpanam | √sthā (caus. sthāpay-) | nom/acc sg neut action noun | placing; establishing |
| svasvagolake | sva-sva-golaka- | loc sg masc/neut | in each one’s own sphere |
| ubhayeṣām | ubhaya- | gen dual | of both |
| indriyāṇām | indriya- | gen pl neut | of the senses |
| saḥ | tad- | nom sg masc | that |
| damaḥ | dama- | nom sg masc | restraint of the senses |
| parikīrtitaḥ | pari-√kīrt | PPP, nom sg masc | has been declared |
| bāhyānālambanam | bāhya-anālambana- | nom/acc sg neut | non-dependence on what is external |
| vṛtteḥ | vṛtti- | gen sg fem | of the mental movement |
| eṣā | etad- | nom sg fem | this |
| uparatiḥ | uparati- | nom sg fem | cessation; withdrawal |
| uttamā | uttama- | nom sg fem | highest |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *svasvagolaka* [reduplicative tatpuruṣa]: *svasmin svasmin golake*.
- *bāhyānālambana* [nañ-tatpuruṣa]: *bāhyeṣu anālambanam*, “non-resting upon externals.”

**Kāraka structure:**
- apādāna: *viṣayebhyaḥ*
- adhikaraṇa: *svasvagolake*
- ṣaṣṭhī: *ubhayeṣām indriyāṇām*, *vṛtteḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *parāvartya*: absolutive
- *parikīrtitaḥ*: past passive participle

**Note:**
*Ubhayeṣām indriyāṇām* means the two sets of senses: cognitive and active. The verse therefore distinguishes *dama* from *uparati*. *Dama* is regulated redirection of the organs; *uparati* is a deeper cessation of the mind’s outward dependence itself. The distinction is standard in Advaita pedagogical literature, and it prevents “sense-restraint” from being reduced to merely behavioral austerity.

### Verse 24

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *sahanaṃ sarvaduḥkhānāmapratīkārapūrvakam / cintāvilāparahitaṃ sā titikṣā nigadyate //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Endurance of all sufferings, not preceded by retaliatory counter-measures,  
> and free from anxiety and lamentation: that is called *titikṣā*.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| sahanam | √sah | nom/acc sg neut action noun | endurance |
| sarvaduḥkhānām | sarva-duḥkha- | gen pl neut | of all sufferings |
| apratīkārapūrvakam | a-pratīkāra-pūrvaka- | nom/acc sg neut compound | not preceded by retaliation/remedy |
| cintāvilāparahitam | cintā-vilāpa-rahita- | nom/acc sg neut compound | free from worry and lament |
| sā | tad- | nom sg fem | that |
| titikṣā | titikṣā- | nom sg fem | forbearance |
| nigadyate | ni-√gad | laṭ, 3 sg, ātmanepada/passive | is said; is called |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *sarvaduḥkha* [karmadhāraya/tatpuruṣa]: *sarvāṇi duḥkhāni*.
- *apratīkārapūrvaka* [nañ-tatpuruṣa]: *pratīkāraḥ pūrvaṃ yasya na bhavati*.
- *cintāvilāparahita* [dvandva + bahuvrīhi/tatpuruṣa]: *cintayā vilāpena ca rahitam*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- ṣaṣṭhī: *sarvaduḥkhānām*
- predicate identification: *sā titikṣā*

**Verbal modality:**
- *nigadyate*: laṭ / passive-middle / passive sense

**Note:**
The verse rules out two distortions at once. *Titikṣā* is not aggressive self-assertion, since it excludes retaliatory response; nor is it inward theatrical misery, since it excludes mental brooding and lament. It is disciplined endurance. This matches the contemplative-ethical temperament described in the secure bhāṣya corpus rather than any quietist collapse into helplessness.

### Verse 25

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *śāstrasya guruvākyasya satyabuddhyavadhāraṇam / sā śraddhā kathitā sadbhiryayā vastūpalabhyate //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Determinate holding of scripture and the teacher’s word by means of the judgment “this is true”:  
> that is called *śraddhā* by the good, by which the Reality is apprehended.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| śāstrasya | śāstra- | gen sg neut | of scripture |
| guruvākyasya | guru-vākya- | gen sg neut | of the teacher’s word |
| satyabuddhyavadhāraṇam | satya-buddhi-avadhāraṇa- | nom/acc sg neut compound | ascertainment through the cognition of truth |

| śraddhā | śraddhā- | nom sg fem | trust; faith |

| sadbhiḥ | sat- | inst pl masc | by the good; by the truthful ones |
| yayā | yad- | inst sg fem | by which |
| vastu | vastu- | nom/acc sg neut | the Reality; the real entity |
| upalabhyate | upa-√labh | laṭ, 3 sg, ātmanepada/passive | is apprehended |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *guruvākya* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *guroḥ vākyaṃ*.
- *satyabuddhyavadhāraṇa* [tatpuruṣa]: *satyā buddhiḥ tayā avadhāraṇam*; a fixing by the cognition “it is true.”

**Kāraka structure:**
- viṣaya of ascertainment: *śāstrasya guruvākyasya*
- karaṇa: *yayā*
- kartṛ (passive agency): *sadbhiḥ*

Compare *Upadeśa-Sāhasrī* II.1.6-8, where the teacher is to ground the student in the Upaniṣadic sentences as authoritative disclosure of the Self.

### Verse 27

**Sanskrit (IAST):**

### Verse 28

## Editorial closing

By the end of this passage, the work has settled three things. First, liberation requires a determinate preparatory profile; it is not the casual outcome of piety or erudition. Second, the decisive orientation of that profile is epistemic and contemplative: discrimination, dispassion, disciplined inwardness, and a sharpened desire for release. Third, *bhakti* is not discarded but interiorized as sustained self-recollection and then exteriorized again as reverent approach to the guru. What remains open is the content of the inquiry itself. The passage has prepared the aspirant and identified the teacher; it has not yet unfolded the ontology of bondage, Self, non-Self, and release.

That next work begins immediately. Verses 35-40 give the disciple’s supplication; verse 49 formulates the explicit questions about bondage, non-Self, Self, and discrimination; verses 71 onward begin the systematic discrimination of body, senses, mind, and witness; later clusters, especially verses 87-100 and 203-206, carry the analysis into gross body, subtle body, and the *mahāvākya*-borne recognition of identity. In that sense, verses 18-34 are genuinely threshold verses: they do not yet give the doctrine in full, but they settle what kind of person can receive it and under what discipline the reception becomes possible.
