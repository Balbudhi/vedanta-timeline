## Editorial introduction

This file has to begin with a textual limitation. The repository does not contain a clean, continuous Sanskrit transcript of Rāmānuja's *Śrī-Bhāṣya*. What is on disk is a damaged OCR witness of the *Catuḥsūtrī* portion, plus public-domain English retrievals and secondary research notes. It would therefore be irresponsible to pretend to offer a full adhikaraṇa-translation in the usual sense. The natural fallback scope is the recoverable opening block of the *brahmajijñāsādhikaraṇa* on *Brahma-Sūtra* 1.1.1: the prefatory Bodhāyana notice, the sūtra itself, the explanation of *atha* and *ataḥ*, the derivation of *brahmajijñāsā*, and the first positive definition of Brahman as *puruṣottama* endowed with innumerable auspicious qualities.

Even in this reduced state, the opening is doctrinally decisive. Rāmānuja does not begin, as Śaṅkara does, with a phenomenology of *adhyāsa*; he begins with a hermeneutic and grammatical delimitation of what the inquiry is about. The object of inquiry is not an indeterminate absolute stripped bare of all determination, but *Brahman* as the supreme Lord, *sarveśvara*, whose greatness consists both in intrinsic nature and in an inexhaustible plenitude of *kalyāṇa-guṇa*-s. The lexical work already carries the polemical burden. What later becomes explicit in *Śrī-Bhāṣya* 1.1.2, 1.1.4, and in the long anti-Advaitin discussions of 1.1.1 is already implicit here: grammar, semantic range, and soteriological eligibility are all ordered toward a *saviśeṣa* Brahman. The same constructive direction reappears in *Vedārtha-Saṃgraha* §§19-34 and in the *Gītā-Bhāṣya*'s insistence that Brahman is the personal Lord who is the proper object of sustained contemplative knowledge.

### Opening notice 1

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *bhagavad-bodhayana-kṛtāṃ vistīrṇāṃ brahmasūtra-vṛttiṃ pūrvācāryāḥ saṃcekṣipuḥ.*

**English (line-by-line):**
> The earlier teachers abridged the extensive exposition on the *Brahma-Sūtra*-s composed by the venerable Bodhāyana.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| bhagavad-bodhayana-kṛtām | *bhagavad-bodhayana-kṛta* | acc. sg. fem. | composed by the revered Bodhāyana |
| vistīrṇām | *vistīrṇa* | acc. sg. fem. | extensive, expanded |
| brahmasūtra-vṛttim | *brahmasūtra-vṛtti* | acc. sg. fem. | exposition on the *Brahma-Sūtra*-s |
| pūrvācāryāḥ | *pūrvācārya* | nom. pl. masc. | the earlier teachers |
| saṃcekṣipuḥ | √*kṣip* with *sam-* | perfect, 3rd pl., parasmaipada | abridged, compressed |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *bhagavad-bodhayana-kṛtām* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa + kṛdanta]: *bhagavatā bodhāyanena kṛtām*.
- *brahmasūtra-vṛttim* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *brahmasūtrāṇāṃ vṛttim*.
- *pūrvācāryāḥ* [karmadhāraya]: *pūrvāś cāryāś ca* in the sense of former teachers.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *pūrvācāryāḥ*
- karman: *bhagavad-bodhayana-kṛtāṃ vistīrṇāṃ brahmasūtra-vṛttim*

**Verbal modality:**
- *saṃcekṣipuḥ*: liṭ / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
The OCR is corrupt at several points in this sentence, but the restoration is secure. The line stakes a lineage-claim: Rāmānuja's exposition stands not as private innovation but as a compression of an already authoritative Bodhāyana-vṛtti. This appeal to prior *ācārya*-transmission is part of the work's self-positioning from the start.

### Opening notice 2

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *tan-matānusāreṇa sūtrākṣarāṇi vyākhyāsyante.*

**English (line-by-line):**
> In accordance with that view, the very words of the sūtra-s will be explained.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| tan-matānusāreṇa | *tan-matānusāra* | inst. sg. masc. | in accordance with that doctrine |
| sūtrākṣarāṇi | *sūtrākṣara* | nom. pl. neut. | the syllables/words of the sūtra-s |
| vyākhyāsyante | √*khyā* with *vi-ā-* | simple future, 3rd pl., ātmanepada | will be explained |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *tan-matānusāreṇa* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *tasya matasya anusāreṇa*.
- *sūtrākṣarāṇi* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *sūtrāṇām akṣarāṇi*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- karaṇa: *tan-matānusāreṇa*
- kartṛ: implicit commentator
- karman: *sūtrākṣarāṇi*

**Verbal modality:**
- *vyākhyāsyante*: lṛṭ / ātmanepada / passive-middle future

**Note:**
The phrase *sūtrākṣarāṇi* is important. Rāmānuja presents himself not as departing from the sūtra-text but as unfolding its very wording under the guidance of a received tradition. Compare the same traditional self-location in the prefatory posture of *Vedārtha-Saṃgraha*.

### 1.1.1

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *athāto brahmajijñāsā.*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Now, therefore, the desire to know Brahman.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| atha | indeclinable | avyaya | now; next in sequence |
| ataḥ | indeclinable | avyaya | therefore; from this reason |
| brahmajijñāsā | *brahma-jijñāsā* | nom. sg. fem. | inquiry into Brahman; desire to know Brahman |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *brahmajijñāsā* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *brahmaṇaḥ jijñāsā*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- no overt finite verb; nominal sūtra
- viṣaya implied by compound analysis: *brahma*

**Verbal modality:**
- no finite verb present

**Note:**
The compressed sūtra is programmatic, and Rāmānuja immediately unpacks both particles rather than treating them as ornamental. Thibaut's public-domain rendering, preserved locally, confirms the same twofold force: sequence and reason.

### Commentary 1

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *atrāyam athaśabda ānantarye bhavati.*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Here, this word *atha* has the sense of immediate succession.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| atra | indeclinable | avyaya | here |
| ayam | *idam* | nom. sg. masc. | this |
| athaśabdaḥ | *atha-śabda* | nom. sg. masc. | the word *atha* |
| ānantarye | *ānantarya* | loc. sg. neut. | in immediate sequence |
| bhavati | √*bhū* | laṭ, 3rd sg., parasmaipada | is; has the force of |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *athaśabdaḥ* [karmadhāraya]: *atha iti śabdaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- adhikaraṇa: *ānantarye*
- kartṛ: *athaśabdaḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *bhavati*: laṭ / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
This is the first grammatical delimitation. The inquiry into Brahman is not random; it comes after something. What that antecedent is becomes explicit in the next sentences and is tied to prior Vedic study and the recognition of the limits of ritual fruit.

**Sanskrit (IAST):**

**English (line-by-line):**
> The word *ataḥ* conveys the sense of causal connection with what has occurred.

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| ataḥśabdaḥ | *ataḥ-śabda* | nom. sg. masc. | the word *ataḥ* |
| vṛttasya | *vṛtta* | gen. sg. neut. | of what has occurred |
| hetubhāve | *hetu-bhāva* | loc. sg. masc. | in the state of being a reason; as causal ground |

- *ataḥśabdaḥ* [karmadhāraya]: *ataḥ iti śabdaḥ*.
- *hetubhāve* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa in sense]: *hetoḥ bhāve*.

**Note:**

### Commentary 3

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *adhīta-sāṅga-śiraska-vedasya adhigatālpa-sthira-phala-kevala-karma-jñānatayā saṃjāta-mokṣābhilāṣasya ananta-sthira-phala-brahmajijñāsā tadanantara-bhāvinī.*

**English (line-by-line):**
> For one who has studied the Veda together with its auxiliaries and head-texts, who through the cognition that mere ritual action has a small and unstable fruit has come to desire liberation, the inquiry into Brahman, whose fruit is infinite and abiding, arises thereafter.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| adhīta-sāṅga-śiraska-vedasya | *adhīta-sāṅga-śiraska-veda* | gen. sg. masc. | of one who has studied the Veda together with limbs and head-texts |
| adhigatālpa-sthira-phala-kevala-karma-jñānatayā | *adhigata-alpa-sthira-phala-kevala-karma-jñānatā* | inst. sg. fem. | through the knowledge that mere ritual action has slight and unstable fruit |
| saṃjāta-mokṣābhilāṣasya | *saṃjāta-mokṣābhilāṣa* | gen. sg. masc. | of one in whom desire for liberation has arisen |
| ananta-sthira-phala-brahmajijñāsā | *ananta-sthira-phala-brahma-jijñāsā* | nom. sg. fem. | the inquiry into Brahman whose fruit is infinite and stable |
| tadanantara-bhāvinī | *tad-anantara-bhāvinī* | nom. sg. fem. | occurring immediately after that |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *adhīta-sāṅga-śiraska-vedasya* [bahuvrīhi]: *adhītāni sāṅgāni śiraskāni ca vedam yasya saḥ*.
- *adhigatālpa-sthira-phala-kevala-karma-jñānatayā* [complex tatpuruṣa]: *kevalasya karmaṇaḥ alpam asthiraṃ ca phalam iti adhigata-jñānatayā*.
- *saṃjāta-mokṣābhilāṣasya* [bahuvrīhi]: *saṃjāto mokṣasya abhilāṣo yasya saḥ*.
- *ananta-sthira-phala-brahmajijñāsā* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *anantaṃ sthiraṃ ca phalaṃ yasyā sā brahmajijñāsā*.
- *tadanantara-bhāvinī* [avyayībhāva-tatpuruṣa]: *tasya anantaram bhāvinī*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- ṣaṣṭhī of qualified aspirant: *adhīta...vedasya*, *saṃjāta...abhilāṣasya*
- karaṇa: *adhigata...jñānatayā*
- kartṛ: *ananta-sthira-phala-brahmajijñāsā*

**Verbal modality:**
- *bhāvinī*: present participial feminine, active sense

**Note:**
This is Rāmānuja's eligibility-structure. The turn to Brahman is neither anti-Vedic nor anti-ritual; it comes after Vedic training and after the discernment that ritual fruit is finite. The sequence anticipates later *Śrī-Bhāṣya* discussions of the relation between *karma* and *brahmavidyā*, and it resonates with the *Gītā-Bhāṣya*'s graduated pedagogy.

> "The desire to know Brahman": that is the compound *brahmajijñāsā*.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|

### Commentary 5

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *brahmaṇa iti karmaṇi ṣaṣṭhī. kartṛkarmaṇoḥ kṛti (Pāṇini 2.3.65).*

**English (line-by-line):**
> In "brahmaṇaḥ," the genitive is in the sense of the object. "With a kṛt-formation, the genitive may denote agent or object" (Pāṇini 2.3.65).

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| brahmaṇaḥ | *brahman* | gen. sg. neut. | of Brahman |
| iti | indeclinable | avyaya | thus; in the expression |
| karmaṇi | *karman* | loc. sg. neut. | in the object-sense |
| ṣaṣṭhī | *ṣaṣṭhī* | nom. sg. fem. | the genitive case |
| kartṛkarmaṇoḥ | *kartṛ-karman* | gen./loc. dual neut. | of agent and object |
| kṛti | *kṛti* | loc. sg. fem. | in a kṛt-derivative formation |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *kartṛkarmaṇoḥ* [dvandva]: *kartṛ ca karma ca*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- grammatical designation rather than propositional syntax

**Verbal modality:**
- no finite verb

**Note:**
Rāmānuja does not leave the compound at the level of paraphrase; he anchors it in Pāṇinian grammar. The appeal to 2.3.65 is not decorative scholarship. It secures the claim that Brahman is the object to be known, not merely something loosely connected with inquiry. This precision will matter once Brahman's semantic range is filled out.

### Commentary 7

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *sarvatra bṛhattva-guṇa-yogena hi brahmaśabdaḥ.*

### Commentary 8

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *bṛhattvaṃ ca svarūpeṇa guṇaiś ca, yatrānavadhikātiśayaṃ so 'sya mukhyo 'rthaḥ.*

**English (line-by-line):**
> And greatness consists both in essential nature and in qualities; where that greatness is unsurpassed, that is the word's primary meaning.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| bṛhattvam | *bṛhattva* | nom. sg. neut. | greatness |
| ca | indeclinable | avyaya | and |
| svarūpeṇa | *svarūpa* | inst. sg. neut. | by essential nature |
| guṇaiḥ | *guṇa* | inst. pl. masc. | by qualities |
| ca | indeclinable | avyaya | and |
| yatra | indeclinable | avyaya | where |
| anavadhikātiśayam | *anavadhikātiśaya* | acc. sg. neut. | unsurpassed excellence |
| saḥ | *tad* | nom. sg. masc. | that |
| asya | *idam* | gen. sg. | of this |
| mukhyaḥ | *mukhya* | nom. sg. masc. | primary |
| arthaḥ | *artha* | nom. sg. masc. | meaning |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *anavadhikātiśayam* [bahuvrīhi/tatpuruṣa sense]: *anavadhikaḥ atiśayo yasmin tat*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- karaṇa: *svarūpeṇa*, *guṇaiḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- no overt finite verb; *asti* is understood

Rāmānuja's semantics is doctrinally loaded. The chief sense of *brahman* belongs where greatness is unlimited both intrinsically and qualitatively. ### Commentary 10

**Sanskrit (IAST):**

### Commentary 11

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *ataḥ sarveśvaro jijñāsākarmabhūtaṃ brahma.*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Therefore Brahman, as the object of inquiry, is the Lord of all.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| ataḥ | indeclinable | avyaya | therefore |
| sarveśvaraḥ | *sarva-īśvara* | nom. sg. masc. | the Lord of all |
| jijñāsākarmabhūtam | *jijñāsā-karman-bhūta* | acc. sg. neut. | having the status of the object of inquiry |
| brahma | *brahman* | acc. sg. neut. | Brahman |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *sarveśvaraḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *sarveṣām īśvaraḥ*.
- *jijñāsākarmabhūtam* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa + kṛdanta]: *jijñāsāyāḥ karma bhūtaṃ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- predicate relation between *sarveśvaraḥ* and *brahma*
- object designation: *jijñāsākarmabhūtam brahma*

**Verbal modality:**
- no overt finite verb; *asti* is understood

**Note:**
The syntactic point and the doctrinal point now coincide. Once the genitive in *brahmajijñāsā* is taken as objective, and once *brahman* is given its principal sense, the object sought is *sarveśvara* and not an undifferentiated absolute.

**Note:**
The nominal derivation matters because *jijñāsā* is not yet fulfilled knowledge but a movement toward it. Rāmānuja's wording preserves the dynamic orientation of the aspirant while still making the object and method susceptible to śāstric regulation.

### Commentary 13

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *icchāyā iṣyamāṇa-pradhānatvād iṣyamāṇaṃ jñānam iha vidhīyate.*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Because in desire the thing desired is primary, what is enjoined here is the knowledge being sought.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| icchāyāḥ | *icchā* | gen. sg. fem. | of desire |
| iṣyamāṇa-pradhānatvāt | *iṣyamāṇa-pradhānatva* | abl. sg. neut. | because of the predominance of what is desired |
| iṣyamāṇam | √*iṣ* | pres. pass. participle, acc. sg. neut. | that which is sought |
| jñānam | *jñāna* | acc. sg. neut. | knowledge |
| iha | indeclinable | avyaya | here |
| vidhīyate | √*dhā* with *vi-* | laṭ, 3rd sg., ātmanepada passive | is prescribed; is enjoined |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *iṣyamāṇa-pradhānatvāt* [bahuvrīhi/tatpuruṣa]: *iṣyamāṇasya pradhānatvāt*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- hetu: *icchāyāḥ ... pradhānatvāt*
- karman: *iṣyamāṇaṃ jñānam*
- adhikaraṇa: *iha*

**Verbal modality:**
- *vidhīyate*: laṭ / ātmanepada / passive

**Note:**
The line shifts from lexical analysis to *vidhi*-logic. What the sūtra institutes is not a bare emotion called "desire," but a regulated movement toward the knowledge of Brahman. This is why the opening seamlessly passes from grammar into soteriology.

---

## Editorial closing

What this opening settles is already substantial. It settles that the inquiry of 1.1.1 is subsequent to disciplined Vedic formation; that its object is grammatically objective, not merely associative; and that the chief sense of *brahman* is *sarveśvara*, the *puruṣottama* possessed of unlimited auspicious qualities. In short, the semantic ground of Viśiṣṭādvaita is laid before the major polemics have even begun. What it does not yet settle is the full metaphysical architecture by which world, selves, and Brahman are related, nor the explicit demolition of rival readings. Those tasks are taken up immediately in the continuation of 1.1.1, then in 1.1.2 (*janmādy asya yataḥ*) and 1.1.4 (*tat tu samanvayāt*), and are given freer constructive statement in *Vedārtha-Saṃgraha* §§19-34 and §§76 ff. The present excerpt therefore belongs to the threshold of the work: not the whole house, but the doorway through which Rāmānuja insists the reader must enter.
