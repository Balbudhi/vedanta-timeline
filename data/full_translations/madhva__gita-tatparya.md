# Gītā-Tātparya-Nirṇaya

**By:** Madhva (Ānandatīrtha)
**Genre:** *tātparya-nirṇaya* (declaration of purport) on the *Bhagavad-Gītā*

---

## Status

The Sanskrit text of Madhva's *Gītā-Tātparya-Nirṇaya* is not currently in our corpus. The Vedānta interactive timeline does not have a defensible Sanskrit witness on disk for this text, and producing an annotated translation would mean fabricating engagement we cannot ground.

What is widely known about this work:

- A separate, more compact companion to Madhva's *Gītā-Bhāṣya*. Where the bhāṣya glosses the verses, the *Tātparya-Nirṇaya* states what each *adhyāya* and each cluster of verses *means to settle*: it is Madhva's declaration of where the polemical and doctrinal weight of the Gītā falls, anchored by *pramāṇa* citations from the *Brahma-Tarka*, *Pāñcarātra*, *Mahābhārata*, and *Purāṇa* literature.
- The work is structured *adhyāya* by *adhyāya*, often quoting external scriptural witnesses to fix the reading of contested verses. It is a primary source for Madhva's doctrines of *taratamya* (real gradation among *jīvas*), *bheda-pañcaka* (the five real differences), and *Viṣṇu-paratva*.

What is in our corpus that bears on it:

- Madhva's `core_thesis` and engaged_works summary in the timeline entry (see https://balbudhi.github.io/vedanta-timeline/ and locate Madhva).
- Files for Madhva's *Gītā-Bhāṣya*, *Brahma-Sūtra-Bhāṣya*, *Anuvyākhyāna*, *Mahābhārata-Tātparya-Nirṇaya*, and *Bhāgavata-Tātparya-Nirṇaya*, which together articulate the *tātparya*-method and the doctrines it supports.
