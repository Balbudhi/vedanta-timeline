## Editorial introduction

This file is necessarily a fallback. The thinker record for Madhva lists *Gītā-Tātparya-Nirṇaya* as a securely authored companion to the *Gītā-Bhāṣya*, but no Sanskrit text of the *Tātparya* itself could be found in the local corpus on 2026-05-10, and the `madhva.json` thinker record supplies no `key_passages` for this work. Under those conditions, the honest task is not to counterfeit a full-text edition, but to mark the absence and to reconstruct, as cautiously as possible, the doctrinal pressure-points that the local materials explicitly associate with the work. Those pressure-points are clear enough. The work-summary in `site/data/thinkers/madhva.json` says that the *Tātparya* establishes the *Gītā*'s purport by selecting decisive verses across the eighteen chapters, especially those that concern Kṛṣṇa's status as Viṣṇu, the *jīva*'s *paratantra* dependence, the ordering of *karma*, *jñāna*, and *bhakti*, and the graded ontology read out of chapters 10, 11, 13, and 15. The same local record explicitly names 7.19, 9.22, 10.20, and 18.66 as load-bearing, while the glossary files identify 15.16-17 as unretrieved but primary Mādhva loci for *kūṭastha* and *puruṣa*.

That is why the present scope is a dossier of representative anchors rather than a fabricated chapter-translation. Each Sanskrit item below is from the base *Bhagavad-Gītā*, not from Madhva's absent *Tātparya* text. The notes, however, state what can and cannot be responsibly inferred about Madhva's use of these verses from the local evidence. The available local secondary materials are these: `site/data/thinkers/madhva.json`; `site/data/glossary/kutastha.json`, `purusa.json`, `karma.json`, `amsa.json`, and allied glossary files; the already completed [madhva__gita-bhasya.md](/orcd/home/002/eeshan/philosophy/site/data/full_translations/madhva__gita-bhasya.md); `sources/sanskrit/10_bhagavad_gita.md`; and one merely index-level mention in `materials/primary_texts/sanskrit/_kashmir_saivism/philosophy-of-madhvacarya-bnk-acrobat-ocr.txt` ("Gita-Tatparya on Visesas 73, 74"). The result is not a substitute for the missing primary text. It is a bounded, explicit reconstruction of the argument-space to which the missing text belongs.

## Translation

### Bhagavad-Gītā 7.19 (anchor explicitly named in local Madhva summaries)

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *bahūnāṃ janmanām ante jñānavān māṃ prapadyate | vāsudevaḥ sarvam iti sa mahātmā sudurlabhaḥ ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> At the end of many births, the one who possesses knowledge takes refuge in me.  
> "Vāsudeva is all": thus, that great-souled one is very hard to find.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| bahūnām | bahu | gen. pl. | of many |
| janmanām | janman | gen. pl. n. | births |
| ante | anta | loc. sg. m. | at the end |
| jñānavān | jñānavant | nom. sg. m. | possessed of knowledge |
| mām | mad- | acc. sg. pronoun | me |
| prapadyate | pra-√pad | laṭ, 3 sg., ātmanepada | resorts to, takes refuge in |
| vāsudevaḥ | vāsudeva | nom. sg. m. | Vāsudeva |
| sarvam | sarva | nom./acc. sg. n. | all, everything |
| iti | iti | indeclinable | thus, "..." |
| sa | tad | nom. sg. m. | that, he |
| mahātmā | mahātman | nom. sg. m. | great-souled one |
| sudurlabhaḥ | su-durlabha | nom. sg. m. | exceedingly hard to obtain, very rare |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *mahātmā* [karmadhāraya]: *mahān ātmā yasya saḥ*.
- *sudurlabhaḥ* [upapada-like prefixal formation]: *suṣṭhu durlabhaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *jñānavān* / *sa mahātmā*
- karman: *mām*
- adhikaraṇa: *bahūnāṃ janmanām ante*
- iti-vākya / predicative content: *vāsudevaḥ sarvam iti*

**Verbal modality:**
- *prapadyate*: laṭ / ātmanepada / active-middle

**Note:**
The local Madhva summary in `site/data/thinkers/madhva.json` explicitly names this verse as a place where the school's reading refuses numerical identity. The reconstructed Mādhva claim is that "Vāsudeva is all" means that all depends upon Vāsudeva, not that all is exhaustively identical with him. That is the same dependence-mode reading which the same local file attributes to Madhva's handling of 10.20. Compare also the work-summary for *Gītā-Bhāṣya*, which states this point in nearly the same terms.

### Bhagavad-Gītā 9.22 (anchor named as carrying the burden of Madhva's bhakti-reading)

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *ananyāś cintayanto māṃ ye janāḥ paryupāsate | teṣāṃ nityābhiyuktānāṃ yogakṣemaṃ vahāmy aham ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Those persons who, thinking on me with no other object, worship me in full attendance,  
> for them, who are perpetually joined to me, I bear acquisition and preservation.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| ananyāḥ | an-anya | nom. pl. m. | having no other, exclusive |
| cintayantaḥ | √cint | pres. part., nom. pl. m. | thinking, contemplating |
| mām | mad- | acc. sg. pronoun | me |
| ye | yad | nom. pl. m. | who |
| janāḥ | jana | nom. pl. m. | persons |
| paryupāsate | pari-upa-√ās | laṭ, 3 pl., ātmanepada | worship, attend upon |
| teṣām | tad | gen. pl. | for them, of them |
| nityābhiyuktānām | nitya-abhi-yukta | gen. pl. m. | of those constantly joined/engaged |
| yoga-kṣemam | yoga-kṣema | acc. sg. m./n. | acquisition and preservation |
| vahāmi | √vah | laṭ, 1 sg., parasmaipada | I carry, bear |
| aham | aham | nom. sg. pronoun | I |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *nityābhiyuktānām* [karmadhāraya-tatpuruṣa]: *nityaṃ abhiyuktāḥ yeṣāṃ teṣām*.
- *yoga-kṣemam* [dvandva]: *yogaṃ ca kṣemaṃ ca*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *ye janāḥ ananyāḥ cintayantaḥ* / *aham*
- karman: *mām*, *yoga-kṣemam*
- sampradāna-sambandha: *teṣāṃ nityābhiyuktānām*

**Verbal modality:**
- *paryupāsate*: laṭ / ātmanepada / active-middle
- *vahāmi*: laṭ / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
The local Madhva work-summary says that the relevant verses of chapter 9, especially *ananyāś cintayanto mām*, bear the weight of the thesis that *bhakti* is the engine by which *karma* and *jñāna* are rightly ordered toward *aparokṣa-jñāna* of Viṣṇu. The detailed local translation of [madhva__gita-bhasya.md](/orcd/home/002/eeshan/philosophy/site/data/full_translations/madhva__gita-bhasya.md) likewise treats *ananya* and *nityābhiyukta* as grammatically decisive. What cannot be recovered here is Madhva's exact *Tātparya* diction; what can be recovered is the doctrinal use of the verse in the local Mādhva map.

### Bhagavad-Gītā 10.20 (anchor explicitly identified as an anti-identity crux)

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *aham ātmā guḍākeśa sarva-bhūtāśaya-sthitaḥ | aham ādiś ca madhyaṃ ca bhūtānām anta eva ca ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> I am the self, O Guḍākeśa, stationed in the inner seat of all beings.  
> I am also the beginning, the middle, and indeed the end of beings.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| aham | aham | nom. sg. pronoun | I |
| ātmā | ātman | nom. sg. m. | self |
| guḍākeśa | guḍākeśa | voc. sg. m. | O Guḍākeśa |
| sarva-bhūtāśaya-sthitaḥ | sarva-bhūta-āśaya-sthita | nom. sg. m. | situated in the inner seat of all beings |
| aham | aham | nom. sg. pronoun | I |
| ādiḥ | ādi | nom. sg. m. | beginning |
| ca | ca | indeclinable | and |
| madhyam | madhya | nom./acc. sg. n. | middle |
| ca | ca | indeclinable | and |
| bhūtānām | bhūta | gen. pl. n. | of beings |
| antaḥ | anta | nom. sg. m. | end |
| eva | eva | particle | indeed, precisely |
| ca | ca | indeclinable | and |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *guḍākeśa* [traditionally ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa in etymological explanation]: *guḍākāyā īśaḥ*; in usage, Arjuna's epithet.
- *sarva-bhūtāśaya-sthitaḥ* [saptamī-tatpuruṣa + kṛdanta]: *sarveṣāṃ bhūtānām āśaye sthitaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *aham*
- predicative complements: *ātmā*, *ādiḥ*, *madhyam*, *antaḥ*
- sambandha: *bhūtānām*
- adhikaraṇa (inside the compound): *sarva-bhūtāśaye*
- sambodhana: *guḍākeśa*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb is expressed; nominal predication is carried elliptically by the repeated *aham*.

**Note:**
Here the local Madhva summary is unusually precise. It states that Madhva glosses the verse not in an identity-sense but as saying that Kṛṣṇa is the self of all beings "*as their inner controller and locus of dependence*"; it further says that the locative force in *sarva-bhūtāśaya-sthitaḥ* licenses dependence, not numerical oneness. That is exactly the sort of grammatical-philosophical pressure a *tātparya-nirṇaya* text would exploit. Compare the same dependence grammar in the local summaries of 7.19 and in the later cluster named in the *Gītā-Bhāṣya* file at 13.2 and 15.15.

### Bhagavad-Gītā 15.16 (anchor locally indexed for *kūṭastha* and *puruṣa*)

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *dvāv imau puruṣau loke kṣaraś cākṣara eva ca | kṣaraḥ sarvāṇi bhūtāni kūṭastho 'kṣara ucyate ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Two *puruṣa*s are here in the world: the perishable and also the imperishable.  
> The perishable is all beings; the *kūṭastha* is called the imperishable.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| dvau | dvi | nom. dual m. | two |
| imau | idam | nom. dual m. | these |
| puruṣau | puruṣa | nom. dual m. | persons, conscious principles |
| loke | loka | loc. sg. m. | in the world |
| kṣaraḥ | kṣara | nom. sg. m. | perishable |
| ca | ca | indeclinable | and |
| akṣaraḥ | a-kṣara | nom. sg. m. | imperishable |
| eva | eva | particle | indeed, just |
| ca | ca | indeclinable | and |
| kṣaraḥ | kṣara | nom. sg. m. | the perishable |
| sarvāṇi | sarva | nom. pl. n. | all |
| bhūtāni | bhūta | nom. pl. n. | beings |
| kūṭasthaḥ | kūṭastha | nom. sg. m. | the fixed, anvil-like abiding one |
| akṣaraḥ | a-kṣara | nom. sg. m. | the imperishable |
| ucyate | √vac (passive) | laṭ, 3 sg., ātmanepada/passive | is called |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *akṣaraḥ* [nañ-formation]: *na kṣarati iti*.
- *kūṭasthaḥ* [upapada-like compound]: *kūṭe tiṣṭhati iti*; by philosophical usage, the unshaken abiding principle.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *dvau imau puruṣau*
- adhikaraṇa: *loke*
- predicative members: *kṣaraḥ*, *akṣaraḥ*
- nominative equation: *kṣaraḥ = sarvāṇi bhūtāni*; *kūṭasthaḥ = akṣaraḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *ucyate*: laṭ / ātmanepada-passive / passive

**Note:**
The local glossary files `kutastha.json` and `purusa.json` both name *Gītā-Tātparya-Nirṇaya* 15.16-17 as primary Dvaita loci, but both also mark them "[NOT YET RETRIEVED]". The recoverable point is therefore limited but real: the Mādhva use of *kūṭastha* is said not to support Advaita's inference from changelessness to one undifferentiated consciousness. Stability is retained; numerical plurality and dependence are not surrendered. The thinker summary for *Gītā-Tātparya-Nirṇaya* likewise says chapter 15 is read in *taratamya*-key.

### Bhagavad-Gītā 15.17 (anchor for *puruṣottama* hierarchy)

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *uttamaḥ puruṣas tv anyaḥ paramātmety udāhṛtaḥ | yo loka-trayam āviśya bibharty avyaya īśvaraḥ ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> But the highest *puruṣa* is another, spoken of as the supreme self.  
> He who, entering the three worlds, sustains them, the imperishable Lord.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| uttamaḥ | uttama | nom. sg. m. | highest |
| puruṣaḥ | puruṣa | nom. sg. m. | person, conscious principle |
| tu | tu | particle | but |
| anyaḥ | anya | nom. sg. m. | other, another |
| paramātmā | parama-ātman | nom. sg. m. | supreme self |
| iti | iti | indeclinable | thus, "..." |
| udāhṛtaḥ | ud-ā-√hṛ | past passive participle, nom. sg. m. | declared, described |
| yaḥ | yad | nom. sg. m. | who |
| loka-trayam | loka-traya | acc. sg. n. | the three worlds |
| āviśya | ā-√viś | absolutive | having entered, entering |
| bibharti | √bhṛ | laṭ, 3 sg., parasmaipada | bears, sustains |
| avyayaḥ | avyaya | nom. sg. m. | imperishable |
| īśvaraḥ | īśvara | nom. sg. m. | Lord |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *paramātmā* [karmadhāraya]: *parama ātmā*.
- *loka-trayam* [dvigu]: *trayo lokāḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *uttamaḥ puruṣaḥ ... yaḥ ... avyaya īśvaraḥ*
- karman: *loka-trayam*
- absolutive-linked prior action: *āviśya*
- predicative designation: *paramātmā iti udāhṛtaḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *bibharti*: laṭ / parasmaipada / active
- *āviśya*: absolutive
- *udāhṛtaḥ*: past passive participle

**Note:**
This verse is the textual safeguard against collapsing the previous duality into a flattened binary. The local Dvaita glossary on *puruṣa* states the doctrinal point plainly: finite *jīva*s remain eternally distinct from Viṣṇu, while *puruṣottama* belongs to Viṣṇu alone as the unique independent reality. Since the *Tātparya* text is absent, we cannot quote Madhva's own exact handling of *anyaḥ* or *paramātmā* here. But the available evidence does let us say that 15.17 functions, for the local Mādhva map, as the hierarchising verse that preserves real difference within an ordered ontology.

### Bhagavad-Gītā 18.66 (anchor named as the chapter-18 burden of the work)

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *sarva-dharmān parityajya mām ekaṃ śaraṇaṃ vraja | ahaṃ tvāṃ sarva-pāpebhyo mokṣayiṣyāmi mā śucaḥ ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Having abandoned all *dharma*s, go to me alone as refuge.  
> I shall release you from all evils; do not grieve.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| sarva-dharmān | sarva-dharma | acc. pl. m. | all duties, all dharmas |
| parityajya | pari-√tyaj | absolutive | having abandoned |
| mām | mad- | acc. sg. pronoun | me |
| ekam | eka | acc. sg. m. | alone, only |
| śaraṇam | śaraṇa | acc. sg. n. | refuge |

| tvām | tvad- | acc. sg. pronoun | you |
| sarva-pāpebhyaḥ | sarva-pāpa | abl. pl. n. | from all evils, sins |
| mokṣayiṣyāmi | √muc (caus.) | lṛṭ, 1 sg., parasmaipada | I shall liberate, release |
| mā | mā | prohibitive particle | do not |
| śucaḥ | √śuc | injunctive / prohibitive 2 sg. | grieve |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *sarva-dharmān* [karmadhāraya]: *sarvān dharmān*.
- *sarva-pāpebhyaḥ* [karmadhāraya]: *sarvebhyaḥ pāpebhyaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: implied *tvam* with *vraja*; *aham* with *mokṣayiṣyāmi*
- karman: *mām*, *tvām*
- goal / predicative object: *śaraṇam*
- apādāna: *sarva-pāpebhyaḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *vraja*: loṭ / parasmaipada / imperative
- *mokṣayiṣyāmi*: lṛṭ / parasmaipada / simple future causative
- *śucaḥ* with *mā*: prohibitive finite form
- *parityajya*: absolutive

**Note:**
The local summary of *Gītā-Tātparya-Nirṇaya* says that the relevant verses of chapter 18, along with chapter 9, bear the thesis that *karma*, *jñāna*, and *bhakti* are ordered means whose terminus is direct knowledge of Viṣṇu and whose operative mediation is *bhakti*. Accordingly, the Mādhva pressure here is not antinomianism, but exclusive refuge in the supreme *svatantra* reality. Compare the closing movement flagged in the local *Gītā-Bhāṣya* file at 18.65-66, and more broadly the dependence-grammar stated in *Tattva-Saṅkhyāna* 1-3 and the bhakti-centred soteriology named in *Anuvyākhyāna* 3.3.

## Editorial closing

Even in fallback form, a pattern has been settled. The local Madhva materials are consistent in assigning the *Gītā-Tātparya-Nirṇaya* a dependence-reading of the *Gītā*: 7.19 and 10.20 are treated against identity-mode construals; 9.22 and 18.66 are treated as showing that *bhakti* is not ornamental but soteriologically constitutive; 15.16-17 secure hierarchy, plurality, and the uniqueness of *puruṣottama*. What remains open is substantial. Without the Sanskrit *Tātparya* text itself, we cannot recover Madhva's exact verse-selection within each chapter, his own lexical choices, his Pāñcarātra citations, or the precise sub-commentarial amplifications later used by Jayatīrtha and Rāghavendra. Those matters must await retrieval of the primary text. The subsequent loci are nonetheless clear from the local corpus: *Gītā-Bhāṣya* 10.20, 13.2, 15.15-17, and 18.65-66; *Tattva-Saṅkhyāna* 1-3 on *svatantra* and *paratantra*; *Anuvyākhyāna* 3.3 on *bhakti* and direct realisation; and, in later reception, the Rāghavendra *prakāśa* corpus described in `site/data/thinkers/raghavendra.json`.
