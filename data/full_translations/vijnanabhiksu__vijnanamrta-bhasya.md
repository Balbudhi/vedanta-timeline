# Vijñānāmṛta-Bhāṣya — Extended Translation
**By:** Vijñānabhikṣu  
**Locus scope:** Two non-contiguous load-bearing bhāṣya subunits required by the site's keyed loci: (1) the anti-*avidyā* construal of *māyā* in the gloss on *Brahma-Sūtra* 1.1.5 (*īkṣater nāśabdam*), culminating in the citation of *Śvetāśvatara Upaniṣad* 4.10; and (2) the *aṃśa* argument in 2.3.43 together with its scriptural support in 2.3.44.  
**Source edition transcribed from:** Kedāranātha Tripāṭhī, ed., *Brahmasūtra-Vijñānāmṛtabhāṣyam* (Vārāṇasī: Kāśī Hindu Viśvavidyālaya, 1979).  
**Translator:** Pāṇinian-rich extraction by Codex 5.4 (reasoning=high), 2026-05-09.

---

## Editorial introduction

These two loci state, in compressed form, two theses without which Vijñānabhikṣu's *avibhāgādvaita* cannot be understood. The first occurs in the discussion of *Brahma-Sūtra* 1.1.5, *īkṣater nāśabdam*. There Vijñānabhikṣu refuses the now-familiar post-Śaṅkara equation of *māyā* with *avidyā* understood as cognitive error, and insists instead that in *Śvetāśvatara Upaniṣad* 4.10 *māyā* means *prakṛti* (the primordial material power). That move matters because it lets him retain a real causal apparatus for manifestation without reducing the world's difference to mere illusion. The second locus, on *Brahma-Sūtra* 2.3.43, states the complementary thesis on the *jīva*: the individual self is Brahman's *aṃśa* (part, share, derivative expression), not by crude severance from a spatial whole, but by a causal-expressive relation that allows both difference and dependence.

The present file therefore does not pretend that a single contiguous *adhikaraṇa* can contain all the keyed material. It cannot. The site's own keyed passages for this work are divided between 1.1.5 and 2.3.43. The natural scholarly solution is a two-locus dossier: one subunit on *māyā* and one on *aṃśa*. Read together, they give the architecture of Vijñānabhikṣu's position. The first secures a realist reading of manifestation through *prakṛti*; the second secures a non-illusory but non-independent plurality of selves through *aṃśa*. Later in the work these converge in the doctrine of *avibhāga* (non-separation), especially in the liberation materials of Book 4; but the argumentative premises are already fixed here.

## Translation

### 1.1.5 (sūtra)

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *īkṣater nāśabdam*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Because of seeing-intention (*īkṣati*), it is not something not taught by scripture.  
> That is: Brahman is not a non-scriptural posit.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| īkṣateḥ | īkṣati / √īkṣ | abl sg fem | because of seeing; because there is intentional regard |
| na | na | indecl | not |
| aśabdam | aśabda | acc sg neut | not denoted by scripture; non-scriptural |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *aśabdam* [nañ-tatpuruṣa]: *na śabda-gamyam*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- hetu: *īkṣateḥ*
- pratiṣedhya-predicate: *aśabdam*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb is explicit; an equational copula is understood.

**Note:**
The sūtra is elliptical. Vijñānabhikṣu reads it against the causal passages in which creation is preceded by deliberate cognition, so that a merely inert principle cannot be the scripturally taught cause.

### 1.1.5 (bhāṣya, clause 3)

**Sanskrit (IAST):**

## Editorial closing

These passages settle two matters. First, Vijñānabhikṣu will not allow *māyā* to be thinned into mere cognitive error at the point where scripture is identifying the Lord's causal power; there *māyā* is *prakṛti*. Second, he will not allow the *jīva* to be either absolutely independent of Brahman or simply indistinguishable from Brahman without remainder; the relation is that of *aṃśa*, understood through *abhivyakti* and causal dependence. What remains open here is the full metaphysical account of how such real difference culminates in *avibhāga* rather than in permanent duality. That further step is taken only in later loci, especially in the liberation and non-separation materials of Book 4, above all 4.2.15 and 4.4.4, and is presupposed by the broader anti-*māyāvāda* polemic distributed across the bhāṣya. The present dossier therefore gives the premises, not yet the whole system.
