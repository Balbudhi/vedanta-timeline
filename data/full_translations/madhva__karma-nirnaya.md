## Editorial introduction

The local Sanskrit corpus for this dispatch contains no on-disk witness of Madhva's *Karma-Nirṇaya* itself. It does contain only secondary metadata: the work-summary in `site/data/thinkers/madhva.json`, a corpus-expansion note identifying the tract as one of the *Daśa-Prakaraṇa*, and a few catalogue-level mentions of the title. The file below is therefore necessarily provisional. I have limited scope to the closing citation-cluster of the work, because that is the portion recoverable with the greatest confidence from an off-disk Archive OCR witness and because it is also, doctrinally, a natural unit. Here Madhva compresses the tract's thesis into a sequence of scriptural citations: bare *iṣṭa-pūrta* ritualism does not exhaust the good; even *naiṣkarmya* without *Acyuta-bhāva* does not suffice; action must be offered to Viṣṇu, undertaken with inward orientation, and retained within the soteriological order of *bhakti*, *jñāna*, and *vairāgya*.

This terminal cluster fits the larger structure of the tract as described by the local metadata. The work as a whole argues against a self-sufficient Mīmāṃsā account of action, against a merely preparatory Advaitin account of action, and for the reality of *karma* under Viṣṇu's *anumati*. The closing digest is where those claims are settled normatively. Madhva does not deny action. He denies autonomous action. The citations from *Bhagavad-Gītā* 3.30-32, 18.46, and 18.65, together with the *Bhāgavata*'s critique of *naiṣkarmya* without devotion, show precisely that. The result is a characteristically Mādhva ordering: *karma* remains real, obligatory, and soteriologically serious, but only as subordinated practice, never as an independent mechanism.

## Translation

### OCR p. 257a

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *bhagavad-bhakti-jñāna-vairāgya-pūrvakaṃ ca karma kartavyam*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Action is to be performed, and it is to be preceded by devotion to the Lord, knowledge, and dispassion.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| bhagavad-bhakti-jñāna-vairāgya-pūrvakam | *bhagavad-bhakti-jñāna-vairāgya-pūrvaka* | acc sg neut; compound adjective qualifying *karma* | preceded by devotion to the Lord, knowledge, and dispassion |
| ca | *ca* | indecl | and |
| karma | *karman* | nom/acc sg neut | action; prescribed act |
| kartavyam | √*kṛ* | kṛtya suffix *-tavya*, nom/acc sg neut, passive obligation | is to be done; ought to be performed |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *bhagavad-bhakti-jñāna-vairāgya-pūrvakam* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa chain used adjectivally]: *bhagavato bhaktiś ca jñānaṃ ca vairāgyaṃ ca pūrvaṃ yasya tat*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- karman: *karma*
- implicit kartṛ: the qualified aspirant (*adhikārin*), not overtly expressed

**Verbal modality:**
- *kartavyam*: kṛtya formation from √*kṛ*; impersonal/deontic passive; obligation rather than descriptive present

**Note:**
The grammar matters. Madhva does not say that action accidentally accompanies *bhakti*, *jñāna*, and *vairāgya*; he makes them grammatically prior through *pūrvaka*. This is exactly the anti-autonomist move summarized in the work-entry at `madhva.json`: *karma* remains real, but its normativity is internally subordinated.

### OCR p. 257b

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *naiṣkarmyam apy acyuta-bhāva-varjitaṃ na śobhate jñānam alaṃ nirañjanam*  
> *kutaḥ punaḥ śaśvad abhadram īśvare na cārpitaṃ karma yad apy akāraṇam*  
> (*Bhāgavata-Purāṇa* 1.5.12, cited in the OCR witness)

**English (line-by-line):**
> Even actionlessness, if devoid of orientation toward Acyuta, does not shine. Knowledge, though complete and stainless, does not shine either.  
> How much less, then, action which is not offered to the Lord, action that remains perpetually inauspicious, even if it be otherwise without ulterior motive.

| na | *na* | indecl | not |
| śobhate | √*śubh* | laṭ, 3rd sg, ātmanepada | shines; is fitting |
| jñānam | *jñāna* | nom sg neut | knowledge |
| alam | *alam* | indecl | fully; sufficiently |
| nirañjanam | *nirañjana* | nom sg neut | stainless; untainted |
| kutaḥ | *kutas* | indecl/ablative adverb | how much less; whence then |
| punaḥ | *punaḥ* | indecl | again; then |
| śaśvat | *śaśvat* | indecl | always; perpetually |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *acyuta-bhāva-varjitam* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa + PPP]: *acyutasya bhāvena varjitam*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: implicit neuter subjects *naiṣkarmyam*, *jñānam*, and *karma*
- adhikaraṇa / relation-locative: *īśvare*

**Verbal modality:**
- *śobhate*: laṭ / ātmanepada / active-middle sense
- *arpitam*: PPP; passive state, not finite verb

**Note:**
This is the most important citation in the cluster. It blocks the easy reading on which Madhva simply privileges outer action over renunciation or knowledge. He does not. He subordinates all three. *Naiṣkarmya* without *Acyuta-bhāva* fails; therefore mere ritual action, if not offered to Viṣṇu, fails a fortiori. The same ordering governs Madhva's *Gītā-Bhāṣya* and *Gītā-Tātparya-Nirṇaya*.

### OCR p. 257c

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *yataḥ pravṛttir bhūtānāṃ yena sarvam idaṃ tatam*  
> *sva-karmaṇā tam abhyarcya siddhiṃ vindati mānavaḥ*  
> (*Bhagavad-Gītā* 18.46, cited in the OCR witness)

**English (line-by-line):**
> From whom there is the activity of beings, by whom this whole world is pervaded,  
> by worshipping him through one's own action, a human being attains perfection.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| yataḥ | *yad* | abl sg masc/neut relative | from whom; from which |
| pravṛttiḥ | *pravṛtti* | nom sg fem | activity; onward functioning |
| bhūtānām | *bhūta* | gen pl neut | of beings |
| yena | *yad* | inst sg masc/neut relative | by whom |
| sarvam | *sarva* | nom/acc sg neut | all |
| idam | *idam* | nom/acc sg neut | this |
| tatam | √*tan* | PPP, nom/acc sg neut | pervaded; stretched through |
| sva-karmaṇā | *sva-karman* | inst sg neut | by one's own action |
| tam | *tad* | acc sg masc | him |
| abhyarcya | √*arc* with *abhi* | absolutive | having worshipped |
| siddhim | *siddhi* | acc sg fem | perfection; accomplishment |
| vindati | √*vid* | laṭ, 3rd sg, parasmaipada | obtains; finds |
| mānavaḥ | *mānava* | nom sg masc | a human being |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *sva-karmaṇā* [tatpuruṣa]: *svasya karmaṇā*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *mānavaḥ*
- karman: *siddhim*
- karaṇa: *sva-karmaṇā*
- karman of gerundive worship: *tam*
- apādāna/source: *yataḥ*
- karaṇa/agentive relative: *yena*

**Verbal modality:**
- *vindati*: laṭ / parasmaipada / active
- *abhyarcya*: absolutive, prior action

**Note:**
This verse gives Madhva the grammar of subordination in its cleanest form. *Sva-karma* is not discarded; it becomes the instrument of worship. The absolutive *abhyarcya* is decisive: perfection comes not from naked performance but from action already transformed into *arcana*.

### OCR p. 257d

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *mayi sarvāṇi karmāṇi sannyasyādhyātma-cetasā*  
> *nirāśīr nirmamo bhūtvā yudhyasva vigata-jvaraḥ*  
> (*Bhagavad-Gītā* 3.30, cited in the OCR witness)

**English (line-by-line):**
> Renouncing all actions into me, with a mind turned to the self,  
> having become free from expectation and free from possessiveness, fight, with fever gone.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| mayi | *mad* | loc sg | in me; into me |
| sarvāṇi | *sarva* | acc pl neut | all |
| karmāṇi | *karman* | acc pl neut | actions |
| sannyasya | √*as* / lexical √*nyas* with prefixes *sam-ni* | absolutive | having laid down; having entrusted |
| adhyātma-cetasā | *adhyātma-cetas* | inst sg neut | with mind centered in the self/spiritual principle |
| nirāśīḥ | *nirāśis* | nom sg masc | without expectation |
| nirmamaḥ | *nirmama* | nom sg masc | without mine-ness |
| bhūtvā | √*bhū* | absolutive | having become |
| yudhyasva | √*yudh* | loṭ, 2nd sg, ātmanepada | fight |
| vigata-jvaraḥ | *vigata-jvara* | nom sg masc | with fever gone; free from inward agitation |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *adhyātma-cetasā* [tatpuruṣa]: *adhyātme cetasā*.
- *vigata-jvaraḥ* [bahuvrīhi-like adjectival compound]: *vigato jvaro yasya saḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: implied 2nd-person agent
- karman: *sarvāṇi karmāṇi*
- adhikaraṇa / recipient-locative: *mayi*
- karaṇa: *adhyātma-cetasā*

**Verbal modality:**
- *yudhyasva*: loṭ / ātmanepada / imperative
- *sannyasya*, *bhūtvā*: absolutives marking prerequisite conditions

**Note:**
Madhva quotes the battlefield imperative, not to glorify violence, but to show that even the paradigmatic act of worldly duty is to be re-read through surrender. The locative *mayi* and the absolutive *sannyasya* together rule out an autonomous causal account of karma of the Mīmāṃsā kind.

### OCR p. 257e

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *ye me matam idaṃ nityam anutiṣṭhanti mānavāḥ*  
> *śraddhāvanto 'nasūyanto mucyante te 'pi karmabhiḥ*  
> (*Bhagavad-Gītā* 3.31, cited in the OCR witness)

**English (line-by-line):**
> Those human beings who constantly follow this teaching of mine,  
> being possessed of faith and not finding fault, they too are released from actions.

| anasūyantaḥ | √*asūy* with privative *an-* | pres. participle, nom pl masc | not cavilling; not resentfully fault-finding |
| mucyante | √*muc* | laṭ, 3rd pl, ātmanepada/passive-middle | are released |

| api | *api* | indecl | too |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- none beyond standard possessive derivative in *śraddhāvantaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *ye ... mānavāḥ*
- karman: *matam idam*
- instrumental of release/source-medium: *karmabhiḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *anutiṣṭhanti*: laṭ / parasmaipada / active
- *mucyante*: laṭ / ātmanepada / passive-middle

**Note:**
The instrumental *karmabhiḥ* is doctrinally dense. Madhva can read it not as "despite actions" but as the transformed field within which release from the binding character of action occurs. This is one reason the work refuses the merely preparatory status often assigned to karma in Advaita.

## Editorial closing

What this passage settles is clear. *Karma* is neither self-sufficient nor dispensable. It is self-sufficiently inadequate, yet still obligatory when re-read as Viṣṇu-oriented practice. The closing cluster therefore resolves the two principal opponents named in the local work-summary: Mīmāṃsā loses because action cannot generate fruit autonomously, and Advaita loses because action cannot be demoted to a merely provisional fiction once its offering-structure is made scripturally central. What remains open, in this file, is textual rather than doctrinal. The earlier ritual-exegetical stretches of the tract, and the precise local discussion of *adṛṣṭa*, *prārabdha*, and *sañcita*, cannot be responsibly edited from the local corpus. Those must await an on-disk primary witness or a critical print edition. The themes settled here are taken up again in Madhva's *Gītā-Bhāṣya* at 3.30-32, 18.46, and 18.65, in the *Gītā-Tātparya-Nirṇaya*, and in Jayatīrtha's lost-to-this-workspace but standard *Karma-Nirṇaya-Ṭīkā*.
