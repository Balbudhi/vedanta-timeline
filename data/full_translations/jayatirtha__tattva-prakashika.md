## Editorial introduction

This file cannot honestly present a continuous line-by-line translation of Jayatīrtha's *Tattva-Prakāśikā*. The work-summary in the local thinker dossier makes clear why the opening locus matters: at *Brahma-Sūtra-Bhāṣya* 1.1.1, Jayatīrtha fixes the inquiry announced by *athāto brahma-jijñāsā* against Śaṅkara's *adhyāsa-bhāṣya*, insisting that the inquiry is into a real Viṣṇu, not into a self-Brahman identity generated by superimposition. But the Sanskrit text of the *Tattva-Prakāśikā* itself is not on disk in machine-readable form. The only directly relevant primary witnesses locally available are the root *Brahma-Sūtras*, Jayatīrtha's *Nyāya-Sudhā* on the *Anuvyākhyāna*, and image-only scans of Madhva's *Brahma-Sūtra-Bhāṣya*. In that situation, the right procedure is recovery, not invention.

The natural representative unit is therefore the opening *adhikaraṇa* at `BSB` 1.1.1. This is where Jayatīrtha's larger labor in the work first becomes visible: he fixes *adhikaraṇa* boundaries, imposes the standard *viṣaya-saṃśaya-pūrvapakṣa-siddhānta-saṅgati* scheme, and resolves contested readings with explicitly philological care. The opening is not merely introductory. It governs the whole commentary's theistic realism. Later loci then work out the consequences: the *ānanda-mayādhikaraṇa* at `BSB` 1.1.12-19 secures the realist reading of *ānandamaya* as Viṣṇu, and the *aṃśa-adhikaraṇa* at `BSB` 2.3.43 gives the asymmetric dependence of *jīva* on Viṣṇu that Jayatīrtha's wider corpus also presupposes. The doctrinal profile seen here is exactly the one the local dossier attributes to Jayatīrtha more broadly: no new doctrine, but a decisive scholastic fixing of Madhva's positions into a defensible *darśana*.

## Translation

The Sanskrit translated in this section is the root *Brahma-Sūtra* text, not the absent prose of Jayatīrtha's *Tattva-Prakāśikā*. Every Jayatīrtha-specific note is therefore marked as reconstruction from local parallel witnesses, especially the opening of the *Nyāya-Sudhā*.

### BS 1.1.1

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *athāto brahmajijñāsā*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Now, therefore, the inquiry into *brahman*.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| atha | *atha* | indeclinable | now; at this point; hereupon |
| ataḥ | *atas* | indeclinable; ablatival adverb | therefore; from this prior ground |
| brahmajijñāsā | *brahma-jijñāsā* | nom. sg. fem.; ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa compound with desiderative action noun from √*jñā* | inquiry into Brahman; desire to know Brahman |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *brahmajijñāsā* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *brahmaṇaḥ jijñāsā*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- embedded karman within the compound: *brahma*
- no overt finite predication; the sūtra is elliptical and functions programmatically

**Verbal modality:**
- No explicit finite verb occurs in the surface line.
- *jijñāsā* is an action noun formed from the desiderative of √*jñā*, expressing the undertaking or impulse to know.

**Note:**
What can be said securely about Jayatīrtha at this locus comes from local parallel evidence, not from the unavailable *Tattva-Prakāśikā* Sanskrit. In the opening of the *Nyāya-Sudhā* on *Anuvyākhyāna* 1.1.9ab, Jayatīrtha explicitly frames the issue as *īśvara eva śāstrasya viṣayaḥ* and treats the paired markers *oṃkāra* and *brahma* as fixing the object of inquiry as a reality distinct from all *jīva* and *jaḍa*. The local thinker dossier assigns the same force to *Tattva-Prakāśikā* 1.1.1: the opening inquiry is into a real Viṣṇu, not into an Advaitic identity thesis front-loaded by the logic of superimposition. Because the target witness is absent, no stronger claim is made here about Jayatīrtha's exact padaccheda, sentence-order, or verbal texture.

## Editorial closing

What this opening settles, even in fallback form, is the orientation of the whole commentary. The inquiry announced at `BSB` 1.1.1 is not neutral between Vedāntic possibilities. In Jayatīrtha's local profile and in the parallel opening of the *Nyāya-Sudhā*, it already points toward a realist, Viṣṇu-centered reading of *brahman* and toward the scholastic fixing of Madhva's exegesis against rival construals. What it does not yet settle is the full scriptural and inferential case by which that reading will be carried through the commentary. Those later determinations are taken up, within the same work, at the *ānanda-mayādhikaraṇa* (`BSB` 1.1.12-19) and the *aṃśa-adhikaraṇa* (`BSB` 2.3.43). In Jayatīrtha's wider corpus the same architecture is formalized more fully in the *Nyāya-Sudhā* on *Anuvyākhyāna* 1.1 and 2.3.43-44, while Vyāsatīrtha's *Tātparya-Candrikā* presupposes this settlement when it builds the next layer of Mādhva exegesis on `BSB` 1.1.1 and `BSB` 2.3.43.
