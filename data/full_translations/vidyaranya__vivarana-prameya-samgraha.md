# Vivaraṇa-Prameya-Saṃgraha

**By:** Vidyāraṇya (Mādhava)
**Genre:** *saṃgraha* / digest of the *Pañcapādikā-Vivaraṇa* of Prakāśātman

---

## Status

A reliably edited Sanskrit text of the *Vivaraṇa-Prameya-Saṃgraha* is not currently in our corpus. The Vedānta interactive timeline does not have a defensible Sanskrit witness on disk for this text, and producing an annotated translation would mean fabricating engagement we cannot ground.

What is widely known about this work:

- A systematic prose digest of the *prameyas* (philosophical theses) of Prakāśātman's *Pañcapādikā-Vivaraṇa*, composed in the fourteenth century. It is the standard programmatic statement of the *Vivaraṇa* school of post-Śaṅkara Advaita, fixing positions on the locus of *avidyā* (Brahman, not the *jīva*), the *abhāsa-vāda* account of the *jīva*, and the irreducibility of *avidyā* to mere absence.
- The work proceeds by extracting and serializing the chief *vāda*-positions implicit in Prakāśātman's commentary on Padmapāda's *Pañcapādikā*, often opening sections with verse digests and then unpacking them in prose. It defines the *Vivaraṇa* line as against the *Bhāmatī* line of Vācaspati, and is presupposed by every later *Vivaraṇa*-school text.

What is in our corpus that bears on it:

- Vidyāraṇya's `core_thesis` and engaged_works summary in the timeline entry (see https://balbudhi.github.io/vedanta-timeline/ and locate Vidyāraṇya).
- Files for Padmapāda's *Pañcapādikā*, Prakāśātman's *Pañcapādikā-Vivaraṇa*, Vidyāraṇya's *Pañcadaśī* and *Jīvanmukti-Viveka*, and Sarvajñātman's *Saṃkṣepa-Śārīraka*, which together situate the *Vivaraṇa-Prameya-Saṃgraha* in its school.
