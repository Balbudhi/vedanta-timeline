# Vivaraṇa-Prameya-Saṃgraha — Extended Translation
**By:** Vidyāraṇya  
**Locus scope:** Opening *maṅgalācaraṇa* (verses 1-3) and the fifteen-verse pedagogical digest quoted in the first *varṇaka* under *atha prathamaṃ sūtram*, ending with "*yena siddhyati viprendrās tad dhi vijñānam aiśvaram*."  
**Source edition transcribed from:** Local mirror of the archive.org scan `vidyaranya_vivarana_prameya_sangraha_1893_archive.pdf`, checked against the Advaita Sharada and Vishva Vidya digital transcriptions.  
**Translator:** Pāṇinian-rich extraction by Codex 5.4 (reasoning=high), 2026-05-10.

---

## Editorial introduction

This opening unit states, in compressed form, what the whole *Vivaraṇa-Prameya-Saṃgraha* will do. The three introductory verses first mark the work as a digest of *bhāṣya*, *ṭīkā*, and *vivaraṇa* material for qualified readers who have already studied the Veda but remain uncertain about Vedāntic purport. The quoted fifteen-verse digest then gives the internal pedagogy of that study: *śravaṇa* as the ascertainment of textual purport, *manana* as the disciplined deployment of supporting reasoning, *nididhyāsana* as one-pointed contemplative stabilization, and finally the arising of the *mānasī vṛtti* whose object is *pratyag-brahmaikya*, the identity of the inward self and Brahman. That is a natural stopping-point because the prose immediately following turns from summary to dialectic, opening the formal debate over whether *śravaṇa* is a *vidhi* and how *brahmajñāna* destroys *avidyā*. The present scope therefore captures the program before the polemic.

Within the larger architecture of the work, this opening stands to the rest as a syllabus stands to a disputation. Later prose in the same first *varṇaka* unfolds the same issues through *Brahma-Sūtra* 1.1.1, the interpretation of "*śrotavyaḥ*," and the analysis of bondage as *avidyātmaka*; still later loci take up *adhyāsa*, the structure of *pramāṇa*, and the Vivaraṇa treatment of ignorance. Within Vidyāraṇya's broader corpus, the unit is recognizably of a piece with *Pañcadaśī* VI.1-10 and VIII.5-7 on the operative role of conceptual procedure in *brahmajñāna*, and with the practice-facing emphasis of *Jīvanmukti-Viveka* I on the disciplined conditions under which liberating knowledge becomes existentially effective. The idiom here is not merely exhortative. It is a concise scholastic statement of how a Vivaraṇa Advaitin thinks sentence-born knowledge works.

## Translation

### Maṅgala 1

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *svamātrayānandayad atra jantūn sarvātmabhāvena tathā paratra |*  
> *yac śaṅkarānanda-padaṃ hṛdabje vibhrājate tad yatayo viśanti ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> That state which, here, delighted beings by its mere own presence, and, there, by the condition of being the Self of all,  
> that *śaṅkarānanda-pada* shines in the lotus of the heart; into that the renunciants enter.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| svamātrayā | sva-mātrā | instr. sg. f. | by mere ownness; by mere presence of itself |
| ānandayad | ānanda-aya / nand | finite verbal form, causative, 3rd sg. | gladdened, delighted |
| atra | atra | indecl. | here |
| jantūn | jantu | acc. pl. m. | beings, creatures |
| sarvātmabhāvena | sarvātma-bhāva | instr. sg. m. | by the condition of being the Self of all |
| tathā | tathā | indecl. | likewise, so too |
| paratra | paratra | indecl. | there, beyond, in the other sphere |
| yat | yad | rel. pron., nom./acc. sg. n. | which |
| śaṅkarānanda-padam | śaṅkara-ānanda-pada | acc. sg. n. | the state or station of auspicious bliss; possibly the state of Śaṅkarānanda |
| hṛdabje | hṛd-abja | loc. sg. n. | in the lotus of the heart |
| vibhrājate | vi-bhrāj | laṭ, 3rd sg. ātmanepada | shines forth |
| tat | tad | acc. sg. n. | that |
| yatayaḥ | yati | nom. pl. m. | renunciants, strivers |
| viśanti | viś | laṭ, 3rd pl. parasmaipada | enter |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *sarvātmabhāva* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *sarvasya ātmā bhāvaḥ*.
- *śaṅkarānanda-pada* [karmadhāraya / name-compound]: either *śaṅkaraḥ ca asau ānandaḥ ca tat padam* or a guru-name reading, "the state of Śaṅkarānanda."
- *hṛdabja* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *hṛdayasya abjam*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *yac ... padaṃ*
- karman: *jantūn*, *tat*
- karaṇa: *svamātrayā*, *sarvātmabhāvena*
- adhikaraṇa: *hṛdabje*

**Verbal modality:**
- *ānandayad*: finite causative verbal form, active
- *vibhrājate*: laṭ / ātmanepada / active-middle
- *viśanti*: laṭ / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
The verse is intentionally multivalent. *Śaṅkarānanda-pada* can be heard both as a doctrinal "state of auspicious bliss" and as a guru-reference. The double contrast of *atra* and *paratra* also matters: the realized principle is not merely post-mortem, but already operative here.

### Maṅgala 2

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *bhāṣya-ṭīkā-vivaraṇaṃ tan-nibandhana-saṅgrahaḥ |*  
> *vyākhyāna-vyākhyeya-bhāva-kleśa-hānāya racyate ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> A compendium based on that *bhāṣya*, *ṭīkā*, and *vivaraṇa*  
> is composed for the removal of the difficulty concerning the relation between exposition and what is to be expounded.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|

| racyate | rac | laṭ, 3rd sg. passive | is composed |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *tan-nibandhana-saṅgraha* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *tasmin nibandhanaḥ saṅgrahaḥ*.
- *vyākhyāna-vyākhyeya-bhāva-kleśa-hāna* [tatpuruṣa-chain]: *vyākhyānasya ca vyākhyeyasya ca bhāve yaḥ kleśaḥ tasya hāniḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *tan-nibandhana-saṅgrahaḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *racyate*: laṭ / passive / ātmanepada-form passive

**Note:**
The line is programmatic. Vidyāraṇya does not claim originality in the modern sense; he claims compression and clarification of an already stratified commentarial inheritance.

### Maṅgala 3

**Note:**

### Digest 1

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *śrotavyaḥ śruti-vākyebhyo mantavyaś copapattibhiḥ |*  
> *jñātvā ca satataṃ dhyeya ete darśana-hetavaḥ ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> One should hear from the sentences of scripture, and one should reflect by means of reasonings;  
> and, having known, one should constantly meditate. These are the means to realization.

| mantavyaḥ | man | gerundive, nom. sg. m. | to be reflected upon |
| ca | ca | indecl. | and |

| ca | ca | indecl. | and |
| satatam | satata | indecl./acc. sg. n. used adverbially | constantly |

| ete | etad | nom. pl. m. | these |
| darśana-hetavaḥ | darśana-hetu | nom. pl. m. | causes of realization |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *śruti-vākya* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *śruteḥ vākyāni*.
- *darśana-hetu* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *darśanasya hetavaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ (implied): qualified seeker
- apādāna: *śruti-vākyebhyaḥ*
- karaṇa: *upapattibhiḥ*
- predicate: *ete darśana-hetavaḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *śrotavyaḥ*, *mantavyaḥ*, *dhyeyaḥ*: gerundives, injunctive force

**Note:**
The sequence is strict. *Śravaṇa*, *manana*, and *dhyāna/nididhyāsana* are not listed as interchangeable pieties but as a graded pedagogy of sentence, reasoning, and stabilization.

### Digest 2

**Note:**

## Editorial closing

This opening unit settles three matters. First, it states who the text is for: the already trained but not yet resolved Vedāntin. Second, it fixes the Vivaraṇa sequence of *śravaṇa*, *manana*, *nididhyāsana*, and the sentence-born *vṛtti* whose object is *pratyag-brahmaikya*. Third, it makes knowledge and ignorance-destruction grammatically and doctrinally coextensive. What it does not yet settle is the contested juridical status of *śravaṇa*: is it a *vidhi*, a rule, or something else? Nor does it yet fully argue the ontology of *avidyā*; those issues are taken up in the immediately following prose of the first *varṇaka* on "*śrotavyaḥ*" and "*athāto brahmajijñāsā*," and then again in the later opening debate beginning "*nanu sūtra-sūcito 'py adhyāso*." For Vidyāraṇya's broader development of the same pedagogical structure, the nearest parallels are *Pañcadaśī* VI.1-10 and VIII.5-7, while the existential afterlife of this knowledge is treated in *Jīvanmukti-Viveka* I and III.
