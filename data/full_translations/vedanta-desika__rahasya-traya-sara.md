## Editorial introduction

The third *adhikāra* of the *Rahasya-Traya-Sāra*, the *Pradhāna-pratitantra-adhikāra*, asks what is most distinctive in Viśiṣṭādvaita. Deśika does not answer by merely repeating that Nārāyaṇa is supreme, nor by appealing only to the authority of the Veda. He identifies the system's governing difference as the doctrine that *cit* (sentient selves) and *acit* (non-sentient matter) stand to Īśvara in the *śarīra-śarīrin* relation, the relation of body to indwelling self. That claim is the hinge on which the later exposition turns. The immediately following chapters, especially *Artha-pañcaka* (adh. 4), *Tattva-traya-cintanā* (adh. 5), and *Para-devatā-paramārthya* (adh. 6), do not replace this thesis; they unpack it. The later mantra chapters, above all the *Mūla-mantra-adhikāra* (adh. 27), read the same structure back into the name *Nārāyaṇa* itself.

The present file therefore takes the opening doctrinal kernel of the chapter, rather than the whole discursive unfolding. That opening is the natural compressed unit within the chapter: first, Deśika defines *pratitantra*; second, he names the school's own *pradhāna-pratitantra*; third, he states the correlative meanings of *śarīritva* and *śarīratva*. In scholastic terms, the rest of the chapter is exposition of the three relations already latent here: *ādhāra-ādheya*, *niyantṛ-niyāmya*, and *śeṣin-śeṣa*. The broader Deśikan project is already visible. The ontology is not a flat monism, because *cit* and *acit* remain real relata; nor is it a mere pluralism, because their reality is articulated only within an asymmetrical dependence upon the Lord. This is why Deśika treats the passage as both *asādhāraṇa* and *pradhāna*: it is the system's differentia and its architectonic principle at once.

## Translation

### p. 103–104

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *pratitantram āvatu - maṟṟumuḷḷa siddhāntikaḷ oruvarum icaiyātē taṉṉuṭaiya siddhāntattukkē asādhāraṇamāṉa artham.*

**English (line-by-line):**
> "*Pratitantra*" means: a thesis peculiar to one's own system, to which none of the adherents of the other systems gives assent.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| *pratitantram* | *prati* + *tantra* | neuter nominative singular compound | a system-distinctive doctrine |
| *āvatu* | Tamil *ā-* "to become/be" | definitional verbal noun / copular form | means; is defined as |
| *maṟṟumuḷḷa* | Tamil *maṟṟum* + *uḷḷa* | adjectival participial form | the others; the remaining |
| *siddhāntikaḷ* | *siddhāntika* | Tamil plural ending *-kaḷ* | adherents of doctrinal systems |
| *oruvarum* | Tamil *oruvar* | inclusive/emphatic *-um* | any one at all |
| *icaiyātē* | Tamil root *icai* "agree" | negative participial form | not agreeing; without assenting |
| *taṉṉuṭaiya* | Tamil pronoun | possessive form | one's own |
| *siddhāntattukkē* | *siddhānta* | dative *-ttukku* + emphatic *-ē* | precisely to the doctrine/system |
| *asādhāraṇamāṉa* | *asādhāraṇa* | Tamil adjectival extension *-māṉa* | peculiar; not common to others |
| *artham* | *artha* | Tamilized nominal form | meaning; doctrinal point |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *pratitantra* [tatpuruṣa]: *prati tantre vartamānam*; a thesis obtaining with reference to one doctrinal system.
- *asādhāraṇa* [nañ-tatpuruṣa]: *na sādhāraṇam*; not common to others.

**Kāraka structure:**
- lakṣya (thing being defined): *pratitantram*
- pratiyogin-paryudāsa (excluded counterpart): *maṟṟumuḷḷa siddhāntikaḷ oruvarum*
- viśeṣaṇa: *asādhāraṇamāṉa*

**Verbal modality:**
- *āvatu*: Tamil definitional copular form; no Sanskrit *lakāra* is overtly present.

**Note:**
This is a Maṇipravāḷa definition: the frame is Tamil, while the technical load-bearing lexemes are Sanskritic. Deśika begins not with ontology but with method. Before stating the doctrine, he marks it as a system-difference, a move that anticipates the later explicit claim that Viśiṣṭādvaita's defining insight is the *śarīra-śarīrin* relation.

### p. 104

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *iṅgu vedāntikaḷāna nammuṭaiya darśanattukkē asādhāraṇamumāy, pradhānamumāna artham eteniṉil, cetanācetanangaḷukkum īśvaranukkumuṇṭāna śarīrātmabhāva-sambandhādigaḷ.*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Here, if one asks what the thesis is that is both distinctive to our Vedāntic system and principal within it, it is the relations beginning with the body-self relation that obtain between sentient beings, non-sentient things, and Īśvara.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| *iṅgu* | Tamil deictic | indeclinable | here; in this context |
| *vedāntikaḷāna* | *vedāntika* | Tamil plural + adjectival *-āna* | being Vedāntins |
| *nammuṭaiya* | Tamil pronoun | possessive | our |
| *darśanattukkē* | *darśana* | dative *-ttukku* + emphatic *-ē* | for the system alone |
| *asādhāraṇamumāy* | *asādhāraṇa* | conjunctive/predicative Tamil form | as distinctive |
| *pradhānamumāna* | *pradhāna* | conjunctive/predicative Tamil form | as principal; as chief |
| *artham* | *artha* | Tamilized nominal form | doctrinal point; thesis |
| *eteniṉil* | Tamil interrogative-definitional form | clause marker | if it be asked "what?" |
| *cetanācetanangaḷukkum* | *cetana* + *acetana* | dvandva base + dative/plural Tamil ending | for sentients and non-sentients |
| *īśvaranukkumuṇṭāna* | *īśvara* | dative Tamil form + existential participle | obtaining with Īśvara; existing in relation to Īśvara |
| *śarīrātmabhāva-sambandhādigaḷ* | *śarīra* + *ātmabhāva* + *sambandha* + *ādi* | compound + Tamil plural | the relations beginning with the body-self relation |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *cetanācetana* [dvandva]: *cetanāś cācetanāś ca*; sentient and non-sentient entities.
- *śarīrātmabhāva-sambandha* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *śarīrasya cātmanaś ca bhāvaḥ sambandhaḥ*; the relation constituted by one term's being body and the other term's being self.
- *sambandhādi* [tatpuruṣa]: *sambandha-prabhṛtayaḥ*; relations beginning with that relation.

**Kāraka structure:**
- viṣaya: *cetanācetanangaḷukkum īśvaranukkumuṇṭāna*
- pradhāna-pada (predicate): *śarīrātmabhāva-sambandhādigaḷ*
- viśeṣaṇa: *asādhāraṇamumāy*, *pradhānamumāna*

**Verbal modality:**
- No overt finite Sanskrit verb; the sentence is a nominal definition carried by Tamil predicative forms.

**Note:**
The photographed print is partially obscured at the central compound; the reading *śarīrātmabhāva-sambandha* is adopted because it exactly matches the 1946 English translation's "the relationship between Iswara and the world ... is that between the soul and the body" and because the same expression is standard in Viśiṣṭādvaita scholastic usage. The philosophical force lies in the double predicate: the doctrine is not only distinctive (*asādhāraṇa*), but architectonically chief (*pradhāna*). Deśika is naming a differentia, not merely one thesis among others.

### p. 104

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *itil īśvaranukku śarīritvamāvatu - cetanācetanadravyangaḷaip paṟṟi niyamana-dhārakanumāy, niyantāvumāy, śeṣiyumāy irukkai.*

**English (line-by-line):**
> In this connection, Īśvara's being the indwelling self of a body consists in this: with respect to sentient and non-sentient substances, he is their supporter in ordered governance, their controller, and the master for whose sake they are.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| *itil* | Tamil *itu* | locative form | in this; in this matter |
| *īśvaranukku* | *īśvara* | dative Tamil form | for Īśvara |
| *śarīritvamāvatu* | *śarīritva* | abstract noun + Tamil definitional form | being the *śarīrin*; body-possessor/selfhood with respect to a body |
| *cetanācetanadravyangaḷaip* | *cetana* + *acetana* + *dravya* | compound base + accusative/plural Tamil ending | sentient and non-sentient substances |
| *paṟṟi* | Tamil verbal particle | postposition | with respect to; concerning |
| *niyamana-dhārakanumāy* | *niyamana* + *dhāraka* | compound + predicative Tamil form | as supporter/bearer under ordered governance |
| *niyantāvumāy* | *niyantṛ* | Tamil predicative form | as controller |
| *śeṣiyumāy* | *śeṣin* | Tamil predicative form | as master; as the one for whose sake they subsist |
| *irukkai* | Tamil root *iru* "be" | verbal noun | being; consisting in |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *cetanācetanadravya* [dvandva-tatpuruṣa]: *cetanāni cācetanāni ca dravyāṇi*; sentient and non-sentient substances.
- *niyamana-dhāraka* [tatpuruṣa]: *niyamane dhārakaḥ*; the bearer/supporter in the relation of ordered governance.
- *śarīritva* [taddhita abstract]: the condition of being *śarīrin*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- viṣaya: *cetanācetanadravyangaḷaip paṟṟi*
- kartṛ/predicate locus: *īśvaranukku*
- predicative set: *niyamana-dhārakanumāy*, *niyantāvumāy*, *śeṣiyumāy*

**Verbal modality:**
- *irukkai*: Tamil verbal noun marking definitional "consisting in"; no overt Sanskrit *lakāra*.

**Note:**
This line gives the lord-side of the asymmetrical relation. The English translation unpacks the same triad as support, control, and purposive mastery; those are precisely the later scholastic labels *ādhāratva*, *niyantṛtva*, and *śeṣitva*. Deśika's compression matters philosophically: dependence is not exhausted by efficient causality. What is asserted is ontological support, normative control, and teleological priority together.

### pp. 104–105

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *cetanācetanangaḷukku śarīratvamāvatu - niyamana īśvaranaip paṟṟi dhāryamumāy, niyāmyamumāy, śeṣamumāna dravyamāy irukkai.*

**English (line-by-line):**
> And the sentient and the non-sentient have bodyhood in this sense: with respect to the regulating Īśvara, they are what is borne, what is controlled, and a substance that is subordinate.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| *cetanācetanangaḷukku* | *cetana* + *acetana* | dvandva base + dative/plural Tamil ending | for sentients and non-sentients |
| *śarīratvamāvatu* | *śarīratva* | abstract noun + Tamil definitional form | bodyhood; being a body |
| *niyamana* | *niyamana* | nominal/adjectival form | regulating; ordering |
| *īśvaranaip* | *īśvara* | accusative Tamil form | Īśvara |
| *paṟṟi* | Tamil verbal particle | postposition | with respect to |
| *dhāryamumāy* | *dhārya* | gerundive/predicative Tamil form | as what is borne/supported |
| *niyāmyamumāy* | *niyāmya* | gerundive/predicative Tamil form | as what is controlled |
| *śeṣamumāna* | *śeṣa* | predicative/adjectival Tamil form | as what is subordinate; accessory to another |
| *dravyamāy* | *dravya* | predicative Tamil form | as a substance |
| *irukkai* | Tamil root *iru* "be" | verbal noun | being; consisting in |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *cetanācetana* [dvandva]: *cetanāś cācetanāś ca*.
- *śarīratva* [taddhita abstract]: the condition of being body.
- *dhārya* [kṛdanta, gerundive from √*dhṛ*]: that which is borne or supported.
- *niyāmya* [kṛdanta, gerundive from √*yam* with *ni*]: that which is to be controlled or is under control.

**Kāraka structure:**
- viṣaya: *īśvaranaip paṟṟi*
- lakṣya (thing defined): *cetanācetanangaḷukku śarīratvam*
- predicative set: *dhāryamumāy*, *niyāmyamumāy*, *śeṣamumāna dravyamāy*

**Verbal modality:**
- *irukkai*: Tamil verbal noun used as definitional copula.

**Note:**
Here the correlative side is stated with equal precision. Deśika does not say merely that the world depends on God; he specifies the modes of that dependence. The pairings are exact converses of the previous line: if Īśvara is *dhāraka*, the world is *dhārya*; if he is *niyantṛ*, it is *niyāmya*; if he is *śeṣin*, it is *śeṣa*. Later in the work, this ontological asymmetry becomes the ground for soteriology: the *jīva* can entrust itself because it already is, in its very being, borne, ruled, and for-the-sake-of the Lord.

## Editorial closing

This opening passage settles one thing decisively: the system's differentia is the asymmetrical but non-illusory relation between Īśvara, *cit*, and *acit*. What is ruled out here is both a merely external relation between God and world and any flattening of the world into a non-substantial appearance. What is not yet settled is the full doctrinal yield of the relation. The next steps are supplied by Deśika himself. The *Artha-pañcaka-adhikāra* (adh. 4) converts the ontology into a fivefold map of what must be known by the seeker; the *Tattva-traya-cintanādhikāra* (adh. 5) and *Para-devatā-paramārthya-adhikāra* (adh. 6) specify the three reals and the supremacy of Nārāyaṇa; the *Mūla-mantra-adhikāra* (adh. 27) re-reads the same doctrine through the semantics of *Nārāyaṇa*. Outside this work, Deśika's *Tattva-Muktā-Kalāpa* and *Sarvārtha-Siddhi* restate the same body-self ontology in a more overtly scholastic register. The present passage is therefore not an aside. It is the compressed grammar of the larger work.
