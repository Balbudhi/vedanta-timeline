# Pāñcarātra-Rakṣā — Extended Translation
**By:** Vedānta Deśika  
**Locus scope:** *Prathamo'dhikāraḥ* — selected doctrinal hinge-passages from the *maṅgalācaraṇa* through the Vaikhānasa-Pāñcarātra *avirodha* settlement (printed pp. 1-24).  
**Source edition transcribed from:** *Sri Pancaratra Raksa of Sri Vedanta Desika*, critically edited by Pandit M. Duraiswami Aiyangar and Pandit T. Venugopalacharya, Adyar Library, Adyar, Madras, 1942.  
**Translator:** Pāṇinian-rich extraction by Codex 5.4 (reasoning=high), 2026-05-10.

---

## Editorial introduction

This opening arc of the *Pāñcarātra-Rakṣā* does four things, and the rest of the first *adhikāra* depends on all four. It first states the positive theological premise: the whole Pāñcarātra issues from the supreme Brahman as four *vyūha*-s (emanational forms). It then states the epistemic claim: the Pāñcarātra is not an extraneous sectarian invention but a body of teaching already vindicated by the Veda-knowing authorities, above all in the *Mahābhārata* and in the line of Yāmuna and Rāmānuja. Third, it classifies the canon into four *siddhānta*-s, not to fragment it, but to show that it has an internal architecture analogous to the Veda's own branching. Fourth, it states the institutional consequence: where two authoritative *śāstra*-s are both non-contradictory and useful, impartial authorities assign each to its proper domain.

That is why this is the natural extended scope, even though the whole first *adhikāra* runs beyond the project's page cap. The passages translated here are the argumentative spine of printed pp. 1-24. Everything later in *prathamo'dhikāraḥ* presupposes them: the detailed refusal of *siddhānta-saṅkara* on pp. 8-19, the discussion of Vaikhānasa and Pāñcarātra jurisdiction on pp. 21-27, and the later refinement of place-specific and rank-specific ritual competence on pp. 34-42. In Deśika's larger corpus this is a characteristic move. He does not defend Vaiṣṇava *āgama* by severing it from Veda, nor by flattening rival ritual lineages into insignificance. He defends it by giving it origin, canon, and jurisdiction. That threefold strategy is already complete in these opening loci.

## Translation

### PR 1.1 (maṅgalācaraṇa, v. 1)

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *cāturvyūhaṃ paraṃ brahma ṣāḍguṇya-parikarmitam |*  
> *pañcarātrasya kṛtsnasya prasūtiṃ paryupāsmahe ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> We reverentially attend upon the supreme Brahman as the four *vyūha*-s (emanational forms), adorned with *ṣāḍguṇya* (the six divine excellences).  
> We reverentially attend upon him as the source of the entire Pāñcarātra.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| cāturvyūham | cāturvyūha | acc sg neut | having four *vyūha*-s |
| param | para | acc sg neut | supreme |
| brahma | brahman | acc sg neut | Brahman |
| ṣāḍguṇya-parikarmitam | ṣāḍguṇya-parikarmita | acc sg neut, past passive participle | adorned, furnished with the six excellences |
| pañcarātrasya | pañcarātra | gen sg neut | of the Pāñcarātra |
| kṛtsnasya | kṛtsna | gen sg neut | entire, whole |
| prasūtim | prasūti | acc sg fem | source, origination |
| paryupāsmahe | pari-upa-√ās | laṭ, 1 pl, ātmanepada | we reverentially worship / attend upon |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *cāturvyūham* [bahuvrīhi]: *catvāro vyūhā yasya tat*.
- *ṣāḍguṇya-parikarmitam* [tṛtīyā-tatpuruṣa]: *ṣāḍguṇyena parikarmitam*.
- *pañcarātra* [lexicalized dvigu-title]: traditionally, the scripture called "Pāñcarātra."

**Kāraka structure:**
- karman: *cāturvyūhaṃ paraṃ brahma*
- karman (appositional): *prasūtim*
- sambandha: *pañcarātrasya kṛtsnasya*

**Verbal modality:**
- *paryupāsmahe*: laṭ / ātmanepada / active-middle devotional present

**Note:**
This opening is not ornamental. Deśika states here the ontological premise that the later defense will never abandon: the Pāñcarātra originates in qualified, determinate Brahman, not in an inferior or merely provisional revelation. Compare the taxonomic defense at PR 1, p. 3 below, where the same canon is treated as internally ordered rather than sectarianly accidental.

### PR 1, p. 2 (opening prāmāṇya claim)

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *atra tāvat pratyakṣita-samasta-vedārtha-tattva-sthitibhiḥ pārāśarya-prabhṛtibhir mahābhāratādiṣu bhagavacchāstrasya sārvabhaumaṃ prāmāṇyaṃ pratyapādi |*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Here, to begin with, the universal *prāmāṇya* (authoritativeness) of the Lord's scripture was set forth in the *Mahābhārata* and related texts by Pārāśarya and the others, who had directly seen the settled truth of the whole meaning of the Veda.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| atra | atra | indeclinable | here |
| tāvat | tāvat | indeclinable | for the present, to begin with |
| pratyakṣita-samasta-vedārtha-tattva-sthitibhiḥ | pratyakṣita-samasta-vedārtha-tattva-sthiti | instr pl fem, used adjectivally | by those for whom the settled truth of the entire Vedic meaning had been directly seen |
| pārāśarya-prabhṛtibhiḥ | pārāśarya-prabhṛti | instr pl masc | by Pārāśarya and those beginning with him |
| mahābhāratādiṣu | mahābhārata-ādi | loc pl neut | in the *Mahābhārata* and the like |
| bhagavacchāstrasya | bhagavat-śāstra | gen sg neut | of the Lord's scripture |
| sārvabhaumam | sārvabhauma | acc sg neut | universal, sovereign |
| prāmāṇyam | prāmāṇya | acc sg neut | authoritativeness |
| pratyapādi | prati-√pad | past narrative finite form, passive sense | was set forth, was established |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *pratyakṣita-samasta-vedārtha-tattva-sthitibhiḥ* [bahuvrīhi used adjectivally]: *pratyakṣitā samasta-vedārtha-tattva-sthitiḥ yeṣāṃ taiḥ*.
- *pārāśarya-prabhṛtibhiḥ* [tatpuruṣa]: *pārāśarya-prabhṛtibhiḥ* = "by Vyāsa and others of that series."
- *bhagavacchāstrasya* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *bhagavataḥ śāstrasya*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ / karaṇa in instrumental: *pratyakṣita-samasta-vedārtha-tattva-sthitibhiḥ pārāśarya-prabhṛtibhiḥ*
- adhikaraṇa: *mahābhāratādiṣu*
- sambandha: *bhagavacchāstrasya*
- karman: *sārvabhaumaṃ prāmāṇyam*

**Verbal modality:**
- *pratyapādi*: past narrative finite form of *prati-√pad*; passive-resultative sense

**Note:**
Deśika does not begin by begging authority for the Pāñcarātra from within a closed sectarian circle. He begins by locating its vindication in Vyāsa's own literary field. The appeal to *Mahābhārata* is strategic: it puts the defense on pan-Vedāntic, not merely intra-Pāñcarātra, ground.

### PR 1, p. 2 (Yāmuna citation)

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *idaṃ mahopaniṣadaṃ caturveda-samanvitam |*  
> *sāṅkhyayoga-kṛtāntena pañcarātrānuśabditam ||*  
> *vedānteṣu yathāsāraṃ saṃgṛhya bhagavān hariḥ |*  
> *bhaktānukampayā vidvān saṃcikṣepa yathāsukham ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> This is the great Upaniṣad, furnished with the four Vedas.  
> It is designated "Pāñcarātra," together with the doctrinal conclusion of Sāṅkhya and Yoga.  
> Bhagavān Hari, having gathered from the Vedāntas the essence as essence,  
> out of compassion for devotees, being fully wise, condensed it into an easy form.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| idam | idam | nom sg neut | this |
| mahopaniṣadam | mahopaniṣad | nom sg neut | the great Upaniṣad |
| caturveda-samanvitam | caturveda-samanvita | nom sg neut | furnished with the four Vedas |
| sāṅkhyayoga-kṛtāntena | sāṅkhyayoga-kṛtānta | instr sg masc | with the settled teaching of Sāṅkhya and Yoga |
| pañcarātrānuśabditam | pañcarātra-anuśabdita | nom sg neut, past passive participle | designated by the name Pāñcarātra |
| vedānteṣu | vedānta | loc pl masc | in the Vedāntas |
| yathāsāram | yathā-sāra | indeclinable / adverbial accusative | according to the essence; as essence |
| saṃgṛhya | sam-√grah | lyap absolutive | having gathered together |
| bhagavān | bhagavant | nom sg masc | the Blessed Lord |
| hariḥ | hari | nom sg masc | Hari |
| bhaktānukampayā | bhakta-anukampā | instr sg fem | out of compassion for devotees |
| vidvān | vidvas | nom sg masc | knowing, fully wise |
| saṃcikṣepa | sam-√kṣip | liṭ / perfect, 3 sg, parasmaipada | he condensed, he abridged |
| yathāsukham | yathā-sukha | indeclinable / adverbial accusative | in an easy or accessible manner |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *mahopaniṣadam* [karmadhāraya]: *mahatī upaniṣat*.
- *caturveda-samanvitam* [tṛtīyā-tatpuruṣa]: *caturbhiḥ vedaiḥ samanvitam*.
- *sāṅkhyayoga-kṛtāntena* [dvandva-tatpuruṣa]: *sāṅkhyasya yogasya ca kṛtāntena*.
- *bhaktānukampayā* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *bhaktānām anukampayā*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *bhagavān hariḥ*
- adhikaraṇa: *vedānteṣu*
- karman (understood from prior line): *idam* / this teaching
- hetu / karaṇa: *bhaktānukampayā*
- pūrvakāla-kriyā: *saṃgṛhya*
- kriyā-viśeṣaṇa: *yathāsukham*

**Verbal modality:**
- *pañcarātrānuśabditam*: kta / past passive participle / predicative
- *saṃgṛhya*: lyap absolutive; no lakāra
- *saṃcikṣepa*: liṭ / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
This is the classic Yāmuna line that lets Deśika describe the Pāñcarātra neither as non-Vedic supplement nor as free invention. It is condensation, not displacement. Compare Yāmuna's *Āgama-prāmāṇya* and Deśika's own later appeal, in this same *adhikāra*, to the Pāñcarātra as Veda-analogous rather than Veda-external.

### PR 1, p. 3 (catvāraḥ siddhāntāḥ)

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *āgamasiddhānto mantrasiddhāntas tantrasiddhāntas tantrāntarasiddhānta iti |*  
> *catvāraś cete siddhāntā ṛgādivad eva śākhābhedair avantaratantrair bhidyante ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> "Āgama-siddhānta," "mantra-siddhānta," "tantra-siddhānta," and "tantrāntara-siddhānta": thus [the four are named].  
> These four *siddhānta*-s are differentiated, just like the Ṛg and the rest, by branch-divisions and by subordinate tantras.

| ca | ca | indeclinable | and |

| ṛgādivat | ṛg-ādi-vat | indeclinable | like the Ṛg and the others |
| eva | eva | indeclinable | precisely, indeed |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *āgamasiddhāntaḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *āgamasya siddhāntaḥ*.
- *mantrasiddhāntaḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *mantrasya siddhāntaḥ*.
- *tantrasiddhāntaḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *tantrasya siddhāntaḥ*.
- *tantrāntarasiddhāntaḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *tantrāntarasya siddhāntaḥ*.
- *ṛgādivat* [avyayībhāva]: *ṛgādīnām iva*.
- *śākhābhedaiḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *śākhānāṃ bhedaiḥ*.
- *avantaratantraiḥ* [karmadhāraya / lexical tatpuruṣa]: *avantarāṇi tantrāṇi*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *ete siddhāntāḥ*
- karaṇa: *śākhābhedaiḥ*
- karaṇa: *avantaratantraiḥ*
- upamāna: *ṛgādivat*

**Verbal modality:**
- *bhidyante*: laṭ / ātmanepada-passive / present

**Note:**
The analogy to Vedic branching is the decisive move. Deśika is not merely multiplying sectarian labels; he is arguing for canon-structure. Later in the same *adhikāra* (pp. 31-33) he will refine this further by correlating these same four with the *svayaṃvyakta*, *divya*, *saiddha*, and *ārṣa* designations.

### PR 1, p. 24 (Vaikhānasa-Pāñcarātra avirodha settlement)

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *avirodhopayogābhyām ubhayor api śāstrayoḥ |*  
> *madhyasthair hi kṛto dharmo vidyāsthāna-niveśanam ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> On the strength of the non-contradiction and the usefulness of both *śāstra*-s,  
> impartial authorities have indeed laid down the norm: the assigning of each to its proper domain of learning and practice.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| avirodhopayogābhyām | avirodhopayoga | instr du masc | by the non-conflict and the usefulness |
| ubhayoḥ | ubhaya | gen du | of both |
| api | api | indeclinable | also, indeed |
| śāstrayoḥ | śāstra | gen du neut | of the two authoritative scriptures |
| madhyasthaiḥ | madhyastha | instr pl masc | by those standing in the middle, by impartial authorities |
| hi | hi | indeclinable | indeed |
| kṛtaḥ | kṛta | nom sg masc, past passive participle | made, established |
| dharmaḥ | dharma | nom sg masc | the norm, rule, settlement |
| vidyāsthāna-niveśanam | vidyāsthāna-niveśana | nom/acc sg neut, appositional | the assignment into proper disciplinary locations |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *avirodhopayogābhyām* [dvandva]: *avirodhena ca upayogena ca*.
- *madhyasthaiḥ* [upapada / lexical derivative]: *madhye sthitaiḥ*.
- *vidyāsthāna-niveśanam* [saptamī-tatpuruṣa]: *vidyāsthāne niveśanam*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- sambandha: *ubhayoḥ api śāstrayoḥ*
- karaṇa / hetu: *avirodhopayogābhyām*
- kartṛ (instrumental agent): *madhyasthaiḥ*
- pradhāna-predication: *kṛto dharmaḥ*
- appositional specification: *vidyāsthāna-niveśanam*

**Verbal modality:**
- *kṛtaḥ*: kta / past passive participle / predicative; no finite lakāra is overtly expressed

**Note:**
This is the jurisdictional settlement on which Deśika's institutional argument turns. He does not say that one valid scripture annihilates the other. He says that non-conflict plus practical usefulness require ordered placement. The immediately following pages (pp. 25-27) make the same point polemically by treating mutual censure-passages as sectarian praise-blame rhetoric, not as literal cancellation of the rival canon.

## Editorial closing

These passages settle three things. They settle origin: the Pāñcarātra proceeds from the supreme four-*vyūha* Brahman. They settle status: it is to be treated as scripturally authoritative and as internally organized, not as a miscellany. And they settle jurisdiction: Vaikhānasa and Pāñcarātra need not be forced into mutual invalidation when competent authorities can assign each its proper scope. What they do not yet settle is the full grammar of inter-mixture, superiority, place-specific exception, and ritual trespass. Those questions are taken up in the subsequent sections of this same *prathamo'dhikāraḥ*: *siddhānta-saṅkara* on pp. 8-19, the Vaikhānasa-Pāñcarātra dispute on pp. 21-27, superior-*siddhānta* claims on pp. 28-33, and place-specific scriptural competence on pp. 34-42. The later *Sajjana-vaibhava* picks up the Vaikhānasa side more directly; the present work gives the governing settlement under which that relation can be made intelligible.
