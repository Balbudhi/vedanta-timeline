# Saṃkṣepa-Śārīraka — Extended Translation
**By:** Sarvajñātman  
**Locus scope:** Composite doctrinal unit comprising Adhyāya I, verses 319 and 327, and Adhyāya III, verses 275–276 and 278; these are the work's existing load-bearing loci in the site data and together form the minimal architectonic set for Sarvajñātman's Brahman-locus thesis and his fourfold semantic analysis of the *mahāvākya*.  
**Source edition transcribed from:** N. Veezhinathan, *The Samkṣepa-Śārīraka of Sarvajñātman: Critically Edited with Introduction, English Translation, Notes and Indexes*, University of Madras, 1972.  
**Translator:** Pāṇinian-rich extraction by Codex 5.4 (reasoning=high), 2026-05-09.

## Editorial introduction

These dispersed verses state, with uncommon economy, two doctrines without which Sarvajñātman's Advaita cannot be reconstructed. The first is the Brahman-locus thesis: *avidyā* is grounded in undivided consciousness itself, not in the later empirical self or in Īśvara as already delimited (SS I.319, with its immediate elaboration in SS I.320-321). The second is the semantic-ontological discipline required for reading the *mahāvākya*: before one can say that *tat* and *tvam* are identical, one must sort the several layers packed into each term, distinguishing *upādhi*, *cidābhāsa*, *bimba*, and *pratibimba* (SS III.275-276, 278). A merely contiguous scope would miss one of these load-bearing joints. For that reason, the present file takes a composite but still natural architectonic unit: the minimal set of passages already marked in the corpus as decisive for Sarvajñātman's own systematic self-definition.

Within the larger structure of the *Saṃkṣepa-Śārīraka*, the first-adhyāya verses belong to the causal and ontological clarification of Brahman, *avidyā*, *jīva*, and Īśvara; the third-adhyāya verses belong to the discriminative analysis by which the pedagogical use of the *mahāvākya* becomes possible. The relation between them is exact. If *avidyā* is not located correctly, the reflection-structure of individuality and lordship cannot be sustained. If the semantic furniture of *tat* and *tvam* is not discriminated, the verbal cognition of non-duality becomes equivocal. Sarvajñātman's broader position therefore turns on both theses at once: Brahman is the sole ultimate support of the entire epistemic-causal appearance, and the identity taught by śruti is not a blunt equation but the result of a rigorously layered discrimination.

## Translation

### SS I.319

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *āśrayatvaviṣayatvabhāginī nirvibhāgacitir eva kevalā |  
> pūrvasiddhatamaso hi paścimo nāśrayo bhavati nāpi gocaraḥ ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Only undivided consciousness itself, and it alone, shares the status of locus and object.  
> For what arises later does not become the locus, nor even the object-field, of the darkness already established beforehand.

|---|---|---|---|
| āśrayatvaviṣayatvabhāginī | bhāginī | nominative singular feminine; compound | sharing locushood and objecthood |
| nirvibhāgacitiḥ | citi | nominative singular feminine; compound | undivided consciousness |
| eva | eva | indeclinable | indeed, alone |
| kevalā | kevala | nominative singular feminine | only, exclusive |
| pūrvasiddhatamasaḥ | tamas | genitive singular neuter; compound | of the darkness already established beforehand |
| hi | hi | indeclinable | for |
| paścimaḥ | paścima | nominative singular masculine | the later one, the subsequent entity |
| na | na | indeclinable | not |
| āśrayaḥ | āśraya | nominative singular masculine | locus, substrate |
| bhavati | √bhū | 3rd singular, laṭ, parasmaipada, active | becomes, is |
| na | na | indeclinable | not |
| api | api | indeclinable | even, also |
| gocaraḥ | gocara | nominative singular masculine | object-field, cognitive range |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *āśrayatvaviṣayatvabhāginī* [tatpuruṣa-type coordinative compound]: *āśrayatvasya viṣayatvasya ca bhāginī*.
- *nirvibhāgacitiḥ* [karmadhāraya]: *nirvibhāgā citiḥ*.
- *pūrvasiddhatamasaḥ* [tatpuruṣa]: *pūrvaṃ siddhasya tamasaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *nirvibhāgacitiḥ* (understood with elliptical *asti/bhavati*) in the first half.
- kartṛ: *paścimaḥ* in the second half.
- sambandha: *pūrvasiddhatamasaḥ*.
- predicative complement: *āśrayaḥ*, *gocaraḥ*.

**Verbal modality:**
- *bhavati*: laṭ / parasmaipada / active.
- first half: no explicit finite verb; existential predication is understood.

**Note:**
The polemical edge lies in *pūrvasiddha*: the empirical self and the lordly reflection are posterior formations, so they cannot retroactively bear the *avidyā* that conditions their very appearance. SS I.320-321 makes the same point discursively by saying that the reflected self in intellect is the *jīva* and the self reflected in *avidyā* is God, and that neither can be locus or object of the prior darkness. The grammar therefore does philosophical work: temporal posteriority defeats ontological candidature.

### SS I.327

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *sukṛtaduṣkṛtakarmaṇi kartṛtāṃ  
> matigatātmacitipratibimbakam |  
> vrajati tadvad adaḥ paramātmano  
> jagati yāti tamaḥpratibimbakam ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> In meritorious and demeritorious action, the reflection of self-consciousness lodged in the intellect comes into agenthood.  
> Likewise, that reflection in darkness, belonging to the supreme Self, goes forth in relation to the world.

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| sukṛtaduṣkṛtakarmaṇi | karman | locative singular neuter; compound | in good and bad action |
| kartṛtām | kartṛtā | accusative singular feminine | agenthood, doership |
| matigatātmacitipratibimbakam | pratibimbaka | nominative singular neuter; compound | the reflection of self-consciousness situated in the intellect |
| vrajati | √vraj | 3rd singular, laṭ, parasmaipada, active | goes to, attains |
| tadvat | tadvat | indeclinable | likewise |
| adaḥ | adas | nominative singular neuter | that |
| paramātmanaḥ | paramātman | genitive singular masculine | of the supreme Self |
| jagati | jagat | locative singular neuter | in the world, in relation to the world |
| yāti | √yā | 3rd singular, laṭ, parasmaipada, active | goes, attains |
| tamaḥpratibimbakam | pratibimbaka | nominative singular neuter; compound | the reflection in darkness |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *sukṛtaduṣkṛtakarmaṇi* [dvandva-tatpuruṣa complex]: *sukṛte duṣkṛte ca karmaṇi*.
- *matigatātmacitipratibimbakam* [tatpuruṣa]: *matau gatāyā ātmaciteḥ pratibimbakam*.
- *tamaḥpratibimbakam* [tatpuruṣa]: *tamasi pratibimbakam*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- adhikaraṇa: *sukṛtaduṣkṛtakarmaṇi*.
- kartṛ: *matigatātmacitipratibimbakam*.
- karman / goal-state: *kartṛtām*.
- kartṛ: *adaḥ ... tamaḥpratibimbakam*.
- sambandha: *paramātmanaḥ*.
- adhikaraṇa: *jagati*.

**Verbal modality:**
- *vrajati*: laṭ / parasmaipada / active.
- *yāti*: laṭ / parasmaipada / active.

**Note:**
The verse operationalizes the reflection-model rather than merely naming it. The *jīva* is the consciousness reflected in *mati* and thereby becomes the bearer of karmic agency; the Īśvara-side is the reflection in *tamas/avidyā*, which acquires world-governing relevance. SS III.278 restates the *tvam*-side of the same schema in more semantic language: *antaḥkaraṇa* is the *upādhi*, *jīvatva* the *ābhāsana*, and consciousness connected with it the *pratibimba*.

### SS III.275

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *upādhim aupādhikam āntaraṃ cidābhāsanaṃ citpratibimbakaṃ ca /  
> cidbimbam evaṃ caturaḥ padārthān vivicya jānīhi tadarthabhājaḥ //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> The limiting adjunct, the adjunct-conditioned inner manifestation of consciousness, the reflection of consciousness, and the original consciousness-image as well:  
> having discriminated thus these four referents, know them as the bearers of the meaning of *tat*.

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| upādhim | upādhi | accusative singular masculine | limiting adjunct |
| aupādhikam | aupādhika | accusative singular neuter | what belongs to, or is conditioned by, the adjunct |
| āntaram | āntara | accusative singular neuter | inner |
| cidābhāsanam | cidābhāsana | accusative singular neuter; compound | manifestation of consciousness |
| citpratibimbakam | citpratibimbaka | accusative singular neuter; compound | reflection of consciousness |
| ca | ca | indeclinable | and |
| cidbimbam | cidbimba | accusative singular neuter; compound | original consciousness-image |
| evam | evam | indeclinable | thus |
| caturaḥ | catur | accusative plural masculine | four |
| padārthān | padārtha | accusative plural masculine | referents, denotata |
| vivicya | vi-√vic | absolutive / gerund | having discriminated |
| jānīhi | √jñā | 2nd singular, loṭ, parasmaipada, active | know |
| tadarthabhājaḥ | bhāj | accusative plural masculine; compound | sharing in, or bearing, the meaning of *tat* |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *cidābhāsanam* [tatpuruṣa]: *citaḥ ābhāsanam*.
- *citpratibimbakam* [tatpuruṣa]: *citaḥ pratibimbakam*.
- *cidbimbam* [tatpuruṣa]: *citaḥ bimbam*.
- *tadarthabhājaḥ* [tatpuruṣa]: *tadarthaṃ bhajante iti*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- karman: *upādhim ... cidbimbam* and, collectively, *caturaḥ padārthān*.
- kartṛ: implicit second-person addressee with *jānīhi*.
- qualifying relation: *tadarthabhājaḥ* construes with *padārthān*.

**Verbal modality:**
- *jānīhi*: loṭ / parasmaipada / active.
- *vivicya*: gerund marking prior discriminative completion.

**Note:**
The imperative is pedagogically decisive. Identity is not granted at the level of an undifferentiated lexical rush; it must be preceded by *viveka*. What is sorted here is not merely ontology and not merely semantics, but the crossing-point where denotation and ontic layering meet. SS III.276 immediately repeats the same discipline on the *tvam* side.

### SS III.276

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *tathā tvamarthe 'pi catuṣṭayaṃ tad vivecanīyaṃ nipuṇena bhūtvā /

**English (line-by-line):**
> Similarly, in the meaning of *tvam* too, that fourfold set is to be discriminated, once one has become exact in analysis.

**Note:**
The verse shifts from imperative command to necessity-language: the fourfold set on the *tvam* side is not merely useful but “to be discriminated.” This is the semantic discipline presupposed by SS III.278, where the four items are named more explicitly in terms of *antaḥkaraṇa*, *jīvatva*, *pratibimba*, and *bimba*. The locative *asyām* fixes the pedagogical order: the intellect is not the self, but it is the immediate site in which the reflected structure must be parsed.

### SS III.278

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *upādhir antaḥkaraṇaṃ tvamarthe  
> jīvatvam ābhāsanam atra tadvat /  
> tadanvitā cit pratibimbam evam  
> ananvitāṃ bimbam āhuḥ ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> On the *tvam* side, the limiting adjunct is the internal organ.  
> There, as in the preceding case, the manifestation is individual-selfhood.  
> Consciousness as connected with that is thus the reflected image.  
> Consciousness as unconnected with it, they say, is the original image.

| tvamarthe | tvamartha | locative singular masculine; compound | in the meaning of *tvam* |
| jīvatvam | jīvatva | nominative singular neuter | jīva-hood, individual-selfhood |
| ābhāsanam | ābhāsana | nominative singular neuter | manifestation, appearance |
| atra | atra | indeclinable | here, in this case |
| tadvat | tadvat | indeclinable | likewise, as there |
| tadanvitā | tadanvita | nominative singular feminine | connected with that |
| cit | cit | nominative singular feminine | consciousness |
| pratibimbam | pratibimba | nominative singular neuter | reflected image |
| evam | evam | indeclinable | thus |
| ananvitām | ananvita | accusative singular feminine | unconnected |
| bimbam | bimba | accusative singular neuter | original image |
| āhuḥ | √ah | 3rd plural, liṭ, parasmaipada, active | they say |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *antaḥkaraṇam* [tatpuruṣa]: *antaḥ sthitaṃ karaṇam*.
- *tvamarthe* [tatpuruṣa]: *tvamaśabdasya arthe*.
- *jīvatvam* [tatpuruṣa]: *jīvasya bhāvaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- adhikaraṇa: *tvamarthe*.
- predicative identification: *upādhiḥ = antaḥkaraṇam*; *jīvatvam = ābhāsanam*.
- kartṛ of reported doctrine: implicit teachers with *āhuḥ*.
- karman of *āhuḥ*: *bimbam*.

**Verbal modality:**
- *āhuḥ*: liṭ / parasmaipada / active, with present-force reporting an established teaching.

**Note:**
This is Sarvajñātman's compactest statement of the *tvam*-analysis. The *jīva* is not the original consciousness, but the manifest individualized mode that arises when consciousness is considered through the internal organ as *upādhi*. The verse is the semantic counterpart to SS I.327: there the reflection-model explains agency and world-order; here the same model explains what exactly the word *tvam* picks out before the identity-statement is purified.

## Editorial closing

These passages settle two points. First, Sarvajñātman will not permit the locus of *avidyā* to migrate downward into the empirical subject; the later *jīva* and Īśvara-forms are themselves products of the reflected structure and therefore cannot underwrite the prior darkness (SS I.319; cf. I.320-321). Second, the identity taught by the *mahāvākya* is reached only through a prior discrimination of the layered referents on both sides of the sentence (SS III.275-276, 278). What remains open here is the fuller defense against rival causal models and the extended unpacking of how the semantic purification yields liberating cognition. Sarvajñātman takes those matters forward in the immediately surrounding first-adhyāya sequence on causality and *avidyā* (SS I.320-333) and in the continuation of the third-adhyāya analysis of *tat/tvam* and pedagogical procedure (SS III.279-283 and following). The present loci therefore do not conclude the argument; they expose the joints on which the later argument turns.
