# Pañcapādikā — Extended Translation
**By:** Padmapāda  
**Locus scope:** Three architectonic loci within Padmapāda's opening exposition of Śaṅkara's *Adhyāsa-Bhāṣya*: V.15–VI.16; XIX.62–63; XXIX.107–109.  
**Source edition transcribed from:** *Śrīpadmapādācāryaviracitā Pañcapādikā*, ed. Śrīrāmaśāstrī Bhāgavatācārya, Vizianagram Sanskrit Series 3 (Kāśī: I.J. Lazarus Company, 1891); checked throughout against D. Venkataramiah, trans., *The Pancapadika of Padmapada* (Baroda: Oriental Institute, 1948).

## Editorial introduction

These three loci are where Padmapāda's opening *adhyāsa* analysis ceases to be merely expository of Śaṅkara and becomes structurally programmatic for the later *Vivaraṇa* line. V.15–VI.16 is the decisive ontological turn: the bhāṣya's *mithyājñāna*-language is parsed into *mithyā* and *ajñāna*, and the *ajñāna*-member is identified with an insentient *avidyāśakti*, a positive explanatory factor without which false-object appearance would be unintelligible. XIX.62–63 gives the most compressed and durable formulation of *adhyāsa* itself: not bare recollection, but the appearance elsewhere, in memory-form, of what was previously seen. XXIX.107–109 then carries the analysis into mahāvākya-hermeneutics: *tat tvam asi* does not annihilate the *jīva* as though it were shell-silver; it teaches the *jīva*, standing as *pratibimba*, to be of one nature with *brahman* as *bimba*. The sequence is exact. First Padmapāda fixes the causal status of *avidyā*; then he fixes the phenomenological form of superimposition; finally he fixes the semantic and liberative use of that analysis in the great identity-sentence.

The larger natural unit is Padmapāda's long gloss on Śaṅkara's prefatory *adhyāsa-bhāṣya*. The present file covers the three load-bearing sub-loci already marked in the site data, with immediate framing where grammar or dialectical force requires it. That is the right scale for this work. The whole opening discussion is continuous, but these are the points at which Padmapāda's later doctrinal legacy is settled: positive *avidyā* at V–VI, the refined definition at XIX, and the *bimba-pratibimba* mahāvākya-reading at XXIX. Prakāśātman's *Pañcapādikā-Vivaraṇa* is unintelligible unless one has these three places firmly in hand.

## Translation

### V.15a

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *mithyā ca tad ajñānaṃ ca mithyājñānam |*

**English (line-by-line):**
> "False" and "ignorance" both: that is *mithyājñāna*.  
> That compound means both "the *mithyā*-element" and "the *ajñāna*-element."

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| mithyā | mithyā | indeclinable | indeterminately; falsely; as *mithyā* |
| ca | ca | indeclinable | and |
| tad | tad | nom./acc. sg. neut. | that |
| ajñānam | ajñāna | nom./acc. sg. neut. | ignorance |
| ca | ca | indeclinable | and |
| mithyājñānam | mithyājñāna | nom. sg. neut. | "mithyā-ignorance"; the compound under analysis |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *mithyājñānam* [itaretara-dvandva re-read analytically]: *mithyā ca tad ajñānaṃ ca*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- predicative analysis: *mithyā* + *ajñānam* are set out as the two members explicating *mithyājñānam*.

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb expressed; the clause is nominal and exegetical.

**Note:**
Padmapāda is not letting the compound rest as "false cognition." He forcibly opens it and thereby blocks the reduction of the passage to mere error-theory. This analytic splitting is the hinge on which the later *Vivaraṇa* ontology turns. Compare the same movement's culmination at VI.16 below, where the *ajñāna*-member becomes an *avidyāśakti*.

### V.15b

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *mithyeti anirvacanīyatocyate |*

**English (line-by-line):**
> By the word "*mithyā*," indeterminability is meant.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| mithyā iti | mithyā + iti | indeclinable expression | "mithyā," thus |
| anirvacanīyatā | anirvacanīyatā | nom. sg. fem. (contracted in sandhi) | indeterminability; inexpressibility |
| ucyate | √vac | laṭ, 3rd sg., passive, ātmanepada usage | is said; is meant |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *anirvacanīyatā* [abstract derivative]: *na nirvacanīyā iti anirvacanīyā; tasyā bhāvaḥ anirvacanīyatā*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- viṣaya of explication: *mithyā iti*
- predicate content: *anirvacanīyatā*

**Verbal modality:**
- *ucyate*: laṭ / passive / ātmanepada-form in passive usage.

**Note:**
Here *mithyā* is not glossed as sheer non-being. That matters. Padmapāda's line is already excluding the collapse of *mithyā* into *asat*. The term marks the indeterminable status later classical Advaita will codify as neither simply real nor simply unreal.

### V.15c

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *ajñānam iti ca jaḍātmikāvidyāśaktiḥ jñānaparyudāsenocyate |*

**English (line-by-line):**
> And by the word "*ajñāna*" there is meant the *avidyāśakti* (power of ignorance) whose nature is insentient, stated by exclusion from knowledge.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| ajñānam | ajñāna | nom./acc. sg. neut. | ignorance |
| iti | iti | indeclinable | thus; by this term |
| ca | ca | indeclinable | and |
| jaḍātmikāvidyāśaktiḥ | jaḍātmikā-avidyā-śakti | nom. sg. fem. | the ignorance-power whose nature is insentient |
| jñānaparyudāsena | jñāna-paryudāsa | instr. sg. masc. | by exclusion of knowledge |
| ucyate | √vac | laṭ, 3rd sg., passive | is stated; is denoted |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *jaḍātmikāvidyāśaktiḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa internally with karmadhāraya force]: *jaḍātmikā yā avidyā, tasyāḥ śaktiḥ*.
- *jñānaparyudāsaḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *jñānasya paryudāsaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- karaṇa: *jñānaparyudāsena*
- predicate nominative: *jaḍātmikāvidyāśaktiḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *ucyate*: laṭ / passive / ātmanepada-form in passive usage.

**Note:**
This is the load-bearing line in Padmapāda's reception history. The move from "ignorance" to a positive *avidyāśakti* is what later anti-*mūlāvidyā* Advaitins will target, and what Prakāśātman will explicitly amplify. The instrumental *jñānaparyudāsena* is philosophically sharp: the entity is introduced not as a mere privation, but precisely through a negation that leaves behind a positive explanatory remainder.

### V.15d

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *tannimittaḥ tadupādāna ity arthaḥ |*

**English (line-by-line):**
> The meaning is: it has that as its occasioning cause, and that as its material basis.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| tad-nimittaḥ | tad-nimitta | nom. sg. masc. | having that as cause/occasion |
| tad-upādāna | tad-upādāna | nom. sg. neut./predicative compound | having that as material cause |
| iti | iti | indeclinable | thus |
| arthaḥ | artha | nom. sg. masc. | the meaning; purport |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *tannimittaḥ* [tatpuruṣa]: *tat nimittaṃ yasya*.
- *tadupādāna* [tatpuruṣa]: *tat upādānaṃ yasya*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- predicative pair: *tannimittaḥ*, *tadupādāna*
- subject implied: the superimpositional process or its product as explained in context

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb expressed; *arthaḥ* supplies the metalinguistic predication.

**Note:**
Padmapāda here sharpens causal vocabulary. The superimpositional order is not merely "occasioned" by ignorance in a loose sense; it depends upon that *ajñāna* also as *upādāna*. This is exactly why the passage became central in disputes over whether post-Śaṅkara Advaita had turned ignorance into a quasi-substantial causal principle.

### VI.16a

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *kathaṃ punar naimittikavyavahārasya naisargikatvam |*

**English (line-by-line):**
> But then how can a transactional order that is causally occasioned be natural or beginningless?

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| katham | katham | indeclinable interrogative | how |
| punaḥ | punaḥ | indeclinable | further; then again |
| naimittika-vyavahārasya | naimittika-vyavahāra | gen. sg. masc. | of the causally occasioned transaction/order |
| naisargikatvam | naisargikatva | nom./acc. sg. neut. | naturalness; congenital or beginningless status |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *naimittikavyavahāraḥ* [karmadhāraya/tatpuruṣa]: *nimittena bhavaḥ vyavahāraḥ*.
- *naisargikatvam* [abstract derivative]: *nisarga-sambandhitvaṃ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- genitive relation: *naimittikavyavahārasya*
- predicate questioned: *naisargikatvam*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb; elliptical interrogative nominal sentence.

**Note:**
This objection is essential. Once *mithyājñāna* has been made causal, one must explain how what is caused is also said to be beginningless. Padmapāda's answer will be to distinguish derivative episodes from the deeper, ever-attached *avidyāśakti* that underlies them.

### VI.16c

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *avaśyam eṣā avidyāśaktir bāhyādhyātmikeṣu vastuṣu tatsvarūpasattāmātrānubandhinī abhyupagantavyā |*

**English (line-by-line):**
> This *avidyāśakti* must indeed be admitted with respect to external and internal things, as invariably connected merely with the existence of their very nature.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| avaśyam | avaśyam | indeclinable | necessarily; certainly |
| eṣā | etad | nom. sg. fem. | this |
| avidyāśaktiḥ | avidyā-śakti | nom. sg. fem. | the power of ignorance |
| bāhyādhyātmikeṣu | bāhya-adhyātmika | loc. pl. neut. | in external and internal |
| vastuṣu | vastu | loc. pl. neut. | things; entities |
| tat-svarūpa-sattā-mātra-anubandhinī | tat-svarūpa-sattā-mātra-anubandhinī | nom. sg. fem. | connected merely with the existence of that very nature |
| abhyupagantavyā | abhi-upa-√gam | gerundive, nom. sg. fem. | must be admitted |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *avidyāśaktiḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *avidyāyāḥ śaktiḥ*.
- *bāhyādhyātmikeṣu* [dvandva used adjectivally]: *bāhyeṣu ca adhyātmikeṣu ca*.
- *tatsvarūpasattāmātrānubandhinī* [complex tatpuruṣa]: *tasya svarūpasya sattāmātrena anubandhinī*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- adhikaraṇa: *bāhyādhyātmikeṣu vastuṣu*
- kartṛ/predicate subject: *eṣā avidyāśaktiḥ ... abhyupagantavyā*

**Verbal modality:**
- *abhyupagantavyā*: gerundive of obligation; "must be admitted."

**Note:**
This is Padmapāda's most explicit move toward a positive ontology of ignorance. The gerundive is not casual. It marks doctrinal compulsion: unless one admits such a power as universally accompanying the very "there-being" of inner and outer entities, the phenomenology of error cannot be saved. The line is picked up programmatically by Prakāśātman in the *Vivaraṇa* tradition.

### VI.16d

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *anyathā mithyārthāvabhāsānupapatteḥ |*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Otherwise, because the appearance of false objects would be inexplicable.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| anyathā | anyathā | indeclinable | otherwise |
| mithyārtha-avabhāsa-anupapatteḥ | mithyārthāvabhāsānupapatti | abl./gen. sg. fem. | because of the impossibility of accounting for the appearance of false objects |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *mithyārthāvabhāsaḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *mithyānām arthānām avabhāsaḥ*.
- *anupapattiḥ* [abstract noun]: non-possibility; failure of explanatory fit.

**Kāraka structure:**
- hetu: *mithyārthāvabhāsānupapatteḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb; ablative of reason governs the understood conclusion.

**Note:**
Padmapāda's argument is explanatory, not merely lexical. Error is treated as a datum demanding a material-causal account. That insistence is what separates this passage from a thinner reading on which *ajñāna* would mean only absence of knowledge.

### XIX.62

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *paratra pūrvadṛṣṭāvabhāsaḥ |*

**English (line-by-line):**
> An appearance elsewhere of what was previously seen.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| paratra | paratra | indeclinable | elsewhere; in another locus |
| pūrvadṛṣṭāvabhāsaḥ | pūrvadṛṣṭa-avabhāsa | nom. sg. masc. | the appearance of what was seen before |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *pūrvadṛṣṭāvabhāsaḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *pūrvaṃ dṛṣṭasya avabhāsaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- adhikaraṇa/adverbial locus: *paratra*
- subject: *pūrvadṛṣṭāvabhāsaḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb; definitional nominal clause.

**Note:**
The crucial word is *paratra*. Padmapāda is ruling out a free-floating or substratum-less account of illusion. Superimposition is not mere memory, nor sheer fantasy; it is appearance "elsewhere," that is, in another locus.

### XXIX.107

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *bhavatu na vastvantaram; tad eva tad iti tu na kṣamyate, śuktikārajatasya mithyārūpasyāpi satyarajatākārāvabhāsitvadarśanāt |*

> Granted: let there be no second, distinct thing.

| bhavatu | √bhū | loṭ, 3rd sg., parasmaipada | let it be; granted |
| na | na | indeclinable | not |
| vastu-antaram | vastvantara | nom./acc. sg. neut. | a distinct thing |
| tad | tad | nom./acc. sg. neut. | that |
| eva | eva | indeclinable | precisely; indeed |
| tad | tad | nom./acc. sg. neut. | that |
| iti | iti | indeclinable | thus |
| tu | tu | indeclinable | but |
| na | na | indeclinable | not |
| kṣamyate | √kṣam | laṭ, 3rd sg., passive | is tolerated; is acceptable |

| satyarajatākāra-avabhāsitva-darśanāt | satya-rajata-ākāra-avabhāsitva-darśana | abl. sg. neut. | because of the seen fact of appearing in the form of real silver |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *vastvantaram* [tatpuruṣa]: *vastunaḥ antaraṃ*.

**Note:**
This frames the ensuing mahāvākya dispute. The issue is not whether image and original are two utterly separate entities, but whether their relation should be read under the model of sublatable illusion. Padmapāda rejects that move before he turns to *tat tvam asi*.

### XXIX.108c

**Sanskrit (IAST):**

**English (line-by-line):**
> There, in the expression "tat tvam," the *jīva* standing in the place of the reflection is taught to be of one nature with Brahman standing in the place of the original.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| tatra | tatra | indeclinable | there; in that case |
| tat tvam iti | tat tvam + iti | quoted expression | "tat tvam," thus |

| jīvasya | jīva | gen. sg. masc. | of the individual self |
| upadiśyate | upa-√diś | laṭ, 3rd sg., passive | is taught; is indicated |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *bimbasthānīya* [tatpuruṣa]: *bimbasya sthāne vartamānaḥ*.
- *pratibimbasthānīya* [tatpuruṣa]: *pratibimbasya sthāne vartamānaḥ*.
- *brahmaikasvarūpatā* [tatpuruṣa]: *brahmaṇa ekasvarūpatā*.

**Kāraka structure:**

- predicate taught: *bimbasthānīyabrahmaikasvarūpatā*

**Verbal modality:**
- *upadiśyate*: laṭ / passive.

**Note:**
This is the semantic use of the reflection model, not a mere illustrative afterthought. Liberation is recognition of shared nature, not annihilation of the *jīva* as though it were a wholly false object. Compare Padmapāda's own earlier definition at XIX.63b: *adhyāsa* is memory-formed appearance elsewhere; the mahāvākya works not by producing that kind of negation, but by rectifying misread relationality.

### XXIX.108d

### XXIX.108e

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *kintu na tvam asīti bhavet na rajatam astītivat |*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Rather, it would become "you are not" itself, just as in the case of "there is no silver."

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |

- *rajatam astīti-vat* [quotational comparison]: *"rajatam asti" iti-vat*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- comparison: *na rajatam astītivat*

**Verbal modality:**
- *asi*: laṭ / parasmaipada.
- *bhavet*: liṅ / parasmaipada.
- *asti*: laṭ / parasmaipada.

**Note:**
The example is exact and devastating. To read *tat tvam asi* as shell-silver sublation would be to make the mahāvākya operate like the judgment "there is no silver." Padmapāda rejects that model because it destroys the sentence's positive teaching-function.

### XXIX.109

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *kiṃ ca śāstrīyāpi vyavahāraḥ pratibimbasya pāramārthikameva bimbaikatvaṃ darśayati |*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Further, even scriptural usage shows precisely the ultimate oneness of the reflection with the original.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| kiṃ ca | kiṃ ca | indeclinable connective | moreover; further |
| śāstrīyā api | śāstrīya + api | nom. sg. masc./fem. used with *vyavahāraḥ* | even scriptural |

| darśayati | √dṛś | laṭ, 3rd sg., causative parasmaipada | shows; makes seen |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *śāstrīyaḥ* [taddhita adjective]: belonging to scripture.
- *bimbaikatvam* [tatpuruṣa]: *bimbena ekatvam*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *śāstrīyo vyavahāraḥ*
- karman: *bimbaikatvam*
- sambandha: *pratibimbasya*

- *darśayati*: laṭ / parasmaipada / causative active.

**Note:**
Padmapāda now appeals beyond local analogy to scriptural diction itself. The move is important because it prevents the *pratibimba* model from being dismissed as merely metaphorical. In the very next line of the text he cites the reflected sun in water to secure that scriptural support.

## Editorial closing

These loci settle three things for Padmapāda's line. First, *avidyā* is not left at the level of mere absence; at V.15–VI.16 it functions as a positive *śakti* whose admission is held explanatorily necessary. Second, *adhyāsa* is not bare recollection but a memory-formed appearance in an alien locus; that formula at XIX.62–63 becomes the compact definition inherited by later scholastic Advaita. Third, the mahāvākya does not liberate by simply cancelling the *jīva* as shell-silver is cancelled; at XXIX.108 it teaches *eka-svarūpatā* under the *bimba-pratibimba* model. What remains open in Padmapāda is the later systematisation of that ontology and of its locus/object structure. That work is undertaken by Prakāśātman in the *Pañcapādikā-Vivaraṇa*, especially on the V–VI locus, and is presupposed again whenever the same *adhyāsa* analysis is reactivated later in the surviving *Pañcapādikā* itself. Within Padmapāda's own arc, XIX.62–63 and XXIX.108 are the internal continuations of V.15–VI.16; within the *Vivaraṇa* tradition, they are the grammar by which the school reads Śaṅkara's opening bhāṣya.
