# Bṛhadāraṇyaka commentary fragments (reconstructed) — Extended Translation
**By:** Bhartṛprapañca  
**Locus scope:** Reconstruction dossier comprising the load-bearing Bṛhadāraṇyaka loci treated by Hiriyanna as doctrinal anchors: BṛU 1.4.7, 1.4.10, 1.4.15, 2.3.1, 2.3.6, 2.4.5.  
**Source edition transcribed from:** M. Hiriyanna, "Bhartṛprapañca: An Old Vedāntin," *The Indian Antiquary* 53 (1924): 77-86; loci normalized against local Vedānta citation witnesses because no Bhartṛprapañca autograph survives.  
**Translator:** Pāṇinian-rich extraction by Codex 5.4 (reasoning=high), 2026-05-09.

## Editorial introduction

This dossier translates, in Upaniṣadic order, the small but decisive cluster of loci through which Bhartṛprapañca's lost *Bṛhadāraṇyaka* commentary is reconstructible. The argument spread across these places is coherent. BṛU 1.4.7 states that what is now the manifold was once *avyākṛta* (undifferentiated, not-yet-articulated), and that it became manifest through *nāma-rūpa* (name-and-form). BṛU 1.4.10 refuses radical separateness by the sentence *ahaṃ brahmāsmi* and by the repudiation of worship that posits an absolute "other." BṛU 1.4.15 and 2.4.5 make liberating knowledge a disciplined sequence of *śravaṇa*, *manana*, *nididhyāsana*, and *upāsanā*, not a bare flash detached from ongoing practice. BṛU 2.3.1 and 2.3.6 then hold together the two-form teaching, *mūrta* and *amūrta*, with the apophatic restraint of *neti neti*. Taken together, these passages yield the architecture later readers ascribe to Bhartṛprapañca: real manifestation, real *abheda* (non-difference), no cancellation of difference, and a soteriology in which contemplative cultivation retains constitutive force.

This is the natural unit for the present file because the work itself survives only as reconstruction. No continuous adhikaraṇa or chapter can honestly be translated as Bhartṛprapañca's own running text. What can be translated with philological seriousness is the exact set of dispersed loci at which Hiriyanna's reconstruction becomes load-bearing. In the larger contour of the lost commentary, these passages are the points where ontology, scriptural hermeneutics, and soteriology touch. In the larger contour of Bhartṛprapañca's position, they show why he cannot be reduced either to a mere pluralist opponent of identity-statements or to an Advaitin avant la lettre. He is recoverable, rather, as an early Vedāntin for whom the manifold is real because Brahman is not robbed of manifestation, and for whom the identity-statements are real because the manifold does not stand outside Brahman.

## Translation

### BṛU 1.4.7a

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *tad dha idaṃ tarhy avyākṛtam āsīt ।*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Then indeed this was in the *avyākṛta* (undifferentiated, not-yet-articulated) state.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| tad | *tad* | pronoun; nom. sg. neut. | that |
| dha | *dha* | indeclinable emphatic particle | indeed, then |
| idaṃ | *idam* | pronoun; nom. sg. neut. | this |
| tarhi | *tarhi* | indeclinable temporal particle | then, at that point |
| avyākṛtam | *a-vyākṛta* < *vi-ā-kṛ* | *kta* derivative; nom. sg. neut. | undifferentiated, unarticulated |
| āsīt | *as* | *laṅ*; 3rd sg.; parasmaipada; active | was |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *avyākṛtam* [nañ-tatpuruṣa]: *na vyākṛtam* - "not differentiated."

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *tad idaṃ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *āsīt*: *laṅ* / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
The sentence is ontologically plain and therefore philosophically weighty. It says not that manifestation is error, but that the presently manifest whole once stood in an undifferentiated condition. Hiriyanna makes this locus decisive because the later realist reading of manifestation requires precisely such a transition; compare the reconstructed use of BṛU 2.3.1 and 2.3.6 below, where differentiation is not denied but bounded.

### BṛU 1.4.7b

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *tan nāmarūpābhyām eva vyākriyata ॥*

**English (line-by-line):**
> That was differentiated precisely by *nāma-rūpa* (name-and-form).

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| tan | *tad* | pronoun; nom. sg. neut. | that |
| nāmarūpābhyām | *nāma-rūpa* | instrumental dual neut. | by name and form |
| eva | *eva* | indeclinable restrictive particle | precisely, just |
| vyākriyata | *vi-ā-kṛ* | *laṅ*; 3rd sg.; ātmanepada; passive-middle value | was differentiated, became articulated |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *nāmarūpābhyām* [itaretara-dvandva]: *nāma ca rūpaṃ ca* - "name and form."

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *tan*
- karaṇa: *nāmarūpābhyām*

**Verbal modality:**
- *vyākriyata*: *laṅ* / ātmanepada / passive-middle

**Note:**
The instrumental dual does real work. Manifestation occurs through the twin determiners of name and form; it is not described as a merely subjective imposition. In the reconstructed dossier this line stands in immediate tension-and-coherence with *ahaṃ brahmāsmi* (BṛU 1.4.10): the differentiated manifold is affirmed without abandoning non-difference.

### BṛU 1.4.10a

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *ahaṃ brahmāsmi ।*

**English (line-by-line):**
> I am Brahman.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| ahaṃ | *aham* | pronoun; nom. sg. | I |
| brahma | *brahman* | nom. sg. neut. | Brahman |
| asmi | *as* | *laṭ*; 1st sg.; parasmaipada; active | am |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- No compound in this line.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *ahaṃ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *asmi*: *laṭ* / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
Nothing in the surviving reconstruction permits this line to be discarded or weakened. Bhartṛprapañca's distinctiveness lies elsewhere: the identity-sentence is accepted without turning the manifold into a nullity. Later scholarship commonly describes this as a doctrine of *abheda* preserved under a non-eliminative reading, often paraphrased through the reconstructed notion of *aupacārika abhinnatva*.

### BṛU 1.4.10b

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *atha yo 'nyāṃ devatām upāste 'nyo 'sāv anyo 'ham asmīti na sa veda ॥*

**English (line-by-line):**
> But whoever worships another deity, thinking, "He is one thing; I am another," that person does not know.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| atha | *atha* | indeclinable connective | but, now |
| yaḥ | *yad* | relative pronoun; nom. sg. masc. | who |
| anyām | *anya* | acc. sg. fem. | other, different |
| devatām | *devatā* | acc. sg. fem. | deity |
| upāste | *upa-ās* | *laṭ*; 3rd sg.; ātmanepada; middle | worships, attends upon |
| anyaḥ | *anya* | nom. sg. masc. | other |
| asau | *adas* | pronoun; nom. sg. masc. | that one |
| anyaḥ | *anya* | nom. sg. masc. | other |
| aham | *aham* | pronoun; nom. sg. | I |
| asmi | *as* | *laṭ*; 1st sg.; parasmaipada; active | am |
| iti | *iti* | indeclinable quotative | thus |
| na | *na* | indeclinable negation | not |
| sa | *tad* | pronoun; nom. sg. masc. | he, that person |
| veda | *vid* | *laṭ*; 3rd sg.; parasmaipada; active | knows |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- No compound in this line.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *yaḥ* (for *upāste*), resumed as *sa* (for *veda*)
- karman: *anyāṃ devatām*

**Verbal modality:**
- *upāste*: *laṭ* / ātmanepada / middle
- *asmi*: *laṭ* / parasmaipada / active
- *veda*: *laṭ* / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
The quotation marks the error precisely: not devotion as such, but devotion under the cognition of radical otherness. That anti-separative force is one reason the Bhartṛprapañca dossier cannot be read as simple pluralism. Compare BṛU 1.4.15 and 2.4.5 below: the path of hearing, reflection, meditation, and *upāsanā* exists to reshape exactly this false cognition of absolute apartness.

### BṛU 1.4.15

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *ātmānam eva lokam upāsīta ॥*

**English (line-by-line):**
> One should contemplate the self alone as the world.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| ātmānam | *ātman* | acc. sg. masc. | the self |
| eva | *eva* | indeclinable restrictive particle | alone, precisely |
| lokam | *loka* | acc. sg. masc. | the world |
| upāsīta | *upa-ās* | optative; 3rd sg.; ātmanepada; middle | should contemplate, should worship |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- No compound in this line.

**Kāraka structure:**
- karman: *ātmānam*
- karmapravacanīya-predicate relation: *lokam* as the form under which the self is to be contemplated

**Verbal modality:**
- *upāsīta*: *vidhi-liṅ* / ātmanepada / middle

**Note:**
This line is crucial for the reconstructed soteriology. *Upāsanā* is not ornamental aftermath but an enjoined contemplative posture in which the self is to be taken as world-inclusive. Read with BṛU 1.4.10, the sentence deepens rather than dilutes non-difference: the self is not to be severed from the world, nor the world from the self's Brahman-reference.

### BṛU 2.3.1

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *dve vāva brahmaṇo rūpe mūrtaṃ caivāmūrtaṃ ca ।*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Brahman has indeed two forms: the formed and the formless.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| dve | *dvi* | nom. dual neut. | two |
| vāva | *vāva* | indeclinable emphatic particle | indeed |
| brahmaṇo | *brahman* | gen. sg. neut. | of Brahman |
| rūpe | *rūpa* | nom. dual neut. | forms |
| mūrtam | *mūrta* | nom. sg. neut. | the formed, the determinate |
| ca | *ca* | indeclinable connective | and |
| eva | *eva* | indeclinable restrictive particle | just, precisely |
| amūrtam | *a-mūrta* | nom. sg. neut.; privative formation | the formless |
| ca | *ca* | indeclinable connective | and |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *amūrtam* [nañ-tatpuruṣa]: *na mūrtam* - "not formed."

**Kāraka structure:**
- sambandha: *brahmaṇo* with *rūpe*

**Verbal modality:**
- No overt finite verb; nominal predication with the copula understood.

**Note:**
The line matters because it positively enumerates dual aspect within Brahman. Bhartṛprapañca's reconstructed position depends on refusing the later pressure to save apophasis by treating such positive duality as a merely provisional teaching. Compare at once the next line, *neti neti*: denial follows affirmation, but does not erase the affirmed two-form structure.

### BṛU 2.3.6

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *neti neti ॥*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Not this. Not this.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| na | *na* | indeclinable negation | not |
| iti | *iti* | indeclinable quotative-demonstrative closure | thus, this |
| na | *na* | indeclinable negation | not |
| iti | *iti* | indeclinable quotative-demonstrative closure | thus, this |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *neti* is sandhi for *na iti*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- No overt kāraka-relations in this elliptical denial.

**Verbal modality:**
- No overt finite verb; repeated apophatic negation.

**Note:**
The force of the repetition is delimitative, not nihilistic. It bars exhaustive capture of Brahman by any finite predicate. In the reconstructed Bhartṛprapañca dossier, therefore, *neti neti* must be read with BṛU 2.3.1, not against it: Brahman truly has *mūrta* and *amūrta* forms, yet is not exhausted by either determinate description.

### BṛU 2.4.5

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *ātmā vā are draṣṭavyaḥ śrotavyo mantavyo nididhyāsitavyaḥ ।*

**English (line-by-line):**
> The self, dear one, is to be realized, to be heard about, to be reflected upon, and to be deeply meditated upon.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| ātmā | *ātman* | nom. sg. masc. | the self |
| vā | *vā* | indeclinable emphatic particle | indeed |
| are | *are* | indeclinable vocative particle | dear one |
| draṣṭavyaḥ | *dṛś* | kṛtya in *-tavya*; nom. sg. masc. gerundive | to be seen, to be realized |
| śrotavyaḥ | *śru* | kṛtya in *-tavya*; nom. sg. masc. gerundive | to be heard about |
| mantavyaḥ | *man* | kṛtya in *-tavya*; nom. sg. masc. gerundive | to be reflected upon |
| nididhyāsitavyaḥ | *ni-di-dhyai* | kṛtya in *-tavya*; nom. sg. masc. gerundive | to be deeply meditated upon |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- No compound in this line.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ-bhāvya / vidheya-base: *ātmā* as the entity subjected to the sequence of gerundive obligations

**Verbal modality:**
- *draṣṭavyaḥ*, *śrotavyaḥ*, *mantavyaḥ*, *nididhyāsitavyaḥ*: kṛtya-gerundives in *-tavya*; prescriptive necessity rather than finite *lakāra*

**Note:**
This is the strongest surviving scriptural base for the reconstructed insistence that liberating cognition has a disciplined temporal structure. Hearing, reflection, and contemplative assimilation are all grammatically enjoined. Read with BṛU 1.4.15, the line explains why later reports connect Bhartṛprapañca with a strong form of *jñāna-karma-samuccaya*: the path is not exhausted by a single sentence-event but unfolds through repeated and normatively structured practice.

## Editorial closing

What this dossier settles is limited but important. It settles that the reconstructed Bhartṛprapañca cannot be honestly described either as a denier of the great identity-statements or as a thinker for whom the world is a dispensable concession. BṛU 1.4.7 secures real manifestation; BṛU 1.4.10 secures anti-separative identity; BṛU 2.3.1 together with 2.3.6 secures the two-form articulation under apophatic reserve; BṛU 1.4.15 and 2.4.5 secure a soteriology of continued contemplative discipline. What remains open is equally significant: the exact metaphysical form of the relation between *bheda* and *abheda*, the fuller shape of the reported *aṣṭa-rūpa* taxonomy, and the precise extent to which ritual action, rather than contemplative *upāsanā* alone, entered the liberating path. Those questions are taken up only indirectly in the hostile witnesses, above all Śaṅkara's *Bṛhadāraṇyaka-Bhāṣya* on BṛU 1.4.7, 1.4.10, 2.3.1-6, 2.4.5, and Sureśvara's *Bṛhadāraṇyaka-Vārttika*, where the anti-*jñāna-karma-samuccaya* reaction becomes explicit.
