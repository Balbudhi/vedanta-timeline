## Editorial introduction

This dossier translates the four places at which Śrīnivāsa's own doctrinal voice is securely recoverable in the present corpus. The opening maṅgala does not merely salute Kṛṣṇa. It states a hermeneutic thesis: the body of *śruti* (revealed Vedic scripture) reaches non-contradiction when referred to him. The upodghāta then fixes the author's self-understanding with unusual precision. The *Vedānta-Kaustubha* is written under the teacher's command, along the teacher's path, and for the learned reader's benefit; it is not announced as a new system, but as an instrument for making Nimbārka's terse intention manifest. That architectural role becomes explicit at BS 1.1.1, where Śrīnivāsa compresses the whole school's starting ontology into three short lines: *tattva* (reality) is threefold because *cit* (sentient selves), *acit* (insentient reality), and Brahman are genuinely distinct, yet Brahman and the *jīvātman* (individual self) stand in an asymmetrical relation of *svādhīna* (independence) and *parādhīna* (dependence). BS 2.3.42 gives the mature formulation of that asymmetry: the *jīva* is neither sheerly other nor sheerly identical, but an *aṃśa* (part, share) of the supreme, and that *aṃśa* is to be understood as *śakti* (power).

This is the natural unit for the present file because the accessible Bose volume does not preserve continuous Sanskrit, while the local thinker file already marks these four loci as the work's load-bearing recoveries. A fictive continuous adhikaraṇa translation would therefore overclaim textual security. A dossier is more exact. It is not arbitrary, however. These loci form a single arc: theological orientation, authorial method, first-principle ontology, and the decisive *aṃśa-aṃśin* clarification. In the word tables below, external sandhi is regularly resolved back into underlying padas.

## Translation

### Maṅgalācaraṇa 1a

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *śrīkṛṣṇapādayugalaṃ śirasā namāmi*

**English (line-by-line):**
> I bow with my head to the pair of Śrī Kṛṣṇa's feet.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| śrīkṛṣṇapādayugalam | *śrīkṛṣṇa-pādayugala* | compound noun; acc. sg. neut. | the pair of Śrī Kṛṣṇa's feet |
| śirasā | *śiras* | instr. sg. neut. | with the head |
| namāmi | √*nam* | *laṭ*; 1st sg.; parasmaipada; active | I bow |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *śrīkṛṣṇapādayugalam* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *śrīkṛṣṇasya pādayugalam*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: understood *aham*
- karman: *śrīkṛṣṇapādayugalam*
- karaṇa: *śirasā*

**Verbal modality:**
- *namāmi*: *laṭ* / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
The line survives in the local corpus only through a recovered quotation, but its grammar is clear. The bodily instrumental *śirasā* makes the invocation an enacted reverence, not a merely decorative preface. Theologically it prepares the stronger claim of the next line: scriptural concord is grounded in Kṛṣṇa.

### Maṅgalācaraṇa 1b

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *yatrānvitaḥ śrutigaṇo na virodham eti //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Connected with whom, the host of *śrutis* (revealed Vedic scriptures) does not enter into contradiction.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| yatra | *yatra* | indeclinable relative adverb | in relation to whom, with reference to whom |
| anvitaḥ | *anvita* | past passive participle; nom. sg. masc. | connected, linked |
| śrutigaṇaḥ | *śruti-gaṇa* | compound noun; nom. sg. masc. | the aggregate of śrutis |
| na | *na* | indeclinable negative particle | not |
| virodham | *virodha* | acc. sg. masc. | contradiction, conflict |
| eti | √*i* | *laṭ*; 3rd sg.; parasmaipada; active | goes to, enters into |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *śrutigaṇaḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *śrutīnām gaṇaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *śrutigaṇaḥ*
- gati-karman: *virodham*
- adhikaraṇa-like relational qualifier: *yatra anvitaḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *eti*: *laṭ* / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
The grammatical center is not Kṛṣṇa as a merely praised deity, but *śrutigaṇaḥ* as the subject that does or does not fall into contradiction. Śrīnivāsa's opening claim is therefore hermeneutic as well as devotional. It anticipates the work's later insistence, at BS 1.1.1 and BS 2.3.42, that plurality and non-plurality must both be read without mutual cancellation.

### Upodghāta 1

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *tadājñayā taduktavartmanā tadanugrahakāmena*

**English (line-by-line):**
> By his command; by the path taught by him; with the desire for his favor.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| tadājñayā | *tad-ājñā* | compound noun; instr. sg. fem. | by his command |
| taduktavartmanā | *tad-ukta-vartman* | compound noun; instr. sg. neut. | by the path spoken by him |
| tadanugrahakāmena | *tad-anugraha-kāma* | compound noun; instr. sg. masc. | with desire for his favor |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *tadājñayā* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *tasya ājñayā*.
- *taduktavartmanā* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *tasya uktaṃ vartma; tena*.
- *tadanugrahakāmena* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *tasya anugrahasya kāmena*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- karaṇa / nimitta: *tadājñayā*
- karaṇa / mārga-indicator: *taduktavartmanā*
- hetu / prayojana: *tadanugrahakāmena*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb occurs in this line; all three instrumentals are construed forward with *viracyate* in line 3.

**Note:**
The line is an instrumental cluster, and that matters. Śrīnivāsa grammatically subordinates authorship to command, inherited path, and hoped-for favor before he names either himself or his text. The commentary is framed as faithful extension, not as autonomous invention.

### BS 1.1.1b

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *cid-acid-brahma-bhedāt |*

**English (line-by-line):**
> because of the distinction among *cit* (sentient selves), *acit* (insentient reality), and Brahman.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| cid-acid-brahma-bhedāt | *cid-acid-brahma-bheda* | compound noun; abl. sg. masc. | because of the distinction among sentient, insentient, and Brahman |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *cid-acid-brahma-bhedāt* [composite tatpuruṣa]: *cit ca acit ca brahma ca; teṣāṃ bhedāt*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- hetu / apādāna: *cid-acid-brahma-bhedāt*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb occurs in the surface line; the ablative gives the reason for the preceding nominal predication.

**Note:**
The line compresses the school's starting ontology into a single ablative of reason. It does not merely list three items; it says that reality's threefoldness is grounded in their genuine differentiation. Later, at BS 2.3.42, that differentiation will be qualified from the side of dependence rather than abolished.

### BS 1.1.1c

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *svādhīna-parādhīnau brahmajīvātmānāv ity arthaḥ ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> This is the meaning: Brahman and the *jīvātman* are the independent and the dependent.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| svādhīna-parādhīnau | *svādhīna-parādhīna* | dvandva compound; nom. dual masc. | independent and dependent |
| brahmajīvātmānau | *brahma-jīvātman* | dvandva compound; nom. dual masc. | Brahman and the individual self |
| iti | *iti* | indeclinable quotative particle | thus |
| arthaḥ | *artha* | nom. sg. masc. | the meaning |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *svādhīna-parādhīnau* [itaretara-dvandva]: *svādhīnaś ca parādhīnaś ca*.
- *brahmajīvātmānau* [itaretara-dvandva]: *brahma ca jīvātmā ca*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *brahmajīvātmānau*
- predicative qualifiers: *svādhīna-parādhīnau*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb occurs in the surface line; dual nominal predication with an understood copula.  
- *iti* marks the author's own paraphrastic gloss.

**Note:**
This is where triadic ontology becomes asymmetrical relation. Difference is not sheer bilateral externality; one pole is *svādhīna*, the other *parādhīna*. The same dependence-grammar returns, in a more elaborated form, at BS 2.3.42e, and it also recalls Nimbārka's *Daśa-Ślokī* 1, where the *jīva* is said to be *hareḥ adhīna*.

This is the decisive interpretive move. *Aṃśa* is not allowed to harden into a crude spatial fragment cut off from a whole; it is read as *śakti*. The half-quoted phrase *parā asya śaktiḥ* points toward the well-known scriptural language of Śvetāśvatara Upaniṣad 6.8, and the point of the citation is to spiritualize parthood without dissolving dependence.

## Editorial closing

What this dossier settles is the governing grammar of Śrīnivāsa's sub-commentary. The opening lines settle that the work is both theologically personalist and hermeneutically unificatory: *śruti* reaches concord in Kṛṣṇa. BS 1.1.1 then settles the first ontological asymmetry: *cit*, *acit*, and Brahman are irreducibly real, yet Brahman and the *jīvātman* stand as *svādhīna* and *parādhīna*. BS 2.3.42 settles how that asymmetry is to be read at the level of relation: neither sheer difference nor sheer identity, but *aṃśa* understood as dependent *śakti*. What is left open here is the larger dialectical defense against rival Vedāntins and the fuller development of causality, bondage, and liberation. Those questions are taken up in the immediately following loci of the work, above all BS 1.1.2-4 on Brahman as source and BS 2.3.43-45 in the continuation of the *aṃśa* discussion, and they are later codified for the tradition in Keśava-Kāśmīrī's *Kaustubha-Prabhā* on the same sūtras.
