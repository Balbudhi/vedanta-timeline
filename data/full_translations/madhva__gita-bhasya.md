# Gītā-Bhāṣya

**By:** Madhva (Ānandatīrtha)
**Genre:** *bhāṣya* on the *Bhagavad-Gītā*

---

## Status

A reliably edited Sanskrit text of Madhva's *Gītā-Bhāṣya* is not currently in our corpus. The Vedānta interactive timeline does not have a defensible Sanskrit witness on disk for this text, and producing an annotated translation would mean fabricating engagement we cannot ground.

What is widely known about this work:

- The *Gītā-Bhāṣya* is Madhva's continuous prose commentary on the *Bhagavad-Gītā*, in eighteen *adhyāyas*, composed in the thirteenth century. It is companion to the *Gītā-Tātparya-Nirṇaya*, which states the doctrinal upshot of the same verses in a separate work.
- The bhāṣya's interpretive signature is its insistence that the Gītā teaches *Viṣṇu-paratva* (Viṣṇu as the supreme reality), real and irreducible difference between *jīva*, *jagat*, and *Īśvara*, and *bhakti* as the principal direct means to liberation. It uses *pramāṇa* citations from across the *prasthāna-traya* and *Pāñcarātra* to anchor each interpretive choice.

What is in our corpus that bears on it:

- Madhva's `core_thesis` and engaged_works summary in the timeline entry (see https://balbudhi.github.io/vedanta-timeline/ and locate Madhva).
- Files for Madhva's *Brahma-Sūtra-Bhāṣya*, *Anuvyākhyāna*, *Viṣṇu-Tattva-Vinirṇaya*, and the smaller dialectical *prakaraṇas* (*Māyāvāda-Khaṇḍana*, *Mithyātvānumāna-Khaṇḍana*, *Tattvodyota*, *Upādhi-Khaṇḍana*), which articulate the metaphysics presupposed by the *Gītā-Bhāṣya*.
