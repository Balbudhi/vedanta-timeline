# Tattva-Saṅkhyāna — Extended Translation
**By:** Madhva  
**Locus scope:** Entire eleven-verse *Tattva-Saṅkhyāna*; this is the natural unit because the work itself is a compact complete ontological synopsis, and its argumentative force depends on the whole progression from *svatantra/paratantra* division through *jīva*-classification to the closing statement of Viṣṇu's universal governance.  
**Source edition transcribed from:** *Tattva-Saṅkhyānam* of Śrīmad Ānandatīrtha Bhagavatpādācārya, with the *Ṭīkā* of Śrī Jayatīrtha, the *Ṭippaṇīs* of Śrī Rāghavendra Tīrtha and Śrī Śrīnivāsa Tīrtha, and selected notes from Śrī Vijayīndra Tīrtha, Śrī Satyadharma Tīrtha, Śrī Roṭṭiveṅkaṭabhaṭṭa, Śrī Keśavācārya, and Kāśī Śrī Timmaṇṇācārya; ed. Vidyāviśārada Nyāyavedāntacūḍāmaṇi Śrī Māgaḍi Raṅganāthācārya; English tr. B. Veṅkaṭeśācārya (Bangalore: Śrī Rāmakṛṣṇappa Dvaita Vedānta Pāṭhaśālā / Śrī Vyāsarāja Maṭha, 1964). Verse text was recovered from the scan-transcription of that edition and checked against the edition's internal English contents and translation.

## Editorial introduction

The *Tattva-Saṅkhyāna* is one of Madhva's shortest works, but it is not a beginner's hand-list. It is a compressed ontology. Verses 1-2 make the decisive first cut: reality is twofold as *svatantra* (independent) and *asvatantra* (dependent), and the dependent in turn divides into *bhāva* (being) and *abhāva* (non-being), then into *cetana* (sentient) and *acetana* (insentient). Verses 3-7 unfold the sentient order by graded difference. Ramā alone stands eternally untouched by sorrow; all other conscious beings are sorrow-touched, then distributed as liberated, release-fit, darkness-fit, and those remaining in transmigratory course, with a further fourfold specification of the *tamo-yogya* class. Verses 8-11 turn to the insentient order and then close by drawing the whole classified field back under Viṣṇu: creation, maintenance, destruction, regulation, ignorance and knowledge, bondage and liberation, pleasure and pain, concealment and manifestation all stand from him.

That is why the whole tract, and not a smaller excerpt, is the proper scope. In an eleven-verse work the order itself is argument. If one detached only the opening binary of verse 1, one would miss the positive ontology of *abhāva* in verse 2, the singular status of Ramā in verse 3, the hierarchy of *jīvas* in verses 4-7, and the final claim of total dependence in verses 10-11. The tract also occupies a precise place in Madhva's larger corpus. *Tattva-Viveka* supplies the scriptural dossier for these same determinations. *Anuvyākhyāna* 1.1.13-15 restates the *svatantra-paratantra* asymmetry in more openly inferential form, and 1.4.111-112 states the fivefold *bheda* that the present hierarchy presupposes throughout. The polemical tractates, from *Tattvodyota* to the anti-Advaita triad, take this ontological field for granted and defend it against rival readings.

## Translation

### Verse 1

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *svatantram asvatantraṃ ca dvividhaṃ tattvam iṣyate |*  
> *svatantro bhagavān viṣṇuḥ, bhāvābhāvau dvidhā itarat || 1 ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Reality is held to be twofold: the independent and the dependent.  
> The independent is the Blessed Lord Viṣṇu.  
> The other, the dependent, is twofold.  
> It is being and non-being.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| svatantram | *svatantra* | nom/acc sg neut | independent |
| asvatantram | *a-svatantra* | nom/acc sg neut | dependent; not independent |
| ca | *ca* | indecl. | and |
| dvividham | *dvi-vidha* | nom/acc sg neut | twofold |
| tattvam | *tattva* | nom/acc sg neut | reality; category |
| iṣyate | √*iṣ* | 3rd sg laṭ, ātmanepada, passive/middle | is held; is admitted |
| svatantro | *svatantra* | nom sg masc | independent |
| bhagavān | *bhagavant* | nom sg masc | the Lord; the Blessed One |
| viṣṇuḥ | *viṣṇu* | nom sg masc | Viṣṇu |
| bhāvābhāvau | *bhāva-abhāva* | nom dual masc | being and non-being |
| dvidhā | *dvidhā* | indecl. | in two ways; twofold |
| itarat | *itara* | nom/acc sg neut | the other; the remaining one |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *asvatantra* [nañ-tatpuruṣa]: *na svatantram*.
- *dvividha* [numeral tatpuruṣa]: *dve vidhe yasya tat*.
- *bhāvābhāva* [dvandva]: *bhāvaś cābhāvaś ca*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- logical subject: *tattvam*
- predicative qualifiers: *svatantram*, *asvatantraṃ*, *dvividham*
- second clause subject: *svatantro bhagavān viṣṇuḥ*
- third/fourth clause subject: *itarat*
- predicative content of *itarat*: *bhāvābhāvau dvidhā*

**Verbal modality:**
- *iṣyate*: laṭ / ātmanepada / passive-middlish impersonal usage

**Note:**
The verse is not merely classificatory. Its grammar isolates Viṣṇu from the subsequent *bhāva/abhāva* split. The dependent alone becomes twofold as being and non-being; the independent is first named, then exempted from that secondary partition. This is the same asymmetry later restated more dynamically at *Anuvyākhyāna* 1.1.13-15 and presupposed throughout *Tattvodyota*.

### Verse 2

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *prākpradhvaṃsasadātvena trividho 'bhāva iṣyate |*  
> *cetanācetanatvena bhāvo 'pi dvividho mataḥ || 2 ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Non-being is held to be threefold: as prior, as posterior-destruction, and as perpetual.  
> Being too is regarded as twofold.  
> That is by reason of sentientness and insentientness.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| prākpradhvaṃsasadātvena | *prāk-pradhvaṃsa-sadātva* | inst sg neut | by the condition of being prior, subsequent-destruction, and perpetual |
| trividhaḥ | *tri-vidha* | nom sg masc | threefold |
| abhāvaḥ | *abhāva* | nom sg masc | non-being; absence |
| iṣyate | √*iṣ* | 3rd sg laṭ, ātmanepada, passive/middle | is held; is admitted |
| cetanācetanatvena | *cetana-acetanatva* | inst sg neut | by sentientness and insentientness |
| bhāvaḥ | *bhāva* | nom sg masc | being |
| api | *api* | indecl. | also; too |
| dvividhaḥ | *dvi-vidha* | nom sg masc | twofold |
| mataḥ | √*man* > *mata* | past passive participle, nom sg masc | regarded; considered |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *prākpradhvaṃsasadātva* [coordinative tatpuruṣa]: *prāk ca pradhvaṃsaś ca sadātvaṃ ca* as the three modes under which *abhāva* is reckoned.
- *trividha* [numeral tatpuruṣa]: *trīṇi vidhāni yasya saḥ*.
- *cetanācetanatva* [dvandva + *-tva* abstract]: *cetanaṃ cācetanaṃ ca, tayor bhāvaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- subject: *abhāvaḥ*
- instrumental of classificatory criterion: *prākpradhvaṃsasadātvena*
- second clause subject: *bhāvaḥ*
- instrumental of classificatory criterion: *cetanācetanatvena*

**Verbal modality:**
- *iṣyate*: laṭ / ātmanepada / passive-middlish impersonal usage
- *mataḥ*: participial predication; no separate finite verb expressed

**Note:**
Madhva here assigns *abhāva* full categorial standing. That matters. The dependent order is not exhausted by positive entities. Jayatīrtha's *Ṭīkā* makes the anti-Prābhākara edge explicit, and the same insistence on the reality of structured absence is part of the larger realist resistance to any flattening of the world into undifferentiated appearance.

### Verse 3

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *duḥkhaspṛṣṭaṃ tadaspṛṣṭam iti dvedhā eva cetanam |*  
> *nityāduḥkhā ramā, anye tu spṛṣṭaduḥkhāḥ samastaśaḥ || 3 ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> The sentient is just twofold: that touched by suffering and that not touched by it.  
> Ramā is eternally without suffering.  
> But all the others are altogether touched by suffering.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| duḥkhaspṛṣṭam | *duḥkha-spṛṣṭa* | nom/acc sg neut | touched by suffering |
| tadaspṛṣṭam | *tad-aspṛṣṭa* | nom/acc sg neut | not touched by that |
| iti | *iti* | indecl. | thus |
| dvedhā | *dvedhā* | indecl. | in two ways; twofold |
| eva | *eva* | indecl. | just; precisely |
| cetanam | *cetana* | nom/acc sg neut | the sentient |
| nityāduḥkhā | *nitya-aduḥkhā* | nom sg fem | eternally free from suffering |
| ramā | *ramā* | nom sg fem | Ramā |
| anye | *anya* | nom pl masc | the others |
| tu | *tu* | indecl. | but |
| spṛṣṭaduḥkhāḥ | *spṛṣṭa-duḥkha* | nom pl masc | touched by suffering |
| samastaśaḥ | *samastaśas* | indecl. | altogether; universally |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *duḥkhaspṛṣṭa* [tṛtīyā-tatpuruṣa sense]: *duḥkhena spṛṣṭam*.
- *tadaspṛṣṭa* [nañ-like negative formation with pronoun]: *tena aspṛṣṭam*.
- *nityāduḥkhā* [nañ-tatpuruṣa / karmadhāraya]: *nityam aduḥkhā*.
- *spṛṣṭaduḥkha* [bahuvrīhi-like adjectival]: *spṛṣṭaṃ duḥkhaṃ yebhyaḥ* or simply “those for whom suffering has made contact.”

**Kāraka structure:**
- subject: *cetanam*
- predicate divisions: *duḥkhaspṛṣṭam*, *tadaspṛṣṭam*
- second clause subject: *ramā*
- predicate: *nityāduḥkhā*
- third clause subject: *anye*
- predicate: *spṛṣṭaduḥkhāḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- Nominal classification; no finite verb is expressed.

**Note:**
The singular treatment of Ramā is doctrinally load-bearing. She is not simply placed at the top of a common scale; she is grammatically separated from the rest of the sentient order. The asymmetry between Ramā and all other *cetanas* underwrites the later hierarchy rather than merely ornamenting it. Compare Madhva's larger theology of Lakṣmī in *Viṣṇu-Tattva-Vinirṇaya*.

### Verse 4

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *spṛṣṭaduḥkhā vimuktāś ca duḥkhasaṃsthā iti dvidhā |*  
> *duḥkhasaṃsthā muktiyogyā ayogyā iti ca dvidhā || 4 ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Those touched by suffering are of two kinds: the liberated, and those established in suffering.  
> Those established in suffering are of two kinds.  
> They are those fit for liberation and those unfit.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| spṛṣṭaduḥkhāḥ | *spṛṣṭa-duḥkha* | nom pl masc | those touched by suffering |
| vimuktāḥ | *vimukta* | nom pl masc | liberated |
| ca | *ca* | indecl. | and |
| duḥkhasaṃsthāḥ | *duḥkha-saṃstha* | nom pl masc | those established in suffering |
| iti | *iti* | indecl. | thus |
| dvidhā | *dvidhā* | indecl. | in two ways; twofold |
| duḥkhasaṃsthāḥ | *duḥkha-saṃstha* | nom pl masc | those established in suffering |
| muktiyogyāḥ | *mukti-yogya* | nom pl masc | fit for liberation |
| ayogyāḥ | *a-yogya* | nom pl masc | unfit |
| iti | *iti* | indecl. | thus |
| ca | *ca* | indecl. | and |
| dvidhā | *dvidhā* | indecl. | in two ways; twofold |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *spṛṣṭaduḥkha* [bahuvrīhi-like adjectival]: *spṛṣṭaṃ duḥkhaṃ yeṣām*.
- *duḥkhasaṃstha* [saptamī-tatpuruṣa]: *duḥkhe saṃsthāḥ*.
- *muktiyogya* [caturthī-tatpuruṣa]: *muktaye yogyaḥ*.
- *ayogya* [nañ-tatpuruṣa]: *na yogyaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- subject of first clause: *spṛṣṭaduḥkhāḥ*
- predicate members: *vimuktāḥ*, *duḥkhasaṃsthāḥ*
- subject of second clause: *duḥkhasaṃsthāḥ*
- predicate members: *muktiyogyāḥ*, *ayogyāḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- Nominal classification; no finite verb is expressed.

**Note:**
Here the famously controversial Madhva hierarchy is stated with complete calmness. There is no dialectical defense yet, only sorted placement. The verse does not argue for the classification; it presupposes that the dependent order is already internally graded. The argumentative defense belongs elsewhere, especially in Madhva's Gītā 16 materials and the later scholastic elaborations of Tattva-vāda (the school often labeled Dvaita).

### Verse 5

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *devarṣi-pitṛ-pā narā iti muktās tu pañcadhā |*  
> *evaṃ vimuktiyogyāś ca tamoyogyāḥ sṛtisaṃsthitāḥ || 5 ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> The liberated are fivefold: gods, seers, fathers, protectors, and men.  
> So too the release-fit [are fivefold].  
> The unfit are the darkness-fit and those established in the current [of transmigration].

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| devarṣi-pitṛ-pāḥ | *deva-ṛṣi-pitṛ-pā* | nom pl masc | gods, seers, fathers, and protectors |
| narāḥ | *nara* | nom pl masc | men |
| iti | *iti* | indecl. | thus |
| muktāḥ | *mukta* | nom pl masc | the liberated |
| tu | *tu* | indecl. | indeed; for emphasis |
| pañcadhā | *pañcadhā* | indecl. | in five ways; fivefold |
| evam | *evam* | indecl. | thus; likewise |
| vimuktiyogyāḥ | *vi-mukti-yogya* | nom pl masc | those fit for complete release |
| ca | *ca* | indecl. | and |
| tamoyogyāḥ | *tamas-yogya* | nom pl masc | those fit for darkness |
| sṛtisaṃsthitāḥ | *sṛti-saṃsthita* | nom pl masc | those established in the current; those remaining in transmigratory course |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *devarṣi-pitṛ-pāḥ* [dvandva]: *devāś cārṣayaś ca pitaraś ca pāś ca*.
- *vimuktiyogya* [caturthī-tatpuruṣa]: *vimuktaye yogyaḥ*.
- *tamoyogya* [caturthī-tatpuruṣa]: *tamase yogyaḥ*.
- *sṛtisaṃsthita* [saptamī-tatpuruṣa]: *sṛtau saṃsthitaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- subject: *muktāḥ*
- enumerated members: *devarṣi-pitṛ-pāḥ*, *narāḥ*
- second clause subject: *vimuktiyogyāḥ* with *pañcadhā* carried over elliptically from the previous half
- third clause gives the two classes of the unfit: *tamoyogyāḥ*, *sṛtisaṃsthitāḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- Nominal classification; no finite verb is expressed.

**Note:**
The verse compresses two classificatory operations. First, the liberated are fivefold. Second, by ellipsis, the same fivefold division extends to the *vimuktiyogya* class. Jayatīrtha explicitly supplies that carried-over structure. The final two terms then state the two branches of the ineligible. The compression is doctrinally efficient but grammatically demanding.

### Verse 7

> And those [darkness-fit beings] are of two kinds: those who have reached blind darkness, and those established in the current [toward it].

- *prāptāndhatamas* [karmadhāraya / tatpuruṣa]: *andhatamaḥ prāptaḥ ye*.

The transition is abrupt because the work is meant as index, not as defended treatise.

### Verse 8

**Sanskrit (IAST):**

> The Vedas are eternal.  
> The eternal-non-eternal is said to be threefold: the Purāṇas and the like, time, and primal matter.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |

| nityāḥ | *nitya* | nom pl masc | eternal |
| vedāḥ | *veda* | nom pl masc | the Vedas |

- *purāṇādi* [tatpuruṣa]: *purāṇāni ādīni yeṣām* / “beginning with the Purāṇas.”
- *nityānitya* [dvandva/karmadhāraya used as category-name]: what is in one respect eternal and in another non-eternal.

**Note:**
The category *nityānitya* is one of the places where Madhva's ontology refuses simple binary cleanup. Time and primal matter are not simply slotted as either absolutely eternal or simply produced. The point is not terminological cleverness. It is an attempt to track dependence without flattening distinct modes of persistence. Compare the supporting scriptural marshaling in *Tattva-Viveka*.

### Verse 9

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *asaṃsṛṣṭaṃ ca saṃsṛṣṭam | asaṃsṛṣṭaṃ mahān ahaṃ |*  
> *buddhir manaḥ khāni daśa mātrā bhūtāni pañca ca || 9 ||*

**English (line-by-line):**
> The non-eternal is of two kinds: the not-wholly-produced and the wholly-produced.

**Kāraka structure:**
- opening subject: the implied *anityam*
- predicate members: *asaṃsṛṣṭam*, *saṃsṛṣṭam*

**Verbal modality:**
- Nominal classification; no finite verb is expressed.

**Note:**
The list is recognizably Sāṅkhya-like, but not Sāṅkhya in system-location. These principles are not independent explanatory ultimates. They are dependent categories within Madhva's *paratantra* order. The reuse of inherited cosmological vocabulary without granting it independent status is part of Madhva's larger strategy across *Tattva-Viveka* and the *Brahma-Sūtra-Bhāṣya*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- enumerated subject-matter: *bandhaḥ*, *mokṣaḥ*, *sukham*, *duḥkham*, *āvṛtiḥ*, *jyotiḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- Nominal sentence completed from verse 10; no finite verb is expressed.

**Note:**
This is the doctrinal seal of the tract. The point is not only that entities depend on Viṣṇu. Processes, states, disclosure and concealment, bondage and release themselves stand within his governance. Compare *Anuvyākhyāna* 1.1.13: *dravyaṃ karma ca kālaś ca svabhāvo jīva eva ca / yadanugrahataḥ santi na santi yadupekṣayā*. Jayatīrtha's introduction also cites the *Bhāgavata* line on exactly this dependence-grammar.

## Editorial closing

What this passage settles is not every proof, but the map to which the proofs answer. It settles, first, that dependence is ontological before it is devotional: Viṣṇu alone is *svatantra*; everything else is *paratantra*. Second, it settles that the dependent order is irreducibly articulated: absence, sentience, insentience, liberation-status, cosmological process, and epistemic condition each have determinate place. Third, it leaves open, by design, the justificatory labor. Why *abhāva* must be admitted as real, why the hierarchy of *jīvas* is scripturally warranted, how the inherited Sāṅkhya categories are to be resituated without surrendering their independence-claim, and how rival systems fail: these belong to other works. For scriptural backing see *Tattva-Viveka* on the same categorial sequence. For the dependence-claim in a more argumentative key see *Anuvyākhyāna* 1.1.13-15. For the fivefold *bheda* presupposed by the entire hierarchy see *Anuvyākhyāna* 1.4.111-112. For the defense against identity-readings see *Tattvodyota* and the anti-Advaita triad. The present tract is therefore not a primitive sketch superseded by later elaboration. It is Madhva's ontology in index-form.
