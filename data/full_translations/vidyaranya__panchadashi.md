## Editorial introduction

This dossier is intentionally dispersed. A short contiguous run of verses cannot contain all the Pañcadaśī loci already treated in the site corpus as load-bearing for Vidyāraṇya's position; yet the dispersion is not arbitrary. Verses 1.15-17 state the causal architecture: *prakṛti* (primordial causal matrix), when differentiated by the purity and impurity of *sattva*, yields the paired explanatory register of *māyā* and *avidyā*, and with that pair the asymmetry between *Īśvara* and the bound *jīva*. Verse 1.42 then gives the chapter's decisive image for discriminative extraction: the self drawn out from the three bodies like the fibre from the *muñja* stalk. Verses 1.43-48 immediately interpret that discrimination through the *mahāvākya* logic of *tat tvam asi*, not by flattening terms into crude identity, but by a controlled *bhāga-tyāga-lakṣaṇā* (indication through discarding mutually incompatible parts). Finally, verses 1.53-55 convert ontology into practice: *śravaṇa* (hearing the teaching), *manana* (rational reflection), *nididhyāsana* (steady contemplative assimilation), and *samādhi* (absorptive stabilization) are presented as a serial discipline rather than as optional embellishment.

Taken together, these thirteen verses form the chapter's middle doctrinal spine. They show how Vidyāraṇya's Advaita moves from causal cosmology to self-discrimination, from self-discrimination to sentence-meaning, and from sentence-meaning to contemplative realization. The same sequence governs the wider architecture of the *Pañcadaśī*: chapter 6 (*Citra-dīpa*) returns to *pratibimba* (reflection), chapter 8 (*Kūṭastha-dīpa*) reworks the witness-self with greater phenomenological precision, and chapter 9 (*Dhyāna-dīpa*) expands the contemplative side only sketched here. What is settled in this arc is therefore not a local aside but the operative grammar of the work.

## Translation

### Pañcadaśī 1.15

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *cidānandamayabrahmapratibimbasamanvitā*  
> *tamorajaḥsattvaguṇā prakṛtirdvividhā ca sā ॥ 15 ॥*

**English (line-by-line):**
> *Prakṛti* (primordial causal matrix), conjoined with the reflection of Brahman consisting of consciousness and bliss,  
> possessing the *guṇas* (constitutive strands) of *tamas*, *rajas*, and *sattva*, is twofold; and that indeed is she.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| cidānandamayabrahmapratibimbasamanvitā | cid-ānanda-maya-brahma-pratibimba-samanvita | nom sg fem | endowed with the reflection of Brahman consisting of consciousness and bliss |
| tamorajaḥsattvaguṇā | tamo-rajaḥ-sattva-guṇa | nom sg fem | having the guṇas tamas, rajas, and sattva |
| prakṛtiḥ | prakṛti | nom sg fem | primordial matrix; *prakṛti* |
| dvividhā | dvividha | nom sg fem | twofold |
| ca | ca | indecl | and |
| sā | tad | nom sg fem | that; she |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *cidānandamayabrahmapratibimbasamanvitā* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa + kṛdanta chain]: *cidānandamayasya brahmaṇaḥ pratibimben samanvitā*.
- *tamorajaḥsattvaguṇā* [dvandva + bahuvrīhi-like adjectival compound]: *tamaś ca rajaś ca sattvaṃ ca guṇā yasmin sā*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *prakṛtiḥ ... sā*
- viśeṣaṇa: *cidānandamayabrahmapratibimbasamanvitā*, *tamorajaḥsattvaguṇā*, *dvividhā*

**Verbal modality:**
- No finite verb is expressed; nominal predication is understood.

**Note:**
The verse compresses the whole causal problem into appositional grammar. *Prakṛti* is not introduced as sheer inert matter; it is already marked by *pratibimba* (reflection), which anticipates the more elaborate reflection-analysis of *Pañcadaśī* 6. The predicate *dvividhā* prepares the next verse's doctrinal bifurcation into *māyā* and *avidyā* without yet naming them.

### Pañcadaśī 1.16

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *sattvaśuddhāviśuddhibhyāṃ māyā'vidye ca te mate*  
> *māyābimbovaśīkṛtya tāṃ syātsarvajña īśvaraḥ ॥ 16 ॥*

**English (line-by-line):**
> By the purity and impurity of *sattva*, the two are considered to be *māyā* and *avidyā*;  
> the reflection in *māyā*, having brought that under control, becomes the omniscient *Īśvara*.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| sattvaśuddhāviśuddhibhyām | sattva-śuddhi-aviśuddhi | instr dual fem | by the purity and impurity of sattva |
| māyā'vidye | māyā + avidyā | nom dual fem | māyā and avidyā |
| ca | ca | indecl | and |
| te | tad | nom dual fem | those two |
| mate | mata | nom dual fem ppp used predicatively | are considered; are held |
| māyābimbaḥ | māyā-bimba | nom sg masc | the reflection in māyā |
| vaśīkṛtya | vaśī-√kṛ | absolutive | having brought under control |
| tām | tad | acc sg fem | that; her |
| syāt | √as | 3 sg vidhi-liṅ parasmaipada | would be; becomes; is to be understood as |
| sarvajñaḥ | sarva-jña | nom sg masc | all-knowing; omniscient |
| īśvaraḥ | īśvara | nom sg masc | the Lord; *Īśvara* |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *sattvaśuddhāviśuddhibhyām* [dvandva-tatpuruṣa]: *sattvasya śuddhiś cāviśuddhiś ca tābhyām*.
- *māyā'vidye* [dvandva]: *māyā ca avidyā ca*.
- *māyābimbaḥ* [saptamī-tatpuruṣa]: *māyāyāṃ bimbaḥ*.
- *sarvajñaḥ* [upapada-kṛdanta sense]: *sarvaṃ jānāti iti*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- hetu/karaṇa: *sattvaśuddhāviśuddhibhyām*
- kartṛ (first clause): *te* understood with *māyā'vidye ... mate*
- kartṛ (second clause): *māyābimbaḥ*
- karman: *tām*
- predicate nominatives: *sarvajñaḥ*, *īśvaraḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *syāt*: vidhi-liṅ / parasmaipada / active
- *mate*: past passive participle used predicatively

**Note:**
The optative *syāt* here is definitional rather than hypothetical. What matters philosophically is that the distinction between *māyā* and *avidyā* is not one of two unrelated substances but of one causal field under two conditions of *sattva*. Compare the later reworking of the same reflection-logic in *Pañcadaśī* 6.21-30.

### Pañcadaśī 1.17

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *avidyāvaśagastvanyastadvaicitryādanekadhā*  
> *sā kāraṇaśarīraṃ syātprājñastatrābhimānavān ॥ 17 ॥*

**English (line-by-line):**
> But another, being under the power of *avidyā*, becomes manifold because of its diversity;  
> that is the causal body, and the one who bears identification there is *Prājña*.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| avidyāvaśagaḥ | avidyā-vaśaga | nom sg masc | subject to the power of avidyā |
| tu | tu | indecl | but |
| anyaḥ | anya | nom sg masc | another |
| tadvaicitryāt | tad-vaicitrya | abl sg neut | because of its variety |
| anekadhā | anekadhā | indecl | in many ways; manifoldly |
| sā | tad | nom sg fem | that |
| kāraṇaśarīram | kāraṇa-śarīra | nom/acc sg neut | causal body |
| syāt | √as | 3 sg vidhi-liṅ parasmaipada | is; becomes |
| prājñaḥ | prājña | nom sg masc | Prājña, the deep-sleep self |
| tatra | tatra | indecl | there |
| abhimānavān | abhimānavant | nom sg masc | possessing identification; self-identifying |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *avidyāvaśagaḥ* [tatpuruṣa]: *avidyāyā vaśaṃ gataḥ*.
- *tadvaicitryāt* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *tasya vaicitryāt*.
- *kāraṇaśarīram* [tatpuruṣa]: *kāraṇa-rūpaṃ śarīram*.
- *abhimānavān* [matup-like possessive]: *abhimāno yasya saḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *anyaḥ*
- hetu: *tadvaicitryāt*
- avasthā/predicate: *anekadhā*
- predicate nominative: *prājñaḥ*
- adhikaraṇa: *tatra*

**Verbal modality:**
- *syāt*: vidhi-liṅ / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
This is the paired counterpart to verse 16. The omniscience of *Īśvara* and the plurality of the bound subject are both explained through different relations to the same causal field. The identification-term *abhimānavān* is philosophically exact: bondage is not mere presence in the causal body, but self-location in it. Compare *Pañcadaśī* 1.24, where the same logic of identification reappears in the transition from *Prājña* to *Taijasa*.

### Pañcadaśī 1.42

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *yathāmuñjādiṣīkaivamātmā yuktyā samuddhṛtaḥ*  
> *śarīratritayāddhīraiḥ paraṃ brahmaiva jāyate ॥ 42 ॥*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Just as the inner fibre is drawn out from *muñja* grass, so the self, drawn out by reasoning,  
> by the steady-minded from the triad of bodies, becomes nothing other than the supreme Brahman.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| yathā | yathā | indecl | just as |
| muñjāt | muñja | abl sg masc | from muñja grass |
| iṣīkā | iṣīkā | nom sg fem | the inner fibre; pith-fibre |
| evam | evam | indecl | so; thus |
| ātmā | ātman | nom sg masc | the self |
| yuktyā | yukti | instr sg fem | by reasoning; by discriminative method |
| samuddhṛtaḥ | sam-ud-√dhṛ | nom sg masc ppp | drawn out; extracted |
| śarīratritayāt | śarīra-tritaya | abl sg neut | from the triad of bodies |
| dhīraiḥ | dhīra | instr pl masc | by the steadfast; by the wise |
| param | para | acc sg neut | supreme |
| brahma | brahman | acc sg neut | Brahman |
| eva | eva | indecl | indeed; just |
| jāyate | √jan | 3 sg laṭ ātmanepada | becomes; comes to be recognized as |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *śarīratritayāt* [dvigu]: *śarīrāṇāṃ tritayāt*.
- *samuddhṛtaḥ* [preverb + kṛdanta]: *samyag uddhṛtaḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kartṛ: *ātmā*
- karaṇa: *yuktyā*
- apādāna: *śarīratritayāt*
- kartṛ-karaṇa (agentive instrumental): *dhīraiḥ*
- predicate complement: *paraṃ brahma*

**Verbal modality:**
- *jāyate*: laṭ / ātmanepada / middle

**Note:**
The middle voice of *jāyate* does not mean that Brahman is produced. The sense is recognitional: once the self is analytically disengaged from the three bodies, it stands forth as Brahman. The verse echoes *Kaṭha Upaniṣad* 2.3.17, where the self is similarly to be drawn out from the body like a fibre from *muñja*.

### Pañcadaśī 1.43

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *parāparātmanorevaṃ yuktyā sambhāvitaikatā*  
> *tattvamasyādivākyaiḥ sā bhāgatyāgena lakṣyate ॥ 43 ॥*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Thus the unity of the higher Self and the lower self, rendered intelligible by reasoning,  
> is indicated by sentences beginning with "tat tvam asi," through the abandonment of incompatible parts.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| parāparātmanoḥ | para-apara-ātman | gen/loc dual masc | of the higher and lower self |
| evam | evam | indecl | thus |
| yuktyā | yukti | instr sg fem | by reasoning |
| sambhāvitaikatā | sambhāvita-ekatā | nom sg fem | unity rendered plausible; inferred unity |
| tattvamasyādivākyaiḥ | tattvamasi-ādi-vākya | instr pl neut | by sentences such as "tat tvam asi" |
| sā | tad | nom sg fem | that |
| bhāgatyāgena | bhāga-tyāga | instr sg masc | by abandoning the parts |
| lakṣyate | √lakṣ | 3 sg laṭ ātmanepada/passive | is indicated; is secondarily signified |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *parāparātmanoḥ* [dvandva-tatpuruṣa]: *parasya cāparasya cātmanaḥ*.
- *sambhāvitaikatā* [karmadhāraya/tatpuruṣa]: *sambhāvitā ekatā*.
- *tattvamasyādivākyaiḥ* [tatpuruṣa]: *tattvamasi-ādīni vākyāni taiḥ*.
- *bhāgatyāgena* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *bhāgānāṃ tyāgena*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- karaṇa: *yuktyā*, *tattvamasyādivākyaiḥ*, *bhāgatyāgena*
- kartṛ: *sā* referring to *sambhāvitaikatā*
- sambandha: *parāparātmanoḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *lakṣyate*: laṭ / ātmanepada / passive

**Note:**
The verse insists on a sequence: first *yukti* makes unity intelligible, then the *mahāvākya* indicates it. Sentence-meaning is not treated as a brute magical event. On the semantic method itself compare the illustrative statement in the next verse-cluster, especially 1.47, where Vidyāraṇya gives the standard "so 'yam" model for *bhāga-tyāga-lakṣaṇā*.

### Pañcadaśī 1.44

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *jagato yadupādānaṃ māyāmādāya tāmasīm*  
> *nimittaṃ śuddhasattvāṃ tāmucyate brahma tadgirā ॥ 44 ॥*

**English (line-by-line):**
> That which, taking up *māyā* in its tamasic form, is the material cause of the world,  
> and that same which, taking her as pure *sattva*, is the efficient cause, is called Brahman by that word.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| jagataḥ | jagat | gen sg neut | of the world |
| yat | yad | nom sg neut relative pronoun | that which |
| upādānam | upādāna | nom/acc sg neut | material cause |
| māyām | māyā | acc sg fem | māyā |
| ādāya | ā-√dā | absolutive | having taken up; assuming |
| tāmasīm | tāmasī | acc sg fem | tamasic; characterized by tamas |
| nimittam | nimitta | nom/acc sg neut | efficient cause |
| śuddhasattvām | śuddha-sattva | acc sg fem | pure-sattva |
| tām | tad | acc sg fem | that; her |
| ucyate | √vac | 3 sg laṭ ātmanepada/passive | is called |
| brahma | brahman | nom sg neut | Brahman |
| tadgirā | tad-gir | instr sg fem | by that word; by that expression |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *śuddhasattvām* [karmadhāraya]: *śuddhaṃ sattvaṃ yasyāṃ tām*.
- *tadgirā* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *tasya girā*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- sambandha: *jagataḥ*
- kartṛ (relative clause): *yat*
- karman of absolutive: *māyām*, *tām*
- predicate complements: *upādānam*, *nimittam*, *brahma*
- karaṇa: *tadgirā*

**Verbal modality:**
- *ucyate*: laṭ / ātmanepada / passive

**Note:**
The verse distributes causal language across one referent under two descriptions. Vidyāraṇya is not multiplying ultimate principles; he is specifying how the term *brahma* functions when linked with cosmological causality. Compare *Pañcadaśī* 6.4-6, where the relation of pure consciousness to reflected and conditioned consciousness is developed with greater analytic care.

### Pañcadaśī 1.45

**Sanskrit (IAST):**

> *ādatte tatparaṃ brahma tvaṃ padena tadocyate ॥ 45 ॥*

**English (line-by-line):**
> When that supreme Brahman takes up that one as possessed of impure *sattva*, defiled by desire, action, and the rest,

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |

| yadā | yadā | indecl | when |

| tām | tad | acc sg fem | that; her |
| kāmakarmādidūṣitām | kāma-karma-ādi-dūṣita | acc sg fem | defiled by desire, action, and the rest |
| ādatte | ā-√dā | 3 sg laṭ ātmanepada | takes up; assumes |

| param | para | nom/acc sg neut | supreme |

| padena | pada | instr sg neut | by the word; by the term |
| tat | tad | nom/acc sg neut | that |
| ucyate | √vac | 3 sg laṭ ātmanepada/passive | is called; is denoted |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *malinasattvām* [karmadhāraya]: *malinaṃ sattvaṃ yasyāṃ tām*.
- *kāmakarmādidūṣitām* [tatpuruṣa]: *kāmaiḥ karmabhiś cādibhiś ca dūṣitām*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- kāla: *yadā*
- kartṛ: *tat paraṃ brahma*
- karman: *tām*
- karaṇa: *padena*
- predicate: *ucyate*

**Verbal modality:**
- *ādatte*: laṭ / ātmanepada / middle
- *ucyate*: laṭ / ātmanepada / passive

**Note:**
Verse 45 provides the counterweight to 44. The *tvam*-term is not read as the empirical ego in all its limitation, but as Brahman under the upādhi (limiting adjunct) of impurity-marked causal individuation. The next verse will therefore require discarding the mutually contradictory adjuncts, not erasing the semantic force of either word altogether.

### Pañcadaśī 1.46

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *tritayīmapi tāṃ muktvā parasparavirodhinīm*  
> *akhaṇḍaṃ saccidānandaṃ mahāvākyena lakṣyate ॥ 46 ॥*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Having abandoned even that triad, mutually opposed to one another,  
> the undivided *sat-cit-ānanda* (being-consciousness-bliss) is indicated by the great sentence.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| tritayīm | tritayī | acc sg fem | the triad |
| api | api | indecl | even |
| tām | tad | acc sg fem | that |
| muktvā | √muc | absolutive | having abandoned |
| parasparavirodhinīm | paraspara-virodhinī | acc sg fem | mutually contradictory |
| akhaṇḍam | akhaṇḍa | nom/acc sg neut | undivided |
| saccidānandam | sat-cit-ānanda | nom/acc sg neut | being-consciousness-bliss |
| mahāvākyena | mahā-vākya | instr sg neut | by the great sentence |
| lakṣyate | √lakṣ | 3 sg laṭ ātmanepada/passive | is indicated |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *parasparavirodhinīm* [avyayībhāva/tatpuruṣa]: *parasparaṃ virodhinīm*.
- *saccidānandam* [dvandva treated as a unity-expression]: *sat ca cit ca ānandaś ca*.
- *mahāvākyena* [karmadhāraya]: *mahat vākyaṃ tena*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- absolutive-complement/karman: *tritayīm ... tām*
- viśeṣaṇa: *parasparavirodhinīm*
- kartṛ: *akhaṇḍaṃ saccidānandam*
- karaṇa: *mahāvākyena*

**Verbal modality:**
- *lakṣyate*: laṭ / ātmanepada / passive

**Note:**
The "triad" is the complex of mutually incompatible adjuncts generated in the causal analysis of the preceding verses. The grammar is important: the text does not discard *sat*, *cit*, and *ānanda*, but the contradictory upādhis through which the same Brahman is provisionally named as cosmic and individual. That is why *akhaṇḍaṃ* stands where it does, as the semantic residue after disciplined negation.

### Pañcadaśī 1.47

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *so'yamityādivākyeṣu virodhāttadidantvayoḥ*  
> *tyāgena bhāgayoreka āśrayo lakṣyate yathā ॥ 47 ॥*

**English (line-by-line):**
> In sentences such as "this is that," because of the incompatibility of "that-ness" and "this-ness,"  
> by abandoning those two portions, a single substratum is indicated, as in that familiar case.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| saḥ | tad | nom sg masc | that |
| ayam | idam | nom sg masc | this |
| iti | iti | indecl | thus |
| ādivākyeṣu | ādi-vākya | loc pl neut | in sentences beginning thus |
| virodhāt | virodha | abl sg masc | because of incompatibility |
| tadidantvayoḥ | tad-idantva | gen/loc dual neut | of that-ness and this-ness |
| tyāgena | tyāga | instr sg masc | by abandonment |
| bhāgayoḥ | bhāga | gen/loc dual masc | of the two parts |
| ekaḥ | eka | nom sg masc | one |
| āśrayaḥ | āśraya | nom sg masc | substratum; common locus |
| lakṣyate | √lakṣ | 3 sg laṭ ātmanepada/passive | is indicated |
| yathā | yathā | indecl | just as |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *ādivākyeṣu* [tatpuruṣa]: *ādiṣu vākyeṣu*.
- *tadidantvayoḥ* [dvandva-tatpuruṣa]: *tat-tvaṃ ca idaṃ-tvaṃ ca tayoḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- adhikaraṇa: *ādivākyeṣu*
- hetu: *virodhāt*
- karaṇa: *tyāgena*
- sambandha: *bhāgayoḥ*, *tadidantvayoḥ*
- kartṛ: *eka āśrayaḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *lakṣyate*: laṭ / ātmanepada / passive

**Note:**
This is Vidyāraṇya's explicit semantic model. The sentence "so 'yam devadattaḥ" is the stock illustration for how secondary indication works when two context-bound qualifiers clash but one referent remains. The same method is then transferred to the *mahāvākya*. Compare Śaṅkara's parallel use of the example in *Bṛhadāraṇyaka-Upaniṣad-Bhāṣya* 1.4.10.

### Pañcadaśī 1.48

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *māyāvidye vihāyaivamupādhī parajīvayoḥ*  
> *akhaṇḍaṃ saccidānandaṃ paraṃ brahmaiva lakṣyate ॥ 48 ॥*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Thus, having abandoned *māyā* and *avidyā*, the limiting adjuncts of the supreme and the individual self,  
> the undivided being-consciousness-bliss alone, the supreme Brahman, is indicated.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| māyāvidye | māyā + avidyā | acc dual fem | māyā and avidyā |
| vihāya | vi-√hā | absolutive | having abandoned |
| evam | evam | indecl | thus |
| upādhī | upādhi | acc dual masc | limiting adjuncts |
| parajīvayoḥ | para-jīva | gen dual masc | of the supreme and the individual self |
| akhaṇḍam | akhaṇḍa | nom/acc sg neut | undivided |
| saccidānandam | sat-cit-ānanda | nom/acc sg neut | being-consciousness-bliss |
| param | para | nom/acc sg neut | supreme |
| brahma | brahman | nom/acc sg neut | Brahman |
| eva | eva | indecl | alone; indeed |
| lakṣyate | √lakṣ | 3 sg laṭ ātmanepada/passive | is indicated |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *māyāvidye* [dvandva]: *māyā ca avidyā ca*.
- *parajīvayoḥ* [dvandva-tatpuruṣa]: *parasya ca jīvasya ca*.
- *saccidānandam* [dvandva treated as unitary designation]: *sat ca cit ca ānandaś ca*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- absolutive-karman: *māyāvidye*, *upādhī*
- sambandha: *parajīvayoḥ*
- kartṛ: *akhaṇḍaṃ saccidānandaṃ paraṃ brahma*

**Verbal modality:**
- *lakṣyate*: laṭ / ātmanepada / passive

**Note:**
Verse 48 states the result of the semantic operation with maximal clarity. The text does not say that *māyā* and *avidyā* were unreal in the trivial sense of never having functioned; it says they are to be relinquished as *upādhis* in fixing the final purport of the sentence. That nuance matters for avoiding the illicit collapse of *mithyā* into sheer non-being.

### Pañcadaśī 1.53

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *itthaṃ vākyaistadarthānusandhānaṃ śravaṇaṃ bhavet*  
> *yuktyā sambhāvitatvānusandhānaṃ mananantu tat ॥ 53 ॥*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Thus, the sustained tracing of the meaning of those sentences by means of the sentences is called *śravaṇa* (hearing);  
> and the tracing of their plausibility by reasoning, that indeed is *manana* (reflective consideration).

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| ittham | ittham | indecl | thus |
| vākyaiḥ | vākya | instr pl neut | by the sentences |
| tadarthānusandhānam | tad-artha-anusandhāna | nom/acc sg neut | tracing the meaning of that |
| śravaṇam | śravaṇa | nom/acc sg neut | hearing; scriptural reception |
| bhavet | √bhū | 3 sg vidhi-liṅ parasmaipada | is to be; is called |
| yuktyā | yukti | instr sg fem | by reasoning |
| sambhāvitatvānusandhānam | sambhāvitatva-anusandhāna | nom/acc sg neut | tracing the plausibility; reflective following-through |
| mananam | manana | nom/acc sg neut | reflection |
| tu | tu | indecl | but; and |
| tat | tad | nom/acc sg neut | that |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *tadarthānusandhānam* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *tasya arthasya anusandhānam*.
- *sambhāvitatvānusandhānam* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa]: *sambhāvitatvasya anusandhānam*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- karaṇa: *vākyaiḥ*, *yuktyā*
- kartṛ/predicate subject: *tadarthānusandhānam*, *sambhāvitatvānusandhānam*
- predicate nominatives: *śravaṇam*, *mananam*

**Verbal modality:**
- *bhavet*: vidhi-liṅ / parasmaipada / active

**Note:**
The definition is operational, not honorific. *Śravaṇa* is not bare auditory reception, and *manana* is not free meditation untethered to scripture; both are forms of *anusandhāna*, disciplined following-through. Vidyāraṇya will expand the contemplative side later in *Pañcadaśī* 9, but the sequence is already fixed here.

### Pañcadaśī 1.54

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *tābhyāṃ nirvicikitse'rthe cetasaḥ sthāpitasya yat*  
> *ekatānatvametaddhi nididhyāsanamucyate ॥ 54 ॥*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Of the mind established, by those two, in a matter free from doubt, whatever one-streamed continuity there is,

| nirvicikitse | nirvicikitsa | loc sg neut | in what is free from doubt |

- *ekatānatvam* [tatpuruṣa]: *ekasya tānasya bhāvaḥ*.

### Pañcadaśī 1.55

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *dhyātṛdhyāne parityajya kramāddhyeyaikagocaram*  
> *nirvātadīpavaccittaṃ samādhirabhidhīyate ॥ 55 ॥*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Abandoning, step by step, meditator and meditation, and becoming directed to the object of meditation alone,  
> a mind like a lamp in a windless place is called *samādhi*.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| dhyātṛdhyāne | dhyātṛ + dhyāna | acc dual neut/masc construed as object-pair | meditator and meditation |
| parityajya | pari-√tyaj | absolutive | having abandoned |
| kramāt | krama | abl sg masc used adverbially | gradually; step by step |
| dhyeyaikagocaram | dhyeya-eka-gocara | nom/acc sg neut | having the object of meditation alone as its field |
| nirvātadīpavat | nirvāta-dīpa-vat | indecl | like a lamp in a windless place |
| cittam | citta | nom/acc sg neut | mind |
| samādhiḥ | samādhi | nom sg masc | samādhi |
| abhidhīyate | abhi-√dhā | 3 sg laṭ ātmanepada/passive | is designated; is called |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *dhyātṛdhyāne* [dvandva]: *dhyātā ca dhyānaṃ ca*.
- *dhyeyaikagocaram* [tatpuruṣa]: *dhyeyam eva ekaṃ gocaro yasya tat*.
- *nirvātadīpavat* [upamāna compound]: *nirvāte dīpavat*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- absolutive-karman: *dhyātṛdhyāne*
- apādāna/adverbial progression: *kramāt*
- predicate adjective: *dhyeyaikagocaram*, *nirvātadīpavat*
- kartṛ: *cittam*
- predicate nominative: *samādhiḥ*

**Verbal modality:**
- *abhidhīyate*: laṭ / ātmanepada / passive

**Note:**
The verse's grammar prevents a crude collapse into blankness. What remains is not nothing, but *dhyeyaika-gocara* mind, mind whose field is the object alone. The lamp simile is of course shared with *Bhagavad-Gītā* 6.19, and Vidyāraṇya is clearly placing his contemplative account within that larger Advaita-Yoga inheritance.

## Editorial closing

What this passage-set settles is Vidyāraṇya's operative sequence. The distinction between *māyā* and *avidyā* fixes the causal asymmetry between lordship and bondage; the *muñja* simile states what discrimination must do to the self-body complex; the *mahāvākya* verses determine how non-dual identity is to be linguistically grasped; and the final triad of *śravaṇa*, *manana*, and *nididhyāsana/samādhi* turns that grasp into stabilized realization. What it does not yet settle is the fuller phenomenology of witness-consciousness, reflection, and contemplative maturation. Those are taken up elsewhere in the same work: *pratibimba* and image-consciousness in *Citra-dīpa* (chapter 6), the witness and the unmoving *kūṭastha* in chapter 8, and a more expansive discipline of meditation in *Dhyāna-dīpa* (chapter 9). On the liberation-side, the practical deepening of this chapter's closing claims is pursued still more explicitly in Vidyāraṇya's *Jīvanmukti-viveka*, especially in its discussions of stabilization, *vāsanā*-attenuation, and post-cognitive freedom.
