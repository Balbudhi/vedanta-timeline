**By:** Madhva

## Editorial introduction

This brief tract is one of the sharpest points in Madhva's anti-Advaita triad. It argues, not yet against a refined *anumāna* for *mithyātva* as in the later *Mithyātvānumāna-Khaṇḍana*, and not yet against the *upādhi*-strategy as in the *Upādhi-Khaṇḍana*, but against the very admissibility of *Māyāvāda* as a coherent doctrinal beginning. The opening inferential form is therefore exacting: the opponent's thesis is said to be *anārambhaṇīya* (not fit to be undertaken), because it presents matters otherwise than they are. What follows is a compressed chain. If *brahmātmaikya* (identity of Brahman and self) were real, ignorance could not be coherently placed; if ignorance cannot be coherently placed, then the whole five-part pedagogical architecture of Advaita collapses with it. Madhva then turns, with characteristic economy, from destructive dialectic to scriptural closure. *Bhagavad-Gītā* 15.16-20 and *Kaṭha Upaniṣad* 1.3.10-11 are marshalled to show that the real burden of scripture lies not on an undifferentiated absolute but on the supremacy of Viṣṇu as *puruṣottama* (the supreme person). The tract is thus naturally whole: its first half destabilizes the opponent's entry-point, and its second half reassigns scriptural authority to Madhva's own *tattvavāda* (realist doctrine of principles). Within Madhva's larger corpus, this text stands between the *Upādhi-Khaṇḍana* and the *Mithyātvānumāna-Khaṇḍana* in both placement and argumentative logic. Compare especially *Upādhi-Khaṇḍana* for the attack on conditioning-factors, *Mithyātvānumāna-Khaṇḍana* for the later formal criticism of the *mithyātva* inference, and *Tattvodyota* for the broader denial that essential difference can be cancelled by a merely verbal non-difference.

## Translation

### Opening benediction (p. 238)

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *nṛsiṃho 'khilājñāna-mata-dhvānta-divākaraḥ*  
> *jayaty amita-saj-jñāna-sukha-śakti-payonidhiḥ //*

**English (line-by-line):**
> Narasiṃha, the sun that dispels the darkness constituted by every doctrine of ignorance, triumphs;  
> he is an unfathomable ocean of true knowledge, bliss, and power.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| nṛsiṃhaḥ | nṛsiṃha | `-ḥ`; nom sg masc | Narasiṃha |
| akhilājñāna-mata-dhvānta-divākaraḥ | compound | `-ḥ`; nom sg masc | sun to the darkness of all ignorance-doctrines |
| jayati | ji | 3 sg laṭ parasmaipada | triumphs, shines victorious |
| amita-saj-jñāna-sukha-śakti-payonidhiḥ | compound | `-ḥ`; nom sg masc | immeasurable ocean of true knowledge, bliss, and power |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *akhilājñāna-mata-dhvānta-divākaraḥ* [ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣa with internal tatpuruṣas]: *akhilānām ajñāna-matānāṃ dhvāntasya divākaraḥ*.

**Kāraka structure:**

**Note:**
The opening is not merely devotional ornament. Madhva names the target in the compound itself: the darkness to be removed is not generic ignorance but the doctrinally articulated *mata* that makes ignorance basic. The tract's polemical burden is announced before the argument begins.

### Inferential thesis (p. 238)

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *vimatam anārambhaṇīyam, anyathā-pratipādakatvāt, yad itthaṃ tat tathā, yathā sampratipannam.*

**English (line-by-line):**
> The disputed thesis is not fit to be undertaken, because it presents matters otherwise than they are.  
> Whatever is of such a kind is indeed so, as is presently admitted.

**Word-by-word:**

| Pada | Stem / root | Pratyaya / morphology | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| vimatam | vimata | `-am`; nom sg neut | the disputed doctrine, the opponent's thesis |
| anārambhaṇīyam | an-ā-rambhaṇīya | `-am`; nom sg neut gerundive | not to be undertaken, not defensible as a starting point |
| anyathā-pratipādakatvāt | anyathā-pratipādakatva | `-āt`; abl sg neut | because of presenting otherwise |
| yat | yad | nom sg neut relative pronoun | whatever |
| ittham | ittham | indecl | in this way, thus |
| tat | tad | nom sg neut correlative pronoun | that |
| tathā | tathā | indecl | so, accordingly |
| yathā | yathā | indecl | as |
| sampratipannam | sam-pra-√pad | past passive participle, nom sg neut | presently admitted, established by agreement |

**Compound resolution (samāsa-vigraha):**
- *anārambhaṇīyam* [upapada/gerundival formation]: *na ārabdhaṃ yogyam*.
- *anyathā-pratipādakatvāt* [taddhita/tatpuruṣa]: *anyathā pratipādakasya bhāvāt*.

**Kāraka structure:**
- pakṣa: *vimatam*
- sādhya: *anārambhaṇīyam*
- hetu: *anyathā-pratipādakatvāt*
- vyāpti formula: *yad itthaṃ tat tathā*

**Verbal modality:**
- Elliptic inferential sentence; no finite verb is expressed.

**Note:**
This is the tract's governing form. Madhva does not begin by saying merely that Māyāvāda is false. He says it is not even a coherent thesis to initiate, because its own descriptive terms misdescribe their object. The inferential compression anticipates the more technical form of the later *Mithyātvānumāna-Khaṇḍana*.

### Nothing can serve as a covering ignorance (p. 238)

**Sanskrit (IAST):**
> *siddhatvāt svarūpasya viśeṣābhāvāc ca nājñānaṃ kasyacid āvarakam.*

## Editorial closing

This tract settles one limited but fundamental issue. It does not yet offer the expanded technical refutation of every formal *mithyātva*-inference; that is left to the *Mithyātvānumāna-Khaṇḍana*. Nor does it yet run the full dilemma against *upādhi*-mediation; that belongs to the *Upādhi-Khaṇḍana*. What it does settle is anterior to both. Madhva argues that once *ajñāna* cannot be coherently located, Māyāvāda loses not merely one supporting premise but its right to doctrinal commencement. The positive remainder is equally important: scriptural purport, read through *Bhagavad-Gītā* 15.16-20 and *Kaṭha Upaniṣad* 1.3.10-11, terminates in Viṣṇu's *sarvottamatva* rather than in an undifferentiated absolute. Later works pick up both sides of this result. *Upādhi-Khaṇḍana* attacks the conditioning-factor solution; *Mithyātvānumāna-Khaṇḍana* formalizes the inferential critique; *Tattvodyota* and *Anuvyākhyāna* restate the realist side by anchoring *bheda* as constitutive, not merely empirical. The tract is therefore brief, but not preliminary in the weak sense. It clears the ground on which the rest of the anti-Advaita corpus stands.
