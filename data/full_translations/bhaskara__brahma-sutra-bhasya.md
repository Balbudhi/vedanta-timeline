# Brahma-Sūtra-Bhāṣya

**By:** Bhāskara
**Genre:** *bhāṣya* on the *Brahma-Sūtra*

---

## Status

The Sanskrit text of Bhāskara's *Brahma-Sūtra-Bhāṣya* is not currently in our corpus. The Vedānta interactive timeline does not have a defensible Sanskrit witness on disk for this text, and producing an annotated translation would mean fabricating engagement we cannot ground.

What is widely known about this work:

- The bhāṣya is a complete commentary on Bādarāyaṇa's *Brahma-Sūtra*, structured in the standard four *adhyāyas* and sixteen *pādas*, dating to roughly the late eighth or early ninth century.
- Its philosophical signature is *aupādhika bhedābheda* (difference-and-non-difference under limiting adjuncts): the world is really differentiated from Brahman through *upādhis*, yet really non-different in essence. Bhāskara is sharply polemical against Śaṅkara's *māyā*-doctrine, which he reads as a Mahāyāna import alien to the *śruti*. He defends *jñāna-karma-samuccaya* (the conjunction of knowledge and ritual action) against Śaṅkara's exclusivist *jñāna-niṣṭhā*.

What is in our corpus that bears on it:

- Bhāskara's `core_thesis` and engaged_works summary in the timeline entry (see https://balbudhi.github.io/vedanta-timeline/ and locate Bhāskara).
- Surveys of Bhāskara's polemic appear in the secondary scholarship engaged in our Rāmānuja, Yāmuna, and Mandana files; later *bhedābheda* thinkers (Yādavaprakāśa, Nimbārka, Vijñānabhikṣu) all stand in some relation to Bhāskara's position.
