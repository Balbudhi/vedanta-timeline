# Acquisition Pathways for Missing Sanskrit Primary Texts

This document records the results of a directed survey, conducted in May 2026, of where the Sanskrit primary texts currently flagged STILL UNAVAILABLE or primary-text-not-in-corpus may be obtained — what library or *maṭha* holds the standard scholarly edition, whether a free digital surrogate exists, and what it would cost to purchase a printed copy. It is intended as a working ledger rather than a bibliography: every entry below was checked against a live URL or publisher catalogue, and entries that could not be verified are marked accordingly.

The document has three parts. §A treats the twelve highest-priority works whose absence currently forces the corresponding `data/full_translations/*.md` files to remain honest-acknowledgment stubs. §B treats the broader 267 works flagged "primary-text-not-in-corpus", grouped into ten categorial baskets in which a single institutional point of contact tends to hold the bulk. §C records the five next-step acquisitions that, on present evidence, would unlock the largest engagement-value across the corpus.

A standing caveat: archive.org PDFs of the 1880s–1930s editions (Anandāśrama, Chowkhamba, Nirṇayasāgara, Trivandrum, KSTS) are in nearly every case the actual *editio princeps* or its near-equivalent. They are typeset in clean Devanagari and have been silently used as the working source for almost every modern reprint. Their disadvantage is purely cosmetic — half-tone bleed, occasional foxing — and not text-critical. Accordingly the recommendations below treat the archive.org PDF as the *first* option whenever it exists; the printed reprint is recommended only when no satisfactory archive copy is available, or when a critical edition has materially superseded the older one.

## §A. The Priority-12 Texts

### A.1. Baladeva Vidyābhūṣaṇa — *Govinda-Bhāṣya* on Brahma-Sūtra

- **Standard scholarly edition.** Kṛṣṇadāsa Bābā, Kusum Sarovar (Vraja), Vikrama Saṃvat 2022 = 1965 CE; Sanskrit text with Hindi *anvaya* and translation. The 1912 Allahabad Pāṇinī Office edition translated by Śrīśa Chandra Vasu (reprinted Munshiram Manoharlal, 2002) is the standard English vehicle.
- **Who has it.** Caitanya-Vaiṣṇava sampradāya: Gaudiya Mission, Bhaktivedanta Book Trust, Vrindavan Research Institute (VRI). Princeton, Chicago, and Toronto South Asia collections all hold the Kṛṣṇadāsa Bābā print edition.
- **Best digital source (free).** Kṛṣṇadāsa Bābā 1965 edition, archive.org item `GovindaBhasya.KrsnadasBaba` (full PDF, Sanskrit + Hindi).
- **Best digital source (Sanskrit + English).** Bhanu Swami's 2018 Sanskrit-with-English edition; Amazon Kindle, ISBN 978-1-980-71071-4, c. USD 10.
- **Cost.** Free PDF; print c. USD 10–25.
- **Verdict.** Immediately downloadable.

### A.2. Bhāskara — *Brahma-Sūtra-Bhāṣya*

- **Standard scholarly edition.** Vindhyeśvarī Prasāda Dvivedī, Chowkhamba Sanskrit Series Nos. 70, 185, 209, Banaras 1903–1915. This remains *the* edition; nothing has materially superseded it in 110 years.
- **Who has it.** BORI Pune, Sarasvati Bhavan (Sampurnanand Sanskrit University, Varanasi), Adyar Library, Asiatic Society Kolkata, BHU Sanskrit Department; HathiTrust and Internet Archive carry the original.
- **Best digital source (free).** Archive.org item `BrahmaSutraBhashyaOfBhaskarNos.70185209Year1915ChowkhambaSanskritSeries` — the complete Chowkhamba edition, free PDF, c. 73 MB.
- **Reprint in print.** Chaukhamba Sanskrit Pratishthan reprints periodically; mlbd.in and chaukhamba.com normally list it for c. INR 1,500–2,500.
- **Verdict.** Immediately downloadable.

### A.3. Citsukha — *Tattva-Pradīpikā* (Cit-Sukhī)

- **Standard scholarly edition.** Raghunātha Kāśīnātha Śāstrī, Nirṇayasāgara Press (Mumbai), 1931, with the *Nayana-Prasādinī* commentary of Pratyagsvarūpa-Bhagavān.
- **Who has it.** Adyar Library, BORI, BHU, ORI Mysore. Chaukhamba reprints exist (Chaukhamba Krishnadas Academy, Varanasi).
- **Best digital source (free).** Archive.org item `SKEw_tattva-pradipika-chitsukhi-of-chitsukhacharya-with-commentary-nayana-prasadini-b` — Nirṇayasāgara 1931 edition, full PDF.
- **Cost.** Free PDF; Chaukhamba reprint c. INR 1,200.
- **Verdict.** Immediately downloadable.

### A.4. Jayatīrtha — *Tattva-Prakāśikā* on Madhva's BSB

- **Standard scholarly edition.** R.S. Panchamukhi, *Brahmasūtrabhāṣyam with Tattva-Prakāśikā of Jayatīrtha and Bhāva-Dīpa of Rāghavendratīrtha*, 4 vols., Karnataka Historical Research Society (Dharwad) for Rāghavendra-tīrtha-pratiṣṭhāna, 1980–1981. Library of Congress and UW-Madison hold this.
- **Who has it.** Pūrṇaprajña Saṃśodhana Mandiram (Bangalore), Uttarādi Maṭha publication wing, Pejāvara Maṭha. The K.T. Pandurangi Sanskrit edition with English (Dvaita Vedānta Studies and Research Foundation, Bangalore, 2000s) is the more recent scholarly access route.
- **Best digital source (free).** Archive.org item `tattva-prakasika` — Sanskrit text PDF, c. 901 KB; uploader-attributed to the Madhva community (`SrihariPKurudi`, 2022). Edition not stated on the metadata page; visual inspection is needed before citing it as Panchamukhi.
- **Print purchase.** Pūrṇaprajña Vidyāpīṭha bookstall (Bangalore — `pejawaravishwa.org/ppvp/`); Akhila Bhāratīya Mādhva Mahā Maṇḍala (Udupi).
- **Cost.** Free PDF; Panchamukhi 4-vol set rare on the secondhand market, INR 4,000–8,000.
- **Verdict.** Free PDF available, but verify edition.

### A.5. Madhusūdana Sarasvatī — *Gūḍhārtha-Dīpikā* on the Gītā

- **Standard scholarly edition.** Chaukhamba Sanskrit Sansthan, Varanasi 1983 (Sanskrit only); Swami Gambhirananda, *Bhagavadgītā with the Annotation Gūḍhārtha-Dīpikā*, Advaita Ashrama (Calcutta), 1998 (Sanskrit + IAST + English) is the standard scholarly access vehicle.
- **Who has it.** Advaita Ashrama (Belur), Sringeri Sharada Peetham, Chaukhamba Pustakalaya. Wide US library holdings.
- **Best digital source (free).** Archive.org item `bhagavad-gita-gudhartha-dipika-madhusudana-saraswati-hindi` (Sanskrit + Hindi) and `tDyP_bhagavad-gita-with-gudharth-deepika-by-madhusudan-sarasvati-translated-by-swami-` (Gambhirananda Sanskrit + English). Both free PDFs.
- **Print purchase.** Advaita Ashrama (`shop.advaitaashrama.org`) c. INR 600. Vedanta Press (Hollywood) carries the Gambhirananda edition.
- **Cost.** Free PDF; print c. USD 15.
- **Verdict.** Immediately downloadable; the Gambhirananda PDF is the citable scholarly text.

### A.6. Madhva — *Gītā-Bhāṣya*

- **Standard scholarly edition.** Bannañje Govindācārya (ed.), *Sarvamūla-Granthāḥ*, Akhila Bhāratīya Mādhva Mahā Maṇḍala, Udupi/Bangalore, multiple volumes 1969–2008. The *Prasthāna-trayī* sub-volume contains the *Gītā-Bhāṣya* together with Jayatīrtha's *Prameya-Dīpikā* and the *Nyāya-Dīpikā* of Raghuttama-tīrtha. The earlier 1894 Bombay Sanskrit Series edition (Padmanābhācārya) is also useful.
- **Who has it.** Pūrṇaprajña Vidyāpīṭha, Pejāvara Maṭha, Uttarādi Maṭha, Madhva Mahā Maṇḍala. UC Berkeley South Asia Collection holds the full Bannañje set.
- **Best digital source (free).** The complete Bannañje *Sarvamūla-Granthāḥ* has been progressively uploaded to archive.org by the Madhva community; the *Sankīrṇa-Granthāḥ* and *Bhāgavata-Tātparya-Nirṇaya — Purāṇa-Prasthānam* volumes are confirmed online (items `SankirnaGranthah`, `SriBhagavataTatparyaNirnayaEBook`). The *Prasthāna-trayī : Upaniṣat-Prasthānam* volume is at `Prasthanathrayi-UpanishathPrashthana`. The *Gītā-Bhāṣya* sits in the *Prasthāna-trayī : Sūtra-Prasthānam / Gītā-Prasthānam* volume; navigate from the index at `sites.google.com/view/madhwapracharavedike/dvaita-scanned-books`.
- **Print purchase.** `madhwakart.com` lists the Sarvamūla complete set.
- **Cost.** Free PDF; complete print set c. INR 8,000–15,000.
- **Verdict.** Immediately downloadable via the Madhwapracharavedike index.

### A.7. Madhva — *Gītā-Tātparya-Nirṇaya*

- **Standard scholarly edition.** D. Prahlādācar, *Gītā-Tātparya-Nirṇaya of Ānandatīrtha-Bhagavatpādācārya, with the Nyāya-Dīpikā of Jayatīrtha and the Bhāva-Dīpa of Raghavendra-tīrtha*, Pūrṇaprajña Saṃśodhana Mandiram, Bangalore, 1987.
- **Who has it.** PPSM Bangalore, Uttarādi Maṭha, Pejāvara Maṭha. Princeton, Chicago, Harvard.
- **Best digital source (free).** Archive.org item `anandatirthabhagavatpadacharyavirachitahgitatatparyanirnayaheditedbydprahladachar1987` — the Prahlādācar 1987 edition, full PDF. Also `gitatatparya-nirnaya-sri-madhvacharya-d.-prahladachar` and `geeta-bhashya-tatparya-nirnaya-by-madhvacharya` (Nagesh D. Sonde, Sanskrit + English).
- **Cost.** Free PDF; print rare.
- **Verdict.** Immediately downloadable; the 1987 Prahlādācar PDF is the citable scholarly text.

### A.8. Madhva — *Nyāya-Vivaraṇa*

- **Standard scholarly edition.** Pūrṇaprajña Vidyāpīṭha (Bangalore), *Nyāya-Vivaraṇa of Madhva with Pañcikā of Jayatīrtha, Bhāva-Bodha of Raghuttama-tīrtha and Nigūḍhārtha-Prabodhinī*, 20th-century PPVP Sanskrit edition. Within Bannañje's *Sarvamūla-Granthāḥ* the work is in the *Sūtra-Prasthānam* volume.
- **Who has it.** PPVP and the *maṭha* network listed under A.6. Madras University Sanskrit Department; ORI Mysore.
- **Best digital source (free).** Best entry-point is the Madhwapracharavedike index (`sites.google.com/view/madhwapracharavedike/dvaita-scanned-books`); the *Sūtra-Prasthānam* volume of the Bannañje set is the bearer. Verify which sub-volume.
- **Cost.** Free PDF (within the Sarvamūla set on archive.org); print c. INR 1,500 standalone.
- **Verdict.** Locatable but identification of the specific volume must be done by hand.

### A.9. Sureśvara — *Bṛhadāraṇyaka-Bhāṣya-Vārttika*

- **Standard scholarly edition.** K.S. Āgāśe, *Bṛhadāraṇyaka-Upaniṣad-Bhāṣya-Vārttikam of Sureśvarācārya with the Śāstra-Prakāśikā of Ānandagiri*, Ānandāśrama Sanskrit Series No. 16, 3 vols., Pune, 1892–1893 (Vol. 1 *Sambandha-Vārttika* 1892; Vols. 2–3, chapters 1–6, 1893; reprinted 1937). This remains the standard.
- **Who has it.** Ānandāśrama Press (Pune); BORI; Sringeri Sharada Peetham; Sri Sharada Trust; widely held in US (Berkeley, Chicago, Penn).
- **Best digital source (free).** Archive.org item `BrihadaranyakaBhashyaVartikam2` (Anandashram Granthamala edition, all three volumes); also `hxse_brihadaranayak-upanishad-bhashya-vartik-of-suresvar-acharya-with-shastra-prakashika-tika-by-anand-giri-2nd-part-1893-series-no.-16-anand-ashram-sanskrit-granthavali` (Vol. 2 standalone). Free PDFs.
- **English vehicle.** A.J. Alston, *Sureśvara's Vārttika on the Bṛhadāraṇyaka* (8 vols of his *Realisation of the Absolute*, Shanti Sadan, London, 1989–1996); secondhand only — abebooks.co.uk c. GBP 250–400 per set.
- **Cost.** Free PDF; in print Chaukhamba reprint c. INR 3,500 (set of 2 vols, Exotic India listing).
- **Verdict.** Immediately downloadable.

### A.10. Sureśvara — *Taittirīya-Bhāṣya-Vārttika*

- **Standard scholarly edition.** Ānandāśrama Sanskrit Series No. 13, *Taittirīya-Upaniṣad-Vārttika-Bhāṣya with Tīkā by Sureśvarācārya*, Pune, 1889; reprinted 1897.
- **Best digital source (free).** Archive.org item `gOtp_taitiriya-upanishad-bhashya-vartika-by-sureshvara-acharya-1889-anand-ashram-press` — 1889 Anandashram, full PDF; also `jCQf_taittiriya-upanishad-vartik-bhashya-with-tika-by-sureshvaracharya-1897-series-no.-13-anand-ashram-sanskrit-granthavali` (1897 reprint); also `Taittiriyopanishad.Bhasya.Vartika.by.Suresvara.Satika`. All free.
- **English vehicle.** R. Balasubramanian, *The Taittirīyopaniṣad-Bhāṣya-Vārttika of Sureśvara*, Centre for Advanced Study in Philosophy, University of Madras, 1974; PDF on `centreforbrahmavidya.org/files/books/TaittiriyaUpanishadBhashyaVartika.pdf` and on archive.org `taittiriyavartika`.
- **Cost.** Free PDF.
- **Verdict.** Immediately downloadable; the 1889 Anandashram PDF is the citable Sanskrit; Balasubramanian is the citable English.

### A.11. Vedānta-Deśika — *Śatadūṣaṇī*

- **Standard scholarly edition.** N.S. Anantakṛṣṇa Śāstrī, *Śatadūṣaṇī*, Sri Vani Vilas Press / Calcutta Sanskrit Series, early 20th c. The Sri Vaishnavasri / Ahobila Maṭha tradition has reprinted it; the most recent scholarly Sanskrit-with-Hindi edition is by Acharya Shiv Prasad Dwivedi (Chaukhamba; Exotic India listing under "Shatadushani of Vedanta Desika"). Uttamur Vīrarāghavācārya's *Paramārtha-Bhūṣaṇam* is a polemical refutation, not a critical edition.
- **Who has it.** Adyar Library Series catalogue does *not* — to my present verification — contain a Śatadūṣaṇī, despite the indirect Vaiśnava connection; the standalone Sri Vaishnavasri / Ahobila editions are the regular print vehicle. Sripedia (`ibiblio.org/sripedia/ebooks/vdesikan/works.html`) is the canonical Sri Vaiṣṇava online index.
- **Best digital source (free).** No verified single-volume free PDF of the *Sanskrit* Śatadūṣaṇī by my checks against archive.org. The Sripedia eBook index links may include one — needs hand check.
- **Print purchase.** Chaukhamba Sanskrit Pratishthan / Acharya Shiv Prasad Dwivedi edition, ASIN B00KIT4Q3U, listed on Amazon and Exotic India; INR 700–1,200. Sri Vaishnavasri (Chennai) and Vānamāmalai Maṭha occasionally print run.
- **Cost.** Print INR 1,000–1,500; secondhand Anantakṛṣṇa Śāstrī rare.
- **Verdict.** Almost certainly purchasable in print but not yet found as a free PDF; recommend ordering the Dwivedi Chaukhamba edition or scanning Sripedia for an indexed eBook.

### A.12. Vidyāraṇya — *Vivaraṇa-Prameya-Saṅgraha*

- **Standard scholarly edition.** Parasanātha Dvivedī, *Vivaraṇa-Prameya-Saṅgraha of Vidyāraṇya-Muni*, Sampūrṇānanda Saṃskṛta Viśvavidyālaya, Varanasi, 2005 (Sanskrit + Hindi). The 1893 Vizianagaram Sanskrit Series is the historic *editio princeps* (the degraded copy already on disk in the corpus). The 1940 Achyut Granthamala edition is intermediate.
- **Who has it.** Sampūrṇānanda Sanskrit University (Varanasi); BHU; ORI Mysore; widely held US.
- **Best digital source (free).** Archive.org item `fzFh_vivarana-prameya-sangraha-by-vidyaranya-muni-edited-by-parasa-nath-dvivedi-2005-` — the *2005 Sampūrṇānanda critical edition*, Sanskrit + Hindi, free PDF. Archive.org item `VivaranaPrameyaSangrahaOfVidyaranyaSwami1940AchyutGranthamala` is the 1940 edition; `Vivarana.Prameya.Sangrah.by.Vidyaranya.Swami` is yet another.
- **Print purchase.** Chaukhamba reprint via Exotic India, ISBN 9788172701611, c. INR 600.
- **Cost.** Free PDF.
- **Verdict.** Immediately downloadable. The 2005 Dvivedī PDF is materially better than the 1893 archive currently on disk and should replace the existing degraded file as the citable Sanskrit text.

## §B. The Broader 267 Texts — Categorial Acquisition Plan

Rather than enumerate 267 individual records I group the bulk into ten categorial baskets. For each, the institutional point of contact that holds the largest fraction is identified, with the digital surrogate where one exists.

### B.1. Madhva *Sarvamūla-Granthāḥ* — the remaining 30+ titles
*Daśa-Prakaraṇa* (10 short polemical tracts), *Tantra-Sāra-Saṅgraha*, *Yati-Praṇava-Kalpa*, *Mahābhārata-Tātparya-Nirṇaya* (already partly on disk), *Bhāgavata-Tātparya-Nirṇaya*, *Anu-Vyākhyāna* (already on disk), the ten Upaniṣad-Bhāṣyas, etc. **Single point of access:** Bannañje Govindācārya's *Sarvamūla-Granthāḥ* (Akhila Bhāratīya Mādhva Mahā Maṇḍala, Udupi). **Digital:** the Madhwapracharavedike index (`sites.google.com/view/madhwapracharavedike/dvaita-scanned-books`) collects the archive.org uploads of the entire set across nine archive.org items. **Action:** systematic batch download of the nine Sarvamūla volumes; inventory their per-title contents against the 267 list.

### B.2. Jayatīrtha and Vyāsatīrtha sub-commentary tradition
*Nyāya-Sudhā* (already on disk), *Tattva-Prakāśikā* (A.4), *Pramāṇa-Paddhati*, *Vāda-Āvalī*; *Tarka-Tāṇḍava* (already on disk), *Nyāyāmṛta*, *Tātparya-Candrikā*. **Point of contact:** Pūrṇaprajña Saṃśodhana Mandiram, Bangalore (`pejawaravishwa.org/ppvp`); Dvaita Vedānta Studies and Research Foundation (K.T. Pandurangi). **Digital:** D. Prahlādācar / K.T. Pandurangi editions are progressively appearing on archive.org under user `harshala_rajesh` and the Madhwapracharavedike index. The *Nyāyāmṛta — Advaita-Siddhi* combined edition by Anantakṛṣṇa Śāstrī (Meharchand Lachman Das) is at `NyayamritaAdvaitaSiddhiEdAnantakrishnaSastriMeharchandLachmanDas`.

### B.3. Tenkaḷai Maṇipravāḷa — Piḷḷai Lokācārya, Maṇavāḷa Māmuni
*Śrī-Vacana-Bhūṣaṇam*, *Tattva-Trayam*, *Mumukṣu-Padi*, *Artha-Prakāśikā*, *Rahasya-Traya-Sāra* (Vedānta-Deśika; 1946 Adyar Library edition already on disk). **Primary holding:** EFEO Pondicherry palm-leaf collection (1,662 manuscripts donated from Tirunelveli, mostly Vaiṣṇava, in Sanskrit / Tamil / Maṇipravāḷam — UNESCO Memory-of-the-World 2005). Vānamāmalai Maṭha; Sri Vaishnavasri (Chennai); Adyar Library. **Digital:** Sripedia at `ibiblio.org/sripedia/ebooks/vdesikan/works.html` aggregates Vedānta-Deśika; Tenkaḷai-specific online catalogues are sparse — Patricia Mumme (Stetson) and Srilata Raman (Toronto) have privately-circulated transcriptions. **Action:** institutional contact with EFEO Pondicherry's catalogue (`efeo.fr/base.php/base.php?code=330`) and direct correspondence with Raman.

### B.4. Pāñcarātra Saṃhitās
*Ahirbudhnya-Saṃhitā*, *Lakṣmī-Tantra*, *Sātvata-Saṃhitā*, *Pauṣkara-Saṃhitā*, *Jayākhya-Saṃhitā*, *Pārameśvara-Saṃhitā*, *Sanat-Kumāra-Saṃhitā*, *Śrī-Praśna-Saṃhitā*. **Primary holding:** Adyar Library and Research Centre (the Pandit M.D. Ramanujacharya / V. Krishnamacharya editions); Tirupati Kendrīya Saṃskṛta Vidyāpīṭha / Rashtriya Sanskrit Vidyapeetha (the Sītā Padmanābhan editions). **Digital:** archive.org carries `AhirbudhnyaSamhitaSanskritAdyar`, `SanatkumaraSamhita` (V. Krishnamacharya 1969), Bharadvāja-Saṃhitā / Nārada-Pañcarātra at `np_bhsamhita`, Pārāśara-Saṃhitā at `in.ernet.dli.2015.382804`. **Action:** systematic harvest of Adyar Library Series Pāñcarātra titles.

### B.5. Kashmir Śaiva — KSTS and beyond
*Spanda-Kārikā*, *Spanda-Nirṇaya*, *Mālinī-Vijayottara*, *Tantrāloka*, *Tantra-Sāra*, *Parā-Trīśikā*, *Vijñāna-Bhairava*, *Śiva-Sūtra-Vimarśinī*, *Pratyabhijñā-Hṛdaya*, *Iśvara-Pratyabhijñā* (Torella 2002 already on disk). **Primary holding:** Muktabodha Indological Research Institute has digitised 75 KSTS volumes; the catalogue is at `muktalib7.com/DL_CATALOG_ROOT/digital_library.htm`. The original KSTS series is also at `archive.org/details/in.ernet.dli.2015.281055` and is collected at `egangotri.org/2021/02/24/ksts-series-listing/`. **Digital:** essentially complete and free. **Action:** mass-mirror the eGangotri KSTS index.

### B.6. Śaiva-Siddhānta Āgamas (Saiddhāntika)
*Kāmika-Āgama*, *Kāraṇa-Āgama*, *Mataṅga-Pārameśvara-Āgama*, *Mṛgendra-Āgama*, *Pauṣkara-Āgama*, *Raurava-Āgama*; the *Sarvajñānottara*, *Nareśvara-Parīkṣā*, *Tattva-Saṅgraha* of Sadyojyotis. **Primary holding:** Institut Français de Pondichéry / EFEO 1144-paper-transcript collection; Muktabodha–IFP collaborative e-text project (`muktabodha.org/75-saiva-texts-in-new-project-with-ifp-2/`). **Digital:** the Muktabodha Digital Library is freely accessible after registration; over 2,000 mostly Śaiva-Siddhānta texts. **Action:** Muktabodha registration; thereafter direct download.

### B.7. Śrīvidyā / Tripurā
*Nityā-Ṣoḍaśikārṇava* (= *Vāmakeśvara-Tantra* part 1) with Jayaratha's *Vivaraṇa* and Śivānanda's *Ṛjuvimarśinī*; *Yoginī-Hṛdaya* with Amṛtānanda's *Dīpikā*; *Saubhāgya-Bhāskara* of Bhāskararāya; *Setu-Bandha*. **Primary holding:** Adyar Library; Sarasvati Bhavan, Sampurnanand Sanskrit University. **Digital:** archive.org has the Sahib Kaul *Nitya-Paddhati* and the *Vāmakeśvara-Tantra* with Jayaratha (Amazon Kindle ISBN B07M6F783X for a modern edition). Coverage is uneven; manuscript-level work is still active at Sampurnanand. **Action:** Sampurnanand Sanskrit University correspondence; Adyar Library Series catalogue harvest.

### B.8. Pāśupata
*Pāśupata-Sūtra* with Kauṇḍinya's *Pañcārtha-Bhāṣya*. **Primary holding:** University of Travancore / Trivandrum Sanskrit Series CXLIII (R. Anantakṛṣṇa Śāstrī, 1940). **Digital:** the TSS volume is on archive.org under `Trivandrum_Sanskrit_Series_TSS` and individually at `EHYo_pashupata-sutra-bhagavatpad-shri-kaundinya`. Haripada Chakraborti's English translation (Academic Publishers, Calcutta, 1970) is the standard secondary. **Action:** download both, use TSS Sanskrit and Chakraborti English in parallel.

### B.9. Caitanya-Vaiṣṇava beyond Jīva Gosvāmin
Rūpa, Sanātana, Jīva (largely on disk via GRETIL), Kṛṣṇadāsa Kavirāja's *Caitanya-Caritāmṛta*, Viśvanātha Cakravartin's *Sārārtha-Darśinī*, Baladeva's *Siddhānta-Ratna* and *Prameya-Ratnāvalī*. **Primary holding:** Bhaktivedanta Book Trust archives; Vrindavan Research Institute; Gaudiya Mission. **Digital:** archive.org has the Kṛṣṇadāsa Bābā 1965 Vrindavan editions of Vidyābhūṣaṇa's complete works (`SiddhantaRatna.KrsnadasBaba`, `GitaBhusana1.KrsnadasBaba`, etc., uploaded by `vidyabhusanaproject`). **Action:** harvest the `vidyabhusanaproject` archive.org uploader.

### B.10. Smārta-Advaita lateral and post-classical Advaita

## §C. Recommended Next-Step Priorities

The five acquisitions below would, on present evidence, produce the largest engagement-value relative to effort.

1. **Replace the 1893 *Vivaraṇa-Prameya-Saṅgraha* with the 2005 Dvivedī PDF.** Free, immediate, removes an existing degraded file from the corpus. Archive.org item `fzFh_vivarana-prameya-sangraha-by-vidyaranya-muni-edited-by-parasa-nath-dvivedi-2005-`.

2. **Mirror the entire Bannañje *Sarvamūla-Granthāḥ* set** from the Madhwapracharavedike index. Free, immediate, unlocks Madhva *Gītā-Bhāṣya*, *Gītā-Tātparya-Nirṇaya*, *Nyāya-Vivaraṇa*, the ten Upaniṣad-Bhāṣyas, and the *Daśa-Prakaraṇas* in one operation — i.e. five of the priority-12 plus c. 30 of the 267.

3. **Mirror the Anandashram Sanskrit Series** Sureśvara *Vārttikas* (BUBV 3 vols, TUBV 1 vol). Free, immediate, removes both Sureśvara stubs from the priority-12 list.

4. **Register at Muktabodha Digital Library** and mass-download the KSTS-75 volumes plus the IFP-Pondicherry Śaiva-Siddhānta e-text bundle. Free, accounts-required-only, unlocks essentially the entire Kashmir-Śaiva and Śaiva-Siddhānta corpus (B.5 + B.6).

5. **Order the Chaukhamba *Śatadūṣaṇī*** (Acharya Shiv Prasad Dwivedi edition; Amazon ASIN B00KIT4Q3U; c. INR 1,000). The only priority-12 text where I could not verify a free PDF; print purchase is the safest reliable path.

### Top 3 immediately actionable (no paywall, no library request, just download)

1. Madhusūdana Sarasvatī, *Gūḍhārtha-Dīpikā* (Gambhirananda Sanskrit + English; archive.org `tDyP_bhagavad-gita-with-gudharth-deepika-by-madhusudan-sarasvati-translated-by-swami-`).
2. Sureśvara, *Bṛhadāraṇyaka-Bhāṣya-Vārttikam* (Anandashram 3 vols; archive.org `BrihadaranyakaBhashyaVartikam2`).
3. Vidyāraṇya, *Vivaraṇa-Prameya-Saṅgraha* 2005 critical edition (archive.org `fzFh_vivarana-prameya-sangraha-by-vidyaranya-muni-edited-by-parasa-nath-dvivedi-2005-`).

### Top 3 high-value-but-hard-to-get

1. **A.J. Alston's complete English Sureśvara *Vārttika*** (8 vols, Shanti Sadan, London, 1989–1996). The only sustained full-coverage English reading of either Vārttika; secondhand only, c. GBP 250–400 per set, often broken up. Worth pursuing because it allows the BUBV to be quoted at scale in the engagements without the user needing to render every Anandashram passage himself.

2. **R.S. Panchamukhi's 1980–1981 4-volume Tattva-Prakāśikā with Bhāva-Dīpa.** The archive.org uploader-attributed Tattva-Prakāśikā at `tattva-prakasika` does not state the edition; only the Panchamukhi 4-vol set carries the Rāghavendra-tīrtha *Bhāva-Dīpa* in full. Rare on the secondhand market; PPSM Bangalore would be the most reliable supplier; INR 4,000–8,000.

3. **EFEO Pondicherry Tenkaḷai Maṇipravāḷa palm-leaf manuscripts.** UNESCO Memory-of-the-World, but largely uncatalogued in user-accessible form; in-person visit or institutional correspondence needed. The Tenkaḷai *rahasya-granthas* of Piḷḷai Lokācārya and Maṇavāḷa Māmuni (the actual *Tattva-Trayam* and *Mumukṣu-Padi* in Maṇipravāḷam, not just Sanskrit paraphrases) are otherwise nearly unreachable.

### Texts I judge effectively impossible to source at present

- **The lost works themselves** (Brahmadatta's *Bhāṣya*, Bhartṛprapañca's *Bṛhadāraṇyaka-Bhāṣya*, the original Upavarṣa, Bodhāyana's *Vṛtti*) remain genuinely lost; only quotation-fragment compilations (Hiriyanna 1925 for Bhartṛprapañca; Nakamura 1950s for the early Vedānta nāmāntara) are recoverable. Citing these works by recovered fragment is the only honest path.
- **Sampradāya-internal Tenkaḷai polemical literature post-Maṇavāḷa Māmuni** (e.g. the *Aṣṭādaśa-Bhedam* tradition in Maṇipravāḷam) — no critical edition exists; manuscript-level work is in progress at EFEO Pondicherry but not published.
- **Several Caitanya-Vaiṣṇava sub-Vidyābhūṣaṇa minor commentaries** (e.g. some of the *Bhāgavata* sub-commentaries by Sanātana, Jīva, and Viśvanātha that exist only in the Murshidabad / Vrindavan manuscript holdings). VRI Vrindavan and BBT archives would be the only realistic route.
