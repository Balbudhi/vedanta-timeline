# DEPTH-OF-ENGAGEMENT AUDIT v1

Audit of all 49 articles in `site/data/articles/source/` against the standard the user named: does the article walk the *natural unit* of the primary work it engages, or does it lean only on opening passages and programmatic claims?

Method. Each article was opened. The primary work it claims to engage was identified. The article's section structure was compared against the structure of the primary work (chapters / *adhyāya*-*pāda* / *kārikā*-ranges / part-numbers). Where the on-disk Sanskrit is the witness (the 14 `engagement-*` files), the article's own `Sampling and scope statement` was read and judged for honesty of scope. The classification A / B / C is applied per the brief.

- **A — Comprehensive.** Walks the full natural unit (whole work, whole *adhyāya*, whole sandarbha) with proportional engagement.
- **B — Partial with honest scope.** Engages a coherent sub-unit and explicitly flags the sub-unit. Not superficial; the limit is named.
- **C — Superficial.** Engages only opening passages or a handful of programmatic claims; the bulk of the cited work is left untouched without acknowledging the gap.

A separate flag **N/A — Architectural** is used for the two methodological frameworks that do not engage a primary work at all (`primitive-model`, `comparative-claims-framework`).
