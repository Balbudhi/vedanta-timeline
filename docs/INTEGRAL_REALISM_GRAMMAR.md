# Integral Realism Grammar

Created: 2026-06-18

This file formalizes the project direction after the source-ledger pass. It keeps the user's central thesis intact while avoiding an Advaita-only frame.

## Core Aim

The site should show how Indian traditions can be read as a structured field of real registers, not as a ladder where one school alone is true and the others are discarded errors.

The working thesis is:

> Advaita is true, but it is not the only true grammar. Other schools preserve real relations, modalities, practices, and standpoints that Advaita can obscure when it is read as a final formless monism rather than as one rigorous discipline of realization.

This must remain a research hypothesis until grounded passage by passage.

## Non-Negotiables

- Do not reduce Ramakrishna, Aurobindo, Chaudhuri, Banerji, Timalsina, Madhva, Rāmānuja, Gauḍīya Vaiṣṇavism, Mīmāṃsā, Tantra, or Kashmir Śaivism to "lower Advaita."
- Do not make `nirguṇa` the only truth and `saguṇa`, form, devotion, ritual, difference, and Śakti merely pedagogical leftovers.
- Do not flatten real doctrinal disagreement. Mark where schools actually contradict each other.
- Do not treat the user's synthesis as already proven by any single tradition.
- Do not use secondary scholarship as a substitute for primary evidence.
- Keep the grammar open enough to describe Buddhist, Jain, Nyāya, Sāṃkhya/Yoga, Śaiva, Śākta, Vaiṣṇava, Vedānta, and Mīmāṃsā sources without forcing them into Vedānta categories.

## Heliocentric / Geocentric / Third Model

Correction after deeper source search: the earlier note was too strong. Debashish Banerji explicitly uses the phrase "heliocentric age" in a 2025 SABDA-published essay/review context while interpreting Sri Aurobindo's yuga/time-structure. He ties the claim to Sri Aurobindo's *Kena and Other Upanishads*, especially "Notes on the Chhandogya Upanishad" pp. 263-264, and to *The Human Cycle* pp. 7-14.

What is confirmed so far:

- Primary Sri Aurobindo source: CWSA 18, *Kena and Other Upanishads*, "Notes on the Chhandogya Upanishad", pp. 263-264. Sri Aurobindo gives the yuga structure: in Satya, `yajna` reigns in the hearts of men and there is no need for external ritual, law, caste, class, or creed; in Treta and Dvapara, law, rule, codification, and formalization progressively appear; Kali destroys by questioning everything to establish a new harmony.
- Banerji's interpretive formulation: in the SABDA August 2025 piece, Banerji writes that "The Satya is the heliocentric age of Viṣṇu himself, residing in the hearts of all creatures." This is not merely our inference.
- Chaudhuri's confirmed correlate: *The Philosophy of Integralism* has a section titled "Ego-Centric and Cosmo-Centric Individuality." Chaudhuri defines ego-centric individuality as a product of Ignorance and cosmo-centric individuality as emerging when a person discovers the ultimate ground of existence, feels kinship with the universe, and acts from responsibility to the whole.
- Primary Sri Aurobindo adjacent analogue: CWSA 28, "Rebirth, Evolution, Heredity", uses the Ptolemaic theory as an example of a once-dominant scientific generalization passing away. This is not the yuga model, but it shows Sri Aurobindo using astronomical model-change analogically.
- Still unresolved: whether Sri Aurobindo himself uses the exact words `geocentric`, `heliocentric`, or `lunocentric` for this spiritual/historical model. A CWSA PDF term sweep did not find the exact words, but that is not final proof. Treat this as a source gap to keep searching, not as evidence against the user's memory.

Working definition:

- `geocentric / ego-centric`: the world is organized around the finite ego, its needs, its fears, its local standpoint, or its inherited conceptual frame. Chaudhuri's "ego-centric individuality" belongs here.
- `heliocentric / cosmo-centric / divine-centered`: the world is re-centered around the Divine, Truth, Brahman, Īśvara, Śiva, Kṛṣṇa, Mother, or the realized center that makes finite standpoints intelligible. Banerji's "heliocentric age of Viṣṇu" and Chaudhuri's "cosmo-centric individuality" belong here, though they are not identical terms.
- `mandalic / aperspectival / integral`: the third model. Multiple centers are not collapsed into one perspective; they are coordinated by register, adhikāra, practice, and manifestation. This is the model needed for "Advaita is true, but the others are also true."

The closest current source trail is Banerji -> Sri Aurobindo CWSA 18 / *The Human Cycle*. Vivekananda's geocentric/heliocentric analogy in `data/sources/english/vivekananda_complete_works/vol_3.txt` remains a useful local parallel, but it is no longer the primary lead.

## Tradition Mapping

| Tradition / figure | What it protects | How the grammar should represent it |
|---|---|---|
| Śaṅkara / Advaita | Identity-realization, negation as method, `adhyāsa`, formless truth, standpoint grammar. | Do not caricature as world-hatred. Distinguish practice-negation from final metaphysical contempt for form. |
| Satchidanandendra | Strict Śaṅkara method, criticism of mūlāvidyā as post-Śaṅkara, adhyāsa-centered reading. | Use to refine the avidyā model and to test whether "where does avidyā arise?" is an ill-posed question. |
| Madhusūdana | Advaita with bhakti and theological richness. | Use as proof that robust devotion need not be outside Advaita. |
| Rāmānuja / Viśiṣṭādvaita | Real qualified unity, world/body of God, devotion and surrender. | Model as real internal differentiation, not "almost Advaita." |
| Madhva / Tattvavāda | Real difference, hierarchy, service, anti-false-identification polemic. | Preserve realist difference as a register. Test whether polemics target actual Advaita or popular distortion. |
| Bhedābheda / Vijñānabhikṣu | Difference and non-difference together; synthesis across Sāṃkhya, Yoga, Vedānta. | Use `bhedābheda` and `avibhāga` as primary relations, not compromises. |
| Caitanya / Jīva Gosvāmin / Gauḍīya | `acintya-bhedābheda`, bhakti-rasa, Kṛṣṇa-centered divine plurality. | Treat `acintya` as a modal operator where logic must preserve relation without flattening paradox. |
| Ramakrishna | `vijñāna`, return from nirvikalpa to līlā, many bhāvas, many paths, personal/impersonal divine. | Central witness for form/formless non-duality and register pluralism after realization. |
| Sarada Devi | Universal motherhood, grace, world-as-one's-own, embodied tenderness. | Use to prevent abstract synthesis from losing relational and ethical embodiment. |
| Vivekananda | Four yogas, practical Vedanta, service, universal religion, Ramakrishna's plural realization. | Use for practice pluralism and public-facing philosophical grammar. |
| Sri Aurobindo | Realistic Advaita, Supermind/vijñāna, involution/evolution, integral transformation, Vedic symbolism. | Core modern source for manifestation and evolution without world-negation. |
| Haridas Chaudhuri | Integralism / integral Advaita around Aurobindo. | Treat as a direct modern precedent; extract exact thesis from private OCR before public claims. |
| Debashish Banerji | Integral Advaita, Aurobindo's yoga psychology, posthuman/integral praxis, Simondon/Deleuze bridges. | Treat as a major predecessor and interlocutor; distinguish his published model from the user's new grammar. |
| Medhananda / Ayon Maharaj | Ramakrishna's Vijñāna Vedanta, manifestationism, personal/impersonal Infinite Reality, religious pluralism. | Ally for Ramakrishna/Vivekananda; also a target for critique if he underreads Śaṅkara or Aurobindo. |
| Ramana | Self-inquiry, source of I-thought, turiya, surrender. | Use as direct identity-realization witness; do not reduce him to anti-bhakti or anti-world rhetoric. |
| Kashmir Śaivism / Trika | Śiva/Śakti, svātantrya, spanda, recognition, self-veiling, real manifestation. | Use as the strongest classical modality grammar. |
| Śākta / Sarvāmnāya / Timalsina | Śakti-centric form-realism, initiation, mantra, visualization, integration of darśanas and āgamas. | Needed so Tantra is not merely an Advaita appendix. Sarvāmnāya should be represented as all-transmissions integration after source grounding. |
| Mīmāṃsā | Injunction, ritual efficacy, apūrva, hermeneutics, externalized practice. | Use to test the user's Aurobindo/Mīmāṃsā symbolic-ritual hypothesis. |
| Sāṃkhya/Yoga | Discrimination, guṇas, prakṛti/puruṣa, samādhi, kaivalya. | Preserve a dualist/discriminative register without immediately assimilating it. |
| Madhyamaka / Yogācāra | Two truths, emptiness, appearance, construction of experience. | Useful comparator for appearance without crude nihilism. |

## Claim Matrix To Build

Every future thesis claim should become a row with these fields:

| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `claim_id` | Stable identifier, e.g. `IR-MAYA-001`. |
| `claim_text` | One concrete claim, not a paragraph. |
| `claim_type` | Primary textual claim, interpretive hypothesis, theological synthesis, product idea, or critique. |
| `traditions_involved` | Which schools/figures are implicated. |
| `source_requirements` | Exact sources needed before publication. |
| `evidence_status` | Missing, located, private-held, public-held, excerpted, verified. |
| `rights_status` | Public-domain, open-access, controlled-open, private/copyrighted, unknown. |
| `risk` | Where the claim could flatten, overstate, or misrepresent. |
| `site_target` | Article, perspective, graph primitive, thinker page, source note, or private note. |

## Immediate Claim Clusters

1. `IR-FORM-001`: Form and formlessness are not enemies; many traditions preserve their non-dual relation differently.
2. `IR-MAYA-001`: `māyā` is not mere nonexistence; it has different meanings across Advaita, Śaiva, Śākta, Aurobindo, and Ramakrishna contexts.
3. `IR-ADVAITA-001`: Advaita's negations often function as practice and standpoint grammar, not as a final hatred of God, world, or form.
4. `IR-VIJNANA-001`: Ramakrishna's `vijñāna`, Aurobindo's Supermind/vijñāna, and Medhananda's Vijñāna Vedanta overlap but are not interchangeable.
5. `IR-REALISM-001`: Madhva, Rāmānuja, Bhedābheda, Trika, and Aurobindo each protect a realism that should not be dismissed as lower truth.
6. `IR-RITUAL-001`: Mīmāṃsā and Aurobindo may be mapped as externalized and internalized sacrificial processes, but this is a hypothesis needing primary-source controls.
7. `IR-TANTRA-001`: Śiva/Śakti and Sarvāmnāya integration offer a modality grammar beyond Advaita-only identity.
8. `IR-PLURALISM-001`: `yata mat tata path` should not mean vague relativism; it should be tested as a disciplined plurality of real relations and adhikāras.
9. `IR-CENTERING-001`: Geocentric, heliocentric, and mandalic/integral centering may be the website's visual grammar. Banerji's heliocentric-language source is now located; the exact Aurobindo primary terminology and any Chaudhuri use still need deeper source verification.
